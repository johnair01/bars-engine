
# BARS Engine — v1 Onboarding Storytelling Grammar

This document defines the **narrative and structural grammar** for onboarding players into the BARS Engine ecosystem.

It is intended for:

- AI agents
- developers
- narrative designers
- campaign authors

The goal is to ensure that **all onboarding flows preserve the correct ordering of meaning** so that players understand the system at three levels:

1. Real-world stakes
2. Shared story container
3. Play mechanics

This grammar should guide the design of:

- onboarding sequences
- campaign introductions
- agent-generated orientation content
- tutorial flows
- narrative scaffolding

---

# Core Principle

Players must first understand **why the game exists**, then **what shared story they are entering**, and only then **how to act within it**.

This preserves motivation, coherence, and agency.

The correct order is:

1. **Why (Real-world stakes)**
2. **Where together (Shared story)**
3. **How (System mechanics)**

Only after these orientations should players begin the core gameplay loops.

---

# The Three Orientations

## Orientation 1 — Real-World Stakes

### Question Answered
Why does this system exist in the real world?

### Domain
Bruised Banana Residency Campaign  
The Allyship Game

### Purpose

This orientation grounds the player in the **actual human stakes** that the system is meant to address.

The player must understand:

- what real-world problems or needs the system engages
- why collaborative play is being used to address them
- why their participation matters

This stage establishes:

- emotional buy-in
- moral orientation
- relevance beyond the app

### Player Experience

Players should feel:

> "This matters outside the game."

### Design Guidelines

This orientation should:

- establish the mission clearly
- reference real-world contexts and stakes
- invite participation rather than lecture
- avoid overwhelming players with lore or mechanics

This stage should **not explain the system mechanics yet.**

---

## Orientation 2 — Shared Story Container

### Question Answered
Where are we entering together?

### Domain
The Construct Conclave  
The Story Engine

### Purpose

This orientation introduces the **collaborative narrative frame** that allows players to engage real-world challenges through shared imagination.

The story container:

- provides metaphor and symbolism
- creates group identity
- enables collaborative storytelling
- frames the kinds of challenges players will face

The story does not replace reality.

Instead it **metabolizes real-world stakes through narrative structure.**

### Player Experience

Players should feel:

> "I see the world we are entering together."

### Design Guidelines

This orientation should:

- introduce the narrative setting
- clarify the player's role within the story
- establish tone and expectations
- provide a sense of shared adventure

This stage should **not yet teach detailed mechanics.**

---

## Orientation 3 — Play Mechanics

### Question Answered
How do we actually move the story and real-world stakes forward?

### Domain
BARS System

Core components:

- BARs
- Emotional Alchemy
- 3-2-1 Process
- Vibeulons

### Purpose

This orientation teaches the **grammar of play**.

Players learn how their inner experience becomes game fuel.

Key ideas:

- insights can become artifacts
- emotional charge can become energy
- reflection can become movement
- actions generate progress in both the story world and the real world

### Player Experience

Players should feel:

> "Now I know how to contribute."

### Design Guidelines

This orientation should:

- explain the minimum necessary mechanics
- show examples of playable artifacts
- emphasize agency and participation
- avoid overwhelming players with full system complexity

Only the **minimum viable rules** should be introduced at this stage.

---

# Transition to Gameplay

After the three orientations, the player is ready to enter the core gameplay loops.

The onboarding should now move into **artifact creation and campaign play.**

---

# Core Gameplay Loops (v1)

## Loop 1 — Orientation

The player experiences:

1. Real-world stakes
2. Story container
3. System mechanics

Outcome:

Player understands the system's **purpose, world, and play grammar**.

---

## Loop 2 — Artifact Creation

The player practices introspection and generates their first **metabolizable artifact**.

Typical artifact types:

- BAR
- quest seed
- insight object
- charge observation

This proves that:

> lived experience can become playable material.

Outcome:

Player experiences the system's **transformational capability**.

---

## Loop 3 — Personal Campaign

The player enters a **quasi-procedurally generated personal campaign**.

The campaign adapts based on:

- artifacts created
- player feedback
- emotional signals
- archetypal resonance

This resembles an **Orb Evaluation style arc** where the system gradually reveals patterns and opportunities.

Outcome:

Player experiences the system as **responsive and personalized**.

The BARS Engine operates across three nested layers.

## Level 1 — Reality

What needs doing in the real world.

# Structural Sequence

The correct onboarding order is:

Real-world stakes  
↓  
Shared story container  
↓  
Play mechanics  
↓  
First artifact creation  
↓  
Personal campaign

This sequence ensures that:

- motivation precedes explanation
- imagination precedes rules
- agency emerges naturally

---

# Design Warnings

Avoid the following pitfalls.

### Lore Before Stakes

If the story appears before the real-world stakes, players may perceive the system as purely fictional.

### Mechanics Before Motivation

If the system mechanics appear before the mission, the experience may feel abstract or gamified without purpose.

### Overloaded Orientation

Each orientation must carry **one primary cognitive task**.

Do not attempt to combine all orientations into a single explanation.

---

# Agent Instruction

AI agents generating onboarding content should:

1. Maintain the orientation order
2. Preserve the distinction between reality, myth, and mechanics
3. Introduce concepts progressively
4. Avoid overwhelming players with system complexity

Agents should always verify:

- Why does this matter?
- What world are we entering?
- How does the player act?

---

# Summary

The onboarding grammar of BARS Engine is:

1. **Why — Real-world stakes**
2. **Where — Shared story container**
3. **How — System mechanics**
4. **Create — First artifact**
5. **Traverse — Personal campaign**

This structure ensures that the system remains:

- meaningful
- playable
- transformative
