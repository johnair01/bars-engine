## The ask

Design **the same single card in 3 different art directions** (Direction A, B, C below), presented
side by side for comparison. This is a *mood/style exploration*, not the final card. We will pick
one direction (or a blend) and then build a 120-card combinatorial system from it.

## The product (one paragraph)

The **Allyship Deck** is a consultable "spellbook for mastering allyship moves." You draw a card for
inspiration, or consult it to work through a real allyship situation. Each card names a **move** you
can make — for your own inner work or for a campaign helping others. The feeling we're selling is
**recoverable agency inside unconscious social reality** — the quiet relief of *"oh, I can play this
differently."* Grounded, human, warm, a little wry. **Never** shaming, preachy, purity-coded,
saccharine, dystopian, or AI-slick. Audience: emotionally literate organizers, artists,
facilitators, founders — Kickstarter backers.

> **Important sensitivity:** the community around this deck is wary of AI-generated art. Whatever the
> direction, it must read as **authored and hand-crafted** — intentional, textured, human — not
> generic or "AI-default." Lean into craft and material.

## The card to depict — `OPEN-GR-SHAMAN`, "The Empty Cup"

This is the deck's **emotional keystone**. Its layered meaning (depict the *feeling*, not a literal scene):

- **Operation: Shaman** — *Notice / Allow.* The one who names what's present. Stance: witnessing, unhurried, unarmored.
- **Move: Open Up** — *receive the charge; feel before fixing.* (The deck's tender move.)
- **Domain: Gather Resources** — need, asking, what's depleted.
- **Together:** sitting with the felt sense of lack/need **before** reaching for a plan or an ask.
- **Card title:** *The Empty Cup*
- **The question on the card (self):** "What does the lack actually feel like in my body, before I reach for a plan?"
- **The practice:** "Name the sensation of lack out loud — where it sits in the body — before you make a single ask."
- **Flavor line:** *"The child holding the empty cup is not a problem to solve yet. First, let yourself see the cup."*
- **Capabilities it restores:** Connection (Water) + Rest (Earth) → so the **accent palette leans Water/Earth** (see colors).
- **Central image cue:** *an empty cup / vessel* — held, waiting, not yet filled. Honest emptiness, not despair. Room, quiet, the pause before the ask.

## Shared constraints (apply to all 3 directions)

**Format & geometry**
- Card ratio **2.5 × 3.5 in (5:7)**. Present each at this ratio.
- Zone layout (leave room for live text — show it with placeholder type):
  - **Header strip (~8% top):** small glyph row — *operation · move · domain* marks.
  - **Art zone (~62%):** the illustration / motif. This is the focus.
  - **Title plate (~7%):** "The Empty Cup."
  - **Text zone (~23% bottom):** the question + practice (use placeholder lorem or the real lines).
- Also note how a **full-bleed** version would extend to **2.75 × 3.75** (0.125″ bleed) for print.

**Palette (canonical hexes)** — accent this card toward **Water** and **Earth**:
- Water (Connection): frame `#1a3a5c`, glow `#1a7a8a`
- Earth (Rest): frame `#b5651d`, glow `#d4a017`
- Frame furniture: deep green `#0F3B2F`, gold `#C9A84C`, cream `#F6F1E8`
- (Full element set for later: Fire `#c1392b`/`#e8671a`, Metal `#8e9aab`/`#bdc3c7`, Wood `#4a7c59`/`#27ae60`.)

**Three-channel encoding (house rule):** meaning must be carried by **≥2 channels** — *color* (element),
*border/frame* (intensity), *density/texture* (stage) — never color alone. Glyphs must be legible at
thumbnail size and in grayscale.

**Type:** pick type that fits each direction; the in-app reader uses serif display (Cormorant) + mono
labels, but you may propose alternatives per direction.

**Do:** warmth, texture, craft, breathing room, honest emotion, a little wit.
**Don't:** stock-photo realism, gradient-mesh "AI gloss," sad-clipart, corporate flat-vector sterility, trauma spectacle.

## The three directions

### Direction A — **Hand-Printed Folk Woodcut**
Bold relief-print / linocut illustration. Visible carving marks, slight mis-registration, a limited
ink palette (one or two inks) **+** the Water/Earth accent. Earthy, labor-print, almanac/tarot folk
warmth. The empty cup rendered as a strong carved silhouette held in cupped hands. Reads unmistakably
**hand-made, anti-slick, rooted.** *Refs: IWW/labor woodcuts, Lubok folk prints, almanac engravings,
a rougher Wild Unknown.*

### Direction B — **Painted Symbolist Gouache**
Soft painterly gouache, a single contemplative **still-life of the cup** — muted, tender, literary.
Quiet negative space, a faint inner glow in the Water/Earth accent, brush texture and paper grain.
Emotionally intimate, gallery-ish, slow. The cup as a meditative object; light and emptiness do the
work. *Refs: gouache botanical/still-life, Slow Holler tarot, the restraint of Hilma af Klint.*

### Direction C — **Modern Sigil / Geometric Glyphwork**
Confident flat graphic design: a constructed **emblem/sigil** for Shaman + Open Up + the cup —
geometric, riso-textured, strong type, minimal illustration. Systematic and scalable (built to
combine cleanly across 120 cards), dodges figuration/identity pitfalls. The cup abstracted into a
geometric vessel mark; the operation/move as composable glyphs. *Refs: risograph prints, Bauhaus
tarot, modern brand/identity systems, the existing card-token minimalism.*

authenticity**, thumbnail + grayscale legibility, and **systematizability to 120 cards**. Pick the
direction that best balances soul and scale.
