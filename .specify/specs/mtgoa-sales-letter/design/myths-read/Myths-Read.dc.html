E · Map metaphor — three ways to draw it You asked to see a few. The book-spread above uses Inner Garden (my lean — root→growth→fruit is belief→myth→resolution). Two alternates below.
Inner Garden Chosen

Seeds (belief) underground, stems (myth) at the surface, fruit (resolution) in the light. Ties to the Inner Garden world; the alive/stuck logic is literal — buried vs. blooming.

Hexagram lattice Alt

The six outer trigram lines = the six beliefs; inner cells = myths keyed to the deck's gates. Most on-brand to the cards, but the belief→myth→resolution arc is harder to read at a glance.

Arcade board Alt

A token snakes a path, lighting myth-tiles it lands on, cashing out at the resolution. Most "arcade, not module," most playful — but risks the Buzzfeed optics the counter-con warns against.
