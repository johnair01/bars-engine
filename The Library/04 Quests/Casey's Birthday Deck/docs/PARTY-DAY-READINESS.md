# Valkyrie Party Day Readiness

## Goal

Get the Valkyrie party app emotionally aligned, usable, and ready for guests today.

The app needs to feel like a Valkyrie-specific ritual party space, not a lightly renamed Casey deck.

## Today-Critical Priorities

### 1. Valkyrie Visual Theme

Need:

- color palette aligned with Valkyrie's vibe
- card back/front atmosphere aligned with the party
- typography/spacing still readable on phones
- public app chrome that feels like shared magic altar, not admin dashboard

Minimum viable today:

- update page colors and labels
- choose 1 visual direction
- apply theme consistently to party page panels, buttons, modal, and card staging

Better if time:

- custom card back
- custom suit accents
- public altar visual treatment

### 2. Prompt Reassessment

Need:

- prompts aligned with Valkyrie
- prompts aligned with the party mood
- prompts that are answerable in a social/party setting
- fewer prompts that feel too intense, clinical, or mismatch the room

Minimum viable today:

- run a pass over the 52 hard prompts used in party mode
- convert them into party-safe prompts
- preserve the original card titles/art for now

Prompt design constraints:

- specific enough to produce interesting answers
- safe enough for mixed party guests
- emotionally real without forcing disclosure
- works for public altar or private card-mail

### 3. Card Art

Need:

- art aligned with Valkyrie's vibe
- composited into card fronts
- consistent crop and card readability

Minimum viable today:

- create a smaller set of hero/anchor art assets first
- use them as card image replacements for most important cards or suits
- keep Casey-derived art where replacement would risk quality

Better if time:

- generate 4 suit-level images
- generate 12-13 key card images
- full 52-card art pass

Risk:

- full 52-card art replacement is the highest time-risk item.

### 4. Immersive Party Story

Need:

- an opening premise
- a reason to shuffle/draw/send/answer
- a reason public altar posts matter
- a simple progression guests can understand

Minimum viable today:

- one opening story block on the page
- one short orientation modal
- story beats tied to app areas:
  - Shuffle: receive a sign
  - Send card: pass a spark
  - Inbox: answer a summons
  - Altar: leave an offering
  - Keepsake deck: gather what touched you

### 5. Orientation Documentation And Features

Need:

- first-time guest guidance
- social norms around private/public sharing
- simple explanation of discovery, inbox, altar, and personal deck

Minimum viable today:

- first-launch orientation modal
- small "How this works" panel
- short privacy/social rule text

Essential orientation copy:

```text
Draw cards to discover them.
Send cards to friends when a prompt feels like theirs.
Answer cards sent to you; your answer returns to the sender.
Use the Shared Altar for public notes, blessings, memories, jokes, and offerings.
Private card answers are for the people in that exchange. Do not repost someone else's private answer publicly unless they have said yes.
Save public notes or player cards that touch you into your keepsake deck.
```

## Recommended Today Build Order

1. Lock Valkyrie's vibe in 5-7 words.
2. Apply visual theme to the party page.
3. Add orientation modal and story block.
4. Rewrite prompt layer for party-safe Valkyrie prompts.
5. Add or prepare art assets.
6. Smoke test guest flow on phone:
   - join
   - shuffle
   - reveal
   - send card
   - answer card
   - post altar note
   - save to deck

## Questions To Answer Before The Next Build Pass

### Valkyrie's Vibe

- What 5-7 words describe Valkyrie's vibe?
- Is the aesthetic more celestial, gothic, fae, cyber, mythic, cozy, punk, oceanic, jewel-toned, soft, sharp, funny, dramatic?
- Any colors to use or avoid?
- Any symbols/motifs that feel right?
- Any symbols/motifs that are wrong?

### Party Vibe

- Is this party intimate, chaotic, sacred, silly, glamorous, cozy, theatrical, game-night, or ritual-night?
- What should guests feel permission to do?
- How intense should prompts get?

### Prompt Boundaries

- Are romantic/flirty prompts allowed?
- Are vulnerable prompts allowed?
- Are roast/joke prompts allowed?
- Should prompts be aimed at Valkyrie, other players, or the person drawing?

### Art Scope

- Do we need full 52-card art today, or is a strong smaller art pass acceptable?
- Should art be portrait characters, symbolic objects, scenes, abstract magic, or party artifacts?

### Story

- What is the party premise?
- Is Valkyrie the hero, host, deity, birthday sovereign, oracle, adventurer, or something else?
- Are guests companions, petitioners, spirits, party knights, witnesses, or co-conspirators?

## Launch Definition

The app is ready for the party when:

- the first screen feels like Valkyrie
- a new guest can understand what to do in under 60 seconds
- the card flow works
- the public/private boundary is explained
- prompts feel socially usable
- at least the visible card/art/theme choices do not feel like Casey's party wearing Valkyrie's name
