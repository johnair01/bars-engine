# Allyship Superpowers

## Canonical Claim

An **allyship superpower** is the distinctive capacity a person brings to allyship because of their backstory, practiced gifts, survival intelligence, and playstyle.

In the MTGOA RPG, superpower is separate from team Role.

- **Team Role** answers: What function does the crew tend to rely on you for?
- **Superpower** answers: What extraordinary capacity do you bring into the fiction?
- **Belief** answers: What false story does pressure activate?
- **School** answers: How are you learning to master the gift under pressure?

Superpowers are not moral rankings, jobs, or fixed identities. They are gifts with burdens. Each can become clean power, overused reflex, or shadow strategy depending on consent, context, and maturity.

## The Seven Superpowers

| Superpower | Core Gift | Clean Use | Shadow / Overuse |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Connector** | Builds trust, belonging, relational circuits, and bridges across difference. | Creates intentional spaces where people, resources, and insight can meet. | Over-connects without consent, confuses access with intimacy, becomes the social glue at personal cost. |
| **Strategist** | Sees the board, hidden constraints, leverage points, and future consequences. | Names the right sequence of action and helps others move with clarity. | Becomes cold, controlling, over-optimized, or resentful when others do not see what they see. |
| **Disruptor** | Interrupts stuck patterns, false inevitability, silence, and compliance. | Breaks the spell of "this is just how things are" so new action becomes possible. | Confuses rupture with change, escalates for identity, or damages trust the mission still needs. |
| **Escape Artist** | Finds exits, loopholes, routes, options, and sovereignty under constraint. | Helps people move through traps without surrendering agency or imagination. | Avoids commitment, slips accountability, or treats every container as a cage. |
| **Catalyst** | Sees latent capacity and helps people practice into power. | Builds courage, skill, momentum, and agency through timely challenge and support. | Optimizes everyone else, over-coaches, or makes other people's growth a hiding place from their own helplessness. |
| **Alchemist** | Transforms adversity, grief, conflict, failure, and stuckness into usable capacity. | Metabolizes pain into growth, ritual, repair, and renewed motion. | Romanticizes suffering, over-processes, or turns every wound into curriculum before people are ready. |
| **Storyteller** | Shapes meaning, memory, morale, narrative, and public imagination. | Gives experience an arc that helps people understand, endure, and act. | Turns pain into performance, simplifies complexity, or makes the story more important than the people inside it. |

## Design Distinctions

## Superpower vs Team Role

The old MTGOA material often blends role, playstyle, and superpower. For the RPG, keep them distinct.

Example:

- A student may be a **Builder** as their team Role because the crew relies on them to make insight usable.
- The same student may be an **Escape Artist** as their superpower because their mythic gift is finding exits from systems designed to hold people in place.

This lets the character sheet preserve both ensemble function and personal myth.

## Superpower vs House

Houses are practice factions.

- Provisioners move resources.
- Weavers build structure.
- Linekeepers interrupt harm.
- Lanternbearers make truth visible.

Superpowers cut across Houses. A Connector Provisioner and a Strategist Provisioner both work with resources, but they notice different openings and create different risks.

## Superpower vs Belief

Beliefs are pressure patterns. Superpowers are gifts.

They can be related without being the same thing.

Example:

- Belief: **I'm Not Capable**
- Superpower: **Catalyst**
- Wound distortion: improves everyone else so they never have to feel their own helplessness
- Clean use: builds capability without making other people's growth a substitute for their own

## Common Belief / Superpower Pairings

These are not mandatory assignments. They are the school's most common admission patterns: the places where a wound often grows a gift around itself.

| Self-Sabotage Belief | Common Superpower | Wound Logic |
|---|---|---|
| **I Don't Belong** | **Connector** | If I can create belonging for others, maybe I can earn mine. |
| **I'm Not Ready** | **Strategist** | If I can plan enough, I never have to risk beginning unprepared. |
| **I'm Not Worthy** | **Disruptor** | If I can expose the gatekeepers as false, I do not have to admit I wanted their blessing. |
| **I'm Not Capable** | **Escape Artist** | If I can always find a way out, I never have to discover whether I could stay and act. |
| **I'm Not Capable** | **Catalyst** | If I can make everyone else more capable, I never have to feel my own helplessness directly. |
| **I'm Not Good Enough** | **Alchemist** | If I can transform every failure into growth, maybe inadequacy never gets the final word. |
| **I'm Insignificant** | **Storyteller** | If I can make meaning of what happened, maybe I can prove it mattered, and that I did too. |

Design note: **Escape Artist** and **Catalyst** share the same root belief but turn it in opposite directions. Escape Artist says, "Capability means finding the exit." Catalyst says, "Capability means helping power come online." Both can be clean. Both can become hiding places.

## Superpower Mechanics For The RPG

Each superpower should eventually support:

- **Clean Use**: what the gift does when bounded and consent-aware
- **Wound Distortion**: how the gift behaves when driven by a self-sabotaging belief
- **Power Move**: an earned move activated with Adversity
- **School Training Hooks**: how each School teaches the gift to mature
- **House Expression Hooks**: how the gift shows up in each House domain

## Belief Move Pattern

The current RPG ruling:

- **Wound Move**: easier in the moment; creates long-term consequences.
- **Growth Move**: costs in real time; creates sustainable outcomes.
- **Power Move**: earned and bought or activated with Adversity tokens.

Power Moves should feel like the superpower becoming clean, bounded, and useful because the student has metabolized failure into skill.

## Superpower Sketches

## Connector

Connector power lives in trust, translation, welcome, belonging, and relational architecture.

Clean Connector questions:

- Who needs to meet, but has not found the bridge yet?
- What relationship already carries enough trust to move this safely?
- Where is belonging being offered without consent?
- Who is being connected to labor, visibility, or risk they did not choose?

RPG hooks:

- Creates temporary Bonds.
- Transfers Trust across a bridge with conditions.
- Opens a scene between factions that would not normally speak.
- Risks overexposure, obligation, and emotional overfunctioning.

## Strategist

Strategist power lives in pattern recognition, sequencing, constraint design, and leverage.

Clean Strategist questions:

- What is the hidden structure beneath this pattern?
- Where is energy leaking?
- What move changes the board without making people into pieces?
- What is the smallest sequence that creates real motion?

RPG hooks:

- Reveals a mission clock's hidden step.
- Converts Clarity into a concrete plan.
- Lets the crew act with better sequence.
- Risks control, analysis as avoidance, and social punishment for visible clarity.

## Disruptor

Disruptor power lives in interruption, provocation, rupture, and refusal of false inevitability.

Clean Disruptor questions:

- What pattern is pretending to be natural?
- What silence is protecting harm?
- What can be interrupted without abandoning repair?
- What becomes possible if the room stops complying?

RPG hooks:

- Interrupts a clock advance.
- Breaks a false public story.
- Forces a hidden conflict into the open.
- Risks escalation, spectacle, and burning needed bridges.

## Escape Artist

Escape Artist power lives in exits, improvisation, evasion, sovereignty, and route-finding.

Clean Escape Artist questions:

- Where is the exit no one was taught to look for?
- What constraint is real, and what constraint is theater?
- Who needs options before they can consent?
- What would let people leave, pause, or refuse safely?

RPG hooks:

- Finds an alternate route through a mission field.
- Creates a way out of coercive terms.
- Protects agency when systems try to trap people in false choices.
- Risks avoidance, commitment panic, and treating every agreement as captivity.

## Catalyst

Catalyst power lives in activation, practice, encouragement, capacity-building, and developmental heat.

Clean Catalyst questions:

- What capacity is trying to come online here?
- What practice room would make this feel possible?
- Who needs encouragement, and who needs a clean challenge?
- Where am I improving others so I do not have to feel my own helplessness?

RPG hooks:

- Helps another student unlock a temporary bonus after practice, reflection, or risk.
- Turns failure into a training scene with a concrete next attempt.
- Names the smallest brave rep that builds real capability.
- Risks over-coaching, optimization as control, and making other people's growth carry their self-worth.

## Alchemist

Alchemist power lives in metabolizing adversity, transforming emotional residue, and turning stuckness into renewed capacity.

Clean Alchemist questions:

- What is this pain becoming if we do not tend it?
- What can be composted into learning, ritual, repair, or resource?
- What must not be transformed yet because it still needs witness?
- What capacity is trying to emerge from this failure?

RPG hooks:

- Converts a consequence into Growth when the table names what changed.
- Turns a failed move into a ritual, resource, or repair scene.
- Helps the crew recover from Tension without pretending nothing happened.
- Risks romanticizing harm, overprocessing, and making people perform transformation.

## Storyteller

Storyteller power lives in narrative, memory, witness, morale, public meaning, and arc.

Clean Storyteller questions:

- What story is currently organizing behavior?
- Whose story is being told without consent?
- What memory needs protection?
- What narrative would help people act without flattening the truth?

RPG hooks:

- Reframes a scene so an ally can act.
- Protects an archive, testimony, or public truth.
- Builds morale through meaningful memory.
- Risks spectacle, simplification, and making narrative clarity more important than material change.
