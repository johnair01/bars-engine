# School Sheet - School of the Line

Face: **Challenger**

Owned Stat: **Act**

Milestone question:

**Can you act without becoming cruel?**

Core lesson:

Allyship sometimes requires the willingness to be unwelcome.

## Vibe

The School of the Line trains in sparring rings, debate pits, emergency drills, boundary courts, and red-lit halls where students learn to move before consensus arrives.

It feels like firebending discipline, X-Men danger room, strike-team ethics, and the awful clean moment when someone finally says no.

## Head of School

Name: **Head Corin Ash**

Title: Master of the Clean No

What they embody:

Decisive action without cruelty.

Teaching style:

Corin interrupts students mid-explanation and asks what they are protecting. If they cannot answer, the exercise starts over.

Blind spot:

They can mistake hesitation for cowardice.

Signature line:

"A line is not a tantrum. Draw it clean or do not draw it yet."

## Lieutenants

### Rook Vey

Title: Drill Captain

Subskill: Timely interruption

What they teach:

Students learn to interrupt harm while the moment is still open.

How they test students:

Rook runs simulations where waiting one more round changes who pays.

Shadow version:

Interrupting to feel powerful.

Move or training prompt:

**Interrupt the Pattern:** When you interrupt harm in progress, roll + Show Up. On a hit, harm is interrupted. On a 7-9, name who becomes upset with you.

### Mara Flint

Title: Boundary Advocate

Subskill: Clean refusal

What they teach:

Students learn to say no without apologizing, overexplaining, or escalating.

How they test students:

Mara asks students to refuse a beloved authority figure three times, each time with fewer words.

Shadow version:

Using boundaries to avoid relationship.

Move or training prompt:

**Say the No:** When you name a real boundary plainly, gain 1 Trust with anyone who needed the clarity.

### Dev Halberd

Title: Consequence Tutor

Subskill: Cost reading

What they teach:

Students learn to measure what action will cost before pretending cost does not matter.

How they test students:

Dev gives students three possible interventions and asks who pays in each version.

Shadow version:

Treating cost as proof of righteousness.

Move or training prompt:

**Count the Cost:** Before Direct Action, name one cost to yourself, one cost to someone vulnerable, and one cost to the mission. If you still act, take +1 forward.

## Trial

The student must interrupt a harmful pattern while preserving the possibility of repair.

They pass if the line protects something specific.

## Shadow Exam

The student is given a scene where fighting would feel satisfying but would harm the people they intend to protect.

They pass by finding the smaller, cleaner intervention.

## Mark / Badge

The **Red Thread**, tied around the wrist after the student draws a clean line.

## Move Unlock: Hold the Line

When you hold a boundary under pressure, roll + Grow Up.

On a 10+, choose 2.

On a 7-9, choose 1.

On a 6-, you hold the line but create a repair need.

Options:

- The boundary is respected for now.
- Someone vulnerable gains room to choose.
- You avoid unnecessary cruelty.
- You learn what the opposition actually wants.

## Downtime Training

During downtime, train with the School of the Line to:

- rewrite your Line with sharper language
- clear Tension by saying the avoided no
- prepare for a confrontation
- learn who benefits from your hesitation

## Campaign Use

Use this School when:

- the table is avoiding necessary action
- a mission needs boundary drama
- a character confuses niceness with care
- escalation and restraint are both live options
