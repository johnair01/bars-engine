# Chapter 4 Source Gap Spec - Core Moves v0.1

Status: implementation spec

Purpose: define how to turn `Core Moves and Dice - v0.4.md` into a book-ready Chapter 4 that teaches play procedure, dice, moves, and tokens in the Regent/manual voice.

Target outputs:

- `Chapter 4 Prose Draft - Core Moves and Dice v0.1.md`
- `BARs Engine Spec - Move Resolver v0.1.md`

## Core Goal

Chapter 4 must prove the game is playable.

Chapter 1 invited the reader aboard. Chapter 2 built the table and ship. Chapter 3 admitted the student. Chapter 4 must teach what happens when that student acts under pressure.

The reader should finish Chapter 4 feeling:

1. I know when to roll.
2. I know which Basic Move is happening.
3. I know which stat applies.
4. I know what 10+, 7-9, and 6- mean.
5. I know how benefits, costs, Adversity, Clarity, Tension, and Growth change play.
6. I can watch a fictional moment become a move without losing the story.

## Source-Informed Requirements

## Dungeon World Requirement: Fiction First, Moves Triggered By Action

The player does not press a move button.

The student does something in the fiction. If that action fits a Basic Move and the outcome is risky or uncertain, the table rolls.

Implement:

- clear "conversation of play" explanation
- move trigger examples
- same action can trigger different moves depending on intent
- same move can use different stats depending on approach

Acceptance criteria:

- The chapter explains that fictional action triggers moves.
- Players cannot use a move without describing what their student does.
- The chapter includes at least one table dialogue showing a move trigger.

## Kids on Bikes Requirement: Flexible Approach And Useful Failure

Kids on Bikes helps players accept uncertainty by making adversity useful. MTGOA should preserve that lesson without making Adversity the whole advancement system.

Implement:

- Adversity as short-term leverage
- Growth as long-term advancement
- failure as truth revealed, not humiliation
- flexible stat choice when multiple approaches fit

Acceptance criteria:

- 6- gives Adversity and changes the fiction.
- Naming impact after a miss can mark Growth.
- The chapter distinguishes Adversity from XP/Growth.

## Hearts Blazing Requirement: Handles For Spotlight And Scene Flow

Moves should make scenes easier to play, not harder to enter.

Implement:

- clear move cards / quick reference language
- examples of benefits and costs as table-facing handles
- visible token pool
- prompts the Guide can ask when the table stalls

Acceptance criteria:

- Each Basic Move has a concise "use when" statement.
- Each Basic Move has selectable benefits and example costs.
- Chapter shows how a cost creates the next scene beat.

## Foundations Requirement: Procedure Creates Artifacts

Every roll should leave a mark somewhere.

Implement:

- Mission Field Map changes
- Crew Sheet updates
- Student Record token updates
- Chronicle hooks after major rolls
- suggested app/event log fields

Acceptance criteria:

- The chapter says what to update after a roll.
- Tokens are not abstract; they are visible state.
- Move resolution produces recordable changes.

## Voice Requirements

Use Regent voice:

- wry
- procedural
- precise
- practical under pressure
- aware that the Headmaster's philosophy needs tables, not fog

Use Council of Joannes marginalia to:

- warn against rolling for everything
- puncture noble language
- clarify costs
- remind players that 6- is not "you are bad"
- call out common table failure modes

Marginalia density:

- 1 note in the opener
- 1 note in move trigger section
- 1 note in token section
- optional notes in Basic Move sections

## Proposed Chapter Structure

## Page 1: The Conversation Of Play

Function:

- explain the core loop
- set tone for action under pressure

Include:

1. Guide describes charged situation.
2. Player says what their student does.
3. Table checks whether a Basic Move triggers.
4. If risky and uncertain, roll.
5. Roll changes situation.
6. Guide asks, "What do you do now?"

## Page 2: When To Roll

Function:

- prevent over-rolling
- preserve fiction

Roll when a move could change:

- trust
- safety
- resources
- consent
- pressure
- belonging
- leverage
- mission clocks
- faction heat
- student growth

Do not roll for ordinary competence or harmless color.

## Page 3: Basic Moves At A Glance

Moves:

- Wake Up: understand what is really happening.
- Open Up: risk honest contact and receive support.
- Clean Up: repair harm and restore integrity.
- Grow Up: build capacity under pressure.
- Show Up: take action that changes the situation.

## Page 4: Stats As School Disciplines

Stats:

- Sense
- Act
- Steady
- Shape
- Tend
- Speak

Explain:

- Basic Move = kind of moment
- stat = discipline used to move through the moment

## Page 5: Choosing Move And Stat

Function:

- teach table negotiation

Include examples:

- public truth could be Wake Up, Grow Up, or Show Up
- private vulnerability could be Open Up, Grow Up, or Clean Up
- private check-in could be Wake Up, Open Up, Clean Up, or Show Up
- resource movement could be Show Up + Tend or Show Up + Shape

## Page 6: The Roll

Use:

**Roll 2d6 + stat.**

Results:

- 10+: choose 2 benefits
- 7-9: choose 1 benefit; Guide adds 1 cost
- 6-: Guide makes a move; gain 1 Adversity; mark Growth if you name impact honestly

## Pages 7-11: The Five Basic Moves

Each move needs:

- trigger
- result structure
- questions or options
- benefits
- costs
- example using Mara / dry-river mission
- one "update the artifact" line

## Page 11: Tokens

Tokens:

- Adversity
- Clarity
- Tension
- Growth

Explain:

- what earns it
- how it is spent or cleared
- where it is recorded

## Page 12: Same Move, Different Stats

Show:

- Wake Up + Sense
- Wake Up + Shape
- Open Up + Steady
- Open Up + Tend
- Show Up + Act
- Show Up + Tend
- Clean Up + Speak
- Grow Up + Steady

## Page 13: Guide Principles For Rolls

Use existing principles:

- Make the move follow the fiction.
- Ask what the student is actually doing.
- Let players be creative with stats, but make the approach visible.
- Give every 7-9 both success and consequence.
- Make 6- reveal truth, not just failure.
- Put costs where the fiction already has pressure.
- Keep consent visible.
- Reward repair more than performance.
- Let the world change.

## Page 14: Quick Reference

Create a concise reference spread for:

- roll ladder
- Basic Moves
- stats
- token meanings

## Running Example Requirements

Use Mara Venn from Chapter 3 in the dry-river mission.

Add at least two crew members:
