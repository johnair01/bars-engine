# House Sheet - Linekeepers

Domain: **Direct Action**

Core question:

**What line must be drawn, and what will it cost?**

## Vibe

Boundary guardians, strike teams, interrupters, oathbreakers when the oath has become rotten, people who know the room will call it rude when harm finally gets named.

Linekeepers are the House of decisive interruption.

They act when delay becomes complicity.

## Motto

**A clean no protects the yes.**

## Symbol

A bright line across an open hand.

## Gift

Linekeepers can move when everyone else is waiting to feel ready.

They understand that some moments close if no one acts.

## Shadow

Linekeepers can become addicted to escalation.

They may confuse intensity with courage, purity with clarity, or confrontation with change.

Shadow tells:

- "If you are not with me, you are against me."
- "The cost proves it was real."
- "Burn it down."
- "They only understand force."

## Known For

- interruption
- boundary setting
- direct protection
- refusing false peace
- acting under pressure
- naming unacceptable terms

## What They Get Wrong

- acting before listening
- making every tension a battlefield
- punishing uncertainty
- isolating allies
- mistaking fearlessness for wisdom

## Starting Move: Draw the Line

When you name a boundary that protects the work or interrupts harm, roll + Grow Up or + Show Up.

On a 10+, choose 2.

On a 7-9, choose 1 and name the cost.

On a 6-, the line is real, but your delivery creates a new repair need.

Options:

- Harm is interrupted now.
- Someone vulnerable gains room to choose.
- The real conflict becomes visible.
- You avoid unnecessary cruelty.
- Gain 1 Trust with someone who needed the boundary.

## House Feature: Stand in the Doorway

Once per episode, when another character would take a consequence from someone crossing a clear boundary, you may take that consequence instead.

If you do, ask the protected character:

**What do you need next, now that there is room?**

If they answer and you honor it, mark Growth.

## House Clock

**Escalation Spiral**

1. A boundary is softened to keep peace.
2. Harm repeats.
3. Anger gathers offscreen.
4. The first clear no arrives too hot.
5. Potential allies scatter.
6. The line holds, but repair becomes harder.

## Relationship to Other Houses

Provisioners:

Linekeepers respect material care but fear it can become endless triage.

Weavers:

Linekeepers need structures worth defending. They resent process that protects harm.

Lanternbearers:

Linekeepers need truth made visible. They distrust discourse that never risks action.

## House Advancement Prompt

When you finish a mission, ask:

**Where did I confuse force with clarity?**

If your answer leads to a repair or cleaner boundary, mark Growth.
