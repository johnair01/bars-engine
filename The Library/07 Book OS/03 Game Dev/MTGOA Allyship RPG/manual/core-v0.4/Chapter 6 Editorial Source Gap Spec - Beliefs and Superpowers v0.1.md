## Core Editorial Claim

Chapter 6 is the book's first true power chapter.

It must make the reader believe three things at once:

1. Their student has a vivid, exciting, gameable superpower.
2. Their student's self-sabotaging Belief is a pressure engine, not a diagnosis.
3. The table can play this material safely because the mechanics keep the focus on action, consequence, repair, and growth.

Chapter 6 v0.1 has the correct mythic premise:

> Everyone has power. Most people learn to survive by pretending they do not.

But it arrived through declaration before proof.

Chapter 6 v0.2 should preserve the premise and rebuild the chapter around a more testable play experience:

1. Choose how close to play.
2. Choose a Belief.
3. Choose a superpower.
4. Name the wound distortion.
5. Name the clean use.
6. See the premise proven in a scene.
7. Build Wound, Growth, and Power Moves.
8. Record hooks the Guide can use later.

## Chapter Job

Chapter 3 admits the student.

Chapter 4 teaches the core dice conversation.

Chapter 5 makes Houses vivid as practice factions.

Chapter 6 must deepen the student into a playable inner engine.

The chapter should answer:

- Why does the ship call these gifts superpowers?
- Why does power feel threatening to the student?
- How does a Belief distort a gift under pressure?
- How does a student choose distance from personally resonant material?
- How does a Wound Move work without becoming a shame button?
- How does a Growth Move create sustainable outcomes?
- How does a Power Move feel earned through Adversity?
- How does this layer feed Chapter 7's Schools?

## Reader Experience Goal

The reader should finish Chapter 6 feeling:

- "This is mythic, but I am not being flattered."
- "This is personal, but I am not being asked to confess."
- "This is emotionally charged, but the table has procedure."
- "My superpower is related to my wound without being determined by it."
- "I know what my student does differently under pressure."
- "I can imagine future missions tempting my Wound Move."
- "I want to earn my Power Move."

The ideal reader is likely to be drawn toward depth and also vulnerable to overconsenting to emotional exposure. The chapter must therefore make consent and fictional distance feel like part of the game, not an afterthought.

## Editorial Decisions For Chapter 6 v0.2

## Decision 1: Add "Choose Your Distance"

Before choosing a Belief, players choose how close the material will be.

| Distance | Use When | Table Instruction |
|---|---|---|
| **Close** | The Belief echoes something personally resonant. | Keep details chosen and bounded. |
| **Masked** | Emotional truth is safer behind invented context. | Translate the pattern into ship, school, family, faction, or mission fiction. |
| **Mythic** | The player wants archetypal distance. | Make the Belief symbolic, magical, or genre-heightened. |

Reason:

This protects the ideal reader from overexposure and gives the Guide language before depth appears.

## Decision 2: Keep Seven Superpowers

Chapter 6 v0.2 should keep:

- Connector
- Strategist
- Disruptor
- Escape Artist
- Catalyst
- Alchemist
- Storyteller

Reason:

The six-source canon is strong, but **Catalyst** solves a real design gap around **I'm Not Capable**. It captures the optimizer/coach/cheerleader survival style that Escape Artist does not.

## Decision 3: Use Common Pairings, Not Deterministic Pairings

Common pairings:

| Belief | Common Superpower |
|---|---|
| I Don't Belong | Connector |
| I'm Not Ready | Strategist |
| I'm Not Worthy | Disruptor |
| I'm Not Capable | Escape Artist |
| I'm Not Capable | Catalyst |
| I'm Not Good Enough | Alchemist |
| I'm Insignificant | Storyteller |

Rule:

Players may choose off-chart pairings if they can complete:

- My Belief distorts my superpower by...
- My superpower becomes clean when...

Reason:

The map creates onboarding, not destiny.

## Decision 4: Add A Premise-Proving Scene

Chapter 6 v0.2 needs a scene that proves the core premise through play.

Use Mara Venn.

Scene:

The local council publicly thanks the ship while local organizers are erased from the ceremony.

Four versions:

1. **Normal helpfulness**: Mara quietly fixes logistics. The scene works, but Mara becomes more depleted and the false story remains intact.
2. **Wound Move**: Mara exposes the council to prove the gatekeepers are false. The truth appears, but organizers become visible without consent and House Heat rises.
3. **Growth Move**: Mara interrupts the ceremony by naming what she is protecting and asking what visibility the organizers consent to.
4. **Future Power Move**: Mara spends Adversity to invalidate false authority in a later episode.

Reason:

This lets the book prove the difference between helpfulness, wound power, clean power, and earned power.

## Decision 5: Treat Move Templates As Starting Templates

The move templates should be labeled as:

- starting templates
- provisional move cards
- examples for immediate play

Reason:

They make the chapter playable, but they still need balance testing against Chapter 4's economy.

## Decision 6: Add A Wound Move Balance Rule

Rule:

When a Wound Move triggers, the player gets the immediate benefit. Then the Guide offers two relevant consequences and the player chooses one, unless the move specifies otherwise.

Consequence menu:

- take 1 Tension
- create 1 House Heat
- strain a Bond
- advance a mission clock
- expose someone before consent
- convert help into obligation
- create a downtime repair scene
- make a later Growth Move harder

Design sentence:

> A Wound Move should solve the immediate problem and create the next problem.

Reason:

This prevents Wound Moves from becoming either pure punishment or pure exploit.

## Decision 7: Add Growth Economy Constraint

Rule:

At most 1 Growth may be marked from Belief advancement per episode unless the table agrees the episode was a Belief spotlight episode.

Reason:

This prevents Belief material from becoming the dominant advancement engine and protects table spotlight.

## Decision 8: Move Safety Earlier And Keep Full Procedure Later

Early safety principle:

> Choose only what you want to make playable.

Later safety procedure:

- Do you want your Belief in this scene, or should we keep this at the mission level?
- What does the Belief make harder right now?
- What action, request, repair, or boundary could move this forward?

Reason:

The chapter should not ask for depth before it has named consent.

## Voice Requirements

## Regent

Use the Regent to:

- translate power into procedure
- preserve wonder without inflation
- name consent as curriculum
- turn big claims into steps

## J. Marginalia

Use J. to:

- puncture over-mythologizing
- warn against overexposure
- clarify that Belief is not an excuse
- name table failure modes
- make the school socially inhabited

Do not use J. to:

- mock sincerity
- turn safety into a joke
- undercut every serious beat

Marginalia density:

- 5-6 notes total
- no more than one note on any major spread
- prefer notes where the reader might misuse the system
