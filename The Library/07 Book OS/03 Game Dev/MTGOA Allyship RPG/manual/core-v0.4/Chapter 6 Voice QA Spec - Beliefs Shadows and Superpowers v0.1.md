# Chapter 6 Voice QA Spec - Beliefs Shadows and Superpowers v0.1

Status: voice and structure QA spec

Purpose: evaluate `Chapter 6 Prose Draft - Beliefs Shadows and Superpowers v0.1.md` against the manual voice spec, the spec-first chapter workflow, the needs of the ideal reader, and the gameplay burden of beliefs/superpowers.

Target follow-up output:

- `Chapter 6 Prose Draft - Beliefs Shadows and Superpowers v0.2.md`

## Core Finding

Chapter 6 v0.1 has the right conceptual engine.

It moved too quickly into declarative myth.

The draft successfully names the premise:

> Everyone has power. Most people learn to survive by pretending they do not.

It also successfully finds the chapter's central gameable structure:

- Beliefs are containment spells around power.
- Superpowers are gifts that can become clean power, overused reflex, or wound strategy.
- Wound Moves are easy now and costly later.
- Growth Moves cost in real time and create sustainable outcomes.
- Power Moves are earned through Adversity.
- Escape Artist and Catalyst share **I'm Not Capable** as sibling expressions.

The risk is not that the draft is wrong.

The risk is that it asks the reader to accept the premise before the game has helped them experience it.

For Chapter 6 v0.2, keep the thesis but add more stairs.

## Voice Diagnosis

## What Is Working

The Regent voice is present:

- wry administrative framing
- Headmaster translation jokes
- procedural concern beneath poetic claims
- direct statements that make the chapter feel like a usable handbook
- ship bureaucracy as texture rather than decoration

Strong examples:

- "The word is not a compliment. It is an intervention."
- "The chart is a lantern. It is not a lock."
- "The school does not ask you to become fearless. Fearless students make terrible records and worse apologies."
- "The scheduling office finds it inconvenient. The asymmetry remains."

The Council of Joannes voice also mostly works:

- snide but protective
- cuts through pious intensity
- names player discomfort without mocking it
- warns against forced vulnerability

Strong examples:

- "If the word 'superpower' makes you want to crawl under a table, congratulations."
- "My Belief is gone now" as a warning
- the remedial consent training marginalia

## What Is Straining

The chapter sometimes becomes a manifesto before it becomes a game text.

The opening has several excellent thesis lines in a row:

- Everyone has power.
- Most people learn to survive by pretending they do not.
- The ship seeks students because power becomes dangerous when it is unnamed.
- This is why the school exists.

These lines are good.

But stacked together, they risk sounding like the book is asking for belief rather than teaching through play.

The ideal reader may love the intensity. They may also flinch if it feels like the chapter is diagnosing them too quickly.

## Ideal Reader Risk

The ideal reader is likely:

- emotionally fluent
- RPG-literate
- drawn to transformative play
- skeptical of corporate DEI language
- hungry for a mythic frame around allyship
- vulnerable to over-identifying with "helpful" roles
- likely to turn self-analysis into self-pressure

They need Chapter 6 to say:

1. You are not being sorted into a pathology.
2. You are not being asked to confess.
3. Your gift is real, but this book will not flatter you into bypassing consequence.
4. Your wound matters, but it is not the table's fuel source.
5. You can play this at a safe distance.
6. The mechanics will prove the premise.

Chapter 6 v0.1 says most of these things.

It needs to say them earlier, more concretely, and with more examples of safe fictional distance.

## Chapter Function

Chapter 6 should not merely explain beliefs and superpowers.

It must let the reader feel the difference between:

- normal helpfulness
- wound-driven power
- clean power
- earned power

The reader should finish Chapter 6 feeling:

1. I understand why the school calls these gifts superpowers.
2. I understand that Belief is a pressure pattern, not a diagnosis.
3. I can choose a Belief without turning character creation into therapy.
4. I can choose a superpower without making trauma glamorous.
5. I understand the common Belief/superpower pairings.
6. I understand why **I'm Not Capable** has both Escape Artist and Catalyst.
7. I can write a Wound Distortion and Clean Use.
8. I can use Wound, Growth, and Power Moves at the table.
9. I know how safety is handled before a Belief enters a scene.
10. I can imagine playing a scene where the premise proves itself.

## Needed Source/Games Lessons

## Dungeon World Lesson: Playbooks Make Identity Actionable

Dungeon World does not just tell a player they are a Fighter, Bard, or Cleric. It gives them moves that express the fantasy immediately.

Chapter 6 should do the same.

Requirement:

- Every Belief/superpower pairing must produce at least one action the player can take in a scene.
- The text should avoid long personality descriptions that do not become moves.
- Wound/Growth/Power examples should be formatted like move cards or sidebars.

Acceptance:

- A player can point to one move and know when to use it.
- A Guide can create a consequence from a Wound Move without improvising the whole system.

## Kids On Bikes Lesson: Ordinary People Need Permission To Be Extraordinary

Kids on Bikes is powerful because it lets normal people interface with strange power while still feeling grounded.

Chapter 6 should make "superpower" feel grounded, not inflated.

Requirement:

- Establish that superpowers may be social, emotional, tactical, magical, or relational.
- Show a mundane expression and a mythic expression of each superpower.
- Avoid making every power sound like an epic destiny.

Acceptance:

- A Connector can introduce two people or create a psychic bridge.
- A Disruptor can ask one devastating question or shatter a false public ritual.
- A Catalyst can practice beside someone or awaken a dormant group capability.

## Hearts Blazing Lesson: Archetypes Need Relational Texture

Hearts Blazing makes role identity vivid by showing how characters relate to each other under pressure.

Chapter 6 should show how superpowers create table chemistry.

Requirement:

- Add short "how this helps the crew" and "how this burdens the crew" language for each superpower.
- Show how two students can have the same Belief and different superpowers.
- Show how two students can have the same superpower and different Beliefs.

Acceptance:

- The reader can imagine why a Catalyst and Escape Artist argue beautifully.
- The Guide can ask a crew-facing question when someone's Wound Move creates fallout.

## Foundations Lesson: Setup Answers Become Durable Artifacts

Foundations-style setup should produce usable campaign material.

Chapter 6 should make Belief and superpower answers create future scenes, NPCs, training scenes, and advancement hooks.

Requirement:

- Every Student Record prompt should point toward later play.
- The final page should instruct the Guide what to do with the answers.
- Belief/superpower choices should feed Chapter 7 Schools and campaign missions.

Acceptance:

- The Guide can use "what kind of mission would tempt your Wound Move?" as a campaign pressure hook.
- The Guide can use "who has only seen the wound version?" as an NPC or relationship hook.

## Voice Requirements For v0.2

## Regent Voice

Use the Regent to:

- translate the Headmaster's grand claim into procedure
- slow down intensity with practical instruction
- acknowledge discomfort without apologizing for the premise
- keep the chapter from becoming therapy text
- make safety feel like institutional procedure, not disclaimers

Regent line pattern to preserve:

1. claim
2. grounding image
3. consequence
4. instruction

Example target:

> Power is not rare aboard the ship. Unsupervised power is. Before you write a Belief on your record, choose the distance at which you want to play it: close, masked, or mythic.

## Council of Joannes Marginalia

Use J. to:

- puncture over-mythologizing
- protect players from overexposure
- warn against using Belief as excuse
- warn against using superpower as branding
- name common table failure modes

J. should not:

- become random comic relief
- mock the reader's tenderness
- turn safety into sarcasm
- deflate every serious moment

Marginalia density target:

- one note in the opener
- one note after Belief selection
- one note after common pairings
- one note in Wound/Growth/Power section
- one note in safety section
- one note in final Student Record section

## Structural Recommendations For v0.2

## 1. Add A Page Before Choosing Belief: "Choose Your Distance"

This is the biggest missing safety and onboarding piece.

Before asking players to choose a Belief, offer three distances:

| Distance | Use When | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Close** | You want the Belief to echo something personally resonant. | A player uses "I'm Not Worthy" because receiving care is hard. |
| **Masked** | You want emotional truth behind fictional details. | A student from a fallen guild distrusts all titles and blessings. |
| **Mythic** | You want archetypal play at a safer remove. | A star-temple orphan challenges every crowned authority. |

This lets the chapter keep depth without pressuring disclosure.

## 2. Move Safety Earlier

Safety currently appears on Page 10 after the chapter has already asked players to choose Beliefs and build moves.

Move a shorter safety principle earlier:

> Choose only what you want to make playable.

Then keep the fuller safety procedure later.

## 3. Add A "Premise Proving Scene"

The chapter needs one small scene that demonstrates:

1. normal helpfulness works but drains
2. Wound Move works but creates fallout
3. Growth Move costs now but creates a sustainable outcome
4. Power Move later fulfills the fantasy

This scene should probably use Mara.

Example:

- The council thanks the ship while local organizers are erased.
- Normal helpfulness: Mara quietly fixes logistics.
- Wound Move: Mara publicly exposes the council and creates backlash for organizers.
- Growth Move: Mara names the false ceremony while shielding the organizers and asking what visibility they consent to.
- Future Power Move: Unblessed And Unafraid invalidates the council's false authority in a later episode.

## 4. Separate "Quick Templates" From "Canon Moves"

Page 9 currently gives many concrete move templates. They are useful, but they may feel too final for v0.1 design.

For v0.2, label them:

- "Starting Templates"
- "Example Pairing Moves"
- "Provisional Move Cards"

This keeps the book playable while signaling that later balance passes may tune them.

## 5. Add Crew Fallout Questions

After Wound Moves, add:

- Who had to carry the cost?
- What did the crew learn about your pattern?
- What future scene did this create?
- Which House noticed?
- Which Bond changed?

This makes Wound Moves produce play instead of shame.

## 6. Add Mundane/Mythic Expression Examples

Each superpower should show both scales:

| Superpower | Mundane | Mythic |
|---|---|---|
| Connector | introduces two people with consent | creates a psychic trust bridge |
| Strategist | names a three-step plan | sees the mission clock's hidden gear |
| Disruptor | asks the forbidden question | breaks a false public ritual |
| Escape Artist | finds a safer exit route | opens a door in a wall that remembers captivity |
| Catalyst | practices beside someone | awakens a dormant group capability |
| Alchemist | turns a failed apology into repair | transmutes toxic residue into resource |
| Storyteller | protects testimony | restores a community's stolen memory |

This will make the power fantasy easier to inhabit without making it feel like superhero cosplay only.

## Acceptance Criteria For Chapter 6 v0.2

- The chapter opens with the superpower premise but proves it through at least one scene.
- The reader can choose a safe play distance before choosing a Belief.
- Belief is clearly not diagnosis, confession, or mandatory personal disclosure.
- The seven superpowers are distinguished by play behavior, not only conceptual language.
- Escape Artist and Catalyst both map to **I'm Not Capable** without feeling redundant.
- Off-chart pairings are explicitly supported.
- Wound Moves are easy now and costly later.
- Growth Moves cost in real time and create sustainable outcomes.
- Power Moves cost Adversity and feel earned.
- Safety appears before deep Belief work and again as table procedure.
- The chapter tells the Guide how to use Belief/superpower answers in future missions.
- The Regent voice remains procedural, wry, and humane.
- J. marginalia protects the reader from overexposure and punctures institutional overclaiming.
