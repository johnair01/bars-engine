# School Sheet - School of the Oath

Face: **Regent**

Owned Stat: **Steady**

Milestone question:

**Can you preserve what matters without becoming the wall?**

Core lesson:

Allyship at scale requires continuity, stewardship, role, and tradition.

## Vibe

The School of the Oath keeps the old halls, records, vows, initiation rooms, memorial gardens, ship protocols, and ceremonies that help the school remember why it exists.

It feels like Jedi archives, monastic vows, ancestral halls, and a student council that actually matters.

## Head of School

Name: **Head Sera Quill**

Title: Keeper of Continuance

What they embody:

Stewardship without stagnation.

Teaching style:

Sera gives students inherited responsibilities and watches whether they preserve the purpose or merely copy the form.

Blind spot:

She can overvalue continuity when rupture is needed.

Signature line:

"Do not inherit the cage and call it lineage."

## Lieutenants

### Oren Bell

Title: Vowkeeper

Subskill: Commitment clarity

What they teach:

Students learn to make commitments that can survive inconvenience.

How they test students:

Oren asks students to define what they will do when nobody is inspired anymore.

Shadow version:

Turning loyalty into obedience.

Move or training prompt:

**Name the Oath:** When you make a commitment, state what you will do, when, and under what limits. If you keep it by next downtime, gain 1 Trust.

### Hessa Vale

Title: Tradition Auditor

Subskill: Preserving purpose, revising form

What they teach:

Students learn to ask which traditions serve life and which traditions now protect fear.

How they test students:

Hessa gives students a beloved ritual with a hidden cost.

Shadow version:

Breaking things because old equals suspect.

Move or training prompt:

**Audit the Tradition:** When you examine a rule, ritual, or role, ask: What did this protect originally? What does it protect now?

### Jun Slate

Title: Succession Steward

Subskill: Passing work forward

What they teach:

Students learn to hand off responsibility without abandonment or control.

How they test students:

Jun gives students a task they care about and requires them to train someone else to carry it.

Shadow version:

Overholding because "no one else will do it right."

Move or training prompt:

**Pass It On:** When you hand off work with enough context for someone else to succeed, restore 1 Capacity.

## Trial

The student must protect a tradition by changing its form.

They pass if the purpose survives and the harmful pattern does not.

## Shadow Exam

The student is asked to defend an old structure that once mattered but now causes harm.

They pass by honoring what the structure served while refusing what it has become.

## Mark / Badge

The **Oath-Knot**, worn on the uniform, bag, weapon, or tool.

## Move Unlock: Steward the Thread

When you preserve a fragile good thing through role, ritual, or handoff, roll + Clean Up.

On a 10+, choose 2.

On a 7-9, choose 1.

On a 6-, the thing continues, but someone becomes trapped by the structure.

Options:

- The work can continue without you.
- A role becomes clear.
- A tradition regains its purpose.
- Trust increases with a group that needed continuity.

## Downtime Training

During downtime, train with the School of the Oath to:

- write or revise an oath
- repair a broken commitment
- create a succession plan
- audit a ship ritual
- restore Trust through kept action

## Campaign Use

Use this School when:

- the mission involves legacy, institutions, or succession
- characters overhold work
- a tradition carries both wisdom and harm
- continuity matters as much as disruption
