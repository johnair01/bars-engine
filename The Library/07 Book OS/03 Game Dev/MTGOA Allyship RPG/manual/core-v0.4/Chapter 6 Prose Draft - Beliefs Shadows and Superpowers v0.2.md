# Chapter 6 Prose Draft - Beliefs Shadows and Superpowers v0.2

Status: book prose draft

Purpose: revise Chapter 6 using the editorial source/gap spec and voice QA. This draft slows the reader down with distance, consent, playable proof, and clearer Wound/Growth/Power procedures.

## Page 1: Why The Ship Calls Them Superpowers

The Headmaster's original title for this chapter was "On The Ethical Custody Of Extraordinary Capacity In Students Who Have Been Socially Rewarded For Pretending To Be Small."

This was accurate.

It was also too long for the chapter tab.

The shorter version is this:

Everyone has power.

Most people learn to survive by pretending they do not.

The ship does not seek students because ordinary people are useless. The ship is not collecting exceptions, prodigies, chosen heirs, or dramatic children with excellent capes, though the admission office has learned to make room for all four.

The ship seeks students because power becomes dangerous when it is unnamed, unmanaged, exploited, shamed, or forced to move through a wound.

Consider a student who notices that every meeting ends with the same three people carrying the work.

They could call this "being helpful" and quietly do the extra labor.

They could call this "being difficult" and resent everyone while fixing the problem alone.

Or they could learn that the noticing itself is power.

The ship is interested in the third option.

Many potential allies hide inside normal helpfulness. They over-volunteer, smooth rooms, explain, donate, disappear into logistics, stay almost ready, and become useful in ways that drain them while flattering the old pattern.

Others hide from the part of them that shines, cuts, gathers, transforms, reveals, routes, or awakens. They fear becoming harmful. They fear being cast out. They fear being wanted only for what they can do. They fear discovering they are capable, because capability changes what can be asked of them.

This is why the school exists.

Not to make you powerful.

To teach you what your power has already been doing.

## Page 2: Superpower Is Not Flattery

The school does not call your gift a superpower because you are better than other people.

It calls it a superpower because you have probably spent years calling it something smaller.

"I just notice things."

"I just like connecting people."

"I just plan ahead."

"I just get frustrated when everyone pretends the obvious problem is fine."

"I just help people get unstuck."

"I just know how to leave."

"I just tell the story."

The word is not a compliment.

It is an intervention.

A superpower is a distinctive capacity that changes the field when you use it. It may be social, magical, strategic, emotional, physical, artistic, logistical, relational, or difficult to categorize without alarming the committee responsible for hallway insurance.

### Mundane And Mythic Scale

The same superpower can appear at ordinary scale or mythic scale.

Both count.

| Superpower | Mundane Expression | Mythic Expression |
|---|---|---|
| **Connector** | Introduces two people with consent. | Creates a psychic trust bridge across a hostile room. |
| **Strategist** | Names a three-step plan. | Sees the hidden gear inside a mission clock. |
| **Disruptor** | Asks the forbidden question. | Breaks a false public ritual before it seals. |
| **Escape Artist** | Finds a safer exit route. | Opens a door in a wall that remembers captivity. |
| **Catalyst** | Practices beside someone until courage becomes possible. | Awakens dormant capability in a group that believed it could not move. |
| **Alchemist** | Turns a failed apology into repair. | Transmutes toxic residue into a usable resource. |
| **Storyteller** | Protects testimony. | Restores a community's stolen memory. |

The power fantasy is not that you become impressive.

The power fantasy is that your gift becomes clean enough to use.

### What This Is Not

Your superpower is not your team Role.

Your Role tells the crew how they tend to rely on you.

Your superpower tells the table what extraordinary capacity enters the fiction through you.

Your House tells the table what kind of work claimed you first.

Your School tells the table how the ship trains your mastery.

Your Belief tells the table what false story pressure activates.

These parts speak to each other.

They are not the same part.

### Student Record

On your Student Record, add a section called **Belief And Superpower**.

Write these fields and leave them blank for now:

- Distance
- Belief
- Superpower
- Power Expression
- Wound Distortion
- Clean Use
- First Growth Move
- Power Move To Earn

This is not because the manual enjoys suspense, though several departments do. It is because a superpower chosen before a Belief can become decoration, and a Belief chosen before a superpower can become a cage.

You will choose both.

You will also choose how close to yourself they are allowed to sit.

## Page 3: Choose Your Distance

Before choosing a Belief, choose your distance.

This is not a test of courage.

It is a safety procedure.

Choose only what you want to make playable.

| Distance | Use When | Table Instruction |
|---|---|---|
| **Close** | The Belief echoes something personally resonant. | Keep details chosen, bounded, and voluntary. |
| **Masked** | Emotional truth is safer behind invented context. | Translate the pattern into ship, school, family, faction, planet, or mission fiction. |
| **Mythic** | You want archetypal play at a safer remove. | Make the Belief symbolic, magical, legendary, or genre-heightened. |

Close play might sound like:

> "My student struggles to receive care without earning it."

Masked play might sound like:

> "My student comes from a guild where every blessing became debt."

Mythic play might sound like:

> "My student was raised under a star-temple that revoked names from children it deemed unworthy."

All three can produce excellent play.

None is more honest than the others.

The table does not need the source of the pattern. The table needs the playable edge.

## Page 4: Beliefs Are Containment Spells

A self-sabotaging Belief is not a diagnosis.

It is not a permanent truth.

It is not the secret explanation for everything wrong with you.

It is a sentence that pressure uses to contain power.

The six common Beliefs are:

| Belief | Pressure Story | Protective Lie |
|---|---|---|
| **I'm Not Good Enough** | My action will reveal my defect. | If I keep improving, I can avoid being exposed. |
| **I'm Not Ready** | Beginning now would be irresponsible. | If I prepare enough, risk will become permission. |
| **I Don't Belong** | This room will reject the real me. | If I become useful enough, I can earn my place. |
| **I'm Insignificant** | What I do will not matter. | If I stay small, I do not have to grieve being overlooked. |
| **I'm Not Worthy** | I have not earned care, trust, or authority. | If I disprove the gatekeepers, I never have to admit I wanted blessing. |
| **I'm Not Capable** | I cannot meet what the moment asks. | If I avoid, optimize, or outsource the test, I never have to feel helpless. |

Beliefs are patterns, not tragedies.

Each Belief protects something.

This is why scolding it rarely works.

The Belief learned in a family, school, job, movement, friendship, campaign, institution, or crisis where power had consequences.

The Belief says:

Do not shine there.

Do not ask for that.

Do not begin yet.

Do not take the seat.

Do not receive.

Do not discover what you can do.

The school does not remove the Belief by force. That tends to make students either collapse, perform enlightenment, or join committees.

The school trains the student to notice the Belief, act with it in the room, and stop letting it drive.

> "My Belief is gone now" is usually something people say twelve minutes before making it everyone else's problem.

## Page 5: Choose A Belief

Choose one self-sabotaging Belief for your student.

Pick the sentence that becomes persuasive under pressure.

Do not pick the sentence you have already solved.

Do not pick the sentence that sounds most dramatic.

Do not pick the sentence you think will make other players understand your actual life.

Pick the sentence that will create playable friction without requiring the table to become your therapist, courtroom, or emergency contact.

### Three Ways To Choose

**Choose directly** if one Belief already has heat.

**Roll 1d6** if you want provocation.

| d6 | Belief |
|---|---|
| 1 | I'm Not Good Enough |
| 2 | I'm Not Ready |
| 3 | I Don't Belong |
| 4 | I'm Insignificant |
| 5 | I'm Not Worthy |
| 6 | I'm Not Capable |

**Answer prompts** if you want the ship to recommend one.

- When help is needed, what fear makes me hesitate?
- When I receive attention, what story tries to shrink me?
- When I act powerfully, what consequence do I expect?
- When someone trusts me, what do I immediately try to prove?
- When I fail, what sentence gets louder?
- When the crew needs me, what part of me wants to hide?

If the prompt answer points to a Belief, use it.

If it points to two, choose the one that will be more interesting to play at your chosen distance.

### Running Example: Mara Venn

Mara's player chooses **Masked** distance.

The player does not want to explain her own relationship to approval at the table. She gives Mara a faction history instead: Mara once trained under a relief council that offered blessings only after students made themselves useful past exhaustion.

Then Mara's player chooses **I'm Not Worthy**.

Not because Mara lacks skill.

Mara is skilled enough to make the problem worse with precision.

Mara has a pattern around blessing. When authority approves of her, she distrusts it. When authority rejects her, she takes grim satisfaction in proving it corrupt. When care is offered, she looks for the invoice.

Mara does not think she wants a seat near the people who decide.

She very much does.

That is the wound.

## Page 6: The Seven Superpowers

The school currently recognizes seven common allyship superpowers.

This number is irritating because there are six common Beliefs. The Headmaster finds this "alive with instructive asymmetry."

The scheduling office finds it inconvenient.

The asymmetry remains.

| Superpower | Core Gift | What It Feels Like In Play | Shadow / Overuse |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Connector** | Builds trust, belonging, relational circuits, and bridges across difference. | You create contact where isolation, mistrust, or fragmentation ruled. | Over-connects without consent, confuses access with intimacy, becomes the social glue at personal cost. |
| **Strategist** | Sees the board, hidden constraints, leverage points, and future consequences. | You change the sequence so the crew can act with clarity. | Becomes cold, controlling, over-optimized, or resentful when others do not see what they see. |
| **Disruptor** | Interrupts stuck patterns, false inevitability, silence, and compliance. | You break the spell of "this is just how things are." | Confuses rupture with change, escalates for identity, or damages trust the mission still needs. |
| **Escape Artist** | Finds exits, loopholes, routes, options, and sovereignty under constraint. | You reveal a way out of a trap, false choice, or coercive container. | Avoids commitment, slips accountability, or treats every container as a cage. |
| **Catalyst** | Sees latent capacity and helps people practice into power. | You make the next brave attempt feel possible. | Optimizes everyone else, over-coaches, or makes other people's growth a hiding place from your own helplessness. |
| **Alchemist** | Transforms adversity, grief, conflict, failure, and stuckness into usable capacity. | You help the crew metabolize what would otherwise poison the field. | Romanticizes suffering, over-processes, or turns every wound into curriculum before people are ready. |
| **Storyteller** | Shapes meaning, memory, morale, narrative, and public imagination. | You give experience an arc without stealing it from the people who lived it. | Turns pain into performance, simplifies complexity, or makes the story more important than the people inside it. |

Choose one superpower for your student.

You may choose freely.

You may choose the common pairing for your Belief.

You may choose against the common pairing because your student is inconvenient in a way the chart cannot anticipate.

The chart is a lantern.

It is not a lock.

## Page 7: Common Belief And Superpower Pairings

The following pairings are the school's most common admission patterns.

They are not law.

They are the shapes the faculty sees often enough to keep spare forms.

| Belief | Common Superpower | Wound Logic |
|---|---|---|
| **I Don't Belong** | **Connector** | If I can create belonging for others, maybe I can earn mine. |
| **I'm Not Ready** | **Strategist** | If I can plan enough, I never have to risk beginning unprepared. |
| **I'm Not Worthy** | **Disruptor** | If I can expose the gatekeepers as false, I do not have to admit I wanted their blessing. |
| **I'm Not Capable** | **Escape Artist** | If I can always find a way out, I never have to discover whether I could stay and act. |
| **I'm Not Capable** | **Catalyst** | If I can make everyone else more capable, I never have to feel my own helplessness directly. |
| **I'm Not Good Enough** | **Alchemist** | If I can transform every failure into growth, maybe inadequacy never gets the final word. |
| **I'm Insignificant** | **Storyteller** | If I can make meaning of what happened, maybe I can prove it mattered, and that I did too. |

The shared root between **Escape Artist** and **Catalyst** matters.

Both grow around the ache of incapability.

The Escape Artist studies constraint until a door appears.

The Catalyst studies capacity until a person can move.

One says, "There is another way out."

The other says, "There is another way in."

Both are gifts.

Both can become hiding places.

### If Your Pairing Is Not On The Chart

Choose the pairing that tells the truest playable story.

A Connector can carry **I'm Not Ready** if they keep arranging relationships instead of beginning the work.

A Storyteller can carry **I'm Not Worthy** if they turn every denied blessing into a public myth.

A Strategist can carry **I Don't Belong** if they map every room because they do not trust any room to want them.

If your pairing is unusual, write one sentence:

> My Belief distorts my superpower by...

Then write one sentence:

> My superpower becomes clean when...

If those two sentences make sense to the table, the pairing works.

Her player chooses **Disruptor**.

This does not mean Mara is loud all the time.

## Page 8: The Premise In Play

The difference between normal helpfulness, wound power, clean power, and earned power is easiest to see in a scene.

The crew arrives in Pelagos Station after a water crisis. The local council holds a public ceremony thanking the ship for its aid. Three local organizers stand at the edge of the square, unthanked, exhausted, and close enough to hear themselves erased.

Mara notices.

The Guide asks, "What do you do?"

### Option 1: Normal Helpfulness

Mara quietly leaves the ceremony, finds the supply quartermaster, and makes sure the organizers get water, shade, and a replacement shift.

This is useful.

It may even be kind.

But if Mara does it while swallowing the false public story, the ceremony remains intact. The council receives credit. The organizers receive relief without recognition. Mara takes 1 Tension because the pattern stays unnamed and her body knows it.

Normal helpfulness can work.

It can also drain the person who keeps using it to avoid power.

### Option 2: Wound Move

Mara steps onto the platform and asks, "How long were you planning to applaud yourselves before naming the people who kept your wells alive?"

The square goes silent.

The council's story cracks.

Mara's Disruptor power works.

But she did not ask the organizers whether they wanted visibility in this moment. Now the council knows exactly who to punish after the ship leaves.

Mara used her Wound Move: **Test The Throne**.

Immediate benefit: reveal hypocrisy.

Future consequence: the Guide offers two costs.

- create 1 Heat with Lanternbearers for exposing people before consent
- advance the council backlash clock by 1

Mara's player chooses the backlash clock.

The truth appeared.

The next problem appeared with it.

### Option 3: Growth Move

Mara feels the same charge.

This time, she turns to the organizers first.

"I can interrupt this," she says quietly. "Do you want your names public, or do you want the pattern named without pointing at you?"

They ask her to name the pattern and leave them covered.

Mara steps forward and says, "Before the ship accepts thanks, the council will name what labor was already happening here and what protections those workers need after we leave."

This is still a disruption.

It costs Mara the satisfaction of pure exposure.

It costs her the clean hit of proving the gatekeepers false.

It creates a more sustainable outcome.

Mara uses her Growth Move: **Break The Spell, Not The Room**.

She rolls **Show Up + Speak**.

On a hit, the room can no longer pretend the pattern is neutral. On a 10+, Mara also shields the organizers from direct backlash.

### Option 4: Future Power Move

Later in the campaign, after Mara has gained Adversity and marked Growth, she may unlock **Unblessed And Unafraid**.

When an institution withholds legitimacy to control the field, Mara can spend Adversity and declare one source of authority invalid in the scene.

The Guide must say what becomes possible now and what backlash will follow later.

This is the fantasy.

Not "Mara becomes loud."

Mara becomes trustworthy with force.

## Page 9: Wound Moves, Growth Moves, And Power Moves

Your Belief and superpower meet through three kinds of moves.

These moves are not all unlocked at once.

They are a map of how power matures under pressure.

### Wound Move

A **Wound Move** is the old survival use of the gift.

It is easier in the moment.

It creates long-term consequences.

The Wound Move often works.

This is why students keep using it.

When a Wound Move triggers, the player gets the immediate benefit. Then the Guide offers two relevant consequences and the player chooses one, unless the move says otherwise.

A Wound Move should solve the immediate problem and create the next problem.

Consequence options include:

- take 1 Tension
- create 1 House Heat
- strain a Bond
- advance a mission clock
- expose someone before consent
- convert help into obligation
- create a downtime repair scene
- make a later Growth Move harder

After resolving the consequence, ask one fallout question:

- Who had to carry the cost?
- What did the crew learn about your pattern?
- What future scene did this create?
- Which House noticed?
- Which Bond changed?

### Growth Move

A **Growth Move** is the practiced use of the gift.

It costs in real time.

It creates sustainable outcomes.

When you use a Growth Move, you choose the harder clean action: asking for help, naming a boundary, receiving care, making a repair, staying without controlling, leaving without vanishing, challenging without humiliating, or telling the truth without making it spectacle.

Growth Moves often require a roll through a Basic Move.

The most common are **Open Up**, **Clean Up**, **Grow Up**, and **Show Up**.

### Power Move

A **Power Move** is the earned use of the gift after the student has metabolized adversity into skill.

Power Moves are bought or activated with **Adversity**.

They should feel like this:

Oh.

This is what my gift does when fear is not driving.

To purchase a Power Move, spend the required Adversity during downtime, advancement, or a supervised School scene. To activate it in play, follow the move text.

Power Moves should be strong.

They should not erase consequence.

They should let a student become useful in a way their old Belief could not imagine.

> [MARGINALIA - J.]
> Wound Moves are the button you press because it has worked before. Growth Moves are the thing you do while your entire nervous system files an objection. Power Moves are what happens after enough consequences become curriculum.

## Page 10: Building Your Belief Move Set

After choosing Distance, Belief, and superpower, write three sentences.

### 1. My Wound Distortion

Complete:

> When my Belief drives my superpower, I tend to...

Examples:

- connect people so I do not have to admit I feel outside the room
- plan until beginning becomes unnecessary
- disrupt authority before I risk wanting approval
- find an exit before anyone can ask me to stay
- improve others so I do not have to feel helpless
- transform failure so quickly nobody sees that it hurt
- turn pain into story before the people inside it are ready

### 2. My Clean Use

Complete:

> When my superpower is clean, I can...

Examples:

- create belonging without becoming the price of admission
- sequence action without turning people into pieces
- interrupt harm without making rupture my identity
- find exits that protect agency instead of avoiding contact
- build capability without making improvement a debt
- metabolize failure without romanticizing harm
- shape meaning without stealing the story

### 3. My First Growth Move

Choose one:

- **Open Up** by naming what the Belief is making hard.
- **Clean Up** by repairing harm your Wound Move created.
- **Grow Up** by staying with the discomfort your gift usually avoids.
- **Show Up** by using your gift cleanly in a scene where the old pattern would be easier.

Write the move in this form:

> When I use [superpower] cleanly while [Belief] is active, I may [fictional action]. If I do, roll [Basic Move + stat]. On a hit, [sustainable outcome]. On a 6-, gain Adversity and name what the Belief was trying to protect.

The Guide may help you finish the mechanical details.

The table should understand the fiction first.

## Page 11: Starting Move Templates

The following are starting templates.

They are playable now.

They are also provisional enough for your table to tune as campaign play reveals what sings, breaks, or tries to become a committee.

### Connector With I Don't Belong

**Wound Move: Earn The Seat**

When you over-connect, over-translate, or become the bridge so no one can exclude you, gain temporary access or reduce immediate social friction. Then the Guide offers two: take 1 Tension, strain a Bond, give someone access they did not consent to, or create 1 House Heat.

**Growth Move: Belong Without Performing**

When you Open Up about what belonging is costing you, roll +Steady or +Tend. On a hit, name one need or boundary and choose one person who can meet you there. On a 10+, also clear 1 Tension or strengthen a Bond.

**Power Move: A Seat With Your Name On It**

Spend 3 Adversity. Once per episode, when you create a circle of trust without making yourself the doorway, everyone who enters with consent may clear 1 Tension or gain 1 Clarity.

### Strategist With I'm Not Ready

**Wound Move: One More Plan**

When you delay action to gather one more map, forecast, or contingency, ask one extra Wake Up question without rolling. Then the Guide offers two: advance a clock, add a complication, create 1 Tension, or ask who paid for the delay.

**Growth Move: Begin Before Certainty**

When you Show Up with an incomplete plan and name the first reversible step, roll +Shape or +Steady. On a hit, the crew gains a workable opening. On a 10+, also name one risk you prepared for without controlling the scene.

**Power Move: The Door Already Opening**

Spend 3 Adversity. Once per episode, when the crew is frozen by too many variables, name the next three actionable steps. If everyone accepts a role, take +1 forward to the first step and reduce a mission clock by 1 segment if it lands.

### Disruptor With I'm Not Worthy

**Wound Move: Test The Throne**

When you provoke, expose, or challenge a gatekeeper to prove the whole system is false, force a reaction or reveal hypocrisy. Then the Guide offers two: create House Heat, damage Trust, make someone who needed protection more visible, or advance a backlash clock.

**Growth Move: Break The Spell, Not The Room**

When you interrupt false legitimacy while naming what you are protecting, roll +Act or +Speak. On a hit, the room can no longer pretend the pattern is neutral. On a 10+, choose who is shielded from backlash.

**Power Move: Unblessed And Unafraid**

Spend 3 Adversity. Once per episode, when an institution withholds legitimacy to control the field, you may declare one source of authority invalid in the scene. The Guide must say what becomes possible now and what backlash will follow later.

### Escape Artist With I'm Not Capable

**Wound Move: Door Before Duty**

When you find a way out before discovering whether you could stay and act, escape immediate pressure or create an exit for yourself. Then the Guide offers two: leave someone unsupported, strain a Bond, make re-entry harder, or create a repair scene for later.

**Growth Move: Sovereign Stay**

When you choose to remain while preserving an exit, roll +Sense or +Steady. On a hit, you name the condition that lets you stay without surrendering agency. On a 10+, another person gains the same option.

**Power Move: No Cage Is Total**

Spend 3 Adversity. Once per episode, when a system presents a false choice, reveal a third route. It may be costly, strange, or politically inconvenient, but it is real.

### Catalyst With I'm Not Capable

**Wound Move: Improve Everyone Else**

When you coach, optimize, or uplift someone else to avoid feeling your own helplessness, give them +1 forward or a clear next step. Then the Guide offers two: take 1 Tension, make their growth feel like debt, avoid naming what you need to practice, or make the next failure feel personal.

**Growth Move: Practice Beside Them**

When you build capacity by practicing with someone instead of fixing them, roll +Tend or +Shape. On a hit, both of you name a next brave rep. On a 10+, one of you clears 1 Tension or gains 1 Growth.

**Power Move: The Room Learns It Can**

Spend 3 Adversity. Once per episode, when a group believes it cannot meet the moment, create a practice container in the scene. Each participant who accepts a role may take +1 forward to their next Growth Move.

### Alchemist With I'm Not Good Enough

**Wound Move: Make It Mean Something**

When you transform failure so quickly no one has to witness your inadequacy, convert a mistake into an immediate lesson or resource. Then the Guide offers two: skip a needed feeling, pressure someone else to grow, hide the repair still needed, or make the lesson cost someone trust.

**Growth Move: Let The Failure Be Seen**

When you Clean Up by naming what failed before transforming it, roll +Speak or +Steady. On a hit, the failure becomes usable without becoming a performance. On a 10+, choose one person who no longer has to carry it alone.

**Power Move: Compost Engine**

Spend 3 Adversity. Once per episode, when the crew takes a serious consequence, you may lead a ritual, repair, or experiment that converts it into one durable resource, lesson, or relationship. The cost remains true.

### Storyteller With I'm Insignificant

**Wound Move: Make It Matter**

When you turn pain, effort, or conflict into a story to prove it mattered, give the scene meaning or morale. Then the Guide offers two: simplify a person, claim a story too early, make yourself narrator instead of participant, or attract attention before consent.

**Growth Move: Witness Before Arc**

When you Wake Up or Open Up by letting the truth remain unfinished, roll +Sense or +Speak. On a hit, someone or something becomes witnessed without being flattened. On a 10+, the crew gains 1 Clarity.

**Power Move: The Story That Gives Back**

Spend 3 Adversity. Once per episode, when a community's memory is being erased, distorted, or used against them, preserve a living account with consent. Anyone named by the account may choose whether it grants them courage, protection, or leverage.

## Page 12: Safety Around Belief Scenes

Belief scenes can cut close.

This is part of their power.

It is also why they need table procedure.

Do not put safety inside individual moves as though safety is a special feature some characters have and others do not.

Safety is global.

It belongs to the table.

Use three rules.

### Consent Before Depth

Before a Belief becomes central in a scene, the Guide asks:

> Do you want your Belief in this scene, or should we keep this at the mission level?

If the player says no, respect it.

### Fiction Before Analysis

If the player says yes, the Guide asks:

> What does the Belief make harder right now?

The answer should point toward a concrete situation, not a free-floating explanation of the player's inner life.

### Exit Before Intensity

Then the Guide asks:

> What action, request, repair, or boundary could move this forward?

Every Belief scene needs a playable next action, pause, repair, or boundary.

The goal is not to extract confession.

The goal is to make the character's pattern playable without making the player's pain into fuel.

Any player may call Pause, Cut, Rewind, or Fade according to the table's safety tools. Chapter 9 contains the fuller safety procedure for Guides.

The ship is not impressed by forced vulnerability.

It is impressed by clean consent.

## Page 13: Advancement Through Belief

At the end of an episode, answer:

1. Did my Belief become active under pressure?
2. Did I use a Wound Move?
3. Did I attempt a Growth Move?
4. Did I name what the Belief was protecting?
5. Did I repair, revise, or recommit after consequence?

If yes to at least two, mark 1 Growth.

At most 1 Growth may be marked from Belief advancement per episode unless the table agrees the episode was a Belief spotlight episode.

If you used a Wound Move and accepted its consequence honestly, gain 1 Adversity.

If you used a Growth Move in a way that changed the field sustainably, mark 1 Growth, respecting the episode cap above.

If you have enough Adversity to purchase a Power Move, you may do so during downtime or a School training scene.

### Suggested Costs

| Power Move Type | Cost |
|---|---|
| Minor Power Move | 2 Adversity |
| Core Power Move | 3 Adversity |
| Major Power Move | 5 Adversity and a School scene |

For most starting students, use **3 Adversity**.

The cost is not punishment.

It is proof that the gift has survived consequence and learned from it.
