# School Sheet - School of the Pattern

Face: **Architect**

Owned Stat: **Shape**

Milestone question:

**Can you design leverage without losing contact?**

Core lesson:

Systems are real, but people live inside them.

## Vibe

The School of the Pattern lives in map rooms, simulation chambers, strategy decks, model cities, puzzle labs, and star charts where students learn to see the machine behind the moment.

It feels like tactical bending, rebel planning rooms, systems theory, and the thrill of finally seeing why the same failure keeps happening.

## Head of School

Name: **Head Irix Vale**

Title: Patternwright

What they embody:

Strategic clarity with moral contact.

Teaching style:

Irix asks students to draw the system, then asks whose face disappeared from the diagram.

Blind spot:

They can become enchanted by elegant models.

Signature line:

"A map that erases the person is already wrong."

## Lieutenants

### Pell Axis

Title: Systems Cartographer

Subskill: Mapping forces

What they teach:

Students learn to map incentives, constraints, flows, roles, and hidden feedback loops.

How they test students:

Pell gives students one visible problem and asks them to find the structure that keeps reproducing it.

Shadow version:

Explaining the system so well that nobody acts.

Move or training prompt:

**Map the Pattern:** When you draw the system behind a recurring problem, gain 1 Clarity if the map includes who benefits, who pays, and what keeps it going.

### Rin Compass

Title: Leverage Tutor

Subskill: Choosing pressure points

What they teach:

Students learn to find the smallest move that changes the most.

How they test students:

Rin gives three possible interventions and asks which one changes the structure instead of the surface.

Shadow version:

Manipulation disguised as strategy.

Move or training prompt:

**Find the Lever:** When you identify a leverage point, ask the Guide what one small action could shift the larger pattern.

### Ossa Threadglass

Title: Contradiction Keeper

Subskill: Holding competing truths

What they teach:

Students learn to keep contradictions visible instead of solving them too quickly.

How they test students:

Ossa assigns students to defend two truths that appear to undermine each other.

Shadow version:

Analysis paralysis.

Move or training prompt:

**Hold the Contradiction:** When two truths both matter, name what each protects. Take +1 forward to Grow Up or Clean Up.

## Trial

The student must redesign a failing structure while staying in relationship with the people affected by it.

They pass if the new structure makes the right action easier.

## Shadow Exam

The student is given a perfect design that nobody can live inside.

They pass by choosing a less elegant structure that real people can use.

## Mark / Badge

The **Open Compass**, a diagram-sigil that never fully closes.

## Move Unlock: Blueprint the Escape

When you design a structure that lets people escape a repeating harm, roll + Wake Up or + Show Up.

On a 10+, choose 2.

On a 7-9, choose 1.

On a 6-, the design works for the problem but fails the people.

Options:

- The next right action becomes easier.
- A hidden incentive becomes visible.
- A role, rule, or resource flow changes.
- Someone affected helps revise the design.

## Downtime Training

During downtime, train with the School of the Pattern to:

- convert Clarity into Leverage
- revise a failed plan
- map a faction relationship
- build a custom mission move
- ask whose face is missing from the strategy

## Campaign Use

Use this School when:

- the crisis is structural
- players keep treating symptoms
- a campaign needs strategy and design
- a beautiful plan needs human friction
