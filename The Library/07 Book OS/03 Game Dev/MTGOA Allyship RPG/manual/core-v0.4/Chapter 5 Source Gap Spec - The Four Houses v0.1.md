# Chapter 5 Source Gap Spec - The Four Houses v0.1

Status: implementation spec

Purpose: define how to turn the four House sheets into a book-ready Chapter 5 that makes the Houses feel socially alive, mechanically useful, and campaign-expandable.

Target outputs:

- `Chapter 5 Prose Draft - The Four Houses v0.1.md`
- `BARs Engine Spec - House System v0.1.md`

## Core Goal

Chapter 5 must make Houses vivid enough to live in the reader's imagination.

The benchmark is not "the reader understands the four domains." The benchmark is that the reader can picture the banners, rooms, rituals, jokes, rivalries, smells, senior students, rumors, and emotional weather of each House the way readers can picture the kingdoms in Avatar, the houses in Harry Potter, or the districts in The Hunger Games.

Chapter 5 must make Houses feel like more than a character option.

Chapter 3 lets a player choose a House. Chapter 4 teaches the core moves. Chapter 5 must show how a House changes what the student notices, what support they can ask for, what mistakes they are likely to make, and how the ship reacts when their practice becomes visible.

The reader should finish Chapter 5 feeling:

1. I know what each House does.
2. I know why Houses are practice factions, not personality types.
3. I know how House Trust and House Heat work.
4. I know how my House gives me moves, features, duties, and advancement prompts.
5. I know how rival Houses create useful friction without splitting the table.
6. I can imagine buying a campaign packet that adds House-specific missions, NPCs, scandals, teachers, and rewards.
7. I can vividly imagine what it feels like to belong to each House.
8. I know the senior student leaders well enough to want them in scenes.

## Source-Informed Requirements

## Dungeon World Requirement: Class Fantasy With Mechanical Teeth

Dungeon World makes a class feel playable because it gives the reader:

- a fantasy they can recognize
- a clear move they can use
- a specific way to solve problems
- advancement hooks that reinforce identity

MTGOA Houses should do the same thing without becoming fantasy occupations.

Implement:

- strong House fantasy
- sensory House identity
- House common rooms, rituals, symbols, and local slang
- concise House domain
- starting move for each House
- once-per-episode House feature
- House advancement prompt
- House shadow that creates meaningful failure

Acceptance criteria:

- Every House has a domain, question, motto, gift, shadow, starting move, feature, clock, and advancement prompt.
- Every House has a vivid cultural spread: space, ritual, objects, senior leader, local reputation, and first impression.
- Every House changes how players read a scene.
- The chapter teaches that Houses create permissions and pressures, not rigid scripts.

## Hearts Blazing Requirement: Ensemble Identity And Table Chemistry

Hearts Blazing uses team roles and social texture to make the table feel like a crew with history. MTGOA Houses should help players enjoy productive friction: House pride, rivalry, old arguments, and cross-House respect.

Implement:

- House rivalries and alliances
- House contact prompts
- rookie-veteran student leaders who speak for each House
- table-facing "what they think of each other" language
- cross-House training
- examples showing different Houses reading the same crisis differently

Acceptance criteria:

- The chapter includes a shared scene where all four Houses notice different truths.
- Rivalry creates play, not permission to be cruel.
- Cross-House training lets students learn from other Houses without abandoning their own.

## Kids on Bikes Requirement: Relationships As Playable State

Kids on Bikes makes relationships matter because social connections are explicit, table-facing, and consequential. MTGOA should use House relationships to create playable faction state.

Implement:

- House Trust
- House Heat
- faction-facing prompts
- relationship changes after missions
- guidance for using House NPCs

Acceptance criteria:

- House Trust and Heat are visible trackers.
- Trust and Heat change available support, suspicion, costs, and scene pressure.
- The chapter explains when to mark, use, reduce, and clear House Trust or Heat.

## Foundations Requirement: Procedures Create Durable Campaign Artifacts

Foundations turns setup answers into durable play material. Chapter 5 must make each House a reusable campaign engine that can generate scenes, NPCs, assets, disputes, and episode hooks.

Implement:

- House registers for campaign packets
- House clocks
- House duties during missions
- House artifact updates after episodes
- app-ready metadata

Acceptance criteria:

- Each House has a clock that can be advanced by consequences.
- Each mission can assign House duties.
- The House system produces records that future episodes can reference.

## Mechanical Decisions

## Houses Are Practice Factions

Houses are not districts and not personality types.

They are enduring factions aboard the ship organized around the four MTGOA domains:

| House | Domain | Core Question |
|---|---|---|
| Provisioners | Gather Resources | What is actually depleted, and how does capacity move? |
| Weavers | Skillful Organizing | What structure lets the work continue? |
| Linekeepers | Direct Action | What line must be drawn, and what will it cost? |
| Lanternbearers | Raise Awareness | What truth must become visible, and with whose consent? |

## House Trust

House Trust means a House believes your work honors its domain.

Use House Trust when:

- asking a House for aid
- calling on a House contact
- reducing a House-related cost
- unlocking cross-House training
- earning a House-specific opportunity

Suggested tracker:

- 0: unknown or neutral
- 1: recognized
- 2: trusted
- 3: sponsored

At 3 Trust, a House can offer one major support during an episode, such as a teacher's help, a specialized resource, a mission lead, a protected meeting, or a cross-House move.

## House Heat

House Heat means a House believes your work is misusing, threatening, or distorting its domain.

Use House Heat when:

- a House imposes scrutiny
- a rival complicates a scene
- a House clock advances
- a support request comes with strings
- a mission packet needs internal ship drama

Suggested tracker:

- 0: no active concern
- 1: watched
- 2: challenged
- 3: summoned

At 3 Heat, the House demands a scene before the tracker can increase again. The scene might be a hearing, confrontation, mentorship session, repair request, public correction, or field evaluation.

Heat is not punishment. Heat is institutional attention.

## Relationship To Chapter 4

House moves slot into the Basic Moves.

They do not replace Wake Up, Open Up, Clean Up, Grow Up, or Show Up. A House move names a special permission, option, or consequence inside a Basic Move.

Examples:

- Provisioners use **Move the Right Resource** to enter Wake Up through depletion.
- Weavers use **Name the Missing Role** to enter Wake Up or Clean Up through structure.
- Linekeepers use **Draw the Line** to enter Grow Up or Show Up through boundaries.
- Lanternbearers use **Light the Pattern** to enter Wake Up or Show Up through visibility.

Chapter 5 should explicitly remind readers that a House does not decide every roll. It changes what the student is trained to notice and how the table frames certain moves.

## Open Design Questions

1. Should each House eventually have ranked House moves, or should House growth mostly happen through Schools and cross-House training?
2. Should Trust be spent down, or should it remain a standing relationship with limited episode uses?
3. Should Heat clear automatically after a repair scene, or only after a changed behavior is demonstrated in the field?
4. Should each campaign packet feature one dominant House and one shadow House?

## Recommendation

For v0.1:

- Treat Trust as standing relationship state, not currency.
- Let 3 Trust grant one major House support per episode.
- Treat Heat as attention that demands scenes, not punishment.
- Let 3 Heat trigger a House scene, then reduce to 1 if the student makes a sincere repair, concession, or learning move.
- Keep House advancement light until playtesting proves whether House ranks are needed.
