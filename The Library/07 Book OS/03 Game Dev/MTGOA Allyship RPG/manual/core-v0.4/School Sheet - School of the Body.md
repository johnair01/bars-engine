# School Sheet - School of the Body

Face: **Shaman**

Owned Stat: **Sense**

Milestone question:

**Can you feel the field before explaining it?**

Core lesson:

Before strategy, relationship, or action, there is signal.

## Vibe

The School of the Body lives in warm rooms, low light, weather decks, herb gardens, dream cabins, sound chambers, and training halls where students learn to notice before naming.

It feels like Avatar's waterbending lessons crossed with somatic therapy, old magic, martial breathwork, and the strange quiet before a storm.

Students here learn that the body is not an obstacle to allyship. It is the first map.

## Head of School

Name: **Head Maera Voss**

Title: Keeper of First Signals

What they embody:

Instinct that has survived becoming wisdom.

Teaching style:

Maera asks students to stop talking. She makes them repeat a scene with fewer words each time until the signal underneath the story becomes impossible to miss.

Blind spot:

She can distrust analysis even when analysis would protect the group.

Signature line:

"Your body spoke three sentences ago. Why are you still negotiating?"

## Lieutenants

### Ilen Rootwake

Title: Breath Tutor

Subskill: Nervous system tracking

What they teach:

Students learn to notice activation, shutdown, urgency, and dissociation before those states choose the move for them.

How they test students:

Ilen runs pressure drills where students must name what is happening in their body before they are allowed to act.

Shadow version:

Treating every sensation as truth.

Move or training prompt:

**Name the Signal:** Before you roll, name the body signal driving your action. If the table agrees the signal is honest, gain 1 Clarity. If you ignore the signal, take 1 Tension Token.

### Sava Embermilk

Title: Dream Archivist

Subskill: Pattern sensing

What they teach:

Students learn to notice repeated images, emotional weather, rumors, dreams, and symbolic patterns in a mission field.

How they test students:

Sava gives students three unrelated details and asks what pattern they are avoiding.

Shadow version:

Magical thinking; making symbols carry more certainty than they deserve.

Move or training prompt:

**Read the Omen Lightly:** When you connect three strange details into a possible pattern, ask one question from Wake Up. The Guide answers with possibility, not certainty.

### Toma Vel

Title: Keeper of the Quiet Room

Subskill: Staying with feeling

What they teach:

Students learn to remain present with fear, grief, anger, joy, or numbness without rushing to fix, flee, explain, or perform.

How they test students:

Toma creates silence after hard feedback and watches who tries to fill it.

Shadow version:

Mistaking endurance for integration.

Move or training prompt:

**Stay One Breath Longer:** When you stay with a hard feeling without fixing it, take +1 forward to Grow Up.

## Trial

The student enters a mission scene where every obvious explanation is wrong.

They pass only if they name the felt signal in the field before choosing a strategy.

## Shadow Exam

The student is given a powerful body signal that is real but incomplete.

They fail if they treat the signal as total truth.

They pass if they honor the signal and still ask what else is true.

## Mark / Badge

The **Listening Pulse**, a small mark worn over the wrist or throat.

## Move Unlock: Feel the Field

When you enter a charged scene and pause to feel the field before speaking, roll + Wake Up.

On a 10+, ask 2.

On a 7-9, ask 1.

On a 6-, ask 1, but the answer also reveals what your own body is projecting.

Questions:

- What emotion is steering the room?
- Who is most activated?
- What need has not been named?
- What story is the body telling before the mouth catches up?

## Downtime Training

During downtime, train with the School of the Body to:

- clear 1 Tension Token through somatic practice
- convert a body signal into 1 Clarity
- prepare for a difficult feedback scene
- ask the Head of School what your body is refusing to ignore

## Campaign Use

Use this School when:

- players are overthinking
- a mission field has hidden emotional pressure
- a character is bypassing their own signal
- a campaign needs dreams, omens, rituals, or embodied intelligence
