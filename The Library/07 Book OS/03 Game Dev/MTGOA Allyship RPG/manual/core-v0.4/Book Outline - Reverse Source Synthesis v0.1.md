# Book Outline - Reverse Source Synthesis v0.1

Status: structural planning draft

Purpose: reverse-outline the inspiration texts and use those patterns, plus the current MTGOA RPG design, to create a proposed full-book outline.

## Design Question

What shape should the MTGOA RPG manual take if it wants to feel:

- beautiful enough to buy
- clear enough to play
- deep enough to revisit
- modular enough to support future paid campaign packets
- flavorful enough to feel like a hidden academy handbook

## Reverse Outline: Kids on Bikes

## What The Book Does Structurally

Kids on Bikes is organized around **table comfort, shared place, character identity, then play procedure**.

It does not start with a long lore chapter. It gives a genre mood, then quickly moves into the social container and collaborative world.

## Functional Outline

1. **Threshold scene / genre invitation**
   - establishes ordinary people facing strange forces
   - creates mood before rules

2. **Setting boundaries**
   - establishes table safety before creation
   - makes comfort and consent part of setup

3. **World building**
   - players create the town together
   - tone, era, landmarks, economy, rumors
   - the town becomes playable through questions

4. **Character creation**
   - choose trope
   - assign stats
   - choose strengths, flaws, fears, motivations, backpack
   - answer relationship questions

5. **Playing the game**
   - explain stat checks
   - planned actions vs snap decisions
   - difficulty numbers
   - adversity tokens
   - consequences

6. **Special / powered character rules**
   - optional focal-point layer
   - adds wonder and asymmetry

7. **GM information**
   - how to facilitate mysteries, threats, powered characters, and tone

8. **Appendices**
   - relationship questions
   - strengths
   - flaws
   - aspects
   - tropes
   - difficulty reference
   - blank sheet

9. **Adventure modules**
   - worked examples and ready-to-play content

## Structural Lesson For MTGOA

The book should place **safety, shared setting, and relationships** before deep mechanics.

MTGOA translation:

- safety as ship protocol early
- school-ship creation and mission field creation before full rules density
- student relationships before mission pressure
- examples that show table behavior, not only mechanics

## Reverse Outline: Dungeon World

## What The Book Does Structurally

Dungeon World is organized around **genre promise, player identity, core play procedure, character options, then GM machinery**.

It recruits the reader into being a fantasy adventurer before explaining the whole system.

## Functional Outline

1. **Introduction**
   - fantasy world promise
   - direct address to the reader
   - class identity vignettes
   - what roleplaying is

2. **Playing the game**
   - conversation structure
   - move triggers
   - dice outcomes
   - equipment, harm, damage, XP, advancement

3. **Character creation**
   - step-by-step player onboarding
   - choose class, race, look, stats, bonds, alignment, gear

4. **Basic moves**
   - the universal action vocabulary
   - moves everyone can use
   - establishes the core gameplay loop

5. **Class playbooks**
   - each class gets identity, stats, moves, advancement
   - book gives many desire objects

6. **GM chapter**
   - agenda
   - principles
   - GM moves
   - how the GM participates as a rules-bound player

7. **First session**
   - practical starting advice
   - ask questions and use answers
   - begin with action

8. **Fronts**
   - offscreen pressure and campaign structure
   - dangers, grim portents, stakes

9. **The world**
   - worldbuilding procedures and questions

10. **Monsters and setting threats**
   - large catalog of opposition and inspiration

11. **Equipment and advanced rules**
   - gear, tags, conversion, NPC tools, references

## Structural Lesson For MTGOA

MTGOA should give the reader **identity heat** early, then make the core loop concrete.

MTGOA translation:

- Houses and Schools should feel like playbooks / class fantasies
- Basic Moves need a strong reference chapter
- Guide chapter needs agenda, principles, and moves
- pressure systems should be MTGOA's equivalent of Fronts
- campaign packets should inherit from the Fronts / world chapter structure

## Reverse Outline: Hearts Blazing

## What The Book Does Structurally

Hearts Blazing is organized around **collaborative setup, ensemble roles, card components, episode pacing, then finale**.

It feels like a story machine on the table.

## Functional Outline

1. **Premise and setup**
   - collaborative spec-fic love and conflict
   - materials
   - tone and assumptions

2. **Assign trope caretakers**
   - setting and history
   - technology and ability
   - organization
   - supporting cast
   - antagonists
   - distributes world authority

3. **Safety / tone**
   - content boundaries
   - Cut and Brake
   - guidelines over hard rules

4. **Choose archetypes**
   - Ace, Leader, Rookie, Engineer, Veteran
   - ensemble identity before individual mechanics

5. **Character decks**
   - cards represent dramatic actions, cliches, frailties
   - components create playable prompts

6. **Motives**
   - hidden goals and keywords
   - character drama engine

7. **Build plotline deck**
   - Pilot / Midseason / Finale cards
   - creates arc shape before play

8. **Episode play**
   - reveal episode
   - bid cards
   - compare to threshold
   - success/non-success changes team state

9. **Scene structure**
   - Launch
   - Bridge
   - Wrap
   - Commercial Break

10. **Finale / denouement**
   - motives reveal
   - keywords matter
   - character endings

## Structural Lesson For MTGOA

MTGOA should give the table **components and handles**, especially because allyship can become abstract.

MTGOA translation:

- Houses and Ensemble Roles distribute table identity
- mission cards / episode cards create pacing
- Briefing / Field / Debrief can replace Launch / Bridge / Wrap
- private Belief replaces hidden motive as drama engine
- Commercial Break becomes a ship protocol pause
- campaign packets should include cards, prompts, NPCs, and clocks

## Reverse Outline: Foundations

## What The Book Does Structurally

Foundations is organized around **minimal premise, immediate setup, then a long prompt engine**.

It makes the reader trust the book by being exceptionally clear about what to do next.

## Functional Outline

1. **Content warning**
   - names historical and thematic risk

2. **Introduction**
   - what the game is
   - what it creates
   - what materials you need

3. **Setup**
   - divide card decks
   - create prompt deck
   - optional magic cards

4. **Conceptual framing**
   - time scale
   - world map
   - chronicle

5. **How to play**
   - draw a card
   - turn to prompt
   - follow prompt
   - update map and chronicle
   - discard or return card

6. **How to finish**
   - stop when satisfied
   - full playthrough length
   - optional jokers

7. **Terminology**
   - culture
   - subculture
   - species
   - subspecies
   - ethical reframing of fantasy language

8. **Prompt sections by suit**
   - Spades
   - Hearts
   - Clubs
   - Diamonds
   - each prompt alters the artifact

9. **Jokers and condensed prompts**
   - optional complexity and reference

## Structural Lesson For MTGOA

MTGOA should make its procedures produce **visible artifacts**.

MTGOA translation:

- ship map
- mission field map
- student record
- House trust tracks
- faction heat clocks
- mission chronicle
- debrief form
- campaign packet prompt engine

The reader should not only understand the game. They should see what play creates.

## Cross-Source Structural Pattern

The source texts repeatedly use this macro-sequence:

1. **Open the emotional door.**
2. **Tell the reader who they are here.**
3. **Give them something to hold.**
4. **Show what play creates.**
5. **Teach the core procedure.**
6. **Give character / role identity.**
7. **Give facilitator tools.**
8. **Give campaign / world tools.**
9. **Give references, appendices, and ready content.**

For MTGOA, this becomes:

1. school-ship threshold
2. student invitation
3. House / School / Offer / Cost / Line / Bond
4. student record / crew / ship / mission field / chronicle
5. Basic Moves and school-owned stats
6. Houses, Schools, Roles, Beliefs
7. Guide agenda, principles, and moves
8. mission field, clocks, campaign packet builder
9. starter mission, sheets, cards, references

## Final Structural Takeaway

The MTGOA manual should not choose between being a beautiful artifact and being usable.

Its structure should do both:

- **Chapter 1** makes the reader want in.
- **Chapters 2-4** make play possible.
- **Chapters 5-7** make identity and growth desirable.
- **Chapters 8-11** make campaigns sustainable.
- **Chapter 12** proves the game works.
- **Back matter** makes the book useful at the table.

The book's job is to make the reader think:

**I know what this is. I know how to start. I know who I might become here. I want to see where the ship goes next.**
