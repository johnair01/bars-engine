# Book Roadmap Spec - Chapters 6-12 and Starter Missions v0.1

Status: planning spec

Purpose: define the next manual build sequence after Chapters 3-5, including Chapter 6-12 goals, target artifacts, the missing superpowers layer, and four starter mission concepts aligned with the four Houses.

## Core Goal

The next build phase should complete the playable spine of the MTGOA RPG manual.

Chapters 1-5 now establish:

- the ship and table premise
- admission / character creation
- Basic Moves and dice
- House culture, moves, Trust, Heat, and mission duties

Chapters 6-12 must now answer:

1. How do self-sabotaging beliefs become playable growth arcs?
2. How do beliefs connect to superpowers?
3. How do students bring those beliefs and powers into mastery traditions?
3. What does an episode actually look like?
4. How does a Guide run the game without becoming the moral judge?
5. How do campaign packets work as paid content?
6. How does advancement and graduation work over time?
7. What starter missions let a table play immediately?

The design should preserve the current method:

- source comparison / gap spec
- Regent or institutional framing voice where appropriate
- voiced in-world layers when they deepen vividness
- prose draft
- BARs Engine product spec where the chapter implies an app feature

## Chapter 6: Beliefs, Shadows, And Superpowers

## Function

Chapter 6 deepens character creation.

Beliefs are personal enough that they belong before Schools. Students do not arrive as blank initiates waiting for a mastery path. They arrive with protective stories, distortions, gifts, powers, and ways of surviving attention.

Houses answer: **What practice tradition claimed you first?**

Beliefs answer: **What false story does pressure activate?**

Superpowers answer: **What gift became powerful because you had to survive with it?**

Schools will later answer: **How are you learning to master yourself under pressure?**

The goal is to make inner work gameable without becoming therapy homework. Beliefs should create field consequences, not confession scenes for their own sake. Superpowers should make the belief material feel exciting, mythic, and playable.

## Source Influence

- Hearts Blazing motives / frailty for dramatic vulnerability
- Dungeon World advancement for growth through risk
- Kids on Bikes adversity for useful failure
- superhero school fantasy for powers as embodied metaphors
- MTGOA self-sabotaging belief framework

## Reader Need

The reader should finish Chapter 6 feeling:

- My belief is not a diagnosis.
- My belief gives me dramatic pressure and growth moves.
- My superpower is not separate from my wound; it is what my gift learned to become under pressure.
- I know how my belief gets activated.
- I know how to play the belief without making the table responsible for fixing me.
- I know how rewriting the belief becomes advancement.

## Chapter 7: The Six Schools

## Function

Chapter 7 completes the mastery loop.

Students bring their beliefs and superpowers to their Schools. The School does not erase the belief. It teaches the student how to practice with the gift and the wound under pressure.

The Schools should feel as vivid as the Houses, but they should not duplicate them. Houses are cultures of practice. Schools are mastery traditions, each governed by a Face of the Game Master and interpreted through six altitudinal ranks.

## Source Influence

- Dungeon World class chapters for progression fantasy and move unlocks
- Avatar for mastery traditions, teachers, trials, and embodied disciplines
- X-Men / school fantasy for training rooms, mentors, and student advancement
- existing MTGOA Faces as milestone archetypes

- I know how each School interprets all six ranks.
- I can picture the teachers and training spaces.
- I know how stats belong to Schools.
- I know what School advancement looks like in play.

## Four Starter Mission Concepts

## 1. Provisioners Starter: The Missing Water

Featured House: Provisioners

Pressure House: Lanternbearers

School Spotlight: School of the Body

Core Question:

**What is actually depleted, and who is carrying the cost of pretending it is not?**

Premise:

The ship arrives at Pelagos Station, where public reservoirs are locked behind emergency rationing after an alleged sabotage attempt. Local organizers insist the water was already being diverted before the explosion.

The first visible problem is scarcity. The deeper problem is that survival labor has been pushed onto exhausted residents while officials praise their resilience.

Opening Scene:

The crew arrives during a public rationing ceremony. Officials give speeches about sacrifice while children wait beside empty containers and a maintenance worker quietly passes coded notes to local organizers.

Primary Clock:

**Reservoir Lockdown**

1. Rations shrink.
2. Informal water networks take greater risks.
3. Officials criminalize "unauthorized distribution."
4. A local organizer is arrested.
5. The emergency order becomes permanent.
6. The station's poorest quarter goes dry.

House Duties:

- Provisioners track water, rest, transport, and who is overholding survival.
- Lanternbearers decide what proof can become public without exposing residents.
- Weavers coordinate local groups who have been isolated from one another.
- Linekeepers decide when a lawful order must be refused.

Why It Works As A Starter:

The mission makes material need concrete immediately. Players learn that helping is not the same as being seen helping.

## 2. Weavers Starter: The Broken Concord

Featured House: Weavers

Pressure House: Linekeepers

School Spotlight: School of the Bridge

Core Question:

**What structure lets the work continue when trust has become too thin to carry it?**

Premise:

The ship is called to Concordia Ring, a habitat governed by three mutual-aid councils that once shared food, medical labor, and transport. A failed emergency vote shattered the coalition. Each council now insists the others betrayed the agreement.

The first visible problem is a deadlocked meeting. The deeper problem is that no one knows which roles, promises, and decision rules still exist.

Opening Scene:

The crew enters a circular council chamber where every chair has a nameplate, half the named people refuse to sit, and one exhausted interpreter is translating for all three factions while everyone calls her "neutral."

Primary Clock:

**Coalition Fracture**

1. Shared calendar collapses.
2. Side agreements replace public process.
3. Load-bearing people burn out.
4. Medical and food systems desynchronize.
5. One council withdraws from the Concord.
6. The Ring becomes three hostile territories.

House Duties:

- Weavers name the missing roles and broken handoffs.
- Linekeepers identify which agreement violations require immediate boundaries.
- Provisioners track who loses material support while leaders argue.
- Lanternbearers reveal the hidden pattern without turning one faction into the villain of the story.

Why It Works As A Starter:

The mission teaches structure as care. It gives players meetings that are not boring because every procedural choice has material consequences.

## 3. Linekeepers Starter: The Quiet Gate

Featured House: Linekeepers

Pressure House: Provisioners

School Spotlight: School of the Line

Core Question:

**What line must be drawn when politeness has become the lock on the cage?**

Premise:

The ship finds Haven Gate, a sanctuary port that advertises itself as safe refuge for displaced travelers. In practice, the port denies entry through a maze of "temporary" requirements, missing forms, moral interviews, and security delays.

The first visible problem is bureaucracy. The deeper problem is that delay is being used as a weapon while staff are trained to call cruelty "process."

Opening Scene:

The crew watches a family turned away at the gate because their witness form has the wrong stamp. A clerk apologizes with practiced warmth. Behind the clerk, guards quietly move the family toward the outer platform before anyone can gather.

Primary Clock:

**Gate Closure**

1. One family is delayed.
2. Requirements change without notice.
3. Witnesses are separated.
4. Protest is labeled disruption.
5. Guards remove people from the intake hall.
6. The port seals the gate under safety protocol.

House Duties:

- Linekeepers decide what boundary must be enforced now.
- Provisioners track immediate safety, shelter, food, and transport for people outside the gate.
- Weavers identify which procedures are real and which are weapons.
- Lanternbearers decide what testimony can be documented without risking deportation or retaliation.

Why It Works As A Starter:

The mission teaches that direct action is not just shouting. It is drawing a line with cost, protection, and follow-through.

## 4. Lanternbearers Starter: The Borrowed Story

Featured House: Lanternbearers

Pressure House: Weavers

School Spotlight: School of the Pattern

Core Question:

**What truth must become visible, and who loses control if we make it visible badly?**

Premise:

The ship arrives at Lumen Fair, a traveling cultural festival that has become famous for telling the story of a marginalized community's survival. The performances raise funds and sympathy, but the community whose story is being performed has lost control of the narrative, the money, and the archive.

The first visible problem is representation. The deeper problem is extraction disguised as awareness.

Opening Scene:

The crew attends a dazzling opening performance. The crowd cries. Donors pledge support. Backstage, a young archivist quietly removes a family name from a display because the person named never consented to be part of the show.

Primary Clock:

**Spectacle Takes The Story**

1. The dramatic version spreads.
2. Donors reward the simplified story.
3. Community members are pressured to perform pain.
4. Archives are edited for emotional impact.
5. Consent objections are framed as ingratitude.
6. The story becomes famous and the people become props.

House Duties:

- Lanternbearers handle visibility, witness, archive, and consent.
- Weavers trace who controls decision-making and money flow.
- Provisioners ask what material support actually reaches the community.
- Linekeepers decide when the performance must be stopped, interrupted, or refused.

Why It Works As A Starter:

The mission teaches awareness as power and danger. It gives players a vivid, emotionally legible conflict where good intentions can still extract harm.

- the complete engine
- vivid House and School culture

- three shorter starter mission seeds
- a clear campaign packet template

- expanded mission packets
- House-specific campaign arcs
- School-focused advancement arcs
- digital character creator
