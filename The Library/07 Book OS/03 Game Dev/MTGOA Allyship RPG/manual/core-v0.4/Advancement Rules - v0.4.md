# Advancement Rules - v0.4

Status: draft rules

## Principle

Characters advance by metabolizing impact.

They do not grow because they looked good.

They grow because something happened, they saw it, stayed with it, repaired or changed course, and became more capable.

## Tracks

There are three advancement tracks:

1. **Growth**
   - personal development

2. **School Rank**
   - training progress through the six Schools

3. **Legacy**
   - campaign-level durable change

## Growth

Growth is marked by individual characters.

Mark Growth when:

- you name your impact after a miss
- you act against your self-sabotaging belief
- you complete a School training prompt
- you repair a meaningful harm
- you hold your Line under pressure
- you receive feedback without disappearing
- you make a small useful move despite feeling insignificant
- you ask for help while keeping agency
- you teach another student something you have actually learned

Do not mark Growth just because you succeeded.

Success may create impact. Growth comes from what you learn and integrate.

## Spending Growth

Spend Growth during downtime.

Options:

- 3 Growth: take a lieutenant-taught subskill move
- 3 Growth: revise Offer, Cost, Line, or Bond after a meaningful scene
- 4 Growth: increase one stat by +1, max +3
- 5 Growth: unlock a new move from your House, Role, or Belief subclass
- listed Adversity cost: unlock or activate a Belief Power Move when its requirements are met
- variable: increase School Rank if requirements are met

## School Rank

Each School has six ranks.

Each rank corresponds to one Face, interpreted through that School.

1. **Initiate** - Shaman interpretation
2. **Practitioner** - Challenger interpretation
3. **Adept** - Regent interpretation
4. **Steward** - Architect interpretation
5. **Integrator** - Diplomat interpretation
6. **Keeper** - Sage interpretation

This keeps the Schools horizontal while preserving altitude.

A Keeper of the Body is not "above" the School of the Horizon. They are someone who can express the full arc of allyship mastery through embodiment.

You start as an Initiate in your Home School.

You can become an Initiate in another School through downtime, story invitation, or a campaign milestone.

## Rank Requirements

### Initiate

Requirement:

- receive invitation, choose Home School, or complete an introductory scene

You gain:

- access to downtime training
- one lieutenant relationship
- one training prompt

### Practitioner

Requirement:

- spend 3 Growth
- complete one lieutenant training
- demonstrate the School's capacity decisively during an episode

You gain:

- the School's Move Unlock
- one subskill move
- permission to attempt the School Trial

### Adept

Requirement:

- spend 5 Growth
- pass the School Trial
- preserve the School's capacity under pressure without collapsing into its shadow
- repeat the capacity through role, ritual, or responsibility

You gain:

- one advanced School move
- ability to teach one subskill
- one resistance against that School's Shadow Exam

### Steward

Requirement:

- spend 5 Growth
- create a durable tool, structure, agreement, or strategy through this School
- show that the structure helps someone else act with more capacity

You gain:

- one design or strategy move from this School
- one School-facing support structure other players can use
- permission to convert Clarity into Leverage through this School once per episode

### Integrator

Requirement:

- spend 6 Growth
- transform a relationship, faction, or team tension through this School
- protect difference and consent while staying in connection

You gain:

- one relational integration move from this School
- permission to reduce Faction Heat through this School once per arc
- authority to mediate between one School and one House during downtime

### Keeper

Requirement:

- spend 8 Growth
- pass the Shadow Exam
- use the full six-rank range of this School during a mission
- teach, protect, or guide someone else using this School's capacity
- complete a mission where this School's lesson changes the outcome

You gain:

- Keeper mark
- once-per-campaign School intervention
- authority to lead a mission through this School's lens

## Rank Limits

Recommended for early play:

- starting characters may have only one Home School at Initiate
- no character can reach Adept before the end of the first campaign arc
- Steward and Integrator are mid-campaign achievements, not starting permissions
- Keeper should be rare and campaign-defining
- no School outranks another School; rank only measures depth inside that School

## School Trials

A Trial is a scene or mission beat where the character must use the School's capacity under pressure.

Trial examples:

- School of the Body: feel the field before explaining it
- School of the Line: draw a clean line without cruelty
- School of the Oath: preserve purpose while changing form
- School of the Pattern: redesign structure without erasing people
- School of the Bridge: hold relationship without vanishing
- School of the Horizon: see the larger game and still descend

Trials should be playable, not ceremonial only.

## Shadow Exams

A Shadow Exam reveals how the character misuses or avoids the School.

The goal is not to punish the player.

The goal is to create a scene where the character must choose a more integrated expression of the School.

Shadow Exam examples:

- Body: a real signal is incomplete
- Line: fighting would feel good but harm the vulnerable
- Oath: tradition once protected life but now protects harm
- Pattern: a perfect design erases people
- Bridge: peace requires self-erasure
- Horizon: perspective becomes escape

## Lieutenant Training

Each lieutenant teaches one subskill.

Training can happen during downtime.

A training scene should include:

- a prompt
- a mistake
- a correction
- a small test
- a reflection

If the player engages honestly, they may mark Growth or qualify for rank advancement.

## Belief Subclass Advancement

Each self-sabotaging belief should eventually have levels.

Temporary rule:

When your belief triggers and you choose the growth move instead of the wound move, mark Growth.

Wound Moves should be easier in the moment but create durable consequences.

Growth Moves should cost something in real time but create sustainable outcomes.

Power Moves should be earned through Belief growth and bought or activated with Adversity tokens.

After doing this three times, write a new sentence:

**I used to believe ___. I am learning ___.**

This sentence becomes a resistance.

Once per session, when the old belief triggers, you may read the new sentence aloud and avoid taking Tension.

## House Advancement

House advancement is separate from School Rank.

Your House may grant:

- new House moves
- faction trust
- mission authority
- access to resources
- title or responsibility

Mark House progress when:

- you use your House gift in a way that changes the mission
- you confront your House shadow
- you improve your House's relationship with another House
- you complete the House advancement prompt

Temporary rule:

After 3 House progress marks, take a new House move or improve House standing.

## Role Advancement

Your Ensemble Role can deepen.

Mark Role progress when:

- you perform your team function under pressure
- you resist your Role's risk
- another player says your Role helped them act

After 3 Role progress marks, upgrade your Role move.

Example:

Spark's Flash of Brilliance no longer automatically draws attention on a miss; instead, the Guide chooses attention or cost.

## Legacy

Legacy belongs to the table.

Earn Legacy when:

- a mission creates durable change
- a local faction is left with more agency
- a resource flow is repaired
- a harmful structure changes
- a truth becomes visible without extraction
- a student passes a Trial in a way that helps others
- the ship becomes more capable because of what was learned

Spend Legacy to:

- reduce Ship Exposure
- stabilize a mission outcome
- strengthen a House
- unlock a new campaign opportunity
- repair a damaged local relationship
- create a new ship resource
- protect the Headmaster or school from consequence

## End of Episode Advancement

At the end of each episode, ask:

1. Did anyone act against their self-sabotaging belief?
2. Did anyone name their impact after a miss?
3. Did anyone protect consent under pressure?
4. Did anyone hold their Line?
5. Did anyone make a repair?
6. Did a House gift change the mission?
7. Did a School lesson become visible?

Characters mark Growth for questions that apply.

The table marks Legacy if the mission outcome produced durable change.

## End of Arc Advancement

At the end of a campaign arc:

1. Resolve or rewrite Bonds.
2. Spend Growth.
3. Check School Rank requirements.
4. Check House progress.
5. Check Role progress.
6. Update belief sentence if applicable.
7. Decide what changed aboard the ship.
8. Decide what world, faction, or crisis now calls the ship.

## Advancement Warning

Do not let advancement become a badge treadmill.

Every new mark, move, rank, or title should come with responsibility.

The question is never:

"How powerful am I now?"

The question is:

"What kind of allyship can I now be trusted to practice?"
