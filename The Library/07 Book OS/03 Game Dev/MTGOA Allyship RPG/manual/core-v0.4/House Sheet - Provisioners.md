# House Sheet - Provisioners

Domain: **Gather Resources**

Core question:

**What is actually depleted, and how does capacity move?**

## Vibe

Quartermasters, hearth-keepers, mutual aid captains, field medics, cooks, logistics witches, supply runners, people who know that a revolution without snacks becomes a meeting about snacks.

Provisioners are the House of practical care.

They notice hunger, exhaustion, missing tools, silent shortages, and the person carrying too much.

## Motto

**Need first. Theater later.**

## Symbol

A cupped bowl with a star inside.

## Gift

Provisioners can tell the difference between visible help and needed help.

They know that support is not real until it reaches the place where depletion lives.

## Shadow

Provisioners can turn care into control.

They may hoard resources, over-function, rescue people who did not ask to be rescued, or turn material support into a performance of goodness.

Shadow tells:

- "I know what they need."
- "If I do not hold this, no one will."
- "Look how much I am helping."
- "They should be grateful."

## Known For

- supply maps
- food, medicine, rest, shelter, tools
- knowing who is close to burnout
- making the invisible labor visible
- moving capacity quietly
- asking cleanly

## What They Get Wrong

- mistaking giving for listening
- rescuing instead of resourcing
- creating dependency
- treating urgency as consent
- confusing exhaustion with virtue

## Starting Move: Move the Right Resource

When you stop before helping and ask what is actually depleted, roll + Wake Up.

On a 10+, choose 2.

On a 7-9, choose 1.

On a 6-, you identify a need, but your first offer would make something worse unless you change course.

Options:

- Name the real missing resource.
- Identify who is carrying the current burden.
- Learn what support is not wanted.
- Find one resource already nearby.
- Gain 1 Clarity.

## House Feature: Supply Cache

Once per episode, you may declare that you brought one ordinary useful item, contact, or preparation that fits the mission.

If it solves the problem too neatly, the Guide names a cost:

- it is limited
- someone else needed it
- it creates an obligation
- using it exposes the ship
- it helps now but complicates later

## House Clock

**Burnout Spiral**

1. One person quietly carries too much.
2. The group praises their resilience.
3. The real need gets renamed as attitude.
4. Help arrives in the wrong form.
5. The overburdened person withdraws or breaks.
6. Trust in support collapses.

## Relationship to Other Houses

Weavers:

Provisioners respect structure when it moves resources. They resent structure when it delays relief.

Linekeepers:

Provisioners admire their courage but fear they create messes others must clean up.

Lanternbearers:

Provisioners value awareness when it brings material change. They distrust awareness that becomes spectacle.

## House Advancement Prompt

When you finish a mission, ask:

**Who needed less admiration and more support?**

If your answer changes how the group allocates resources, mark Growth.
