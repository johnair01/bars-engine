# House Sheet - Weavers

Domain: **Skillful Organizing**

Core question:

**What structure lets the work continue?**

## Vibe

Coalition architects, ritual designers, schedulers, governance nerds, bridge builders, minutes-takers with knives behind the smile, people who understand that "we should all just communicate" is not a plan.

Weavers are the House of containers.

They build roles, handoffs, agreements, rhythms, decision rules, and repeatable practices.

## Motto

**Care needs a container.**

## Symbol

Four threads knotted around an open center.

## Gift

Weavers can turn fragile good intentions into durable coordination.

They see where work is falling through the gaps and create the minimum structure that lets people keep going.

## Shadow

Weavers can worship process.

They may create meetings instead of movement, control through structure, or protect the system after it stops serving life.

Shadow tells:

- "We need a process for that."
- "If people followed the structure, this would not happen."
- "The container matters more than the people complaining about it."
- "Let's circle back."

## Known For

- role clarity
- handoffs
- decision rules
- coalition maps
- group agreements
- succession plans
- making invisible labor assignable

## What They Get Wrong

- overdesigning
- hiding fear inside procedure
- delaying action through consultation
- treating dissent as disruption
- making care feel like administration

## Starting Move: Name the Missing Role

When a group keeps failing because no one owns a needed function, roll + Wake Up or + Clean Up.

On a 10+, choose 2.

On a 7-9, choose 1.

On a 6-, you name the missing role, but someone hears it as blame.

Options:

- Identify the missing role.
- Clarify who currently carries it unofficially.
- Create a clean handoff.
- Reduce a group's confusion.
- Restore 1 Trust with someone who needed structure.

## House Feature: The Smallest Container

Once per episode, you may propose a small structure:

- a role
- a timebox
- a handoff
- a decision rule
- a check-in
- a shared note

If the table accepts it and uses it, gain 1 Legacy if it still matters by the end of the episode.

## House Clock

**Process Collapse**

1. Everyone assumes someone else owns the work.
2. One person informally becomes load-bearing.
3. Decisions happen in side channels.
4. Trust erodes around unclear authority.
5. The group creates process too late.
6. The container survives but the people leave.

## Relationship to Other Houses

Provisioners:

Weavers rely on Provisioners to keep structures embodied. They resent being told process is not urgent.

Linekeepers:

Weavers need Linekeepers when agreements are violated. They fear Linekeepers will burn bridges too early.

Lanternbearers:

Weavers need Lanternbearers to name patterns. They worry naming without structure creates heat without change.

## House Advancement Prompt

When you finish a mission, ask:

**What worked only because someone overheld it?**

If your answer creates a cleaner handoff, mark Growth.
