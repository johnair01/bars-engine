# Canonical Premise - School Ship

Project: **Mastering the Game of Allyship RPG**

Status: v0.4 canonical premise draft

## The Game

**Mastering the Game of Allyship** is a school-ship roleplaying game about students learning how to become useful under pressure.

The player characters are young allies, late bloomers, exiles, prodigies, dropouts, misfits, and unlikely recruits aboard a hidden traveling academy. The school moves from world to world, answering crises that ordinary institutions cannot or will not resolve.

The game is not about proving your character is good.

It is about what happens when your help meets reality.

## The School-Ship

The school is a vessel, sanctuary, monastery, academy, and mission platform.

It may look different from campaign to campaign:

- a skyship crossing impossible weather
- a living temple hidden between worlds
- a fugitive orbital academy after a purge
- a spelljammer full of students and secrets
- a rebel school moving faster than empire
- a monastery that docks only where it is needed

The vibe can change. The function stays the same.

The ship carries:

- the four Houses of practice
- the six Schools of mastery
- a Headmaster with a long memory
- students still learning what their power is for
- unfinished history
- a reason to keep moving

## The Headmaster

The Headmaster is the recurring elder NPC.

They are not only a quest-giver. They are the guardian of the school's mission, the keeper of its contradictions, and the person most responsible for deciding when students are ready enough to be useful.

The Headmaster should have:

- a philosophy of allyship
- a secret grief
- a political enemy
- a blind spot
- a reason the ship is hidden
- a reason students must act before they feel ready

The Headmaster can be wise, evasive, funny, compromised, exhausted, radiant, terrifying, or wrong.

They should never be merely decorative.

## The Four Houses

The four Houses are enduring factions aboard the school-ship. They are the social and practice structure of the academy.

They are not tied to one campaign world.

Every campaign has these four functions, even if the campaign renames them.

| House | Allyship Domain | Core Question |
|---|---|---|
| Provisioners | Gather Resources | What is actually depleted, and how does capacity move? |
| Weavers | Skillful Organizing | What structure lets the work continue? |
| Linekeepers | Direct Action | What line must be drawn, and what will it cost? |
| Lanternbearers | Raise Awareness | What truth must become visible, and with whose consent? |

The Houses are like Hogwarts Houses crossed with activist lineages, guilds, ship departments, and practice communities.

A player may belong to one House, train with another, and clash with a third.

## The Six Schools

The six Schools are milestone paths. They are not Houses, and they do not outrank each other.

Each School is associated with one Face of the Game Master. Each School also contains a six-rank altitude ladder, so every School teaches its own interpretation of all six Faces.

| School | Face | Owned Stat | Milestone |
|---|---|---|---|
| School of the Body | Shaman | Sense | Feel the field before explaining it. |
| School of the Line | Challenger | Act | Act without becoming cruel. |
| School of the Oath | Regent | Steady | Preserve what matters without becoming the wall. |
| School of the Pattern | Architect | Shape | Design leverage without losing contact. |
| School of the Bridge | Diplomat | Tend | Hold relationship without vanishing. |
| School of the Horizon | Sage | Speak | See the game and still stay in it. |

The Schools function like gym leaders, temples, or mastery gates without collapsing into a single linear ladder.

Each School has:

- a teacher
- an owned stat
- a trial
- a shadow exam
- a badge or mark
- a move unlock

## Missions

The ship travels to worlds in crisis.

Each campaign packet defines one world, settlement, station, kingdom, school, city, ship, planet, movement, or social field that needs help.

The ship arrives because something has gone wrong:

- a resource is being redirected
- a coalition is collapsing
- a powerful institution is hiding harm
- a community is being praised instead of supported
- a boundary must be drawn
- a story must be told with care

The players do not save worlds by being impressive.

They help by practicing allyship moves under pressure.

## Campaign Packets

The core manual teaches the engine.

Campaign packets provide the worlds.

A campaign packet includes:

- the field / world
- local factions
- mission clocks
- NPCs
- episode cards
- custom moves
- House tensions
- School milestones
- downtime hooks
- BAR prompts
- real-world reps

This makes the business model modular:

- the core manual is the entry artifact
- campaign packets are expandable paid content
- guided campaigns are the premium experience

## Core Play Loop

1. The ship receives a mission.
2. The table enters a world in crisis.
3. Players read the field.
4. Players make allyship moves.
5. Mission clocks and faction heat change.
6. The table returns to the ship.
7. Downtime reveals what the mission taught.
8. Players train, repair, recover, and advance.
9. Each player records a BAR.
10. The next mission calls.

## Core Promise

**Practice allyship before the moment costs someone real.**

The game gives players a fictional training ground where help has consequences, repair matters, and becoming useful is more important than being seen as good.
