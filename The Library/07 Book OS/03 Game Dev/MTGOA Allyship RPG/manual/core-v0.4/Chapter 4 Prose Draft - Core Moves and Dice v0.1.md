# Chapter 4 Prose Draft - Core Moves and Dice v0.1

Status: book prose draft

Purpose: teach the core play procedure, Basic Moves, school-owned stats, roll ladder, and token loop in Regent voice with Council of Joannes marginalia.

## Page 1: The Conversation Of Play

The Headmaster once summarized the rules of field action as "attend to the living pattern, move without severing contact, and let consequence become curriculum."

This is lovely.

It is also why the Office of Practical Instruction now maintains this chapter.

At the table, play is a conversation.

1. The Guide describes a charged situation.
2. A player says what their student does.
3. The table checks whether that action triggers a Basic Move.
4. If the outcome is risky and uncertain, the player rolls.
5. The roll changes the situation.
6. The Guide asks, "What do you do now?"

The rules do not replace the conversation.

They enter when the conversation reaches pressure.

> [MARGINALIA - J.]
> "Charged situation" means something can actually change. If nothing can change, you are not in a scene. You are in scenery.

## Page 2: When To Roll

Do not roll because a student is doing something dramatic.

Roll because the outcome matters and nobody honestly knows what happens next.

Roll when a move could change:

- trust
- safety
- resources
- consent
- pressure
- belonging
- leverage
- a mission clock
- faction heat
- a relationship
- a student's training

Do not roll to see whether a character can cross a quiet hallway, remember their own locker combination, or pour tea without incident unless the hallway is listening, the locker is lying, or the tea has entered the curriculum.

The ship contains all three possibilities. Use judgment.

### The Roll Test

Before rolling, ask:

1. Is the student doing something in the fiction?
2. Is the outcome risky or uncertain?
3. Could the result change the field?
4. Does one of the Basic Moves fit?

If yes, roll.

If no, let the action happen and keep the conversation moving.

## Page 3: Basic Moves At A Glance

Every risky allyship action enters through one of five Basic Moves.

| Basic Move | Use It When You... | Core Question |
|---|---|---|
| **Wake Up** | notice, read, ask, reveal, orient, or diagnose | What is really happening? |
| **Open Up** | reveal, receive, ask for help, risk trust, or create honest contact | What becomes possible if you let yourself be known? |
| **Clean Up** | repair, own impact, restore trust, or close a loop | What needs repair? |
| **Grow Up** | stay with discomfort, feedback, complexity, or limits | What capacity is being built? |
| **Show Up** | act, intervene, resource, organize, witness, or follow through | What needs to happen now? |

The Basic Move names the kind of moment.

The stat names the discipline the student uses to move through it.

If this distinction feels fussy, remember that the ship once allowed students to roll "help" for everything. The records from that semester are mostly apologies.

## Page 4: Stats As School Disciplines

Stats are School-owned approaches.

They are not virtues, talents, or proof of moral worth. They are trained ways of meeting pressure.

| Stat | Owning School | Use It When You... |
|---|---|---|
| **Sense** | School of the Body | read the room, notice emotion, track consent, feel the field |
| **Act** | School of the Line | intervene, interrupt, follow through, take visible action |
| **Steady** | School of the Oath | stay present, regulate, keep commitments, hold a line |
| **Shape** | School of the Pattern | organize, design, map systems, build containers |
| **Tend** | School of the Bridge | repair, support, resource, care, reduce burden |
| **Speak** | School of the Horizon | name truth, ask hard questions, translate perspective |

A student does not need to belong to a School to use its stat.

The School owns the curriculum.

The student owns their relationship to it.

## Page 5: Choosing The Move And The Stat

Choose the Basic Move by asking what the action is trying to do.

- Understand the situation? **Wake Up.**
- Risk honest contact, reveal a need, or receive support? **Open Up.**
- Repair harm or restore integrity? **Clean Up.**
- Build capacity under pressure? **Grow Up.**
- Change the situation through action? **Show Up.**

Then choose the stat by asking how the student is doing it.

- Reading signal, emotion, consent, or atmosphere? Roll +Sense.
- Interrupting, intervening, or following through visibly? Roll +Act.
- Holding pressure, commitment, discomfort, or a clean line? Roll +Steady.
- Designing a system, plan, container, or leverage point? Roll +Shape.
- Repairing, caring, resourcing, or reducing burden? Roll +Tend.
- Naming truth, asking a hard question, or translating perspective? Roll +Speak.

If two moves fit, ask what would make the scene more honest.

If two stats fit, the player chooses which discipline their student leans on. The Guide names what kind of cost each path risks.

Example:

Mara wants to approach the local organizers before the welcome ceremony ends.

If she watches the crowd to understand who is carrying the cost, that is **Wake Up + Sense**.

If she tells the crew that the organizer's warning hit her Belief and asks someone else to take the next public exchange, that is **Open Up + Steady**.

If she brings water, shade, and a replacement shift after asking what is needed, that is **Show Up + Tend**.

If she publicly names that the council has hidden the organizers' labor, that might be **Show Up + Speak**.

The fictional action decides.

> [MARGINALIA - J.]
> If someone chooses the highest stat and then reverse-engineers an action after, the table is allowed to squint at them.

## Page 6: The Roll

When your student makes a risky allyship move and the outcome is uncertain:

**Roll 2d6 + the relevant stat.**

On a **10+**, the move lands. Choose 2 benefits from the Basic Move.

On a **7-9**, the move lands with friction. Choose 1 benefit from the Basic Move, and the Guide adds 1 cost.

On a **6-**, the move reveals a truth. The Guide makes a move, the situation changes, and you gain 1 Adversity.

If you name your impact honestly after a 6-, also mark 1 Growth.

A 10+ does not mean you are good.

A 7-9 does not mean you failed.

A 6- does not mean the ship has discovered your secret unworthiness and will now eject you through a disciplinary chute. There is no disciplinary chute. There was a proposal. It was defeated in committee.

The dice show what your move does to the field.

## Page 7: Wake Up

When you read a person, room, pattern, system, silence, or signal, roll +stat.

On a **10+**, ask 3.

On a **7-9**, ask 1.

On a **6-**, ask 1, but the answer implicates you, costs you, or reveals what you missed.

Questions:

- What is actually needed here?
- Who is carrying the cost?
- What is being avoided?
- What resource is missing or misdirected?
- What does this person or group not consent to?
- What pattern keeps repeating?
- What am I assuming?
- What would help before it looks impressive?
- What is the smallest true thing I can notice?
- What does the room already know but will not say?

Benefits:

- A hidden pattern becomes visible.
- Someone names a real need.
- You gain 1 Clarity.
- You learn what not to do.
- You expose a false story without making yourself the center.
- You create an opening for another student.

Costs:

- You become implicated in what you noticed.
- Someone realizes they have been seen.
- The truth arrives before the room is ready.
- You learn that the first problem was only a symptom.
- You take 1 Tension because the signal is hard to stay with.

### Running Example: Mara Wakes Up

The councilor is thanking the ship while three local organizers stand at the edge of the square.

Mara's player says, "I stop watching the stage and watch the organizers. Who looks exhausted? Who looks angry? Who is pretending not to look at the locked water barrels?"

The table agrees this is **Wake Up + Sense**.

Mara rolls an 8.

She asks: "Who is carrying the cost?"

The Guide answers: "The organizers. They built the emergency water network, but the council took control of the barrels this morning so the ship would see order instead of crisis."

Cost: one organizer notices Mara noticing and gives her a look that says, do not turn us into your lesson.

Update the Mission Field Map: council controls barrels; organizers carry cost; organizer distrust of ship increases.

## Page 8: Open Up

When you reveal a need, limit, fear, desire, stake, or truth that makes honest contact possible, roll +stat.

On a **10+**, choose 2.

On a **7-9**, choose 1, and the Guide adds 1 cost.

On a **6-**, your opening is met imperfectly, too late, or by the wrong person. Gain 1 Adversity. If you name what the moment cost you, mark 1 Growth.

Options:

- Someone knows what you actually need.
- You receive support without turning it into debt.
- A relationship becomes more honest.
- You name a limit before crossing it.
- You invite help without giving away agency.
- You reveal the stake without making yourself the center.
- You reduce 1 Tension by saying the true thing.
- Another student gains +1 forward if they respond to what you revealed.

Costs:

- The truth changes how someone sees you.
- Someone offers help that is partial, clumsy, or costly.
- You reveal more than you intended.
- The wrong person hears enough to matter.
- Your self-sabotaging belief gets louder.
- You must ask for a concrete response, not just understanding.
- You take 1 Tension if you confuse vulnerability with permission to spill impact onto others.

### Running Example: Mara Opens Up

The organizer tells Mara not to rescue them in front of the council. Mara feels her Belief rise: if she receives trust, she must repay it until nobody can regret giving it.

Mara turns to the crew and says, "I want to overdo this. I need someone else to ask the next question while I stay with what she actually said."

The table calls this **Open Up + Steady**.

On a 7-9, Mara chooses: someone knows what she actually needs.

The Guide adds a cost: Jun can take the next question, but Mara's truth changes how Tavi sees her. Tavi realizes Mara's care can become control.

Update the Crew Sheet: Mara named a support need; Tavi knows a warning sign; reduce 1 Tension if the table agrees the truth relieved pressure.

> [MARGINALIA - J.]
> Open Up is not "make everyone process your feelings immediately." That is not a move. That is a weather event with paperwork.

## Page 9: Clean Up

When you own impact, repair trust, close a loop, restore integrity, or change course after harm, roll +stat.

On a **10+**, choose 2.

On a **7-9**, choose 1, and the Guide adds 1 cost.

On a **6-**, your repair reveals that the harm is deeper, older, or less about you than you thought. Gain 1 Adversity.

Options:

- The harmed person or group names what repair would mean.
- You reduce defensiveness in the room.
- You restore 1 Trust.
- You clarify what you can actually do.
- You stop a false story from spreading.
- You make a future mistake less likely.
- You return a resource, role, or choice to the right person.
- You make the repair without demanding reassurance.

Costs:

- Repair takes more time than the mission wants to give.
- Someone asks for a concrete change, not an apology.
- You lose 1 Leverage with a person or faction.
- The repair exposes someone else's unresolved harm.
- You must give up a benefit you gained from the mistake.
- You take 1 Tension if you make the repair about proving you are good.

### Running Example: Tavi Cleans Up

Tavi, a Scout and Lanternbearer, blurts out in public that the council hid the organizers' work. It is true. It also exposes an organizer who asked not to be named.

Later, Tavi finds her and says, "I made your risk bigger. What would repair mean?"

The table calls this **Clean Up + Speak** because Tavi is correcting public meaning.

On a 7-9, Tavi chooses: the harmed person names what repair would mean.

The Guide adds a cost: repair requires Tavi to correct the record in front of the council and give up the spotlight they accidentally took.

Update the Crew Sheet: Tavi owes public repair; organizer names condition for trust.

> [MARGINALIA - J.]
> "Sorry you felt exposed" is not Clean Up. It is debris wearing perfume.

## Page 10: Grow Up

When you stay with discomfort, receive feedback, hold complexity, tell the truth about your pattern, or keep a line under pressure, roll +stat.

On a **10+**, choose 2.

On a **7-9**, choose 1, and take 1 Tension or accept a cost.

On a **6-**, you react from your pattern before you catch it. Gain 1 Adversity. If you name the pattern honestly, mark 1 Growth.

Options:

- You stay present without fixing.
- You receive feedback without disappearing.
- You name a clean no.
- You hold two truths at once.
- You do not make their pain about you.
- You gain 1 Clarity.
- You resist your self-sabotaging belief.
- You ask for help without giving away agency.
- You keep a commitment when inspiration fades.

Costs:

- You cannot keep everyone comfortable.
- You must disappoint someone to stay honest.
- Your self-sabotaging belief gets louder.
- You need support before you can continue.
- You lose face, status, certainty, or belonging.
- You take 1 Tension because the old pattern still wants control.

### Running Example: Mara Grows Up

The organizer tells Mara, "Do not rescue us in front of them. Ask what we need, or leave us alone."

Mara's Belief, I'm Not Worthy, wants to overpay immediately. She wants to do something big enough that nobody can regret trusting her.

Instead she says, "I can ask. I can also wait. What would reduce the burden without taking your lead?"

The table calls this **Grow Up + Steady**.

On a 10+, Mara chooses: she receives feedback without disappearing, and she resists her self-sabotaging belief.

Update Mara's Student Record: mark Growth if this meaningfully resists her Belief; reduce Tension if the table agrees she stayed in contact.

## Page 11: Show Up

When you act, follow through, move a resource, interrupt harm, organize a response, bear witness, or make a specific offer, roll +stat.

On a **10+**, choose 2.

On a **7-9**, choose 1, and the Guide adds 1 cost.

On a **6-**, you act, but the impact is not what you intended. Gain 1 Adversity.

Options:

- A needed resource moves.
- Harm is interrupted.
- Someone receives concrete support.
- A commitment becomes visible.
- A boundary is honored.
- A new ally joins the work.
- You create a handoff instead of overholding.
- A plan becomes actionable.
- A person or group is protected from unwanted attention.
- The Mission Clock does not advance when it would have.

Costs:

- The action works but draws attention.
- The resource arrives with obligations attached.
- The interrupted harm changes form.
- Someone mistakes action for resolution.
- A faction's Heat increases by 1.
- You spend 1 Capacity.
- You leave a necessary repair undone.
- You take 1 Tension if you act from urgency instead of contact.

### Running Example: Jun Shows Up

Jun, a Builder and Weaver, asks the organizers what handoff would help. They say the line needs shade, translation support, and someone to keep council aides from redirecting people toward the staged ceremony.

Jun builds a quick rotation with two students, one local volunteer, and a visible sign that points away from the stage.

The table calls this **Show Up + Shape**.

On a 7-9, Jun chooses: a plan becomes actionable.

The Guide adds a cost: the council notices the alternate line and Faction Heat increases by 1.

Update the Mission Field Map: relief line stabilized; council Heat +1; organizer Trust +1 if the handoff respected their lead.

## Page 12: Tokens

Tokens are visible weather.

Place them where the table can see them, or track them clearly on the Student Record, Crew Sheet, and Mission Field Map.

### Adversity

Adversity is short-term leverage earned from failed or costly action.

Adversity is not XP. Growth is XP.

Gain 1 Adversity when:

- you roll 6-
- you name your Cost before paying it
- a Belief wound move meaningfully complicates your action
- the Guide offers Adversity for accepting a harder truth, ugly choice, or delayed consequence

Spend Adversity to:

- spend 1 to add +1 to your own roll after rolling
- spend 1 to add +1 to another student's roll if your character can help
- spend 1 to ask one honest question about the scene
- spend 1 to introduce a useful but costly resource
- activate a move that requires Adversity, including a Belief Power Move with a listed Adversity cost

### Clarity

Clarity is momentary orientation.

Gain 1 Clarity when a move gives it to you, you pause long enough to name what is actually happening, or your School training helps you perceive the scene cleanly.

Spend 1 Clarity to ask "What is the cleaner move here?", ignore one Tension cost if you act on the truth immediately, or give another student +1 forward by naming what you see.

Clarity is not certainty. If you use Clarity to avoid risk, lose it.

### Tension

Tension marks unresolved pressure.

Take 1 Tension when you stay silent around real disagreement, perform goodness, notice a consent problem and keep moving, act from your Belief, or accept a move cost.

Clear 1 Tension by naming the real tension without blame, asking the avoided question, making a repair attempt, saying no where you would usually fawn, or choosing a smaller cleaner move.

If the table has 5 unresolved Tension, the Guide advances a pressure clock.

### Growth

Growth is long-term advancement.

Mark Growth when you name your impact after a miss, act against your self-sabotaging belief, complete a School training prompt, repair meaningful harm, receive feedback without disappearing, or teach another student something earned through play.

Growth is not awarded for looking good.

Growth is awarded for metabolizing impact.

## Page 13: Same Move, Different Stats

The same Basic Move can use different stats depending on how the student approaches it.

### Wake Up + Sense

You enter a tense meeting and notice that the person asking for "neutrality" keeps flinching whenever the missing workers are named.

### Wake Up + Shape

You map the last three failed relief efforts and realize the food always disappears at the same checkpoint.

### Clean Up + Tend

You check in with the student whose story was shared without consent and ask what repair would actually reduce the burden.

### Clean Up + Speak

You correct the public record after your team took credit for work done by locals.

### Open Up + Steady

You tell your crew that you are too angry to speak cleanly and need someone else to take the next exchange.

### Open Up + Tend

You ask for support and name the care that would actually help before someone has to guess.

### Grow Up + Steady

Your mentor criticizes your plan in front of everyone. You stay present, ask what impact they are seeing, and do not collapse into shame.

### Grow Up + Act

You are not ready, but waiting would leave someone alone in danger. You make the imperfect useful move now.

### Show Up + Act

You step between a guard and a student before the guard can escalate.

### Show Up + Tend

You redirect food, blankets, and translation support to the people doing the overnight watch.

### Show Up + Shape

You build a rotation so the same three people stop carrying every urgent task.

### Show Up + Speak

You raise the alarm in a way that names the harm without exposing the person who reported it.

## Page 14: Guide Principles For Rolls

The Guide does not use rolls to punish sincerity.

The Guide uses rolls to let the field answer.

Principles:

- Make the move follow the fiction.
- Ask what the student is actually doing.
- Let players be creative with stats, but make the approach visible.
- Give every 7-9 both success and consequence.
- Make 6- reveal truth, not just failure.
- Put costs where the fiction already has pressure.
- Keep consent visible.
- Reward repair more than performance.
- Let the world change.

Useful Guide questions:

- What are you trying to change?
- Who could pay the cost?
- What would help before it looks impressive?
- Which School discipline are you leaning on?
- What would make this a cleaner move?
- What changes on the map if this works?

## Page 15: Quick Reference

Roll when a risky allyship move is uncertain.

**Roll 2d6 + stat.**

- **10+**: choose 2 benefits.
- **7-9**: choose 1 benefit; Guide adds 1 cost.
- **6-**: Guide makes a move; gain 1 Adversity; mark Growth if you name impact honestly.

Basic Moves:

- **Wake Up**: understand what is happening.
- **Open Up**: risk honest contact and receive support.
- **Clean Up**: repair harm and restore integrity.
- **Grow Up**: build capacity under pressure.
- **Show Up**: take action that changes the situation.

Stats:

- **Sense**: Body
- **Act**: Line
- **Steady**: Oath
- **Shape**: Pattern
- **Tend**: Bridge
- **Speak**: Horizon

Tokens:

- **Adversity**: what failure taught you that success could not
- **Clarity**: a clean line of sight through pressure
- **Tension**: unresolved pressure asking to be named
- **Growth**: impact metabolized into capacity

The result of a roll is not the end of the scene.

It is the next honest thing the table must respond to.
