# Advancement Architecture - Schools and Ranks

Status: v0.4 design draft

## Core Decision

The six Schools are **horizontal paths with ranks inside them**.

They are not a single linear ladder where Sage is "better" than Shaman.

Each School teaches a different allyship capacity. A mature ally may have deep training in one School, basic fluency in several, and a dangerous gap in another.

## Why Not a Pure Ladder?

A pure ladder creates the wrong lesson.

If the Schools rank from Shaman to Sage as a simple vertical ascent, players may learn:

- body intelligence is low-level
- direct action is immature
- stewardship is less advanced than strategy
- relational work is only a stage before integration
- Sage is the final boss and therefore the best mode

That is not the MTGOA model.

The better model:

**Every Face is a valid allyship intelligence. Every Face has a shadow. Mastery means knowing which Face the moment requires.**

## Why Not Purely Horizontal?

Pure horizontality loses the developmental arc.

The book still needs the feeling of:

- entering the academy
- meeting teachers
- earning marks
- becoming more capable
- passing trials
- integrating multiple capacities
- eventually holding the whole board

So each School needs ranks.

## The Hybrid Model

There are six Schools.

Each School has six ranks.

Each School also owns one stat. That stat is the practice path the School uses to teach mastery.

| School | Owned Stat | Mastery Path |
|---|---|---|
| School of the Body | Sense | mastery through embodied signal, consent, and field awareness |
| School of the Line | Act | mastery through clean intervention and decisive protection |
| School of the Oath | Steady | mastery through commitment, continuity, and principled presence |
| School of the Pattern | Shape | mastery through systems, containers, and leverage |
| School of the Bridge | Tend | mastery through repair, care, translation, and relational support |
| School of the Horizon | Speak | mastery through perspective, meaning-making, and truthful translation |

1. **Initiate**
2. **Practitioner**
3. **Adept**
4. **Steward**
5. **Integrator**
6. **Keeper**

Players may train in any School, but campaign modules can spotlight one School as the primary milestone for that arc.

Example:

In a campaign about resource depletion, the main House may be Provisioners, while the featured School may be the School of the Body because students must learn to feel depletion before solving it.

In a campaign about coalition fracture, the featured School may be the School of the Bridge.

## Rank Meanings

### Initiate

Altitude correspondence: **Shaman interpretation**

You can recognize the School's core signal in the body, field, or immediate pattern.

You gain:

- access to the School's downtime training
- one lieutenant as a mentor
- one training prompt

### Practitioner

Altitude correspondence: **Challenger interpretation**

You can use the School's capacity under pressure, especially when the moment demands decisive movement.

You gain:

- the School's basic move unlock
- one lieutenant-taught subskill
- permission to take School Trials

### Adept

Altitude correspondence: **Regent interpretation**

You can preserve and repeat the School's capacity through role, discipline, ritual, and responsibility.

You gain:

- an advanced move
- the ability to teach another student a related support move
- one resistance against the School's shadow exam

### Steward

Altitude correspondence: **Architect interpretation**

You can design systems, tools, and leverage structures that let this School's capacity scale beyond your personal performance.

You gain:

- one design or strategy move
- the ability to create a School-facing support structure
- one way to convert Clarity into Leverage through this School

### Integrator

Altitude correspondence: **Diplomat interpretation**

You can use this School's capacity relationally across conflict, difference, coalition, and consent.

You gain:

- one relational integration move
- the ability to reduce Faction Heat through this School
- permission to mediate between this School and another School or House

### Keeper

Altitude correspondence: **Sage interpretation**

You can hold the School's capacity as a whole game: body, action, tradition, system, relationship, and horizon.

You gain:

- a Keeper mark
- the ability to lead a mission through that School's lens
- a once-per-campaign intervention

Keeper does not mean "done."

It means the School trusts you to carry the practice.

## Starting Rank

At character creation:

- Choose one **Home School** at Initiate.
- Choose one **Curiosity School** where you have a contact or unresolved tension.
- Choose one **Avoided School** that your character distrusts, fears, or dismisses.

Your House is your practice faction.

Your Home School is your growth path.

These do not have to match.

Example:

A Provisioner can train in the School of the Line because their arc is learning boundaries.

A Linekeeper can train in the School of the Bridge because their arc is learning relationship after confrontation.

## Advancement Currency

Use two tracks:

### Growth

Growth is personal development.

Mark Growth when:

- you name your impact after a miss
- you act against your self-sabotaging belief
- you complete a School training prompt
- you repair a meaningful harm
- you hold your Line under pressure
- you receive feedback without disappearing

Spend Growth to:

- increase a School rank
- unlock a move
- revise a character feature
- reduce a belief trigger

### Legacy

Legacy is campaign-level progress.

The table earns Legacy when:

- a mission creates durable change
- a faction relationship improves through impact
- a resource flow is repaired
- a structure can continue without the players
- consent is protected under pressure
- a truth becomes visible and useful

Spend Legacy to:

- stabilize the ship
- reduce Ship Exposure
- unlock a campaign packet opportunity
- strengthen a House
- recover a harmed local relationship
- preserve a mission outcome

## School Rank Costs

Suggested costs:

- Initiate: chosen at start or earned through story
- Practitioner: 3 Growth + complete one lieutenant training
- Adept: 5 Growth + pass the School Trial
- Steward: 5 Growth + create a durable tool, structure, or strategy through the School
- Integrator: 6 Growth + resolve or transform a relationship/faction tension through the School
- Keeper: 8 Growth + pass the Shadow Exam + teach or protect someone else using the School's full capacity

These costs are placeholders until playtest.

## Altitude Fairness Rule

Every School contains all six altitudes.

This means:

- Shaman is not "low level" in the whole game.
- Sage is not "the best School."
- A character can become Sage-level inside the School of the Body.
- A character can be Shaman-level inside the School of the Horizon.
- Mastery is not which School you choose; mastery is how deeply you can inhabit that School's full developmental range.

Example:

A Keeper of the School of the Line is not merely "good at confrontation." They can feel when a line is needed, act cleanly, preserve what the line protects, design structures that prevent repeated violations, hold relationship after the no, and understand when to put the line down.

This keeps the altitudinal structure of MTGOA while letting the Schools remain horizontal and fair.

## Campaign Milestones

Campaigns can award School rank opportunities.

Example:

The Missing Water campaign may offer:

- Provisioner House advancement
- School of the Body Initiate or Practitioner opportunity
- School of the Pattern training if players map the resource flow
- School of the Bridge training if players negotiate repair without exposure

Campaign packets should say which Schools are featured.

## School Marks

Every rank has a visible mark.

Suggested:

- Initiate: thread
- Practitioner: pin
- Adept: sigil
- Keeper: mantle

The marks should feel collectible but not gamified into emptiness.

They are reminders of responsibility.

## Teaching Others

At Adept or Keeper rank, a character can teach.

Teaching is a downtime action.

When you teach another student a subskill you have earned, both players answer:

- What did you learn?
- What did they misunderstand?
- What did teaching reveal about your own shadow?

If the scene creates a real change, both mark Growth.

## Advancement Principle

The advancement system should reward **metabolized impact**, not performance.

The player does not grow because they looked good.

They grow because something happened, they saw it, they stayed with it, and they changed how they move.
