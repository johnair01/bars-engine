# Implementation Plan - v0.4 to Playtest Packet

Status: actionable implementation plan

Goal: turn the school-ship fork into a playable, sellable MTGOA RPG artifact.

## North Star

Build a core manual and first campaign packet for:

**Mastering the Game of Allyship: The School Ship RPG**

The product should support:

- a curious reader buying the manual
- a table running a one-shot
- a group playing a campaign
- Wendell selling campaign modules
- Wendell or trained facilitators running premium guided campaigns

## Phase 1 - Lock the Core Premise

Deliverable: one-page canonical premise.

Decisions to make:

- final working title
- name of the ship/school
- name of the student/player role
- name of the Headmaster role
- default tone statement
- what stays constant across all campaigns
- what campaign packets are allowed to replace

Draft answer:

The core game takes place aboard a hidden traveling school-ship that moves between worlds in crisis. Players are students/fellows learning allyship through missions. The four Houses teach the four domains. The six Schools teach the six Faces. Campaign packets define the worlds the ship visits.

## Phase 2 - Replace Districts with Houses

Deliverable: four House/Faction sheets.

Each House needs:

- name
- domain
- motto
- symbol
- vibe
- gift
- shadow
- starting move
- faction clock
- relationship to the other Houses
- what members are known for
- what members get wrong

Current placeholders:

- Provisioners - Gather Resources
- Weavers - Skillful Organizing
- Linekeepers - Direct Action
- Lanternbearers - Raise Awareness

Open decision:

Do we keep these names or make them more mythic / school-like?

## Phase 3 - Build the Six Schools

Deliverable: six School milestone sheets.

Each School needs:

- Face
- teacher / Gym Leader NPC
- trial
- badge / mark / title
- lesson
- shadow exam
- unlocked move
- advanced move
- what it teaches about allyship

Schools:

- Shaman - School of the Body
- Challenger - School of the Line
- Regent - School of the Oath
- Architect - School of the Pattern
- Diplomat - School of the Bridge
- Sage - School of the Horizon

Open decision:

Are Schools sequential levels, selectable training tracks, or both?

Recommended:

Both. Campaigns can feature one School as the main milestone, while characters may train with any School during downtime.

## Phase 5 - Design Stats and Move Architecture

Deliverable: move system reference.

Recommended architecture:

The four Basic Moves remain top-level categories:

- Wake Up
- Clean Up
- Grow Up
- Show Up

But characters also gain specific moves from:

- House
- Ensemble Role
- Belief subclass
- School milestone
- campaign packet

Each specific move belongs to one Basic Move category.

Needed:

- core roll procedure
- category-specific benefits
- category-specific costs
- 4 basic moves
- 4 House starting moves
- 5 Ensemble Role moves
- 6 Belief subclass moves
- 6 School milestone moves

## Phase 6 - Replace Static

Deliverable: pressure system.

Remove Static as the primary mechanic.

Replace with:

1. **Mission Clock**
   - tracks the crisis getting worse during the episode

2. **Faction Heat**
   - tracks trust/strain with Houses and local factions

3. **Ship Exposure**
   - tracks risk to the hidden school-ship

4. **Tension Tokens**
   - tracks emergent table/character pressure

This gives pressure specific places to live.

## Phase 7 - Split Episode and Downtime Play

Deliverable: two-phase play procedure.

### Episode Play

Mission-facing play:

- arrive at a world
- read the field
- engage local factions
- make moves
- advance/resolve mission clocks
- record BARs

### Downtime Play

School-ship-facing play:

- recover Capacity
- train with a School
- resolve Tension Tokens
- spend Growth
- deepen Bonds
- talk to the Headmaster
- choose next mission
- reflect on real-world reps

Needed:

- Downtime actions list
- recovery rules
- training rules
- relationship scene rules
- advancement rules

## Phase 8 - Build the First Campaign Packet

Deliverable: first playable campaign module.

Recommended first packet:

**The Missing Water**

New frame:

The school-ship arrives above a drought-struck world where a city is celebrating a miracle fountain while the outer settlements run dry.

This teaches:

- Gather Resources
- Raise Awareness with consent
- false help
- public symbol vs material repair

Packet contents:

- premise
- map / relationship diagram
- local factions
- House involvement
- Headmaster briefing
- 8 NPCs minimum
- 1 Pilot Episode
- 4 Midseason Episodes
- 1 Finale Episode
- 3 Mission Clocks
- 3 custom moves
- fast-start one-shot route
- four-session route
- debrief and real-world reps
