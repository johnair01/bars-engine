# Core Architecture Revision - v0.4 School Ship Engine

Status: synthesis of the school-ship hard fork

## One-Sentence Design

**Mastering the Game of Allyship is a school-ship RPG where students of six allyship schools travel from world to world, resolving crises through four enduring practice factions while confronting their own self-sabotaging beliefs.**

## Core Product Fork

The game is no longer "The Living City."

The core setting is:

**A hidden traveling academy aboard a flying ship / temple / vessel that visits worlds in crisis.**

The exact ship vibe can be reskinned by campaign:

- skyship
- spelljammer
- space temple
- moving island
- living monastery
- rebel school
- fugitive academy
- dimensional ark

But the structural constants remain:

- the ship is the home base
- the players are students / fellows / junior allies
- the Headmaster holds the long arc
- the four domains appear as factions/houses
- the six Faces appear as schools / milestone masters
- campaigns are missions to worlds in need

## Core Setting Constants

### The Ship

The ship is the default campaign frame.

It is:

- a school
- a sanctuary
- a mission platform
- a community in motion
- a place with teachers, houses, rituals, rivalries, secrets, and training

The ship should be recreated from a vibes standpoint each campaign, but its engine functions stay stable.

### The Headmaster

The Headmaster is the recurring NPC who:

- protects the school
- gives or withholds mission context
- embodies the long arc of allyship mastery
- knows more than they say
- may be wise, compromised, grieving, playful, evasive, or secretly afraid

The Headmaster should not be a quest dispenser only.

They should have:

- a philosophy
- a blind spot
- a relationship to each of the six schools
- a reason the ship is in hiding
- a reason the students must act before they are fully ready

### The Four Houses / Practice Factions

The four domains become enduring school factions.

Working default names:

| House / Faction | Domain | Core Practice | Shadow |
|---|---|---|---|
| The Provisioners | Gather Resources | Move capacity where need is real. | Charity theater, hoarding, savior logistics. |
| The Weavers | Skillful Organizing | Build structures, handoffs, agreements, and continuity. | Bureaucracy, control, process worship. |
| The Linekeepers | Direct Action | Interrupt harm and draw necessary boundaries. | Escalation addiction, purity tests, reckless heroics. |
| The Lanternbearers | Raise Awareness | Make hidden patterns visible with consent. | Optics, exposure, spectacle, discourse loops. |

These are the Hogwarts-House-like factions.

Each campaign can rename them, but the four functions remain.

Players may belong to one House, train with multiple Houses, or be assigned to a mixed mission team.

### The Six Schools

The Faces become six schools / milestone paths.

They are closer to Pokemon Gym Leaders than character classes.

Each School has:

- a master teacher
- a trial
- a lesson
- a move unlock
- a shadow exam
- a badge / mark / title

| School | Face | Milestone Question |
|---|---|---|
| School of the Body | Shaman | Can you feel the field before explaining it? |
| School of the Line | Challenger | Can you act without becoming cruel? |
| School of the Oath | Regent | Can you preserve what matters without becoming the wall? |
| School of the Pattern | Architect | Can you design leverage without losing contact? |
| School of the Bridge | Diplomat | Can you hold relationship without vanishing? |
| School of the Horizon | Sage | Can you see the game and still stay in it? |

The six Schools are advancement milestones, not starting factions.

## Revised Character Architecture

Character creation should include:

1. Name
2. Look / vibe
3. Superpower archetype
4. House / Practice Faction
5. Ensemble Role
6. Self-sabotaging belief
7. Starting move package
8. Offer
9. Cost
10. Line
11. Bond

### Calling Replacement

Remove or rename "Calling."

Possible replacements:

- Superpower
- Spark
- Gift
- Allyship Gift
- Origin Gift
- What You Bring

This may connect to the superpower archetypes.

### Ensemble Role

Keep the five-man-team / Hearts-Blazing style orienting structure.

Possible roles:

- Ace / Spark
- Leader / Anchor
- Engineer / Builder
- Rookie / Heart
- Veteran / Shield

Or more MTGOA-native:

- Spark
- Anchor
- Builder
- Scout
- Witness

Need to decide whether to stay close to recognizable five-man-team language or rename fully into MTGOA tone.

### Self-Sabotaging Belief as Subclass

Private Motive should probably become the subclass/shadow lane.

Six subclass beliefs:

- I'm not Good Enough
- I'm not ready
- I don't belong
- I'm insignificant
- I'm not worthy
- I'm not capable

Each belief should provide:

- shadow trigger
- temptation
- starting wound move
- growth move
- repair move
- level unlocks

This is stronger than secret motives because it plugs directly into the book's Clean Up layer.

## Stats and Move Categories

The four Basic Moves are categories, not the entire move list:

- Wake Up
- Clean Up
- Grow Up
- Show Up

Stats may still be these four, but the system needs richer move acquisition.

Players should get moves from:

- House
- Ensemble Role
- Belief subclass
- School milestone
- campaign-specific training

Every inherited move should slot under one of the four categories.

Example:

Wake Up category:

- Read the Field
- Spot the Game
- Ask the Better Question
- Sense the Missing Resource

Clean Up category:

- Own the Miss
- Repair the Handoff
- Dissolve the Belief
- Return the Burden

Grow Up category:

- Stay in the Room
- Hold the Line
- Receive the Feedback
- Question the Certainty

Show Up category:

- Make the Specific Offer
- Move the Resource
- Interrupt the Pattern
- Pass the Work On

## Benefits by Move Category

Replace the universal benefit list with category-specific benefits.

### Wake Up Benefits

- Reveal what is actually needed.
- Identify who carries the cost.
- Name the repeating pattern.
- Discover a hidden resource.
- Learn what someone does not consent to.
- Gain Clarity.

### Clean Up Benefits

- Restore Trust.
- Reduce defensiveness.
- Clarify a commitment.
- Stop a false story.
- Name the repair needed.
- Prevent the same miss from repeating.

### Grow Up Benefits

- Stay present through discomfort.
- Receive feedback without disappearing.
- Name a clean no.
- Hold two truths without collapse.
- Resist the subclass belief.
- Mark Growth.

### Show Up Benefits

- Move a resource.
- Interrupt harm.
- Create a handoff.
- Protect a boundary.
- Bring in an ally.
- Complete a promise.

## Feature Moves

Offer, Cost, Line, and Bond are useful if they have mechanical teeth.

### Offer Move

When you make your specific Offer in a scene, you may spend 1 Capacity to add +1 to a Show Up roll.

If the Offer is not actually wanted, mark Tension.

### Cost Move

When your Cost is at risk, you may name it to gain 1 Adversity Token before rolling.

If you ignore it, the Guide or table may convert the Cost into a complication.

### Line Move

When you hold your Line under pressure, gain 1 Trust with anyone who needed the boundary and take +1 forward to Grow Up.

If you cross your Line, mark Growth only if you name the impact.

### Bond Move

When your Bond is directly affected by a move, you may reroll one die.

If the reroll still misses, the Bond changes.

## Pools Revision

Capacity, Trust, Leverage, and Clarity are promising but unclear.

Possible tightened model:

- **Capacity** = personal fuel. Spend to push, absorb, or activate features. Refreshes in downtime.
- **Trust** = relationship currency with factions/NPCs. Spent to ask for help, access, or patience. Earned through impact.
- **Leverage** = situational advantage. Spent to change position, timing, resources, or stakes.
- **Clarity** = insight currency. Spent to ask better questions, reveal patterns, or upgrade a partial success.

Question:

Are these four all player-facing, or should Trust and Leverage mostly live at faction/mission level?

## Adversity Tokens

Adversity Tokens are probably not XP.

They are pressure-conversion currency.

Possible rule:

- On a miss, gain 1 Adversity Token.
- The Guide offers it.
- In GMless play, another player offers it.
- Spend Adversity to help, push, activate certain moves, or introduce a costly resource.

XP / advancement should probably be **Growth**, not Adversity.

Dungeon World marks XP on misses because failure teaches. MTGOA should mark Growth when the player metabolizes impact, not merely because they failed.

## Tension Tokens

Keep.

Tension Tokens work because they emerge from play and can be self-administered.

They should track:

- avoided truth
- unspoken disagreement
- consent concern
- shadow activation
- belief trigger
- table friction

Need:

- clearer spend/clear rules
- stronger relation to downtime
- possible faction consequences when ignored

## Static Replacement

Static is not working.

Replace with one or more clearer pressure systems:

### Option A: Mission Pressure

A clock for the current episode.

### Option B: Exposure

The ship is hidden. Certain actions reveal its presence.

### Option C: Drift

The mission team drifts from allyship into performance, control, rescue, or avoidance.

### Option D: Faction Heat

Each faction tracks trust/strain.

### Option E: Harm Clock

The local crisis worsens.

Best current replacement:

Use **Mission Clocks + Faction Heat + Ship Exposure**.

Static can remain as a poetic term in prose, but should not be the main mechanic.

## Episode vs Downtime Play

The game needs two phases.

### Episode Play

This is mission play.

It includes:

- arriving at a world
- reading the field
- encountering local crisis
- making moves
- resolving clocks
- changing faction status
- recording BARs

### Downtime Play

This is school/ship play.

It includes:

- recovering Capacity
- training with a School
- resolving Tension Tokens
- spending Growth
- relationship scenes
- House politics
- Headmaster scenes
- choosing next mission
- reflecting on real-world reps

Downtime is where the school fantasy becomes emotionally useful.

It is also where long-term campaign attachment lives.

## Episode Threshold Issue

Episode Threshold plus Role Card bidding may compete with Guide adjudication.

Possible fork:

### Guided Mode

No bidding for success/failure.

Use Role Cards for:

- spotlight
- bonus
- complication
- narration rights during Commercial Break

### Table Mode

Use bidding to resolve episode pressure and narration rights.

This lets the same engine support both Guide-led and Hearts-Blazing-style play without forcing both systems at once.
