# Mastering the Game of Allyship

## The Living City Roleplaying Game

Version: v0.2 manual spine

Product form: tabletop roleplaying manual, playable with paper, pencils, index cards, one deck of standard playing cards, and six-sided dice.

Working sales promise:

**Practice allyship before the moment costs someone real.**

## What This Is

This is a tabletop roleplaying game about becoming useful under pressure.

Players enter a fictional city where every crisis is also an allyship problem. The game is not about proving that your character is good. It is about noticing what is needed, choosing a move, making contact, handling impact, and repairing when the move misses.

The manual is designed as a low-friction artifact in the *Mastering the Game of Allyship* value stack. A facilitated campaign is powerful, but expensive. A manual is the curiosity purchase: the thing a nerd, organizer, educator, coach, or friend group can buy because they want to see how allyship becomes playable.

## Design Lineage

This manual integrates four source patterns:

- **Ensemble episode play:** role-based protagonists, hidden drives, dramatic episode cards, shared narration, and scene control.
- **Fiction-first moves:** clear triggers, partial success, facilitator principles, and consequences that follow from the fiction.
- **Map and chronicle play:** the world changes because players answer prompts, redraw maps, and keep a record.
- **Collaborative safety and relationships:** shared setting creation, relationship questions, boundary tools, and failure tokens that become future leverage.

The point is not to copy any one game. The point is to make MTGOA playable as its own practice technology.

## The Core Loop

Every session follows this loop:

1. **Draw pressure.** Reveal an Episode, Crisis, or Domain card.
2. **Read the field.** Players identify what is happening and who is affected.
3. **Make a move.** A player acts through Wake Up, Clean Up, Grow Up, or Show Up.
4. **Pay the cost.** Capacity, Trust, Leverage, Consent, or Time changes.
5. **Update the city.** The map, chronicle, bonds, and crisis clocks change.
6. **Record a BAR.** Each player captures Breakthrough, Action, and Reflection.
7. **Carry one rep forward.** The session ends with a small real-world practice.

## Modes of Play

### Guided Mode

One player is **The Guide**. The Guide portrays the city, holds the safety container, tracks consequences, and asks the table what they do.

Use this mode for:

- first-time groups
- paid sessions
- classrooms
- team trainings
- groups that want a strong container

### Table Mode

There is no single Guide. Players divide responsibility through **Caretakers**.

Use this mode for:

- friend groups
- experienced roleplayers
- game nights
- groups who want ensemble storytelling

Caretakers:

- **Setting Caretaker:** districts, geography, resources, institutions.
- **Relationship Caretaker:** NPCs, bonds, rumors, trust, consent.
- **Static Caretaker:** pressure, distortion, avoidance, consequences.
- **Practice Caretaker:** BARs, debrief, real-world reps.
- **Safety Caretaker:** lines, veils, pause, rewind, redirect.

Caretakers do not own their domain. They have final say only when the table needs a quick call.

## Safety: The Boundary Stack

Allyship play can touch power, shame, identity, harm, repair, and grief. The game only works if safety is mechanical, not decorative.

Before play:

1. **Tone Rating:** Choose Bright, Tender, Sharp, or Heavy.
2. **Lines:** Things that will not appear.
3. **Veils:** Things that may exist but stay offscreen.
4. **Care Signals:** Choose words or gestures for pause, rewind, redirect, and cut.
5. **Debrief Boundary:** No one has to disclose personal trauma to prove they learned.
6. **Fictionalization Rule:** Real issues may inspire play, but real marginalized people at the table are never used as training props.

During play:

- **Pause** means stop and check in.
- **Rewind** means back up and choose a different path.
- **Redirect** means keep the scene but change its angle.
- **Cut** means the scene ends now.

Guide rule:

**Never ask a marginalized player to become the lesson.**

## The World: The Living City

The game takes place in **The Living City**, a fantastical civic organism made of districts, relationships, promises, histories, and systems.

The Living City is not utopia. It is alive, which means it has needs, contradictions, memory, wounds, and appetite.

The force that distorts the city is **The Static**.

The Static is what happens when living contact becomes:

- performance
- urgency
- optics
- silence
- role lock
- savior fantasy
- institutional defensiveness
- polished harm
- endless debate
- care without consent

The Static is not evil. It is avoidance with infrastructure.

## The Four Districts

| District | Domain | Core Question |
|---|---|---|
| The Wells | Gather Resources | What is actually depleted? |
| The Loom | Skillful Organizing | What structure would let the work continue? |
| The Thresholds | Direct Action | What line or move is being avoided? |
| The Lanterns | Raise Awareness | What truth needs to become visible, and with whose consent? |

## The Map and Chronicle

At the start of a campaign, draw a rough map of the Living City. Do not make it beautiful. The map is a working surface.

The map must include:

- the four districts
- one boundary
- one resource route
- one public symbol
- one place people avoid
- one place people gather
- one institution
- one rumor

The **Chronicle** records what changes.

Add to the Chronicle after every Episode:

- a changed place
- a named NPC
- a trust shift
- a resource shift
- a rumor
- a harm
- a repair
- an unanswered question

Rule:

**If it changed because of play, put it on the map or in the Chronicle.**

## Creating the City

Answer these together.

1. What does the city publicly claim to value?
2. What does it actually reward?
3. What resource is unevenly distributed?
4. Which district feels unseen?
5. Which institution has too much control?
6. What public ritual hides a private cost?
7. What rumor is everyone repeating?
8. What truth does almost everyone know but few people say?

Each player adds one landmark and one rumor.

Rumors may be true, false, partial, or propaganda.

## Character Creation

Each player creates a **Fellow of the Table**.

Your character is someone with a reason to help and a pattern that complicates their help.

Choose:

- Name
- Calling
- Face
- Ensemble Role
- Private Motive
- Reflex Offer
- Cost
- Line
- Bond
- Stats

## Faces

Your Face is your strongest developmental lens.

| Face | Gift | Shadow |
|---|---|---|
| Shaman | Feels the field before the plan forms. | Gets lost in signals and intensity. |
| Challenger | Acts when others freeze. | Turns tension into war. |
| Regent | Preserves what matters and builds continuity. | Protects the structure after it stops serving life. |
| Architect | Sees the system and designs leverage. | Plans elegantly while contact disappears. |
| Diplomat | Holds relationship across difference. | Smooths conflict until truth vanishes. |
| Sage | Sees the larger game. | Uses perspective to avoid commitment. |

Face gives you one **Face Gift** and one **Shadow Trigger**.

### Face Gifts

Shaman: Once per session, ask "What is the field telling us before anyone explains it?"

Challenger: Once per session, interrupt a harmful pattern before you have permission.

Regent: Once per session, turn a fragile good thing into a repeatable practice.

Architect: Once per session, reveal the structure producing the visible problem.

Diplomat: Once per session, hold two valid positions without collapsing either one.

Sage: Once per session, name the game beneath the game.

### Shadow Triggers

When your Face shadow appears, take a **Tension Token**.

Shaman: You treat intensity as proof.

Challenger: You escalate before you understand.

Regent: You defend continuity over life.

Architect: You design instead of contacting.

Diplomat: You smooth over a necessary truth.

Sage: You rise above instead of staying with.

## Ensemble Roles

Your Ensemble Role is your position in the story.

| Role | Story Function | Allyship Function |
|---|---|---|
| The Spark | Makes the first move. | Turns stuck energy into motion. |
| The Anchor | Holds the group steady. | Keeps care from becoming chaos. |
| The Builder | Creates tools and structures. | Makes the work repeatable. |
| The Scout | Sees routes others miss. | Finds openings, risks, and hidden costs. |
| The Witness | Names what is happening. | Makes truth visible without extraction. |

Each Role has a small deck of **Role Cards**. If you are playing without printed cards, write five index cards for your Role:

- +1 Small Move
- +2 Strong Move
- +3 Defining Move
- +1 Assist
- -2 Frailty

Role Cards are used in Episode Bidding.

## Private Motives

Your Private Motive is the emotional reward your character is tempted to chase.

Keep it secret if your table likes dramatic reveal. Keep it open if your table prefers transparency.

| Motive | Temptation | Keywords |
|---|---|---|
| Recognition | I want to be seen as good. | Visibility, Praise |
| Control | I want the uncertainty to stop. | Order, Certainty |
| Rescue | I want to be needed. | Safety, Gratitude |
| Justice | I want the wrong named and answered. | Truth, Consequence |
| Belonging | I want the group to keep me. | Loyalty, Harmony |
| Liberation | I want everyone free, including me. | Release, Possibility |

Keywords are not points for moral superiority. They are evidence of what your character is drawn toward.

At the end of a session, the player with the most Keywords gets to narrate their character's denouement last.

## Stats

Assign +2, +1, 0, and -1.

- **Wake Up:** notice what is happening.
- **Clean Up:** repair, own impact, restore integrity.
- **Grow Up:** stay with discomfort and increase capacity.
- **Show Up:** act, follow through, make the move.

## Pools

Each character starts with:

- **Capacity 3**
- **Trust 1**
- **Leverage 1**
- **Clarity 0**

Capacity is what you can spend before you distort.

Trust is relational credibility.

Leverage is access, timing, resources, or influence.

Clarity is earned by seeing a pattern cleanly.

## Reflex Offer, Cost, Line, Bond

Answer:

- **Reflex Offer:** When I help too fast, I usually offer ___.
- **Cost:** I spend ___ too freely.
- **Line:** Even for a good cause, I will not ___.
- **Bond:** I am tied to ___ because ___.

## The Shared Stake

Create one shared stake called **The Living Thread**.

This is a person, group, place, promise, or fragile possibility that everyone cares about.

The Living Thread has:

- three Aspects
- one Need
- one Boundary
- three Checkpoints

Example:

Living Thread: The Night School

Aspects:

- kids with nowhere else to go
- old library under threat
- volunteer teachers close to burnout

Need:

- stable funding without losing community control

Boundary:

- the students are not publicity material

Checkpoints:

- funding hearing
- student walkout
- founder's exhaustion

The Living Thread can be narrated by any player, but the table must honor its Need and Boundary.

## Basic Resolution

When your character makes a risky allyship move and the outcome is uncertain:

**Roll 2d6 + the relevant stat.**

On a **10+**, the move lands. Choose 2 benefits.

On a **7-9**, the move partly lands. Choose 1 benefit. The Guide or table adds 1 cost.

On a **6-**, the move reveals a truth. The situation worsens, and you gain 1 Adversity Token.

If you name your impact honestly after a 6-, also mark 1 Growth.

## Benefits

Choose:

- Someone receives what they actually needed.
- A hidden pattern becomes visible.
- Trust increases with a person or group.
- A resource moves to the right place.
- A structure becomes clearer or sturdier.
- Harm is interrupted.
- A new ally becomes available.
- A false story loses power.
- You gain 1 Clarity.
- You create an opening for another player.

## Costs

Choose or assign:

- Spend 1 Capacity.
- Risk 1 Trust.
- Lose 1 Leverage.
- Trigger your Shadow.
- Take a Tension Token.
- Create a new obligation.
- Expose someone to attention they did not consent to.
- Move a Crisis Clock forward.
- Put the Living Thread at risk.
- Leave a necessary repair undone.

## Adversity Tokens

When you miss a roll, take 1 Adversity Token.

You may spend Adversity Tokens:

- +1 to your own roll, after rolling.
- +1 to another player's roll, if your character can help.
- Activate a Strength.
- Ask the table one honest question about the scene.
- Introduce a useful but costly resource.

Adversity means the failed move taught you where the next leverage lives.

## Tension Tokens

Take a Tension Token when:

- you disagree but stay silent
- you feel unseen
- you want to perform goodness
- you notice a consent problem
- your Face shadow appears
- your Private Motive tempts you
- the table is avoiding the real question

Clear a Tension Token by:

- naming the real tension without blame
- asking the avoided question
- making a repair attempt
- saying no where you would usually fawn
- choosing a smaller cleaner move
- protecting consent under pressure

If the table has 5 unresolved Tension Tokens, The Static advances.

## The Four Basic Moves

### Wake Up

When you read a person, room, pattern, system, or silence, roll + Wake Up.

10+: Ask 3.

7-9: Ask 1.

6-: Ask 1, but the answer implicates you or costs you.

Questions:

- What is actually needed here?
- Who is carrying the cost?
- What is being avoided?
- What resource is missing?
- What does this person not consent to?
- What pattern keeps repeating?
- What am I assuming?
- What would help before it looks impressive?

### Clean Up

When you own impact, repair trust, close a loop, or restore integrity, roll + Clean Up.

10+: Choose 2.

7-9: Choose 1 and accept a cost.

6-: Your attempt reveals the repair is deeper than you thought.

Options:

- The harmed person or group names what repair would mean.
- You reduce defensiveness in the room.
- You restore 1 Trust.
- You clarify what you can actually do.
- You stop a false story from spreading.
- You make a future mistake less likely.

### Grow Up

When you stay with discomfort, receive feedback, hold complexity, or name a line, roll + Grow Up.

10+: Choose 2.

7-9: Choose 1 and take a Tension Token.

6-: You react from your pattern before you can catch it. Mark Growth if you name it.

Options:

- You stay present without fixing.
- You receive the feedback without disappearing.
- You name a clean no.
- You hold two truths at once.
- You do not make their pain about you.
- You gain 1 Clarity.

### Show Up

When you act, follow through, move a resource, interrupt harm, or make a specific offer, roll + Show Up.

10+: Choose 2.

7-9: Choose 1 and accept a cost.

6-: You act, but the impact is not what you intended.

Options:

- A needed resource moves.
- Harm is interrupted.
- Someone gets concrete support.
- A commitment becomes visible.
- A boundary is honored.
- A new ally joins the work.
- You create a handoff instead of overholding.

## Support Moves

### Ask the Better Question

When you want to help but do not yet know what help means, ask:

**What would support look like right now?**

If you wait for the answer without filling the silence, gain 1 Clarity.

### Make the Specific Offer

When you offer help, name:

- what you can do
- by when
- for how long
- what you cannot do

If the offer matches a named need, restore 1 Trust.

### Handoff Honestly

When you cannot help, name the limit and identify the next viable support.

On a 10+, the handoff preserves Trust.

On a 7-9, the handoff works but costs Leverage or Capacity.

On a 6-, the delay has already cost someone.

### Name the Consent Line

When visibility could help but could also expose someone, ask:

**Who becomes more vulnerable if this becomes public?**

If the affected person or group has not consented, public action costs 1 Trust unless the table names why immediate interruption is necessary.

## Episode Play

The game can be played as a chain of Episodes.

An Episode has:

- Title
- Domain
- In-Focus Role
- Opening Pressure
- Supporting Cast
- Keywords
- Threshold
- Crisis Clock

Build a session deck:

- 1 Pilot Episode
- 3 to 5 Midseason Episodes
- 1 Finale Episode
- Optional Static cards

For a one-shot, use 1 Pilot, 2 Midseason, and 1 Finale.

For a four-session campaign, each session gets one Pilot and one Finale, with Midseason cards between.

## Episode Threshold

Each Episode has a Threshold.

Default Threshold:

**1 + number of Episodes already resolved + current Static**

Players may bid Role Cards to determine whether the table enters the Episode with advantage or pressure.

If the combined bid meets or beats the Threshold:

- the group begins with 1 shared Clarity
- the top bidder claims the first Keyword
- the second bidder claims the second Keyword
- the opening scene begins with a useful detail

If the bid misses the Threshold:

- The Static advances
- no Keywords are claimed yet
- the opening scene begins with an immediate cost

Frailty cards reduce the bid but earn the player 1 Adversity Token if they let the Frailty shape the scene.

## Scene Structure

Each Episode has three scenes.

### Launch

The In-Focus Role frames the first scene.

They answer:

- Where are we?
- Who is present?
- What is visibly wrong?
- What does my character notice first?

### Bridge

All players build the situation together.

The Bridge should:

- bring in supporting cast
- reveal competing needs
- complicate the obvious solution
- invite at least one Basic Move
- update the map or Chronicle

### Wrap

The highest bidder or Guide frames the consequence.

The Wrap answers:

- What changed?
- Who benefited?
- Who paid?
- What repair is now available?
- What does The Static do next?

## Commercial Break

The table may call **Commercial Break** between scenes.

Use it to:

- pause for safety
- check table energy
- ask what the scene is really about
- shift narrative control
- add a map detail
- record a Chronicle note
- spend Adversity Tokens
- clear Tension Tokens

Commercial Break is a mechanic, not a joke. It keeps the game breathable.

## The Static

The Static starts at 0.

Advance Static when:

- players choose optics over need
- the table avoids the real question
- someone acts without consent
- a promise is made vaguely
- a harmed person becomes a symbol
- Tension Tokens pile up
- a Crisis Clock fills

Reduce Static when:

- someone makes a clean repair
- a resource reaches the right place
- a hidden pattern is named with consent
- a role or handoff becomes clear
- the Living Thread's Need is honored
- a player chooses impact over identity

At Static 3:

- rumors distort.
- Trust becomes harder to gain.

At Static 6:

- institutions defend themselves.
- every Show Up move carries an extra cost.

At Static 9:

- a district locks down, fractures, or goes silent.

## Crisis Clocks

A Crisis Clock has 4, 6, or 8 segments.

Use 4 for one-shots.

Use 6 for standard sessions.

Use 8 for campaign fronts.

Example:

**The Wells Runs Dry**

1. Residents begin rationing.
2. Informal carriers burn out.
3. Council issues a defensive statement.
4. Residents split over going public.
5. The fountain becomes the dominant story.
6. The Wells loses trust in outside help.

Advance clocks when players delay, miss, avoid, or choose costly action.

Move clocks back only through concrete change, not good intention.

## Fronts

A Front is a pressure that gets worse without player intervention.

Each Front has:

- Name
- Impulse
- Cast
- Stakes Question
- Clock
- Moves
- Grim Future

Example Front:

**The Civic Hope Campaign**

Impulse:

Turn repair into symbolism.

Cast:

- council liaison
- donor circle
- exhausted district stewards
- press office

Stakes Question:

Will the city choose visible unity over material repair?

Moves:

- announce a beautiful substitute
- praise resilience
- redirect blame
- ask affected people for patience
- turn one person into a symbol

Grim Future:

The district becomes a story everyone tells and no one serves.

## Guide Agenda

The Guide's agenda:

- Portray a living city.
- Make allyship concrete.
- Play to find out what repair costs.
- Protect the table.
- Let impact matter more than intention.

## Guide Principles

- Begin and end with the fiction.
- Ask what is needed, not what looks helpful.
- Name every person.
- Give every institution an appetite.
- Give every affected group dignity.
- Make the obvious move incomplete.
- Make costs visible.
- Offer repair before punishment.
- Use the map.
- Use the Chronicle.
- Address characters by name.
- Ask questions and use the answers.
- Never make real trauma the spectacle.
- Let the city change.

## Guide Moves

When everyone looks to you, when players miss a roll, or when they give you an opening, make a Guide Move.

Guide Moves:

- Reveal an unwelcome impact.
- Show who is carrying the cost.
- Advance a Crisis Clock.
- Spend their Capacity.
- Risk their Trust.
- Use up Leverage.
- Put the Living Thread in danger.
- Offer a costly opportunity.
- Separate intention from impact.
- Turn visibility into exposure.
- Turn urgency into error.
- Show the downside of their Face.
- Bring in a person with a name and need.
- Ask what repair would require.
- Tell them the consequence and ask.

Never announce the name of the Guide Move. Show it in the fiction.

## End of Session

At the end of each session:

1. Update the map.
2. Add three Chronicle entries.
3. Resolve one Bond if it changed.
4. Each player records one BAR.
5. Each player names one real-world rep.
6. The table answers the End of Session questions.

End of Session questions:

- Did we move a resource to where it was needed?
- Did we repair something we affected?
- Did we stay with discomfort instead of escaping?
- Did we make a specific offer or action?
- Did we protect consent?
- Did the city change because of us?

For each yes, the table gains 1 Legacy.

## Legacy

Legacy is campaign-level progress.

Spend Legacy between sessions to:

- reduce Static by 1
- create a new ally
- stabilize a district
- preserve a repair
- upgrade the Living Thread
- unlock a new campaign question

Legacy is not triumph. It is proof that the work can continue without the characters having to hold everything themselves.

## Advancement

When you mark 5 Growth, choose one:

- Increase one stat by +1, max +3.
- Add a new Face Gift from another Face.
- Create a custom Support Move.
- Add a new Bond.
- Reduce your Shadow Trigger.
- Rewrite your Line with more precision.

When you resolve a Bond, write a new one.

## Appendix C: Starter Static Cards

### Optics Substitute

Someone offers visibility where material support was needed.

Advance Static unless the players ask who consented and what resource actually needs to move.

### Praise Resilience

Someone celebrates an affected person for enduring a burden.

Advance Static unless the players reduce the burden.

### Endless Process

The group creates a meeting to discuss whether a meeting is needed.

Advance a Crisis Clock unless someone names the next concrete action.

### Savior Spotlight

The room turns toward the helper instead of the affected person.

Lose 1 Trust unless someone redirects attention.

### Polite Harm

An institution uses kind language to preserve a harmful structure.

Gain 1 Clarity if someone names the structure without attacking the person.

## Appendix E: First Real-World Rep

After your first session, ask someone you already have permission to support:

**What kind of support would actually help right now?**

Then do one small version of what they ask, or honestly name what you can and cannot do.

Do not post about it.

Do not turn it into proof of goodness.

Just complete the rep and record the BAR.
