# Hexagram Oracle Cards — Chapter 7: The Sage
**Upper trigram:** Gen ☶ Mountain
**Chapter goal:** Develop the capacity to see the whole game without leaving it — altitude awareness in service of showing up more precisely.
**Chapter context:** The Sage sees from height — panoramic, committed, still. Each card asks how this gate's gift makes altitude awareness a way of showing up more precisely, not a way of stepping above the game. The Sage who leaves is the shadow. The Sage who stays in it while seeing it whole is the gift.

**Card formula:** TYPE A/B/C component order per hexagram; Image text (Wilhelm/Baynes) as C2 opening; Gate / WAVE / 3 tasks with containers / 3-2-1 handoff / Appendix routing

---

## Card 1: Protector in the Sage
**Hex 26 · Great Taming**
*Gate: Protector · WAVE: Show Up · Trigram: Qian ☰ × Gen ☶*

The protection is keeping the altitude small — staying at the view that costs less, rather than taking the one the situation needs. Heaven within the mountain: great power held in restraint; what has been gathered over time shapes what becomes possible when the restraint is chosen deliberately rather than by default. The Protector's gift in the Sage's territory is naming which capacity the higher view will protect — signal clarity, staying-power, the container itself — so that altitude awareness serves the field rather than protecting you from it. What you protect from height determines what height makes visible.

**1.** Feel what it would cost to take a higher view of this situation. Which position would you have to stop defending — the familiar altitude, the role, the read? Name it in the body. *Specific. Before moving.*
**2.** Name the capacity the higher view protects — not the position, but what keeps you functional at altitude. Set that limit deliberately before you move to the wider view.
**3.** Capture: what did you protect, and what became visible because you held the capacity rather than the position? *Open the app. Two minutes.*

*Skilled players: If the Protector is keeping the altitude small to protect a comfortable position rather than a functional capacity, take it into the 3-2-1 (Show Up) first.*

## Card 2: Controller in the Sage
**Hex 41 · Decrease**
*(I Ching: Sǔn 損)*
*Gate: Controller · WAVE: Clean Up · Trigram: Dui ☱ × Gen ☶*

*⚑ Matrix note: architecture file lists Hex 31 for this position — formula (Dui lower, Gen upper) gives Hex 41. Flagged for Wendell review; Hex 41 used here.*

At the foot of the mountain, the lake: the image of decrease — what flows down deliberately nourishes what is below; the mountain gives by reducing. The Controller is sorting which altitude is acceptable rather than choosing which voice the moment actually needs — a standard for the right way to see has arrived before the seeing. The meta-move in the Sage's territory is decrease: subtract the standard you arrived with and route from what the situation calls for, not from what altitude should look like.

**1.** Name the standard for altitude you're running right now. What does "seeing this correctly" look like to you? *Say it out loud. One sentence.*
**2.** Decrease deliberately: what voice — at what WAVE stage — does this moment actually need? Route from that, not the standard you came with. *Solo: write the new standard before speaking. With: say it to someone who will be in the conversation.*
**3.** Capture: which voice you chose, what routing from it felt different about the exchange, and what became available because you chose it deliberately. *One sentence in the app.*

*Skilled players: If the Controller is sorting which altitudes are acceptable rather than choosing the voice the situation needs, take it into the 3-2-1 (Clean Up) first.*

## Card 3: Skeptic in the Sage
**Hex 22 · Grace**
*Gate: Skeptic · WAVE: Wake Up · Trigram: Li ☲ × Gen ☶*

Fire at the foot of the mountain: illumination from below shapes what is visible from above; the clarity lives in the light, not only in the height. The Skeptic's gift in the Sage's territory is deliberate deceleration — the pause that lets the second look reveal what altitude is actually running, not just what altitude appears to be running. The doubt is aimed at you instead of the situation: not "what altitude is this at?" but "who am I to name it?" — and the question worth asking keeps getting blocked by the one that isn't.

**1.** Stop. Before naming the altitude — what's underneath your first read of this situation? What is it actually about, at the level below the surface? *One sentence. Not the altitude: the essence.*
**2.** Move from the second look. Name the altitude that's actually running — the specific gap between where the conversation is and what the situation needs. *Clear. One sentence.*
**3.** Capture: what did the second look reveal about the altitude that the first read would have confirmed rather than questioned? *In the app. That's the BAR.*

*Skilled players: If the Skeptic is auditing your credentials ("who am I to name this altitude?") rather than auditing the situation, take it into the 3-2-1 (Wake Up) first.*

## Card 4: Fixer in the Sage
**Hex 27 · Nourishment**
*Gate: Fixer/Healer · WAVE: Grow Up · Trigram: Zhen ☳ × Gen ☶*

The Fixer is trying to fix the altitude — routing the conversation toward a level it can manage, rather than finding the structural condition that generated the mismatch. At the foot of the mountain, thunder: what stirs at the base determines what nourishment rises; the arousal at the ground shapes what is possible from height. The Fixer's gift in the Sage's territory is scope discipline at the structural level — naming specifically what is within your stewardship to address, so the altitude work that follows has a foundation. Fix what's at the base. The view from height follows.

**1.** Map your scope: what in this altitude mismatch is yours to address? Not the conversation, not the other person's map — the one structural condition within your stewardship that is generating the gap. *Name it. One sentence.*
**2.** One surgical intervention at the base — the structural condition, not the altitude itself. *Before you try to move anyone's view.*
**3.** Capture: what did you address at the base level, and did the altitude conversation shift without you having to manage it? *In the app.*

*Skilled players: If the Fixer is trying to correct the altitude mismatch directly — managing the conversation's level rather than the structural condition beneath it — take it into the 3-2-1 (Grow Up) first.*

## Card 5: Victim in the Sage
**Hex 18 · Work on Decay**
*Gate: Victim · WAVE: Wake Up · Trigram: Xun ☴ × Gen ☶*

The story about the altitude mismatch is generating sympathy for your read rather than awareness of what's running — narrative from above the game rather than from inside it. The wind blows low on the mountain: Work on Decay; what moves gently at the base renews what has grown stagnant above. The Victim's gift in the Sage's territory is the story that stirs awareness about the stuck altitude from within it — felt, naming, structural, relational, meta — whichever kind moves the field, told in the form that meets people where they are, not from above where they should be.

**1.** Name the kind of story this moment needs: felt / naming / contextual / structural / relational / meta. Not the one you're already comfortable telling — the one that moves awareness about what altitude is running.
**2.** Tell that story from inside the altitude, not from above it. *Solo: write it. With: tell it in the form that meets the room where the room actually is.*
**3.** Capture: which kind of story you told and from which altitude, and what awareness it moved that the map alone couldn't have. *In the app.*

*Skilled players: If the story is generating sympathy for your altitude read rather than awareness of the situation's altitude, take the Victim into the 3-2-1 (Wake Up) first.*

## Card 6: Emotional Body in the Sage
**Hex 4 · Youthful Folly**
*Gate: Emotional Body · WAVE: Clean Up · Trigram: Kan ☵ × Gen ☶*

A spring wells up at the foot of the mountain: the source is clear even when the path ahead is not; the water knows its direction before the route does. The Emotional Body's gift in the Sage's territory is reading altitude from the signal before the analysis — the felt channel tells you what altitude is running more accurately than the map does. The analysis is running instead of the signal: you're reading the altitude from the outside, from the map, when the body already knows.

**1.** Before naming the altitude: which channel is running in you right now — Anger (creates/destroys), Fear (detects threat), Sadness (shows what you care about and how far away), Joy (lightens), Neutrality (detaches to see the whole)? *One word. From the body, not the analysis.*
**2.** Use that channel's function as your altitude move. Neutrality reveals the full pattern. Fear names the real threat altitude. Sadness shows what the mismatch is costing. Anger clears space for a different level. Joy shifts what level is accessible. *Deploy the channel. Don't suppress it or perform it.*
**3.** Capture: what did the channel tell you about the altitude that the analysis would have missed — or would have arrived at later? *Open the app before the signal fades.*

*All 5 channels are available. This card asks which one is actually running.*

*Skilled players: If you're reading altitude from analysis while the emotional signal is already running beneath it, take the Emotional Body into the 3-2-1 (Clean Up) first.*

## Card 7: Damaged Self in the Sage
**Hex 52 · Keeping Still**
*(I Ching: Gèn 艮)*
*Gate: Damaged Self · WAVE: Show Up · Trigram: Gen ☶ × Gen ☶*
*⬛ Double-trigram — Mountain doubled. The deepest card in this chapter.*

Mountains standing close together: stillness complete; the panoramic view from height requires no movement because the stillness itself is the seeing. The Damaged Self is absorbing every altitude cost without choosing — damage landing without decision, accumulation without return. The damage the Sage is built for is specific: being wrong, letting better information revise the map, the cost of staying in the game while seeing it clearly. Indiscriminate absorption is not resilience — it is the Mountain before it learned that stillness is a choice.

**1.** Name the damage this altitude work is asking you to take. Specifically: being wrong? Being revised? Staying in a game your map says is below you? Losing a position you'd been right from? *One sentence.*
**2.** Choose deliberately: is this the damage worth taking — the kind that deepens the view — or damage that accumulates without building anything? *Name the choice. Before the altitude work continues.*
**3.** If yes — take it on purpose. Capture: what did you absorb, and what became possible in the view from height because you chose it rather than having it land? *In the app.*

*Skilled players: If damage from altitude work is accumulating without decision — every mismatch costing you without a choice — take the Damaged Self into the 3-2-1 (Show Up) first.*

## Card 8: Vulnerable Child in the Sage
**Hex 23 · Splitting Apart**
*Gate: Vulnerable Child · WAVE: Grow Up · Trigram: Kun ☷ × Gen ☶*

The altitude map already knows what this situation is. You've read this before — seen the pattern, named the level, arrived with the answer. The mountain rests on the earth: Splitting Apart; what stands elevated depends entirely on what supports it from below — when the foundation thins, what was high comes apart. The Vulnerable Child's gift in the Sage's territory is holding the map lightly — staying genuinely open to the altitude being different from what you arrived with, to the view from height updating rather than confirming what you already knew. Did anything surprise you?

**1.** Identify which mode is live: grow (let the altitude revise you), learn (find out what altitude is actually running), play (try the read without commitment), explore (go somewhere the map doesn't reach). *The most available one. Not the one that feels most like altitude work.*
**2.** Do the altitude work from that orientation — not to confirm the map, but to find out what is actually there. *Toward what you haven't decided about yet.*
**3.** Capture the surprise: what updated the map that you didn't expect? *In the app, immediately after.* If nothing surprised you, the map was still running the read.

*Skilled players: If altitude awareness feels like demonstrating what you already see — the credential-collector has replaced the child. Take the Vulnerable Child into the 3-2-1 (Grow Up) first.*

## Design Notes

**Hexagram name decisions:**
- Hex 26 "Great Taming" — keep (Heaven held within Mountain; great power in restraint; the Protector's restraint at altitude is exactly this)
- Hex 41 "Decrease" — keep with I Ching credit Sǔn 損; deliberate reduction for higher purpose; Controller subtracting the arriving standard
- Hex 22 "Grace" — keep (fire illuminating from below; the Skeptic's deceleration reveals what the light is actually showing)
- Hex 27 "Nourishment" — keep (thunder at the base; what stirs below nourishes above; the Fixer's scope discipline at the structural base)
- Hex 18 "Work on Decay" — keep (wind renewing the stagnant; the Victim's story from inside the stuck altitude)
- Hex 4 "Youthful Folly" — keep (spring at foot of mountain; clear source, uncertain path; the Emotional Body's signal knows before the map does)
- Hex 52 "Keeping Still" — keep with I Ching credit Gèn 艮; Mountain doubled; the double-trigram deepest card; stillness chosen vs. stillness accumulated
- Hex 23 "Splitting Apart" — keep (mountain rests on earth; what's elevated depends on its foundation; map-knowing vs. genuine openness)

**Matrix discrepancy — Card 2:**
Architecture file lists Hex 31 (Influence) for G2 (Dui lower) × Ch7 (Gen upper). Formula gives Hex 41 (Decrease): upper = Gen, lower = Dui = Mountain over Lake. Hex 31 (Influence) = Lake over Mountain = Dui upper, Gen lower — which would reverse the Gate/Chapter assignment. Cards for G7 (Gen lower) × Ch6 (Dui upper) = Hex 31 Influence correctly (see HEXAGRAM_CARDS_CH6_DIPLOMAT.md Card 7). Conclusion: Hex 41 is correct for Card 2; architecture file has a digit transposition (31 → 41). Flagged for Wendell review before finalizing.

**Relational modifier applied:**
- Card 2 (Controller): Task 2 has "Solo / With:" modifier — relational gate
- Card 5 (Victim): Task 2 has "Solo / With:" modifier — relational gate

**Double-trigram card:**
- Card 7 (Damaged Self × Sage = Hex 52, Mountain doubled) — complete stillness; the deepest altitude card in the book; the shadow is indiscriminate absorption, the gift is chosen stillness; the panoramic view is sustainable only from stillness that was decided, not accumulated

**Notable card intersections:**
- Hex 26 (Great Taming) for Protector: Heaven restrained within the Mountain — the power of the wider view is available but held; the Protector's move is releasing it in service of the capacity, not the position
- Hex 41 (Decrease) for Controller: The lake at the foot of the mountain giving generously downward — the meta-move is subtracting the standard you arrived with; what you give up determines what the altitude work can receive
- Hex 22 (Grace) for Skeptic: Fire illuminating the mountain from below — the Skeptic's second look is what the light is doing; the Grace is in the seeing, not the height
- Hex 27 (Nourishment) for Fixer: Thunder stirring at the mountain's base — what is nourished at the structural level rises; the Fixer's scope discipline determines what altitude work is possible
- Hex 18 (Work on Decay) for Victim: Wind renewing what has grown stagnant at the mountain's base — the Victim's story from inside the stuck altitude is what stirs awareness; from above, it only describes
- Hex 4 (Youthful Folly) for Emotional Body: Spring welling up at mountain's foot — the signal is clear even when the path isn't; the body knows the altitude before the analysis does
- Hex 52 (Keeping Still, doubled) for Damaged Self: Mountains together, complete stillness — the deepest card; altitude without movement; the chosen damage is what makes panoramic stillness possible
- Hex 23 (Splitting Apart) for Vulnerable Child: Mountain on earth, foundation dependency — the map that cannot be surprised is the mountain whose base has thinned; the Sage who is still learning is the one whose view stays accurate
