# Gate Voices — Canonical Breakdown
**How each voice actually operates in allyship contexts**
**Source:** Interview with Wendell, 2026-05-12 + 2026-05-19 sessions
**Status:** All 8 gates complete. Developmental arcs added 2026-05-19.
**Full developmental detail:** See `GATE_DEVELOPMENTAL_STAGES_RD.md`

---

## Intellectual Lineage

The 8 Gates are a marriage of two frameworks:
- **Big Mind Process (Genpo Roshi):** The gate architecture — specific voice names, the "speak as this voice" methodology, giving each voice a narrower job rather than eliminating it
- **Existential Kink (Carolyn Elliott):** The philosophical approach — shadow as information not pathology, asking what the voice has been protecting, the "having" practice

See `SOURCE_INTEGRATION_SPEC.md` for full attribution plan.

## Gate Hierarchy (Foundational)

The gates are not peers — they have a hierarchy:
- **Protector** is the first voice. Job: survival. Question: "Will I live?"
- **Controller** is the Protector's implementation. Job: standards. Question: "How will I live?"
- **Skeptic** is a lieutenant of the Protector. Job: discernment. Question: "Is this safe?"
- The remaining gates each serve a function within this protective structure

The Controller and Protector can merge — the Controller takes over the Protector's job and the two become indistinguishable. This merger is especially common in allyship contexts.

**All gates protect the Vulnerable Child.** The Emotional Body is where this becomes visible at the emotional level — each specific emotion is deployed in service of a specific protection. When the Vulnerable Child is running the show, all gates repurpose correctly.

## WAVE/Gate Pairing (confirmed 2026-05-19)

The 8 Gates divide into 4 WAVE-stage pairs. Each pair identifies the kind of work that gate primarily contributes at the chapter level. All four WAVE stages are always present (AQAL Tetra-arising) — the pairing identifies which move is most alive.

| WAVE Stage | Internal Gate | External Gate |
|------------|--------------|--------------|
| **Wake Up** | Skeptic (G3) — noticing the problem | Victim (G5) — noticing the story |
| **Clean Up** | Emotional Body (G6) — the actual charge | Controller (G2) — the standard that defines clean |
| **Grow Up** | Vulnerable Child (G8) — trust in natural growth | Fixer (G4) — steward of conditions |
| **Show Up** | Damaged Self (G7) — known capacity | Protector (G1) — calibrated defense |

**Structural dependencies:**
- Fixer and Victim both work **for the Controller** — stories enforce standards; fixing executes toward them
- Controller works **for the Protector** — the standard serves survival
- All gates ultimately protect **the Vulnerable Child**

## Gate 1: Protector
**WAVE slot:** Show Up (External) | **Trigram:** Qian ☰ Heaven

### Core Function
Emerges to protect the physical body. Primary survival voice.

### Three Directions of Protection
1. **Self from others** — classic danger sensing
2. **Others from others** — the savior/hero move; wanting to be the protector of other people from external harm
3. **Self from self (self-policing)** — protecting others from what you might do; not trusting yourself to show up correctly

### How It Shows Up in Allyship
- People want to BE the Protector of others — this is the "white savior" impulse dressed as safety
- Self-policing is the most common and least visible shadow: "I might say the wrong thing," "I need to check my privilege before I speak" — this burns emotional energy that could go toward actual contribution
- The self-policing version arrives at the real moment depleted

### Shadow (Unchecked)
The canceller — can identify and eliminate harm with precision, but has no energy left to build anything. Pure protective function with no creative residue. Knows how to cancel, doesn't know how to construct.

### Gift in Allyship (Repurposed)
The person who knows when a room is unsafe before anyone has named it. Can sense threat, name a boundary, and protect the conditions for genuine contact.

### Repurpose Move
Give the Protector a specific threat to scan for rather than a general field to defend. "What is the single real threat in this room right now?" — narrows the job from total vigilance to useful scouting.

### Developmental Arc
**Lower stage:** Protects from pain and discomfort as proxy for understanding damage. Doesn't know true capacity because it's never been tested. Self-polices. The canceller — identifies harm but has no energy left to build.

**Wake Up:** Notices that protection is preventing encounters that would reveal real capacity. Asks: what am I actually afraid of vs. what can I genuinely not survive?

**Clean Up:** Calibrates with the Damaged Self — begins distinguishing necessary damage (builds capacity, creates real contact) from unnecessary damage (depletes without return).

**Grow Up:** Learns to walk with you rather than block you. Gives permission to enter encounters it previously closed. Accompanies rather than bars the gate.

**Show Up:** Enters the world with the Damaged Self as its compass. Knows what it can take. Protects against what genuinely needs deflecting. Creates real contact because protection is calibrated, not total.

**Allyship failure without Damaged Self:** Over-protective of emotional and physical resources. Doesn't show up where it costs something. People being allying with can feel the absence of real skin in the game.

## Gate 2: Controller
**WAVE slot:** Clean Up (External) | **Trigram:** Dui ☱ Lake

### Core Function
Decides what is good and bad. Source of satisfaction and dissatisfaction states.
Operates in sequence after the Protector: Protector answers "Will I live?", Controller answers "How will I live?"

### Relationship to Protector
Works FOR the Protector — there is nothing to control if the self is dead. But the Controller is addicted to control and will attempt to take over the Protector's job, identify AS the Protector, and merge with it. The two voices can become indistinguishable when the merger runs long enough.

### How It Shows Up in Allyship
- Controls the discourse — defines what counts as real allyship, what language is acceptable, what positions are permitted
- Has conflated control of discourse with safety: feels in control = feels safe. These are not the same thing.
- The merger with the Protector produces someone who enforces discourse standards while framing it as keeping people safe

### Shadow (Unchecked)
Manages allyship to death. Effective allyship requires people to be genuinely out of control — to be moved, changed, surprised, uncertain. The Controller blocks exactly this. It doesn't attack allyship, it scripts it until there's no life left in it.

### Gift in Allyship (Repurposed)
The capacity to name what's working and what isn't — real discernment about quality, not just rule enforcement. The Controller's "good/bad" function, aimed at outcomes rather than compliance, is genuine strategic intelligence.

### Repurpose Move
Relief, not removal — relieve the Controller of the job it has been overworking. "I can hold this now. Point me to the specific thing that needs managing." Narrows from total discourse control to useful quality evaluation.

### Key Insight: The Controller Decides What Clean Is
You need a standard of cleanliness in order to clean up. The Controller is the gate that decides what "metabolized" means. Without it, Clean Up has no direction. The Emotional Body brings the charge; the Controller decides when it's complete.

**Allyship conflict is often Controllers battling for supremacy** — who gets to decide how the world should be, what counts as good allyship, whose standard wins. The person who controls the standard controls the discourse.

**The Controller → Victim dependency:** The Victim works for the Controller. Its stories enforce the Controller's standard. Sympathy-seeking maintains the standard in place. To change the standard, work with the Controller directly — changing the story (Victim layer) without changing the standard (Controller layer) produces new stories in service of the old standard.

### Developmental Arc
**Lower stage:** Sets the standard and enforces it through shame and discourse control. Frames compliance as safety. Believes the standard is objective reality. Battles other Controllers. The addiction: the satisfaction of being right.

**Wake Up:** Sees that it IS setting a standard — not observing reality. The dissatisfaction and satisfaction states are its own creation. "I'm not describing what allyship is. I'm enforcing what I decided allyship should be."

**Clean Up:** Asks whether the standard serves the Vulnerable Child or the Controller's need for control. Runs the standard itself through scrutiny: where did this come from? What is it protecting?

**Grow Up:** Can update the standard — not abandon it, but revise it. Distinguishes load-bearing standards (protect what matters) from habitual ones (maintain position).

**Show Up:** Sets standards others adopt voluntarily because they work — not because shame enforces them. The culture-setter rather than the enforcer.

## Gate 3: Skeptic
**WAVE slot:** Wake Up (Internal) | **Trigram:** Li ☲ Fire

### Core Function
Lieutenant of the Protector. Job: discernment about what is safe, harmful, or transformative.

### The Mushroom Problem (Origin Story)
In the forest, the primordial challenge: how do you tell if the mushroom is food, poison, or psychedelic? The Skeptic emerged to solve this — to classify experience before committing to it. Three categories: safe (proceed), dangerous (stop), transformative-but-disorienting (proceed with caution).

Doubting is a fear response. It knows how to stop. It does not know how to move forward.

### The Abjuration Spell
When the Skeptic doubts itself or others, it casts an abjuration spell — the D&D school of magic that creates protective barriers, wards off threat, banishes. The Skeptic doesn't attack possibility; it banishes it. Nothing can enter the field.

### How It Shows Up in Allyship
**Primary target of the spell: the self.**
- "I'm going to get this wrong"
- "I don't have what it takes"
- "I know what I need to do but I don't trust myself to do it"
- Self-abjuration → paralysis. The person knows the move. They don't make it.

**Shadow escape from self-doubt: hero-seeking.**
- When self-doubt becomes unbearable, outsource the discernment to a designated authority
- "I'll just follow [correct voice] and not have to think"
- In allyship: waiting for the right POC voice to validate the next step instead of developing your own read
- Produces two shadow types: the person who does nothing, AND the person who blindly follows

### Gift in Allyship (Repurposed)
The person who asks the question nobody wants to ask. The one who notices the gap between what the initiative promises and what the implementation can actually deliver. Pattern recognition from experience — compressed history of what has failed before.

### Repurpose Move
Give the Skeptic a specific question instead of a general field to doubt. Not "will this work?" (too broad, always produces doubt) but "what's the single most likely failure point?" — uses the discernment without the blanket abjuration.

### Lower Stage → Higher Stage: Doubt → Wonder
Lower Skeptic **doubts**. Higher Skeptic **wonders**. Doubt closes; wonder opens.

**The seeing prerequisite:** To doubt something, you have to see it first. The Skeptic is a perceptual gate — noticing precedes doubting. Wake Up at the Skeptic gate is learning to see the problems in the world before doubting your capacity to address them.

**The enlightenment paradox:** A perfectly developed Skeptic would achieve enlightenment — it would realize the impermanence of all things and conclude nothing needs fixing. Therefore, to be an ally, one must be *purposefully deluded* into thinking one is necessary. This is not a failure of the Skeptic — it is the Skeptic making a deliberate choice: I see the impermanence AND I choose to act anyway.

### Developmental Arc
**Lower stage:** Overused doubt paralyzes. Abjuration spell casts automatically. Blocks movement before seeing what's actually there. Self-directed: "I'm going to get this wrong."

**Wake Up:** Learns to see the problem before doubting the capacity to address it. The book's origin: Wendell's Skeptic doubted that allyship was something people knew how to do — that doubt required seeing the gap first.

**Clean Up:** Right-sized doubt applied to specific objects rather than general fields. "What's the single most likely failure point?" — discernment rather than blanket abjuration.

**Grow Up:** Doubt converts to wonder. "What is this really?" rather than "is this safe?" The mushroom problem solved at full capacity — food, poison, psychedelic, and combinations not yet named.

**Show Up:** The person who asks the question nobody wants to ask. Compressed history of what has failed before offered as pattern recognition, not obstacle. The purposeful delusion held consciously and lightly.

## Gate 4: Fixer/Healer
**WAVE slot:** Grow Up (External) | **Trigram:** Zhen ☳ Thunder

### Core Function
The gate most people mistake for allyship itself — the drive to fix, repair, restore.

### Two Definitions of "Fix"
English gives "fix" two opposite meanings: to repair, and to hold in place. Both run in allyship:
- **Repairing Fixer:** tries to fix what is already working, or imposes their version of "fixed" on someone who didn't ask
- **Holding Fixer:** stabilizes broken situations because change is more threatening than current dysfunction — manages conflict rather than resolving it

### The Fundamental Category Error
You can only fix objects. Healing is an inside job — it can only be done by the person themselves. The Fixer who tries to fix persons substitutes their own judgment for the other person's autonomy. Well-intentioned. Harmful.

### How It Shows Up in Allyship
- The Fixer/Healer IS what most people think allyship means — allies fix problems or heal the sick
- People try to fix themselves to become better allies (applying "fix" to a person — doesn't work)
- People try to fix others (same error)
- The holding Fixer keeps broken coalitions, broken DEI programs, broken dynamics intact because disruption is scary

### Gift in Allyship (Repurposed)
Aimed at circumstances — systems, structures, conditions — not persons. Fix the conditions that lead to emergent allyship outcomes. Build what people can heal into.

### Repurpose Move
"What is the specific circumstance I can actually change here?" — redirects from person-fixing to condition-fixing.

### The Grow Up Transition
The Fixer grows up the moment it **stops trying to fix the self.** Most allyship fixing is self-directed — "am I good enough yet?" — and fails because you cannot fix a person. Identity-fixing is impossible. Skill development is possible.

**Fixer works for the Controller.** The Controller sets the standard; the Fixer executes toward it. If the standard is "fix yourself to be a good ally," the Fixer is trapped in an impossible loop. If the standard evolves to "steward the conditions for growth," the Fixer has meaningful work it can complete.

**The Steward Shift:** The grown-up Fixer becomes a steward of the conditions for the Vulnerable Child's growth — not the grower, the groundskeeper. Trees grow up naturally. The Fixer clears obstacles and creates conditions, then trusts the process.

### Developmental Arc
**Lower stage:** Fixes self toward impossible standard. Identity-fixing loop. Depletes. Never arrives at "fixed."

**Wake Up:** Notices the fixing is self-directed. Sees the loop: try → fail → shame → try harder.

**Clean Up:** Distinguishes identity (cannot be fixed) from skills (can be developed). Redirects from "fix me" to "build something."

**Grow Up:** Stops being identified with skills. Can develop and release them without self-verdict. The Fixer as steward: clears obstacles, builds conditions, steps back.

**Show Up:** The structure others can enter and flourish in. Success = obsolescence, not indispensability. Structural generosity: the blueprint as act of love.

## Gate 5: Victim
**WAVE slot:** Wake Up (External) | **Trigram:** Xun ☴ Wind
*(Corrected from Gate 6 — 2026-05-19, see BARS_ICHING_ARCHITECTURE.md)*

### Core Function
The Victim voice in the Karpman Drama Triangle. Its job is to get sympathy from others by telling stories about harm.

### Two Ways It Shows Up in Allyship
1. **Seeking victims to ally with** — the ally needs a Victim to complete their Rescuer role; they are actively looking for someone to save
2. **Self-as-victim** — the ally treats themselves as a victim of being a bad ally ("I'm such a terrible person for what I said"); this makes the ally the protagonist of the marginalized person's experience

### The Drama Triangle Connection
Karpman's Drama Triangle: Victim → Villain → Rescuer. Most allyship operates inside this triangle without knowing it. The Victim must stay a Victim to keep the Rescuer engaged. The Rescuer needs Victims to feel purposeful. The Villain is the external enemy that holds the whole structure together.

The Victim's stories are not necessarily false — but they are told for sympathy, not for truth. The Victim will exaggerate to keep the drama going.

**Distinction from Damaged Self:** The Damaged Self HAS taken damage. The Victim TELLS THE STORY about it. The Damaged Self doesn't engage in drama.

**Why Victim precedes Emotional Body:** The Victim is the surface defense against feeling. Its move — "I can't feel this, look what was done to me" — blocks access to the Emotional Body beneath. You walk through the story before you can reach the charge.

### Shadow in Allyship
- Allies perform the Rescuer role and call it justice
- The people being "helped" remain characters in someone else's story
- Self-victimization consumes the ally's energy and centers them in someone else's experience

### Gift Repurposed (Allyship Triangle)
The Victim becomes the **Hero** — the protagonist of their own story, not waiting to be rescued. They tell stories to invite others into effective allyship, not for clout or sympathy.

The Rescuer becomes the **Ally** — holds space, works toward their own obsolescence, succeeds when the Hero no longer needs them in that role.

See `ALLYSHIP_TRIANGLE.md` for the full framework.

### Repurpose Move
Step off the Drama Triangle. See the person you're allying with as a Hero — someone authoring their own story — not a Victim waiting for rescue.

### Structural Relationship to Controller
**The Victim works for the Controller.** Its stories enforce the Controller's standard. Sympathy-seeking maintains that standard in place. The Victim can only take in sympathetic charge — emotions that don't generate sympathy (anger especially) get locked out, narrowing the available emotional vocabulary.

**Lower-stage Victim cannot hold multiple emotions simultaneously** because it cannot tell stories with multiple satisfactions. Each emotion has its own satisfaction. The Victim can only hold the emotional line that its current story supports.

**"Woke" culture = the Wake Up of victim consciousness.** Waking up means becoming aware of the story itself.

### Developmental Arc
**Lower stage (Egocentric):** Wants sympathy to enroll others' Protectors. Can only receive sympathy. Marks as Villain anyone who blocks it. Locks emotional grammar to sympathy-generating channels only.

**Wake Up:** Sees the story itself — "I am inside a narrative" rather than "I am describing reality." Can now take the sympathy flow as an object and run it through emotional alchemy.

**Clean Up:** Metabolizes the charge beneath the story. What emotion is this story managing? What's actually happening in the body under the narrative?

**Grow Up (Advocate):** Can give sympathy to others. Discerns which stories increase well-being vs. which hold people in unproductive cycles.

**Show Up (Storyteller):** Gives AND receives sympathy, knowing that without rapport, advocacy is less effective. The story becomes a craft, not an identity.

## Gate 6: Emotional Body
**WAVE slot:** Clean Up (Internal) | **Trigram:** Kan ☵ Water
*(Corrected from Gate 5 — 2026-05-19, see BARS_ICHING_ARCHITECTURE.md)*

### Core Function
In original Big Mind: the voice of fear ("this is not safe").
In MTGOA: expanded to hold all five emotional channels — Fear, Grief, Anger, Shame, Joy — because the book's emotional alchemy framework requires a gate for the full emotional body.

### The Five Channels
Each channel carries specific intelligence:
- **Fear:** where the real risk is
- **Grief:** what mattered
- **Anger:** where a boundary was crossed
- **Shame:** where values and behavior split
- **Joy:** what is aligned

### How It Shows Up in Allyship
The emotional charge arrives before the language does. Performers of allyship manage the charge rather than reading it. The gate is the moment before metabolization — the raw signal.

Shadow: charge that runs unprocessed shows up as disconnection, reactivity, performed empathy, misdirected anger, shame that collapses into paralysis.

### Repurpose Move
Locate the signal before acting on it. Name which channel. The location changes what happens next.

### The Five Channels — Functional Intelligence (deepened 2026-05-19)
Each emotion does a specific job. The Emotional Body is not "feeling emotions" — it is knowing what each emotion *does*:

| Emotion | Job |
|---------|-----|
| **Anger** | Creates boundaries AND creates things — destroys to make space, builds from that space |
| **Fear** | Detects threats |
| **Joy** | Takes delight; lightens things up |
| **Sadness** | Shows what you care about and how far away you are from it |
| **Neutrality** | Detaches from anything being experienced — full-system perspective |

**The Victim locks the grammar.** If a story requires anger-as-villain, the Victim blocks anger, narrowing the available emotional vocabulary to sympathy-generating channels only. The Emotional Body restores the full range.

**All gates protect the Vulnerable Child** — and the Emotional Body is where this becomes visible. A player at the Emotional Body knows how each specific emotion is being used to protect the Vulnerable Child. Not "I have emotions" but "this fear is protecting the child from X."

### Developmental Arc
**Lower stage:** Emotions run without identification. Acts from charge without knowing which channel is active. Victim's locked grammar means whole emotional registers are unavailable.

**Wake Up:** Notices that an emotion is present and doing something. Begins distinguishing "I feel bad" from "I'm feeling fear right now, and fear is showing me something."

**Clean Up (the home stage):** Names the channel. Identifies what the emotion is protecting. Runs it through the WAVE-Spiral. Asks: is this protection still needed?

**Grow Up:** Range — can bring the emotion the situation calls for rather than defaulting to the available one. Can access anger when only grief has been allowed. Can bring joy to what has only known fear.

**Show Up:** Is the emotional presence the room needs. Brings the actual charge that serves the moment, not the charge that's most familiar.

### Relationship to Ch2
Gate 6 in Ch1 is the door. Ch2 (The Shaman) is the room — the full WAVE-Spiral practice of emotional alchemy. Ch1 teaches you the gate exists. Ch2 teaches you how to walk it.

## Gate 7: Damaged Self
**WAVE slot:** Show Up (Internal) | **Trigram:** Gen ☶ Mountain

### Core Function
The voice that has survived everything. Not a story about damage — the actual accumulated weight of damage taken and absorbed. Job: make sure the self doesn't die. Take the hit. Keep going.

### Key Distinction from the Victim
- The Victim tells stories about the damage (may exaggerate for drama)
- The Damaged Self has taken the damage and doesn't need to perform it
- The Damaged Self doesn't engage in drama — it's too busy surviving
- The essence of "did you die though" — undefeated, not because the damage wasn't real, but because survival is the job

### How It Shows Up in Allyship
Most allies don't see the Damaged Self in the people they're allying with. They see the Victim narrative. They respond with charity and parental energy. They treat as fragile someone who is actually the most durable thing in the room.

The Damaged Self is the reason allyship doesn't need to be as hands-on as allies think. The people being "helped" have survived things that would have broken the helper.

**The allyship failure:** missing resilience, responding to damage as if it means helplessness.

### When Vulnerable Child Is Running the Show
The Damaged Self shifts its function: it knows what kind of damage is necessary for growth, learning, exploration, and play. It calibrates risk rather than avoiding it. It distinguishes necessary difficulty from unnecessary harm.

### Gift Repurposed
Resilience, endurance, calibration of necessary risk. The Damaged Self knows the difference between damage that breaks and damage that builds.

### Repurpose Move
Recognize the resilience in the person you're allying with before you recognize their damage. Ask: what has this person survived that I would have needed help with? Offer support only where genuinely needed, not where the ally needs to feel useful.

### The Show Up Pair: Damaged Self + Protector
To show up effectively is to **take necessary damage AND protect yourself from unnecessary damage.** Both are required.

**Damaged Self without Protector:** Still survives, but props up the Victim's narratives unconsciously. Takes all damage indiscriminately — cannot distinguish necessary from unnecessary. Burns out. The Damaged Self's resilience gets mined rather than honored.

**Protector without Damaged Self:** Doesn't know its true capacity. Protects from pain as proxy for understanding actual damage. Over-protective of emotional resources. Doesn't show up where it costs something.

**Together:** The Damaged Self provides calibration data — "I've survived this, I know what it costs." The Protector screens what comes in — "this specific damage is unnecessary." Real-time calibration in the encounter.

### Developmental Arc
**Lower stage:** Takes all damage. Cannot distinguish necessary from unnecessary. Stories accumulate; Victim uses them. Resilient but exhausted.

**Wake Up:** Notices some damage is being taken unnecessarily. "Did this have to happen? Was this encounter worth what it cost?"

**Clean Up:** Works with the Protector to establish criteria. "I have survived this — but that doesn't mean I need to keep surviving it."

**Grow Up:** When Vulnerable Child is running the show — calibrates against growth, not just survival. "Is this damage necessary for the Vulnerable Child to grow?"

**Show Up:** The Mountain. Carries what it has absorbed without performing the absorption. Brings full weight of experience into the room as capacity, not wound. Doesn't announce what shaped it — shows up with it.

## Gate 8: Vulnerable Child
**WAVE slot:** Grow Up (Internal) | **Trigram:** Kun ☷ Earth

### Core Function
The source of creativity. The voice that feels emotions and acts on them. Job: learn, grow, explore, create, play.

### Why This Is Gate 8 (Not Just Last)
The Vulnerable Child is the destination, not just the final gate. When the Vulnerable Child is running the show, every other gate repurposes correctly:

| Gate | Default function | Function when Vulnerable Child leads |
|---|---|---|
| Protector | Guards against threat | Protects the child's ability to learn, grow, explore, feel, and play |
| Controller | Controls discourse | Sets standards for growth, play, exploration, and learning |
| Skeptic | Doubts possibility | Doubts things that prevent play, growth, learning, exploration |
| Fixer/Healer | Fixes persons | Maintains the playground — fixes circumstances |
| Victim | Tells stories for sympathy | Tells stories about learning and growing to invite others into effective allyship |
| Damaged Self | Survives | Calibrates what damage is necessary for growth |

### How It Shows Up in Allyship
When allyship comes from the Vulnerable Child — curiosity-driven, genuinely exploratory, willing to be surprised and changed — it is the most effective version of the work. The Vulnerable Child ally doesn't need to be right. It needs to learn.

Most of the game of allyship is creating space for the Vulnerable Child to run the show: both in the ally (recovering permission to not know, to try, to fail and learn) and in the people being allying with (protecting their right to grow and explore, not just be protected from harm).

### Shadow
The Vulnerable Child in exile: allyship as obligation, performance, credential-collecting. No curiosity. No genuine contact. No learning. The ally goes through the motions with the weight of doing it right instead of the freedom of doing it real.

### Repurpose Move
Let the Vulnerable Child back in. Let allyship be genuinely curiosity-driven.
- "What would I do in this situation if I wasn't afraid of getting it wrong?"
- "How would I approach this if it felt like play rather than obligation?"
- "What am I genuinely curious about here, underneath the performance?"

### Growth Is the Vulnerable Child's Essential Nature
**Trees grow up naturally.** The Vulnerable Child understands this — growth is not something you do to yourself, it's what you do when conditions are right. The Fixer's job is not to make the child grow. It is to steward conditions and trust the process.

The Vulnerable Child needs the Fixer to trust its essential nature. When the Fixer over-engineers the growth process, it becomes a Controller of development rather than a steward of it.

### Developmental Arc
**Lower stage (Exiled):** Allyship as obligation, credential-collecting, performance. No curiosity. No genuine contact. The child cannot be seen without risk.

**Wake Up:** Feels the difference between allyship as performance and allyship as genuine contact. The first moment of "I actually don't know, and I'm curious."

**Clean Up:** Lets the child's actual needs come forward. What does this child want that performance has been substituting for?

**Grow Up (the home stage):** Trusts natural growth. Allows the Fixer to steward conditions rather than forcing development. Can receive challenge and failure as part of growth — not as evidence of brokenness.

**Show Up:** Shows up to allyship with genuine curiosity — "what am I going to learn here?" — not "how do I perform this correctly?" Brings the specific, unrepeatable medicine that was shaped by this person's specific survival.

## Cross-Gate Structural Map (updated 2026-05-19)

### Hierarchical Dependencies
- **Fixer** works for the Controller (executes toward its standard)
- **Victim** works for the Controller (stories enforce its standard)
- **Controller** works for the Protector (standard serves survival)
- **All gates** protect the Vulnerable Child

### The Clean Up Pair Codependency
Controller decides what "clean" means. Emotional Body provides the charge to be cleaned. Neither can complete Clean Up alone: the Emotional Body without the Controller has charge but no direction; the Controller without the Emotional Body sets standards without material to work with.

### The Show Up Pair Calibration
Protector without Damaged Self = over-protection (doesn't know true capacity).
Damaged Self without Protector = under-protection (takes all damage, props up Victim unconsciously).
Together = real-time calibration: take necessary damage, deflect unnecessary damage.

### The Wake Up → Show Up Sequence
You cannot Show Up sustainably without having:
- Woken Up to the problems and stories (Skeptic + Victim)
- Cleaned Up the emotional charge (Emotional Body + Controller)
- Grown Up the capacity (Fixer + Vulnerable Child)

The Show Up gates (Protector + Damaged Self) are the last pair because showing up with calibrated protection requires all prior WAVE work to be available.
