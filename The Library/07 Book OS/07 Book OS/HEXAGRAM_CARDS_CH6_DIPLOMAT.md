# Hexagram Oracle Cards — Chapter 6: The Diplomat
**Upper trigram:** Dui ☱ Lake
**Chapter goal:** Develop the capacity to hold multiple valid perspectives without collapsing the tension — to create conditions where real things can happen.
**Chapter context:** The Diplomat holds the field. Each card asks how this gate's gift helps you keep the container open — without rescuing people from the discomfort, without managing the field into false agreement, without needing the tension to resolve before you've let it do its work.

**Card formula:** TYPE A/B/C component order per hexagram; Image text (Wilhelm/Baynes) as C2 opening; Gate / WAVE / 3 tasks with containers / 3-2-1 handoff / Appendix routing

---

## Card 1: Protector in the Diplomat
**Hex 43 · Breakthrough**
*Gate: Protector · WAVE: Show Up · Trigram: Qian ☰ × Dui ☱*

The lake has risen up to heaven — breakthrough; what has been building finally clears. The Protector is managing the field to prevent the intensity rather than to protect the conditions for genuine contact — holding the container against the release rather than through it. The Protector's gift in the Diplomat's territory is naming which capacity this moment requires so that when the field clears, what remains is real contact, not just relief.

**1.** Which capacity does this exchange require from you — signal clarity, staying-power, generative capacity, container stability, altitude? Name it specifically. *One word. Before the field opens.*
**2.** Set the one limit that keeps that capacity intact through the breakthrough — not preventing the intensity, but holding yourself through it. *Name it out loud before you enter the room.*
**3.** Capture: what did you protect, and what did that make possible for genuine contact? *Open the app. Two minutes.*

*Skilled players: If the Protector is managing the field to prevent intensity rather than to protect your capacity for genuine contact, take it into the 3-2-1 (Show Up) first.*

## Card 2: Controller in the Diplomat
**Hex 58 · Joy**
*(I Ching: Duì 兌)*
*Gate: Controller · WAVE: Clean Up · Trigram: Dui ☱ × Dui ☱*
*⬛ Double-trigram — Lake doubled. The deepest card in this chapter.*

Lakes resting one on the other — mutual nourishment; the joy that sustains comes from genuine exchange, not from managed pleasantness. The Controller's gift in the Diplomat's territory is updating the standard: "real contact happened" is different from "everyone felt comfortable" — and routing the field from that distinction is the organizing move. When the standard for how the exchange should feel arrives before the exchange does, the container is managing rather than holding.

**1.** Name the standard you're running for this exchange — what does "this going well" look like? *Say it out loud.*
**2.** Update it: "real contact happened" is different from "everyone felt comfortable." Route the field from that distinction. *Solo: write the new standard before the gathering. With: say it to one other person who will be in the room.*
**3.** Capture: what holding the new standard felt like in the room, and what the exchange could do because the standard changed. *One sentence in the app.*

*Skilled players: If the Controller is managing the field toward comfort rather than toward genuine contact, take it into the 3-2-1 (Clean Up) first.*

## Card 3: Skeptic in the Diplomat
**Hex 49 · Revolution**
*Gate: Skeptic · WAVE: Wake Up · Trigram: Li ☲ × Dui ☱*

Fire in the lake — revolution from within; the field that transforms doesn't announce it, it reorganizes what was already there. The Skeptic has already audited whether holding this tension is worth it — run the scenarios for how it goes wrong, doubted whether the discomfort produces anything real. The Skeptic's gift is the second look: does this field have actual heat in it, or has it been managed into comfortable circulation?

**1.** Stop before you hold the field. What is this tension actually about — underneath the presenting conflict? Name the heat, not the surface. *One sentence.*
**2.** Take the second look: does this field have actual transformative heat in it — something that could reorganize what's here — or has it been managed into pleasant circulation? Name the difference. *Before you decide whether to hold it.*
**3.** Capture: what did the second look name about the field that the first read missed? *In the app.*

*Skilled players: If the Skeptic is auditing the field's legitimacy ("this won't go anywhere") rather than the state of its heat, take it into the 3-2-1 (Wake Up) first.*

## Card 4: Fixer in the Diplomat
**Hex 17 · Following**
*Gate: Fixer/Healer · WAVE: Grow Up · Trigram: Zhen ☳ × Dui ☱*

Thunder in the middle of the lake — movement within the contained field; Following means adapting to what is actually moving, not to what you came in wanting to move. The Fixer's gift in the Diplomat's territory is the one structural move that follows the field's own direction — not fixing the tension, but clearing the specific condition that's preventing the field from doing its own work. When the Fixer is trying to fix the discomfort, it's mistaken the weather for the obstacle.

**1.** Map your scope — what specific condition is preventing this field from doing its own work? Not the discomfort, not the conflict, not the other person — the one structural condition within your scope. *Name it. One sentence.*
**2.** Follow what the field is actually moving toward. One structural move that clears that condition. *Not what you came in wanting to fix — what the field is pointing to. Before you speak.*
**3.** Capture: what did you clear, and did the field move after you stepped back — or is it still waiting for you to manage it? *In the app.*

*Skilled players: If the Fixer is trying to fix the tension rather than the condition preventing the field from doing its own work, take it into the 3-2-1 (Grow Up) first.*

## Card 5: Victim in the Diplomat
**Hex 28 · Great Excess**
*Gate: Victim · WAVE: Wake Up · Trigram: Xun ☴ × Dui ☱*

The story about what holding this field has cost you — the weight of staying, of not collapsing the tension, of being the one who didn't leave — arrived before the field had a chance to do its work. The lake rises above the trees: Great Excess, the beam bending — and the Victim's gift is the story told after the field holds, not the story that makes staying harder. The awareness this field needs is: someone stayed in the excess and something real came from it.

**1.** Notice the story running about what holding this field has cost you — the weight of staying, of not collapsing the tension. Hold it. *One sentence. This is not the story you'll tell.*
**2.** Hold the field first. Then tell the story that came from having stayed — the one that shows what became possible when someone didn't leave. *Solo: in your own notes, after the field clears. With: one person who also held this field with you.*
**3.** Capture: what you stayed for, what it cost not to leave, and what became possible in the field because someone held it. *In the app, after the field clears.*

*Skilled players: If the story is making staying harder rather than showing what becomes possible by staying, take the Victim into the 3-2-1 (Wake Up) first.*

## Card 6: Emotional Body in the Diplomat
**Hex 47 · Exhaustion**
*(I Ching: Kùn 困)*
*Gate: Emotional Body · WAVE: Clean Up · Trigram: Kan ☵ × Dui ☱*

The charge is running and the field is asking you to hold everyone's emotional weather before you've named your own channel — the container running empty while still being expected to hold. There is no water in the lake: Exhaustion; the Emotional Body's gift in the Diplomat's territory is naming your own channel first. The field can only hold what you can actually carry right now — Anger, Fear, Sadness, Joy, Neutrality — and knowing which one is running in you is the first condition for holding the room.

**1.** Before you hold anyone else's emotional weather: which channel is running in you right now? Anger / Fear / Sadness / Joy / Neutrality. *One word. In the body, not the analysis.*
**2.** Name it — to yourself, or aloud. The container can only hold from what it actually has. Let that channel do its work: Anger clears space, Fear names what's at stake, Sadness shows what matters, Joy lightens, Neutrality reveals the pattern. *Before you open the field.*
**3.** Capture: what did your named channel make possible in the room that running empty would have collapsed? *Open the app before the signal fades.*

*Skilled players: If the field is being asked to run on an empty container — holding others' charge before naming your own — take the Emotional Body into the 3-2-1 (Clean Up) first.*

## Card 7: Damaged Self in the Diplomat
**Hex 31 · Influence**
*Gate: Damaged Self · WAVE: Show Up · Trigram: Gen ☶ × Dui ☱*

A lake on the mountain — influence through receptivity; the field that draws people in does so by being genuinely open to being changed by what arrives. The Damaged Self's gift in the Diplomat's territory is the damage worth choosing: being moved, being altered by what the tension reveals — taking that damage deliberately, not by accumulation. When damage is landing without a decision — just absorbing whatever the field presses — influence runs on depletion.

**1.** Name the damage this field is asking you to take — specifically. Being moved, being altered by what arrives, being changed by the tension rather than managing it? *One sentence.*
**2.** Choose deliberately: is this the damage that builds your capacity to hold fields — the kind that deepens receptivity — or damage that depletes the container? *Name the choice before you're deep in the holding.*
**3.** If yes — take it on purpose. Capture: what did you absorb, and what did the field hold because you were genuinely open to it? *In the app, after the gathering.*

*Skilled players: If damage is accumulating without a decision — absorbing whatever the field presses without choosing — take the Damaged Self into the 3-2-1 (Show Up) first.*

## Card 8: Vulnerable Child in the Diplomat
**Hex 45 · Gathering**
*Gate: Vulnerable Child · WAVE: Grow Up · Trigram: Kun ☷ × Dui ☱*

Over the earth, the lake — gathering; what belongs together finds its way to the same place when the field is genuinely open to receiving it. The Vulnerable Child's gift in the Diplomat's territory is genuine not-knowing: staying open to who and what arrives, not managing toward a predetermined configuration of the field. When the child is preparing for the gathering — deciding in advance what genuine contact will look like — the preparation is the closure.

**1.** Which mode is live right now: grow / learn / play / explore? Not the one the field requires — the one that's actually available. *One word.*
**2.** Open the field from that orientation — not to produce genuine contact, but to find out what arrives when the field is genuinely open. *Toward who and what you haven't decided about yet. Right now.*
**3.** Capture the surprise — who or what arrived that the preparation couldn't have predicted? *In the app, immediately after. If nothing surprised you, the preparation was still running the field.*

*Skilled players: If the gathering feels like something to facilitate correctly — the child is in exile. Take the Vulnerable Child into the 3-2-1 (Grow Up) first.*

## Design Notes

**Hexagram name decisions:**
- Hex 43 "Breakthrough" — keep (the lake breaking through to heaven; the Protector managing against breakthrough vs. through it)
- Hex 58 "Joy" — keep with I Ching credit Duì 兌; Lake doubled; the deepest Diplomat card — managed pleasantness vs. genuine joy is the whole chapter in one card
- Hex 49 "Revolution" — keep (fire in the lake; field with real transformative heat vs. comfortable circulation)
- Hex 17 "Following" — keep (adapting to what's actually moving; the Fixer following the field's direction rather than imposing)
- Hex 28 "Great Excess" — keep (lake above the trees; holding beyond capacity; the beam bending; load-bearing for Victim card)
- Hex 47 "Oppression" → replaced with "Exhaustion" (both are valid Wilhelm translations of Kùn 困; "Exhaustion" is more accessible and directly describes the no-water-in-the-lake energy; Kùn credit added)
- Hex 31 "Influence" — keep (lake on mountain; influence through receptivity; the Damaged Self whose openness draws the field in)
- Hex 45 "Gathering" — keep (over the earth, the lake; the assembly that happens when the field is genuinely open)

**Relational modifier applied:**
- Card 2 (Controller): Task 2 has "Solo / With:" modifier — relational gate
- Card 5 (Victim): Task 2 has "Solo / With:" modifier — relational gate

**Double-trigram card:**
- Card 2 (Controller × Diplomat = Hex 58, Lake doubled) — the standard that determines what "good exchange" means is the whole game in the Diplomat chapter; Joy doubled is its deepest expression

**Notable card intersections:**
- Hex 43 (Breakthrough) for Protector: the lake finally breaking through to heaven — the Protector's move is not preventing the intensity but protecting your capacity to be in it
- Hex 58 (Joy, Lake doubled) for Controller: Lakes nourishing each other — genuine mutual exchange vs. managed comfort; the Controller's discriminating move determines which one the field produces
- Hex 49 (Revolution) for Skeptic: fire in the lake — the Skeptic's second look names whether the heat is real or whether it's been managed into pleasant circulation
- Hex 28 (Great Excess) for Victim: the lake above the trees, the beam bending — someone staying in the excess is the story worth telling; told after, not before
- Hex 47 (Exhaustion) for Emotional Body: no water in the lake — the container that holds everyone else's charge before naming its own; naming your channel first is the first condition for holding the room
- Hex 31 (Influence) for Damaged Self: the mountain receiving the lake — influence through genuine receptivity; the damage worth choosing is being changed by what arrives
- Hex 45 (Gathering) for Vulnerable Child: the gathered waters over the earth — what belongs together arrives when the field is genuinely open; the child's not-knowing is what makes gathering possible
