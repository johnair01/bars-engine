# Hexagram Oracle Cards — Chapter 4: The Regent
**Upper trigram:** Xun ☴ Wind
**Chapter goal:** Develop the capacity to carry something worth belonging to across time — to steward without owning, to hold without hoarding.
**Chapter context:** The Regent carries what lasts. Each card asks how this gate's gift helps you hold something worth carrying — without owning it, controlling it, or needing to remain the one who holds it.

**Card formula:** TYPE A/B/C component order per hexagram; Image text (Wilhelm/Baynes) as C2 opening; Gate / WAVE / 3 tasks with containers / 3-2-1 handoff

---

## Card 1: Protector in the Regent
**Hex 9 · Small Taming**
*Gate: Protector · WAVE: Show Up · Trigram: Qian ☰ × Xun ☴*

Wind drives across heaven — small taming, the persistent refinement that doesn't force what won't be forced. The Protector's gift in the Regent's territory is naming which capacity this stewardship requires across time — not just for today's gathering, but for what the thing needs to still be healthy in a year. When the Protector is protecting your position in the institution rather than the capacity the institution needs, the limit has the wrong object.

**1.** Which capacity does this stewardship require from you right now — signal clarity, staying-power, generative capacity, container stability, altitude? Name it specifically. *One word. Before you proceed.*
**2.** Set the one limit that keeps that capacity intact across the long work — not just today but for what you're carrying in a year. *Name it out loud.*
**3.** Capture: what did you protect, and what does that make possible for what you're carrying? *Open the app. Two minutes.*

*Skilled players: If the Protector is protecting your position rather than the institution's capacity, take it into the 3-2-1 (Show Up) first.*

## Card 2: Controller in the Regent
**Hex 61 · Inner Truth**
*(I Ching: Zhōng Fú 中孚)*
*Gate: Controller · WAVE: Clean Up · Trigram: Dui ☱ × Xun ☴*

Wind over the lake — the surface moves and what lies beneath becomes visible; inner truth lands before procedure does. The standard for "right stewardship" arrived before the felt sense of what this institution is actually for — the Controller is running the institution, not the purpose. The Controller's gift is the organizing move: route everything from what the thing is actually in service of, not from what correct stewardship is supposed to look like.

**1.** Name the standard you're running for stewardship — what does "doing this right" look like? *Say it out loud.*
**2.** Now ask: does this standard route from what the institution is actually for, or from what good stewardship looks like? Update it. *Solo: write the new standard before your next stewardship move. With: say it to one other person carrying this thing.*
**3.** Capture: what you updated the stewardship standard to, and what the institution could do once you routed from purpose rather than procedure. *One sentence in the app.*

*Skilled players: If the Controller is enforcing how a steward is supposed to act rather than calibrating what the institution actually needs, take it into the 3-2-1 (Clean Up) first.*

## Card 3: Skeptic in the Regent
**Hex 37 · The Family**
*Gate: Skeptic · WAVE: Wake Up · Trigram: Li ☲ × Xun ☴*

Wind comes forth from fire — warmth radiates outward; what holds an institution together is not the rules but the living warmth at its center. The Skeptic's gift in the Regent's territory is the second look that names whether that fire is still burning or whether the institution is running on procedure. When the Skeptic's audit is aimed at the institution's legitimacy rather than the state of its center, the doubt is running the stewardship.

**1.** Stop before any stewardship move. What is the center of this institution — the fire it was built around? Name it. *One sentence.*
**2.** Take the second look: is that fire still burning, or has procedure replaced it? Name one difference between what it was built for and what it's currently running on. *Name the difference before you act.*
**3.** Capture: what did the second look name about the institution's center that the first read missed? *In the app.*

*Skilled players: If the Skeptic's audit is casting doubt at whether you have the standing to steward rather than at whether the center still holds, take it into the 3-2-1 (Wake Up) first.*

## Card 4: Fixer in the Regent
**Hex 42 · Increase**
*Gate: Fixer/Healer · WAVE: Grow Up · Trigram: Zhen ☳ × Xun ☴*

Wind and thunder together — each amplifies the other; Increase is what happens when you add your move to what is already moving, not to what is already stuck. The Fixer's gift in the Regent's territory is the one structural action that amplifies the institution's own momentum — not repairing what's broken, but reinforcing what's working. When the Fixer is already inside what needs to be fixed, the question of what to amplify hasn't been asked.

**1.** Map your scope — what in this institution is yours to strengthen? Not what's broken, not someone else's responsibility — the specific condition you can add force to. If you can't name it, you don't have permission. *One sentence.*
**2.** Make the one move that amplifies what's already working. Add your force to the momentum that's already there. *One action, not a renovation. Before you step back.*
**3.** Capture: what did you increase, and does it still need you — or does it run with more force without you managing it? *In the app.*

*Skilled players: If the Fixer is repairing what's broken rather than amplifying what works, take it into the 3-2-1 (Grow Up) first.*

## Card 5: Victim in the Regent
**Hex 57 · The Gentle**
*(I Ching: Xùn 巽)*
*Gate: Victim · WAVE: Wake Up · Trigram: Xun ☴ × Xun ☴*
*⬛ Double-trigram — Wind doubled. The deepest card in this chapter.*

The story about what this institution has been through — what it cost, who wasn't recognized, what was sacrificed — has been circulating so long it feels like the institution's identity, not its context. Winds following one upon the other: the penetrating story doesn't stop, but the Victim's gift is knowing which story is the institution's story versus which is yours. The story worth telling in a Regent's territory is the one that carries the purpose forward — told after the institution holds, not before.

**1.** Notice the story running about this institution — what it has been through, what it cost. Hold it. Just name it. *One sentence. This is not the story you'll tell.*
**2.** Identify which story the institution needs now: felt / naming / contextual / structural / relational / meta. The story that carries purpose forward is different from the story that carries injury. *Solo: write it before the next gathering. With: tell it to one person who carries this alongside you.*
**3.** Capture: which kind of story you told and to whom, and what it made possible for the people who belong to the institution. *One sentence in the app.*

*Skilled players: If the story is generating sympathy for what you've sacrificed rather than awareness for what the institution is for, take the Victim into the 3-2-1 (Wake Up) first.*

## Card 6: Emotional Body in the Regent
**Hex 59 · Dispersion**
*Gate: Emotional Body · WAVE: Clean Up · Trigram: Kan ☵ × Xun ☴*

Wind drives over the water — what was frozen disperses; the held charge finally breaking open is a clearing, not a loss. The channel is running in this institution — unnamed, it has been operating as "the tension we've all been pretending isn't here." The Emotional Body's gift is naming it before the move: which one is this — anger, fear, grief, joy, neutrality — and what does its function want to do with what's been frozen here?

**1.** Before any institutional move: which channel is running in this room right now? Anger / Fear / Sadness / Joy / Neutrality. *One word. In the body, not the analysis.*
**2.** Name it — the unnamed channel running in an institution is institutional weather. Let it do its work: Anger clears space, Fear names what's actually at stake, Sadness shows what matters, Joy lightens, Neutrality reveals the pattern. *Before you speak.*
**3.** Capture: what did the named channel make possible that unnamed charge would have let fester? *Open the app before the signal fades.*

*Skilled players: If the room has a charge nobody is naming and the stewardship feels like navigating weather, take the Emotional Body into the 3-2-1 (Clean Up) first.*

## Card 7: Damaged Self in the Regent
**Hex 53 · Gradual Progress**
*Gate: Damaged Self · WAVE: Show Up · Trigram: Gen ☶ × Xun ☴*

A tree grows on the mountain — gradual progress, roots going down as the branches go up; the institution that lasts is shaped by years of unchosen work, and the Damaged Self's gift is choosing which of those years are yours to give. The damage of long stewardship — invisibility, load-bearing work that others build on without knowing, credit that doesn't come — is worth taking on purpose when the tree is worth the mountain. When damage is accumulating without a decision, the Damaged Self is running the institution rather than serving it.

**1.** Name the damage this stewardship is asking you to take — specifically. Invisibility, load-bearing work without credit, being wrong in public, holding without being thanked? *One sentence.*
**2.** Choose deliberately: is this the damage that builds your capacity to carry this across time — or damage that depletes without return? *Name the choice before you're deep in it.*
**3.** If yes — take it on purpose. Capture: what did you absorb, and what does the institution now carry because you did? *In the app, when the work is done.*

*Skilled players: If damage is accumulating without a decision — just landing from the long work — take the Damaged Self into the 3-2-1 (Show Up) first.*

## Card 8: Vulnerable Child in the Regent
**Hex 20 · Contemplation**
*Gate: Vulnerable Child · WAVE: Grow Up · Trigram: Kun ☷ × Xun ☴*

Wind blows over the earth — contemplation, the wide gaze that receives before it speaks; the Regent who doesn't know yet is the one who can see the most. The Vulnerable Child's gift in the Regent's territory is genuine not-knowing: staying open to what this institution actually needs, not what responsible stewardship is supposed to look like. When the child is preparing to steward correctly — getting the approach right, the knowledge right — the preparation is the distance from the thing worth carrying.

**1.** Which mode is live right now: grow / learn / play / explore? Not the one responsible stewardship requires — the one that's actually available. *One word.*
**2.** Engage the institution from that orientation — not to steward it correctly, but to find out what it actually needs. *Toward the thing you've been approaching strategically. Right now.*
**3.** Capture the surprise — what did the institution show you that strategic stewardship couldn't have found? *In the app, immediately after. If nothing surprised you, the preparation was still running.*

*Skilled players: If stewardship feels like obligation, credential-earning, or something you have to be qualified for — the child is in exile. Take the Vulnerable Child into the 3-2-1 (Grow Up) first.*

## Design Notes

**Hexagram name decisions:**
- Hex 9 "Small Taming" — keep (the Regent's work is incremental refinement, not force; "small taming" is exactly right)
- Hex 61 "Inner Truth" — keep (load-bearing: the Controller must route from what is actually true about the institution's purpose; Zhōng Fú credit added)
- Hex 37 "The Family" — keep (the institution as family; the first and smallest thing worth carrying across time)
- Hex 42 "Increase" — keep (amplification of what works; distinct from repair)
- Hex 57 "The Gentle" — keep with double-trigram marker (Wind doubled; Xùn credit added)
- Hex 59 "Dispersion" — keep (the frozen charge finally breaking open; clearing rather than loss)
- Hex 53 "Gradual Progress" — keep (the long work; roots and branches; exactly the Regent's arc)
- Hex 20 "Contemplation" — keep (the wide gaze before action; the child who receives before stewardship begins)

**Relational modifier applied:**
- Card 2 (Controller): Task 2 has "Solo / With:" modifier — relational gate
- Card 5 (Victim): Task 2 has "Solo / With:" modifier — relational gate

**Double-trigram card:**
- Card 5 (Victim × Regent = Hex 57, Wind doubled) — the story that has been circulating so long it feels like governance; the deepest card in Ch4

**Notable card intersections:**
- Hex 9 (Small Taming) for Protector: the Regent's Protector is not heroic defense but persistent refinement — protecting the arc through small limits that compound
- Hex 61 (Inner Truth) for Controller: wind over lake — the Controller who updates the standard from what is actually true rather than what stewardship looks like; the inner truth precedes the procedure
- Hex 37 (The Family) for Skeptic: the institution as family — warmth at the center vs. rules managing the surface; the Skeptic's second look names which is running
- Hex 42 (Increase) for Fixer: amplification rather than repair; the Fixer in the Regent's territory has permission to increase what works, not mandate to fix what's broken
- Hex 57 (The Gentle, Wind doubled) for Victim: the perpetually circulating story of what the institution has been through — the Victim's gift is knowing whose story it is
- Hex 59 (Dispersion) for Emotional Body: the wind dissolves frozen water; the institutional charge that has been unnamed, running as weather, finally breaking open
- Hex 53 (Gradual Progress) for Damaged Self: the tree on the mountain — roots and branches; the long unchosen work that shapes the institution without announcement
- Hex 20 (Contemplation) for Vulnerable Child: the wide-open receiving gaze; the steward who doesn't know yet sees the most
