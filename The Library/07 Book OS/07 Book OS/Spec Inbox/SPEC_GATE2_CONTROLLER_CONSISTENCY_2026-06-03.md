# SPEC — Gate 2 Controller Consistency

## Problem Statement

Gate 2 language is directionally consistent across chapters, but the current Ch1 Controller deep dive emphasizes control, grip, and functioning more than the canonical Gate 2 ontology.

The canonical Controller is not only the part that controls emotion or keeps the person functional. It is the voice that:

- sets the standard
- decides what counts as good, clean, right, enough, safe, complete, or acceptable
- creates satisfaction/dissatisfaction states
- manages discourse by enforcing standards
- can mistake control for safety
- can repurpose into discernment about quality
- can update the standard consciously
- at its highest level, manages polarities by holding both sides as the standard to maintain

Before compressing Ch1 Gate 2, align its language with this ontology.

## Core Controller Definition

**Gate 2: Controller = the standard-setter.**

The Controller answers:

- What counts as doing this right?
- What standard am I enforcing?
- Who gave me this standard?
- What does this standard protect?
- Is this standard serving growth, play, and life, or serving the Controller's need for control?
- What polarity does this standard need to hold instead of collapse?

The Controller follows the Protector:

- Protector asks: **Will I live?**
- Controller asks: **How will I live? What standard will keep me acceptable, functional, and safe?**

## Polarity Function

The Controller also manages polarities.

At lower development, it collapses polarity into one-sided control:

- safety over aliveness
- correctness over contact
- order over responsiveness
- belonging over truth
- perfection over shipment
- harmony over honesty
- perspective over presence

At higher development, the Controller sets and maintains a standard that can hold both sides:

- safety **and** aliveness
- correctness **and** contact
- order **and** responsiveness
- belonging **and** truth
- good-enough structure **and** live adaptation
- harmony **and** honest terms
- perspective **and** presence

This makes the mature Controller a polarity-integrating voice. It does not abandon standards. It updates the standard so both poles remain alive.

## Ch1 Specific Need

Ch1 is "how to use this book." Therefore Ch1 Gate 2 should explicitly connect the Controller to the standard the reader brings to the book itself.

The reader may be running standards such as:

- I have to understand the whole map before I move.
- I have to feel the right thing.
- I have to prove I am not appropriating body knowing.
- I have to be a good student of the system.
- I have to do the Forest correctly.
- I have to make the book work for me quickly or I am failing again.

The Ch1 Controller's mature move is to update the standard:

> The standard is not "perform the journey correctly." The standard is "find out what's here and make one honest move."

## Required Ch1 Move

Preserve the existing dignity and relief language:

- Controller has a real gift.
- Controller kept the reader functional.
- Controller is not the enemy.
- The move is relief, not removal.

But add or sharpen the standard-setting frame:

- Name the standard the Controller is enforcing.
- Show that the standard may be old, inherited, or performance-based.
- Update the standard to fit the game pedagogy.
- Connect to polarity: not control vs chaos, but structure and aliveness.

## Consistency Principles

Use these across future Gate 2 edits:

1. **Do not reduce Controller to suppression.**
   Suppression is one behavior; standard-setting is the function.

2. **Do not make Controller the enemy.**
   Its gift is quality discernment and standard management.

3. **Do not remove standards.**
   The mature move is standard revision, not standard abandonment.

4. **Name the polarity.**
   Each Face's Controller is trying to manage a polarity. Lower Controller collapses it; mature Controller holds both.

5. **Keep relief, not removal.**
   The Controller is relieved when the self can consciously hold the standard.
