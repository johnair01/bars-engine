# Gate Variation Matrix

## Purpose

The 8-Gate walk stays structurally stable across the manuscript, but the question asked at each gate must vary by chapter altitude and by the role-pattern the chapter is teaching. The book is not repeating the same inner-parts exercise six times. It is showing how the same gate logic changes when the developmental sphere changes.

## Rule of Thumb

- **Protector** is about what is being protected from loss, exposure, or premature movement.
- **Controller** is about the standards, conditions, or constraints being enforced.
- **Skeptic** is about what is being seen or perceived, and how that perception is being doubted; at higher altitudes it turns toward wonder.
- **Fixer** is about what is being held in place or repaired; in a more feminine register it can be the Healer.
- **Emotional Body** is the gate that asks how emotion is being used to perceive reality. It includes the full emotional field: fear, anger, sadness, joy, neutrality.
- **Victim** is about how sympathy is received through story; it grows into Advocate, then Storyteller.
- **Damaged Self** is about what is still survivable — the last line of defense.
- **Vulnerable Child** is about the living gift underneath the defenses.

## Chapter-Specific Anchors

### Ch2 — Shaman
- **Protector:** what feeling are you protecting from surfacing?
- **Controller:** what feeling standards are you upholding as good or bad?
- **Skeptic:** what emotions are being perceived, and what needs to be doubted about them?
- **Fixer:** what emotions are being held in place or repaired?
- **Emotional Body:** how are emotions being used to perceive reality?
- **Victim:** what stories about one’s emotions are being used to receive and give sympathy?
- **Damaged Self:** what emotional damage is survivable?
- **Vulnerable Child:** what gift is underneath the defenses, waiting to be felt?

### Ch3 — Challenger
- **Protector:** where is resistance strongest, and what push is it protecting from?
- **Controller:** what standard of acceptable force are you enforcing?
- **Skeptic:** what challenges are real, and which need to be doubted?
- **Fixer:** what challenger, conflict, or confrontation is being held in place, and what would it look like to repair it through challenge?
- **Emotional Body:** how does the emotional body challenge things?
- **Victim:** what conflicts are you creating to preserve the story sympathy in the direction you want?
- **Damaged Self:** what emotional damage is survivable?
- **Vulnerable Child:** what clean no is waiting to be spoken from the center?

### Ch4 — Regent
- **Protector:** what responsibility or role are you protecting yourself from?
- **Protector secondary:** how are you protecting others by not taking responsibility?
- **Controller:** what inherited standards of stewardship are being maintained?
- **Skeptic:** what is being perceived about the tradition, and how is that perception being doubted?
- **Fixer:** what structure is being held in place or repaired instead of simply escaped?
- **Emotional Body:** how are feelings being used to tell the truth about what must be carried?
- **Victim:** what story is receiving sympathy, and how does it become accountable stewardship?
- **Damaged Self:** what emotional damage is survivable, and what line of defense is being held?
- **Vulnerable Child:** what responsibility do you want to inherit because it matters?

### Ch5 — Architect
- **Protector:** what design risk are you protecting yourself from?
- **Controller:** what standards are you protecting through the systems you use or build?
- **Controller secondary:** how are you systemically administrating and engineering your values?
- **Skeptic:** what is being perceived as possible, and how is that possibility being doubted?
- **Fixer:** what system is being held in place or repaired rather than overbuilt?
- **Emotional Body:** how are emotions being used to perceive leverage, friction, and fit?
- **Victim:** what story is receiving sympathy, and how does it become useful feedback?
- **Damaged Self:** what emotional damage is survivable, and what does the system think would break you?
- **Vulnerable Child:** what do you want to build because it would make the right thing easier?

### Ch6 — Diplomat
- **Protector:** what relationship cost are you protecting yourself from?
- **Controller:** what terms or standards are you keeping in place so the field can stay?
- **Skeptic:** what is being perceived in the field, and how is that perception being doubted?
- **Fixer:** what rupture is being held in place or repaired before it hardens?
- **Emotional Body:** how are emotions being used to perceive the relational field?
- **Victim:** what story is being used to receive sympathy, and how does it become shared sympathy?
- **Damaged Self:** what emotional damage is survivable, and what closeness feels too costly to risk?
- **Vulnerable Child:** what kind of belonging are you trying to make possible?

### Ch7 — Sage
- **Protector:** what altitude or clarity are you protecting from exposure or misunderstanding?
- **Controller:** what standard of seeing or credibility are you maintaining?
- **Skeptic:** what is being seen, and how is that seeing being doubted or turned toward wonder?
- **Fixer:** what perspective is being held in place or repaired so it can serve?
- **Emotional Body:** how are emotions being used to perceive altitude and commitment?
- **Victim:** what story is being used to receive sympathy, and how does it become story-with-wisdom?
- **Damaged Self:** what emotional damage is survivable, and what does it cost to keep calling the view dangerous?
- **Vulnerable Child:** what does it mean to want both presence and perspective at once?

### Ch8 — Player
- **Protector:** what unfinished building or identity are you protecting?
- **Controller:** what standard of readiness is keeping you in preparation instead of play?
- **Skeptic:** what is being perceived as possible, and how is that possibility being doubted?
- **Fixer:** what build is being held in place or repaired so it can actually ship?
- **Emotional Body:** how are emotions being used to perceive what the build is for?
- **Victim:** what story is receiving sympathy, and how does it become a path forward?
- **Damaged Self:** what emotional damage is survivable, and what keeps you from handing the work off?
- **Vulnerable Child:** what game do you want to play because it feels like life?

## Editing Use

When revising the gates, keep the chapter's altitude visible in the question itself. Do not simply swap the same fear-based wording through every gate. The question should tell the reader what the gate is doing in that chapter's sphere:

- Shaman: feeling
- Challenger: willing / pushing
- Regent: responsibility / inheritance
- Architect: design / standards / system
- Diplomat: terms / staying / relationship
- Sage: altitude / clarity / commitment
- Player: building / play / handoff

The repetition is in the walk. The variation is in the question.
