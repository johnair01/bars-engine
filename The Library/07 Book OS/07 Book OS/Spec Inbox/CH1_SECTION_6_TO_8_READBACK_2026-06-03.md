## Verdict

Section 6 through Section 8 works as a playable onboarding arc.

The reader is not merely being given a list of voices. They are being taught how to walk a charged issue through the Forest and turn recognition into one move.

The sequence now has a clear tutorial logic:

1. Section 6 gives the map and lets the reader meet the Gates.
2. Section 7 walks one lived moment through the map.
3. The first Shaman move turns the walk into practice.
4. Section 8 converts the practice into an opening game loop.

That is the right shape for Chapter 1.

## What Works

### Section 6: The Gates

The section now starts cleanly:

- lineage is acknowledged without taking over
- the reader is told not to study the Gates
- the Gates are framed as how help gets received
- the first-person guardrail is clear

The eight-Gate sequence remains long, but the length is currently justified because each Gate gives the reader a playable encounter.

### Section 7: Journey to the Center

This section is doing crucial conversion work.

It takes the eight Gates out of taxonomy and puts them into one lived moment. That is what keeps the chapter from becoming a manual.

Strong line:

> Not a mystical place. A lived moment where you stop performing and tell the truth about what is happening in you.

The move list after that is clean and practical:

- name one feeling instead of a theory
- name one boundary instead of ten arguments
- ask one true question instead of managing the whole room
- stay present one breath longer

### First Shaman Move

The Egan attribution is longer than the Genpo attribution, but it lands better here because it introduces the practice format immediately before the first application.

The body-first instruction is mostly safe from enforced somatic register because it allows the reader to locate, name, and notice without demanding a specific kind of spiritualized body performance.

Watch this in Chapter 2, where the somatic material will carry more weight.

### Section 8: The Game

Section 8 does what it needs to do:

- spot the Gate
- honor the gift
- name the cost
- choose one counter-move
- log the learning

This finally gives the reader a small, repeatable game loop.

Strong line:

> You are already a player. Now you're becoming a deliberate one.
