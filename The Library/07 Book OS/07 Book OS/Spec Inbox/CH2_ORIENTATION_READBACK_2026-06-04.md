## Somatic Register Watch

`EA_CHANNEL_REFERENCE` gives the key editorial rule:

> The body responds to good story, not to prescription.

Ch2 currently has body-first strength, but it also contains some prescription-risk language:

- "Your body tenses before you notice you're afraid."
- "Your chest tightens before you realize you're angry."
- "Your throat closes before you can say 'sad.'"
- "Your body should know you're doing it."
- "Your body says..."
- "Come back to the body. Feel what's actually present."

Some of this may stay because Ch2 is explicitly teaching practice. The question is not "remove somatic language." The question is:

> Does the prose let the reader generate the body signal, or does it instruct them into a preapproved bodily response?
