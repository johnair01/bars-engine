# Ch1 Full Trust Readback

## Passage Read

Read full manuscript export:

- `/Users/wendellbritt/The Library /mtgoa-manuscript/chapters/ch1-SHAMAN/CHAPTER1_FULL_DRAFT.md`

Lens:

- Capability: does the chapter demonstrate writer capability and increase reader capability?
- Integrity: does the chapter show clean lineage, honest self-positioning, and coherent standards?
- Benevolence: does the chapter make the reader feel more capable of goodness without flattering self-erasure or performance?

## Verdict

Ch1 is strong enough to pause.

The chapter now works as the book's first real onboarding chapter. It does not merely tell the reader why allyship fails. It teaches the reader how to hold the book, how to enter the Forest, how to meet the Gates, how to make a first Shaman move, and how to convert the experience into a BARs loop.

The biggest improvement from the recent work is that the chapter now has a consistent pedagogy:

> recognition -> one honest move -> reflection -> repeat

That makes the length feel earned.

## Capability Trust

Strong.

The chapter increases reader capability in several ways:

- names the felt failure of old allyship
- distinguishes public performance from durable capacity
- gives the Village / Forest distinction
- names the Six Guides without forcing total comprehension
- teaches how the game teaches
- frames Gates as playable thresholds, not personality types
- turns Gates into a sequence of trigger, gift, cost, move, example, prompt
- converts the Gates into a lived walk in Section 7
- gives the first Shaman move
- gives a five-move opening game
- gives the first BARs loop

The reader is not left with an insight only. They are given a usable first practice.

Capability risk:

The chapter is dense. But the humane teaching frame now tells the reader how to carry that density:

> You are going to meet more language than you can use all at once. That is normal.

That sentence earns a lot of trust.

## Integrity Trust

Strong.

The chapter now names lineage at the places where lineage matters:

- Carolyn Elliott for Shadow / shadow retrieval
- Genpo Roshi / Big Mind for inner voices / Gates
- Karpman for Drama Triangle
- Gerard Egan for the practice format

The origin story also builds integrity because it does not pretend the book came from neutral abstraction. It came from lived irritation, anger, and the author's own tools being aimed at allyship.

Best integrity move:

> Not from a framework. Not from a research review. From a rock in my shoe.

This line works because the chapter still names its frameworks elsewhere. The author is not claiming no lineage; he is claiming the book's animating spark came from lived experience.

Integrity watch item:

Karpman is named, but there is no source context in the body beyond "mapped it fifty years ago." This is likely fine for body prose, but source notes should carry fuller attribution.

## Benevolence Trust

Strong, with earned sharpness.

The chapter is not sentimental. It names old allyship's failures, performance, exhaustion, rescue patterns, self-victimization, and charity energy sharply. But it also dignifies the reader's protective parts:

- Shadow protected something once.
- Protector has a gift.
- Controller is not the enemy.
- Victim is not an insult.
- Damaged Self is undefeated.
- Vulnerable Child is the destination.

This matters for the ideal reader. The chapter does not ask them to suffer more to prove they are good. It asks them to become more capable of honest movement.

Best benevolence move:

> The map is not the assignment. The move is the work.

This line replaces moral performance with playable agency.
