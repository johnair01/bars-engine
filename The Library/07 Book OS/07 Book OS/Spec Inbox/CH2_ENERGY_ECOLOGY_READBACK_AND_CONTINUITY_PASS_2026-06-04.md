# Ch2 Energy Ecology Readback + Continuity Pass

## Scope

Focused readback from:

- `Quick Triage Rule`
- `The Energy Ecology`
- `How to Practice: Four Scenarios`
- `The Shaman's Superpower`

Continuity check against:

- Ch0 token/fuel/ticket language
- Ch1 cost/capacity/gate language
- Ch2 emotional alchemy/ecology language

## Readback Verdict

The energy ecology replacement is working. It removes the rejected accounting feel and gives the reader a more humane question:

> What did this move do to my capacity to keep showing up truthfully?

The strongest section is the ecology explanation itself. It now connects directly to Ch0's burnout mechanics without asking the reader to run math in a charged room.

## What Was Catching

The scenarios originally showed WAVE practice, but they did not clearly help the reader feel the new ecology distinction. After naming replenishing, sustaining, and costly moves, the chapter needed the examples to model those categories directly.

The reader should not have to infer:

- which move sustains the field
- which move returns capacity
- which move spends capacity on purpose
- why restoration matters after costly moves

## Edits Implemented

In Ch2 Book OS canon and manuscript export:

- Added a bridge sentence before the four scenarios:
  - the point is to feel the difference between keeping the field workable, returning capacity, and spending capacity on purpose.
- Renamed Scenario 1 from `10-Second WAVE-Spiral` to `Sustaining Move`.
- Added a closing ecology sentence to Scenario 1.
- Renamed Scenario 2 from `Full WAVE-Spiral` to `Replenishing Integration`.
- Added a closing ecology sentence to Scenario 2.
- Renamed Scenario 3 to `Replenishing Move Into Action`.
- Renamed Scenario 4 to `Costly Move With Restoration`.

## Continuity Findings

Ch0 was otherwise aligned with Ch2, especially:

- non-renewable tokens/fuel
- tired but not empty
- stretched but not hollow
- practice replenishes as it costs
- tickets as emotional return from playing well

Initial continuity pass revised one Ch0 line from `personal energy economy` to `personal fuel system`. A later metaphor pass reversed that simplification because it papered over a useful distinction.

Current doctrine:

- **Personal energy economy** is Ch0's player-facing stamina meter: what the move costs you.
- **Energy ecology** is Ch2's living-field frame: what the move does to the system over time.
- The bridge is a farming game: swinging the axe, watering the field, and entering the mine all cost energy, but the point is not infinite stamina. The point is spending stamina in ways that make the farm more alive.

Ch1 was otherwise aligned with Ch2, especially:

- burnout as depletion but not cause
- cost as gate consequence
- durable capacity
- pattern cost
- charge becoming usable when met

One Ch1 line still used capital-T `Transcend` in Gate 2. Because this can reintroduce old move vocabulary, it was revised to ordinary prose:

> the feeling that was waiting underneath can finally complete.
