# SPEC — Ch2 Energy Ecology Replacement

## Problem Statement

Ch2 currently contains an "Energy Economy" section built around:

- `Transcend: Complete Within a Channel (+2 Energy)`
- `Generative Translate: Move Along the Flow Cycle (+1 Energy)`
- `Control Translate: Precision Pivot (-1 Energy)`

This model has already been rejected by author decision in `AD-2026-0522-001_ENERGY_ECONOMY.md`.

The rejected part is the numeric scoring frame and the current move vocabulary. The approved part is the underlying insight:

> Allyship has an energy ecology.

Later continuity decision: `personal energy economy` remains valid as the Ch0 player-facing stamina meter. `Energy ecology` is the Ch2 field-level stewardship frame. Economy tells the player what they can afford to spend; ecology tells them what the spending does to the living field.

Ch2 needs to explain why Emotional Alchemy makes allyship sustainable without asking the reader to calculate points, yields, or penalties in a charged room.

## Governing Decision

From `AD-2026-0522-001_ENERGY_ECONOMY.md`:

- **Keep:** allyship has an energy ecology
- **Keep:** generative vs. depleting allyship moves
- **Keep:** practical application: knowing when to step in or step out
- **Keep:** "what is this costing me?" as the core self-awareness question
- **Reject:** `+2/+1/-1`
- **Reject:** current move names as written
- **Reject:** "Transcend" as a reader-facing move name in this section
- **Reject:** "Control" as a negative category

## Replacement Frame

Use energy **ecology** for the Ch2 field-level frame. Do not use the rejected Ch2 **energy economy** scoring model.

Ch0 can still use **personal energy economy** as the stamina-meter metaphor: the player-facing measure of what a move costs you. The bridge is a farming game: swinging the axe, watering the field, and walking into the mine all cost energy, but the point of the game is not infinite stamina. The point is spending stamina in ways that help the farm become more alive.

The old model asks:

> How many points did this move cost or generate?

The replacement asks:

> What did this move do to my capacity to keep showing up truthfully?

This keeps the practical discernment without turning the reader into an accountant during a charged moment.

Governing distinction:

> Personal energy economy is the meter. Energy ecology is the farm. Mastery is learning how to spend the meter in ways that make the farm more alive.

## Proposed Three-Part Ecology

### 1. Replenishing Moves

Definition:

> A replenishing move costs effort while you make it, but returns more life, clarity, agency, or truth than it spends.

Examples:

- naming the real fear and realizing what matters
- grieving what was lost and feeling love return under the grief
- speaking a clean boundary and walking away tired but clear
- letting joy become guidance instead of fuel for more output
- finding the whole view and feeling the room become less impossible

Reader-facing test:

> After the move, are you tired but more yourself?

### 2. Sustaining Moves

Definition:

> A sustaining move does not necessarily give you a rush of new energy. It keeps the field workable and protects your capacity to remain present.

Examples:

- pausing before responding
- naming one real risk
- asking for one needed condition
- choosing the smaller honest move instead of the heroic one
- holding enough structure for the feeling to teach without flooding the room

Reader-facing test:

> After the move, is there more room to continue?

### 3. Costly Moves

Definition:

> A costly move spends real capacity. Sometimes it is still the right move. The danger is pretending costly moves are renewable.

Examples:

- staying in a hard conversation longer than you wanted because the relationship matters
- softening anger into risk assessment so the truth can land
- turning grief into protective action before you feel ready
- giving care when you are not fully resourced but the moment genuinely needs you

Reader-facing test:

> If this move costs me, what will restore me, and is the cost mine to spend?

Important nuance:

Costly is not bad. Costly without restoration is the burnout path.

## Ch0 Continuity

This replacement should harmonize with Ch0's token/fuel language:

- non-renewable fuel: guilt, obligation, optics, inherited debt
- renewable fuel: care, sustainable joy, earned capacity, real connection
- practice replenishes as it costs
- performance is high cost, low payout, no residue
- real ticket: return of capacity, agency, truth, or aliveness

Ch2 should not re-explain the full Ch0 token system. It should show how Emotional Alchemy teaches the reader to recognize what kind of fuel a move is drawing from.

## Candidate Replacement Section

Replace:

> ## The Energy Economy: Why Some Moves Drain You and Others Fuel You

with:

> ## The Energy Ecology: Why Some Moves Restore You and Others Hollow You Out
>
> The WAVE-Spiral does not make every move easy. That is not the promise.
>
> The promise is discernment.
>
> Some moves replenish as they cost. You draw the boundary, and you walk away tired but clear. You name the grief, and the grief gives you back the love underneath it. You say the thing that needed saying, and something in you comes back online because you stopped abandoning yourself.
>
> Some moves sustain. They do not refill you all at once, but they keep the field workable. A pause. A clean question. One honest sentence instead of the whole speech. Enough structure for the feeling to teach without flooding the room.
>
> And some moves cost. Sometimes they are still the right moves. Staying in the room may cost you. Softening anger so the truth can land may cost you. Turning grief into protective action before you feel ready may cost you. The danger is not cost. The danger is pretending costly moves are renewable.
>
> This is where a lot of allyship burns people out. They keep spending non-renewable fuel — guilt, obligation, optics, inherited debt — and calling the spending virtue. They confuse depletion with devotion.
>
> Emotional Alchemy gives you a different test:
>
> - Did this move return capacity, agency, truth, or aliveness?
> - Did it keep the field workable enough for the next honest move?
> - Or did it hollow you out while asking you to call the hollowing care?
>
> You do not need to score the move. You need to tell the truth about what it did to your capacity to keep showing up truthfully.
