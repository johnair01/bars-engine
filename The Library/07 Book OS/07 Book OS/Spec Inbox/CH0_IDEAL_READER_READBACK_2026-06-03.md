# Ch0 Ideal Reader Readback — 2026-06-03

## Summary Verdict

Ch0 now has the right load-bearing architecture: confession, advanced allyship trap, allyship definition, myths, self-sabotage beliefs, resistance to well-being, game frame, Game Master frame, and BAR exit-door.

The suspicious ideal reader is likely to continue into Ch1, but the chapter is not yet structurally landed. The main issue is not missing ideas. The issue is proportion and sequence. Ch0 currently earns trust strongly in the opening and definition/myths section, then spends a long middle stretch explaining the game systems before returning to embodied action at the end.

The chapter passes the off-ramp test after the BAR door. It partially passes the trust/sequence test. It still needs a compression and embodiment pass before acceptance.

## Updated Frame — Ch0 As Tutorial Level

Do not treat the middle-third issue as simply "cut the onboarding." Ch0 is the onboarding for a game. The reader needs to learn the controls: Token, Ticket, Three Games, Six Faces, character sheet, and BAR.

The problem is not that onboarding exists. The problem is that some onboarding currently feels like reading a game manual instead of playing the tutorial.

The next edit should test whether the systems can be taught through one live issue: the allyship knot that drew the reader to buy this book.

The tutorial object:

> Before we go further, bring one thing with you.
>
> Not the abstract reason you care about allyship. The actual situation that made this book feel necessary.
>
> A relationship you do not know how to repair. A room you keep trying to hold. A person you want to help but keep exhausting yourself around. A conflict where every available move feels wrong. A pattern in your own care that you are tired of repeating.
>
> Choose one.
>
> You do not have to solve it here. You do not even have to understand it yet. Just keep it nearby.
>
> We are going to use it to teach you the controls.

This creates the chapter spine:

**bring the thing -> learn the controls -> make the first move**

If this works, Ch0 can keep more onboarding because the reader is participating in the onboarding. If it does not work for a specific system, that system may be premature or too detailed for Ch0.

## First-Body Reaction

The reader softens early because the opening confession is specific, costly, and non-performative. "I was writing a book about allyship, and it was making me a worse ally" earns immediate trust because it does not posture.

She tightens at any sentence that sounds like theory arriving too soon after recognition. The chapter works best when it names her lived experience first, then gives the framework second.

She laughs or half-laughs at the lines that turn allyship pieties sideways: the summons to court, the debt with no receipt, pressure in a nicer outfit, the game knowing the difference between reading rules and making a move.

She gets most curious when the chapter names the private bargain underneath allyship: using care to answer "Am I good enough? Do I belong? Do I matter?"

She is most likely to drift in the Token/Ticket/Three Games middle, not because the material is bad, but because the embodied recognition drops and the explanatory load rises.

- "I was writing a book about allyship, and it was making me a worse ally."
- "You are burned out from trying to retire a debt that was never meant to be paid off."
- "The other person stops being a person and becomes evidence in the case you are trying to win against yourself."
- "Henchmanship is not allyship."
- "Saviorism protects my usefulness."
- "People resist their own well-being."
- "Pressure in a nicer outfit."
- "Did the seeing start a move, or did it end one?"
- "The game knows the difference between reading the rules and making a move."

Where prose becomes easier to reject:

- The lineage paragraph cluster can feel like sources arriving to certify the argument after the reader already understands it.
- The Token/Ticket middle section risks over-teaching.
- The Three Games section is conceptually useful but under-embodied relative to its size.
- The Six Faces orientation may be too much map before the Forest.

## Top 3 Editorial Moves Before Ch0 Acceptance

1. **Make the conceptual middle playable before cutting.**
   Treat Token, Ticket, Three Games, and Six Faces as tutorial controls applied to the issue that brought the reader to the book. Cut only what remains unnecessary after the reader has been invited to use the system.

2. **Add embodied examples to the two sharpest claims.**
   The claims "people resist their own well-being" and "henchmanship is not allyship" both deserve one concrete image or moment.

3. **Consolidate the game-frame repetition.**
   The chapter currently explains why a game in multiple places. Preserve the strongest version: game as healthy distance for the over-identified and healthy re-engagement for the dissociated.

## Tutorial Micro-Move Candidates

These are not all required. Use only the ones that create momentum without making the chapter feel like a worksheet.

### Debt Frame

Ask:

> What is the debt you have been trying to retire through this situation?

Purpose: make the unpayable debt personal.

### Myths / Self-Sabotage Beliefs

Ask:

> Which question is hiding inside this issue?
>
> Am I good enough? Do I matter? Am I worthy of belonging here? Am I ready? Am I capable? If I get this wrong, will I be cast out?

Purpose: connect the live issue to the private bargain underneath allyship.

### Henchmanship

Ask:

> Where have you stopped disagreeing because being useful felt safer than being honest?

Purpose: make henchmanship embodied rather than theoretical.

### Resistance To Well-Being

Ask:

> What would get harder if this actually got better?

Purpose: show resistance as protection, not pathology.

### Token System

Ask:

> What did this issue cost you: guilt, obligation, fear, love, clarity, anger, devotion, joy, time?

Purpose: make tokens felt as currency.

### Ticket System

Ask:

> After engaging this issue, did you have more life available or less?

Purpose: make ticket/payout immediate.

### Three Games

Ask:

> In this issue, what part was Chance? What part was Skill? What part was Passion?

Purpose: teach the game types by applying them to the same live issue.

### Six Faces

Ask:

> Which Face showed up first: the one that felt, the one that drew the line, the one that protected what mattered, the one that designed the system, the one that held the field, or the one that saw the whole game?

Purpose: introduce the Six Faces as playable capacities, not just a map.

### First BAR

The final BAR should become the culmination:

> Now capture the moment you have been circling all chapter.

Purpose: turn the tutorial object into the first saved move.
