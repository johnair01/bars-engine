# Ch2-Ch4 Ecology Sequence Readback

## Scope

Readback sequence:

- Ch2: `The Energy Ecology`
- Ch3: `The Will Ecology`
- Ch4: `The Stewardship Ecology`

Question:

> Does the ecology language feel like one doctrine deepening across faces, or like three new systems piling up?

## Verdict

The sequence works, with one naming boundary.

The underlying doctrine is coherent:

- Ch0 teaches the meter: what a move costs the player.
- Ch2 teaches the living field: what the move does to capacity, agency, truth, and aliveness.
- Ch3 localizes the doctrine to fire/will: did the charge become a line, or did it become smoke?
- Ch4 localizes the doctrine to loyalty/stewardship: does the carrying make the inheritance more alive, or does it preserve the shell while draining the person?

This progression feels intentional, not patched.

## Naming Risk

`Energy Ecology` should remain the master term.

`Will Ecology` and `Stewardship Ecology` work as local section titles, but they should not become appendix-level key terms yet. Adding them to Appendix C would make the reader feel like every chapter is minting a new system. The face-specific titles are useful as chapter-local applications of the same doctrine, not durable independent frameworks.

Appendix C is currently correct:

- includes `Personal Energy Economy`
- includes `Energy Ecology`
- does not include `Will Ecology`
- does not include `Stewardship Ecology`

## Reader Burden

The risk is not the concept. The risk is naming density.

The repeated opening line in Ch2/Ch3/Ch4 helps:

> Chapter 0 taught you to read the meter...

That continuity makes the sequence feel like an upgrade path rather than a pile of models. The strongest protection is to keep the face-specific question concrete:

- Ch2: what did this move do to my capacity to keep showing up truthfully?
- Ch3: did this charge become a line, or smoke?
- Ch4: did this way of carrying make the inheritance more alive?

The more the prose stays in those questions, the less the reader will collect the headings as systems.

## Recommendation

Keep current Ch2/Ch3/Ch4 section titles for now.

Do not add `Will Ecology` or `Stewardship Ecology` to Appendix C unless later chapters also require durable face-specific ecology terms.

Next pass should check Ch5-Ch8 for old numeric energy language and decide whether each chapter needs:

- no ecology section at all
- a local face-specific ecology section
- or a lighter callback to the Ch2 master term
