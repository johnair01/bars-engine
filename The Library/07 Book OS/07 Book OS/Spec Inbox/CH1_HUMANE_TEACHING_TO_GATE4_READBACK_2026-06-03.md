> Before the map gets bigger, let me tell you how this game teaches.

## Core Question

Does this sequence now feel like the reader is learning to play the book, or does it still feel like reading a manual?

## Verdict

Mostly playable.

The humane teaching/consent frame is doing real work. It tells the reader how to relate to the map before the map expands, and it lowers the inherited school/compliance/court standard. The reader is explicitly told:

- do not memorize everything
- recognition is the first move
- stop when something lands
- one honest move matters more than total understanding

That frame makes the Gates feel more humane. It also makes Gate 2 stronger because the Controller's old standard is now visibly the thing the teaching frame is trying to replace.

## What Is Working

### The Teaching Frame Works

The game pedagogy is clear:

- map
- move
- feedback
- try again with better information

This is a strong alternative to pedagogies based on compliance, correctness, and avoiding shame.

### The Consent Frame Works

The passage gives the reader permission to encounter the Gates without having to hold the whole system at once. This is good for the suspicious ideal reader because it lowers performance pressure while keeping agency intact.

### Gate 1 Feels Like Practice

Protector works well as the first playable encounter. It has:

- common trigger
- gift
- cost
- repurpose move
- worked example
- app prompt

The structure is manual-like on the surface, but the content is active enough to feel usable.

### Gate 2 Now Has the Right Center

Gate 2 is stronger after the Controller alignment. The old standard from the teaching frame reappears inside the Gate itself:

> understand the map, feel the correct thing, perform the journey properly...

This makes the Gates sequence feel integrated rather than bolted on.

### Gate 3 Transitions Cleanly

Gate 3 now follows Gate 2 coherently:

- Gate 2 updates the standard.
- Gate 3 asks whether the new standard can survive evidence.

The Skeptic's gift as discernment makes sense after the Controller's gift as standard-setting.

### Gate 4 Lands As Allyship-Specific

Gate 4 is the first place where the reader probably feels, "Oh, this is why the book is about allyship." The Fixer/Healer distinction between fixing persons and fixing circumstances is clear, useful, and morally important.

## Main Risk

The transition into the Gates preview still briefly turns into system-explanation mode.

The issue is not the eight-item list itself. The issue is that the sequence goes:

1. humane tutorial frame
2. lineage/provenance paragraph
3. conceptual explanation of Gates
4. eight-item preview
5. deep dives

This briefly makes the reader leave the game and listen to the rules again.

The Genpo/Big Mind provenance matters, but this placement may be doing academic/integrity work before the reader has fully entered the playable sequence.

## Specific Friction

This paragraph is important but may be too explanatory in this exact spot:

> The gate architecture comes from Genpo Roshi (Dennis Genpo Merzel), a Zen teacher who developed the Big Mind Process...

It demonstrates integrity, but it interrupts the tutorial momentum. It may be better later in the chapter, in a source note, or shortened into a lighter provenance line here with fuller acknowledgement elsewhere.

## Possible Future Move

Keep the provenance but make the transition more playable.

Candidate principle:

> Give the reader the lineage without making them put down the controller.

Possible light revision strategy:

1. Replace the long provenance paragraph with a short acknowledgement.
2. Move fuller Genpo/Big Mind context into a note, source section, or later chapter note.
3. Add one sentence that keeps the reader inside the live issue they brought into the chapter.

Example direction, not final prose:

> These Gates adapt Genpo Roshi's Big Mind insight that inner voices become workable when you meet them directly instead of trying to exile them. For now, do not study the Gates. Walk them with the issue you brought.

## Compression Decision

Do not compress the Gates deep dives yet.

The playable structure is functioning:

- Gate name
- trigger
- gift
- cost
- repurpose move
- worked example
- app prompt

Compression, if needed, should start at the transition into the preview, not inside Gates 1-4.

## Recommendation

Next best edit:

Make a small transition/provenance adjustment before `Here is the preview map:` so the reader stays in the game while receiving the lineage.

This is a trust edit, not a compression edit:

- Capability: keep the reader oriented toward practice.
- Integrity: preserve source acknowledgement.
- Benevolence: do not make the suspicious reader feel like they have been handed a doctrine to learn.
