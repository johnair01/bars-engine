# SPEC: Council of Joanne's Punch-Up Engine

## Purpose

Use the Council of Joanne's as an **author-side antagonist system** for sharpening manuscript prose.

The Council should help the editor identify where the prose is too polite, too abstract, too soft, or too evasive about the social forces the ideal reader is actually trying to survive.

The Council does **not** need to be named in the manuscript. The reader should feel the antagonist force; the text does not need to explain the author-side machine.

---

## Rule

The Council is a diagnostic and punch-up engine, not a character system.

Use it to ask:

- What voice is the reader still trying to impress?
- What progressive rule is being treated as moral law?
- Where has care become control?
- Where has safety become surveillance?
- Where has accountability become a ritual of permanent insufficiency?
- Where is the reader obeying the Council instead of playing the game?

Then sharpen the passage in Wendell's voice.

---

## What Not To Do

- Do not name "the Council of Joanne's" in manuscript prose by default.
- Do not turn them into villains who need a backstory.
- Do not caricature Black lesbians, progressive women, movement elders, or harmed people.
- Do not make jokes at the expense of actual vulnerability.
- Do not use the Council to dodge the author's own complicity.

The Council is funny because the system is absurd, not because the people are disposable.

---

## Registers

### Clown

Use when the reader is implicated but still tender.

Function: "Oh no, I recognize that."

Example move:

> You built a whole life around never stepping on toes, which is a strange way to learn dancing and an excellent way to become furniture.

### Cult Leader

Use when the rule system itself needs to be exposed.

Function: "Oh no, we learned this somewhere."

Example move:

> The rule sounded holy because it was impossible to satisfy. That is how you knew it belonged to the Council.

### Jerk

Use when the passage has earned a hard cut.

Function: cathartic truth, cleanly aimed.

Wendell decision, 2026-06-02: **the Jerk can be very sharp when earned.** Do not pre-soften. Wendell will flag anything that goes too far.

Example move:

> Correctness without care is cruelty with paperwork.

## Punch-Up Procedure

For each candidate passage:

1. Identify the Council rule underneath the passage.
2. Identify the reader's likely shame-valenced state.
3. Choose Clown, Cult Leader, Jerk, or self-immolation.
4. Draft one sharp line.
5. Check whether the line:
   - metabolizes shame rather than adding it
   - leaves the raw signal intact
   - points at behavior/system rather than identity
   - sounds like Wendell, not a comedian hired after the fact
6. Mark risk:
   - Low: safe punch-up
   - Medium: Wendell voice check
   - High: trauma/community/cultural risk
   - Very high: author self-immolation only

---

## Approval Rule

Council-driven punch-up lines are proposal-only until Wendell approves them.

High and very-high risk lines require explicit approval before manuscript insertion.
