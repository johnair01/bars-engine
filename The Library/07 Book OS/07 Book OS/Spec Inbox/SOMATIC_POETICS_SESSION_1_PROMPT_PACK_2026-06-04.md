# Somatic Poetics — Session 1 Prompt Pack

**Purpose:** capture Wendell's living body voice for the manuscript without collapsing it into teaching language.

**Working principle:** evoke body awareness, do not prescribe it. The best material will feel like a true first-person body report before it feels like a lesson.

---

## Before You Start

Do the full `321` practice first.

Then come back and do not try to be elegant. We want the body before the explanation.

If a line starts sounding like:
- a workshop prompt,
- a therapeutic instruction,
- or a framework summary,

mark it as drift and keep going until the voice feels alive again.

---

## What We Need

1. Five felt-body channel descriptions
2. Two van-tweet moments
3. A few “do not say it this way” notes
4. Editorial placement notes for the manuscript

For each channel, aim for:
- 3 to 5 first-person sentences for the body state
- 3 short van-tweet-style sentences
- 1 placement note

The job is not to explain the emotion. The job is to describe what the body did before meaning arrived.

---

## Channel 1 — Metal / Fear

### Prompt
Think of the first signal fear gives you before it becomes a story.

What happens in the body first?
- Where does it show up?
- What does it do to breath, jaw, hands, eyes, stomach, or spine?
- What does fear make you want to do immediately?
- What does it feel like when you know something is off before you can justify that knowledge?

### Good material sounds like
- a warning signal
- a tightening, narrowing, bracing, or delay
- a body that has already started moving before the mind catches up

### Avoid
- “Fear means…”
- “This is because…”
- any explanation that turns sensation into a framework

### Placement note
Which manuscript passage should this anchor?

## Channel 2 — Water / Sadness

### Prompt
Describe sadness as a pace, a weight, a texture, or a weather system.

What does sadness do to:
- gravity,
- energy,
- breath,
- attention,
- and the speed of thought?

What does sadness feel like when it is not dramatized?

### Good material sounds like
- heavy, slow, wet, dim, distant, cooling
- a body that is moving through something, not performing it

### Avoid
- translating sadness into wisdom too quickly
- turning the paragraph into grief theory
- making it sound noble on purpose

### Placement note
Which manuscript passage should this anchor?

## Channel 3 — Wood / Joy

### Prompt

What does joy do to:
- posture,

- playfulness,
- appetite,
- invention?

What does joy feel like before it becomes productivity or meaning?

### Good material sounds like
- a spark,
- forward motion,
- curiosity with force behind it,
- the body choosing contact with life

### Avoid

- softening it into positivity
- protecting it from being seen

## Channel 5 — Earth / Neutrality

## Van-Tweet Moment 1 — Fear / Sadness Axis

### Prompt
Write one real moment where the feeling arrived before the meaning.

Start with the body, not the lesson.

Possible entry points:
- the phone in your hand
- the room after the message
- the moment your hands started doing something on their own
- the second you knew you were not okay before you had words for it

### Requirements
- 3 to 8 sentences
- first person
- unpolished
- specific
- no summary at the end

### Good material sounds like
- “The phone was still warm and I set it down and my hands shake.”
- “I knew I was in trouble before I knew what trouble was.”

### Avoid
- “What I learned was…”
- “This made me realize…”
- any tidy moral closure

---

## Van-Tweet Moment 2 — Joy / Anger Axis

### Prompt
Write one specific moment where feeling became action before it became a story.

Pick **one** of these:
- a moment where anger became a clean line
- a moment where joy made something possible
- a moment where the body moved before the identity did

Choose a scene with one clear setting, one clear body signal, and one clear action.

### Requirements
- 3 to 8 sentences
- first person
- alive before polished
- no explanation sentence at the end

### Good material sounds like
- body in motion
- decision before doctrine
- “I moved before I knew I was moving”
- the action lands before the meaning does

### Avoid
- making it instructional
- explaining the mechanism too soon
- sounding like you are writing after the fact for a class
- choosing a general emotion instead of a specific event
- choosing a concept instead of a scene
- ending with “what this means is…”

### Sample scene from Wendell

The beginning of *Mastering the Game of Allyship* was when George Floyd died. A bunch of my friends reached out to see if I was okay. I could feel my gut tightening and my jaw clenching every time the well-wishes came in. I wanted to lash out, but what kind of monster lashes out when people are trying to care? Still, there was energy there I didn’t have a name for yet. Later I recognized it as anger, specifically anger at the injustice of having to hold space for other people’s feelings about something that should have been mine alone to carry.

### Why this works
- specific scene
- body signal first
- social pressure is visible
- anger is recognized later
- no tidy moral ending

## Do Not Say It This Way

Use this section to catch drift.

If a sentence sounds like:
- “The reader should…”
- “This means…”
- “What you want to feel is…”
- “This is important because…”
- “The body learns…”

mark it and try again.

The best sentence should feel like it came from inside the moment, not from outside the moment.

---

## Output Format

When the session is done, return the material in this order:

1. Channel 1 through 5 body-state descriptions
2. Van-Tweet Moment 1
3. Van-Tweet Moment 2
4. Placement notes
5. Drift notes

Keep the raw voice intact. We can shape it later.
