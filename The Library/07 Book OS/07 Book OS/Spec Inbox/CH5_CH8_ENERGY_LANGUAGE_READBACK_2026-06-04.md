# Ch5-Ch8 Energy Language Readback

## Readback Question

After the Ch5-Ch8 sweep, does the revised language feel native to each chapter, or does it merely rename the old numeric energy economy?

## Verdict

The sweep works. The old formal mechanic is no longer visible in live Ch5-Ch8, and the replacement language mostly lands as native prose.

The strongest decision was not forcing every later chapter to carry a local ecology title. Ch5-Ch8 are later-game chapters; they need capacity, alchemy, field, and design language more than another system label. This keeps **Energy Ecology** as the master term without making every chapter explain it again.

## Chapter Readback

### Ch5: Architect

**Verdict: stable.**

The change from **Transcend Outcome** to **Integrated Outcome** works because Architect is about design logic, system clarity, and integration. The phrase is a little abstract, but abstraction is native to Architect and the table immediately grounds it in concrete modes.

The follow-up cleanup from **transcend move / transcend is** to **integrated move / integrated outcome** improved continuity. Ch5 now speaks in the same logic as the table.

No further immediate prose work needed.

### Ch6: Diplomat

**Verdict: conceptually strong, rhythmically watch.**

The move from **Transcend 1/2** to **Alchemy 1/2** lands well. Diplomat is already relational and process-oriented, so "alchemy" feels more human than "transcend." It also keeps the emotional alchemy architecture without sounding like a scoreboard.

The replacement of numeric energy costs with **sustaining move / costly move / capacity cost** preserves the real stakes. This matters because Diplomat must not become "be endlessly relational for free." The chapter now says: staying, translating, repairing, and closing are real costs, and naming the cost is part of integrity.

Potential later polish: **This is a costly move** repeats enough that it could start sounding like a template. The repetition is acceptable for now because it teaches the frame, but a later prose pass could vary the rhythm:

- "This spends real capacity..."
- "The cost here is..."
- "This asks more of the player..."
- "The price is not a penalty; it is the honesty that keeps the move clean."

No structural rewrite needed.

### Ch7: Sage

**Verdict: stable, and the magic survives.**

Changing **Transcend Move 1-5** to **Alchemy Move 1-5** avoids reinstalling the old mechanic. The body prose still carries the Sage altitude: committed seeing, returning, naming, and not using perspective as escape.

Important distinction: Ch7 should be allowed to keep conceptual words like **transcendence**, **transcending while including**, and **transcendence-as-escape** when they are about altitude and Wilber/integral meaning. Those are not the old energy economy mechanic. They are part of Sage's native vocabulary.

No further immediate prose work needed.

### Ch8: Player

**Verdict: leave clean.**

Ch8 did not need explicit energy-ecology language. The chapter already resolves the sequence by moving from map to design, from preparation to contact, and from identity to practice.

Adding an energy-ecology bridge here would likely over-explain. Ch8's native language is game design, practice, building, passing on, and contact with someone real. It already carries the cost frame through sentences like: "The practice is the thing you do when it costs you something to do it."

No further immediate prose work needed.

## Acceptance Notes

The live Ch5-Ch8 surfaces no longer contain old formal mechanic phrases:

- `Transcend Outcome`
- `Transcend Move`
- `[DISSATISFACTION → SATISFACTION] Transcend`
- `The Transcend`
- `Transcend alchemy`
- `Energy cost`
- `Energy economy`
- `generative translate`
- `control translate`

Remaining lower-case uses of **transcendence/transcending/transcend** in Ch7 are intentional Sage/integral vocabulary, not energy-economy residue.

## Recommended Next Move

Do not keep editing Ch5-Ch8 yet. The terminology continuity pass is complete.
