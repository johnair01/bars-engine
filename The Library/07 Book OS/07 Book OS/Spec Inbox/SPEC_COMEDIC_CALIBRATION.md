## What This Is

The operational bridge between the wound map and the manuscript chapters. The 7 wounds are mapped. The 3 comic archetypes are defined. The shame parasite ontology is established. This spec answers: *how do we deploy each wound into each chapter so comedy becomes systemic, not ornamental?*

---

## Core Principle: Wounds Appear Across Chapters, Not One-to-One

A wound is not a chapter assignment. A wound is a subsurface tension that appears at every altitude — it just looks different each time.

Microaggressions (Wound 1) in the Forest (Ch1, Shaman altitude): you just realized you might be the monster. Microaggressions in the Regent's court (Ch4): you're defending the institution that produced them. Microaggressions in the Player's playground (Ch8): you're free enough to laugh at how seriously you used to take them.

Same wound, three different comic moves.

## How the Comic Variation Works

Each wound's primary archetype is fixed — but the **tell** changes with altitude:

| Archetype | Low Altitude (Ch0–Ch2) | Mid Altitude (Ch3–Ch6) | High Altitude (Ch7–Ch8) |
|-----------|----------------------|----------------------|------------------------|
| **Clown** | The reader IS the clown — naive, well-meaning, in over their head | The clown is looking back at who they were — recognition without cruelty | The clown has integrated — laugh at yourself, not at your past self |
| **Cult Leader** | The external voice — the news anchor in their head, the rules they inherited | The internalized voice — "I enforced these rules myself" | The voice named and released — "I hear it, I don't obey it" |
| **Jerk** | Rarely deployed — the reader isn't ready to laugh at others yet | Targeted at abusers and institutional forces — cathartic release | Targeted at self — the Jerk that bites inward, the freedom to call your own bullshit |

## What the Pass Actually Does

The Comedic Calibration pass is an editorial pass that:

1. **Reads each chapter draft** against this grid
2. **For each wound that is subsurface in that chapter:** locates the passage where it's alive but unjoked
3. **Drafts 1-3 comic lines** in the appropriate archetype(s) with the correct-altitude tell
4. **Checks against the Council depth threshold:** funny → freeing, not funny → cruel, too funny → shallow
5. **Produces a calibration report:** which wounds landed, which need Wendell's rewrite, which were too hot to touch

**The agent can do steps 1-4.** Step 5 requires Wendell's judgment — specifically on Wounds 6 and 7, where the depth is deepest and the agent is most likely to miss.

---

## Editorial Placement Rules

- **Wounds deploy at Gates, not in exposition.** The joke lands when the reader is already metabolizing the framework — never during teaching.
- **Wound 7 (Self-Immolation) deploys ONLY in Ch8.** The reader can't approach their own harm-doing until they've been through all 7 gates. Deploying it earlier is cruelty.
- **Wound 4 (Family cut-off) deploys in Ch2 and Ch6.** Different tell each time — somatic in Ch2, relational in Ch6.
- **Wound 6 (Community abuse) spans Ch2, Ch4, Ch6, and Ch8.** It's the longest arc because it's the meta-wound.
