# Polarity Teaching Grammar — MTGOA

**Wendell rule (2026-05-25):** Polarity labels must be **neutral**. Neither pole is "the good one." Each pole has **healthy** expressions and **unhealthy (shadow)** expressions. **Unhealthy overreach on one pole necessitates the healthy version of the other.**

**Book job:** Pull the **healthy aspects** out of allyship **and** name the **shadowy ways** people practice unhealthy allyship — without collapsing into hero/villain poles.

**Aligns with:** FSR Polarity Rules (`../FSR/Polarity Rules.md`), Barry Johnson polarity maps (upsides of both; downsides of over-focus).

**Does not align with:** Moral binaries, "care = bad / impact = good," or copy that treats one pole as the destination and the other as mistake.

---

## 1. Structure of a taught polarity

Every governing polarity in MTGOA (book, chapter, craft) should be documentable as:

```
Pole A ↔ Pole B          (neutral names — peer capacities)

Pole A — healthy gift    |  Pole B — healthy gift
Pole A — shadow/overreach |  Pole B — shadow/overreach
```

**Teaching move:** When reader (or ally) is stuck in **A-shadow**, the chapter/card/book asks for **B-healthy** — not "become a B person."

**Johnson map (optional copy layer):**

| Quadrant | Content |
|----------|---------|
| Upside of A | Healthy A |
| Upside of B | Healthy B |
| Downside of over-A | A-shadow |
| Downside of over-B | B-shadow |

---

## 2. What failed in early framing

| Bad framing | Why it fails Wendell's rule |
|-------------|----------------------------|
| Practice = good, Performance = bad | Moralizes poles; performance has healthy forms (real showing up) |
| Forest = good, Village = bad | Village is where allyship **lives**; forest hoarding is shadow |
| Care = bad, Impact = good | Care has healthy forms; impact has shadow (savior sprint) |
| "Stop performing, start practicing" | Collapses to one-sided advice |

**Fix:** Name **shadow forms** explicitly on **each** pole. Progress = **rhythm between healthy A and healthy B**, not permanent residence on one side.

---

## 3. Book overarch — revised model

**Working pair label:** **Performance ↔ Practice** — *labels under review; poles must read neutral in copy.*

| | **Performance** | **Practice** |
|---|-----------------|--------------|
| **Healthy gift** | Visible enactment; showing up where it costs; public courage; moves others can see and trust | Interior preparation; evidenced capacity; BAR truth; sustainable rhythm; knowing your gates before you act |
| **Shadow / overreach** | Optics, credentials, busy avoidance, unwinnable game, debt discharge, choir preaching, stress-as-OK | Infinite interior loop, forest hoarding, analysis without enactment, "still doing my work" as excuse for no impact |
| **When A-shadow shows up, invite…** | → **Healthy Practice** (pause, gate walk, somatic read, evidence) | → **Healthy Performance** (village move, domain action, leveraged show-up) |

**Book goal (Wendell's words):** Pull healthy allyship forward **and** illuminate shadow allyship — on **both** sides of the pair.

**One sentence (revised):** The book teaches you to **recognize when you're in the shadow of one pole** and **return to the healthy expression of the other** — until you can hold **healthy Performance and healthy Practice** in rhythm.

*Alternate pole labels welcome if Performance/Practice still sound moralized in player copy.*

## 4. Ch1 — Forest ↔ Village (neutral model)

| | **Forest** | **Village** |
|---|------------|-------------|
| **Healthy gift** | Inner learning; parts; gate walk; finding center; somatic truth before strategy | Application; real others; allyship moves; gift to collective; impact in relationship |
| **Shadow / overreach** | Endless inner work; forest tourism; credential interiority; no return | Village-first performance; fix the world without mapping self; optics; burnout sprint |
| **When shadow… invite…** | Forest-shadow → healthy **Village** (one enacted move with what you learned) | Village-shadow → healthy **Forest** (pause, name gate, walk toward center) |

**Ch1 teaches sequencing**, not forest supremacy: unhealthy village-first is the **entry shadow**; unhealthy forest-hoarding is the **exit shadow** to name before Ch2.

## 5. Rules for chapter polarities (Ch2–Ch7)

Before logging any **GP-A-ChN**:

1. **Neutral pole names** — peer grammar, no hero/villain
2. **Healthy + shadow** on **both** poles documented
3. **Milestone BAR** proves **healthy integration**, not one pole victory
4. **Card rejection test** — reject cards that only affirm one **healthy** pole while ignoring shadow risk on the other
5. **Hexagram Task 2** — may name shadow on one pole and invite healthy opposite

**Integral design session must produce the 2×2 table** before pair is validated.

## 7. Copy lint (facilitator / editor)

| Fail | Pass |
|------|------|
| "Stop performing" | "Notice performance-shadow; what healthy practice is asking for?" |
| "Leave the forest and finally act" | "Return to the village with what the forest gave you" |
