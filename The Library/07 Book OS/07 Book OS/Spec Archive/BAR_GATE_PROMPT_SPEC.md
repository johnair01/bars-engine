# BAR Gate Prompt Spec — Writing the 8 Gate Captures
**Purpose:** Spec the writing problem and ideal reader goal for every gate-embedded BAR prompt. Piloted on Ch2 (Shaman). Template applies to all chapters.

---

## The Writing Problem

Each chapter has an 8 Gates walk. Each Gate section closes with some version of "When you're ready, keep walking." The reader moves through 8 encounters with interior voices, arrives at the Vulnerable Child, and then continues to the chapter's Game section.

**The problem:** The reader — especially the ideal reader — will interpret each gate encounter immediately. She will turn the felt-sense into insight before it has a body. By the time she reaches Gate 4, she has already processed Gates 1–3 into meaning and forgotten what actually happened in her chest when she said "thank you" to the Protector.

**The BAR prompt's job:** Interrupt the interpretation reflex. Ask for the felt-sense before the story about it. Get the body-level capture on record — in the app — before the reader moves to the next gate.

This is not journaling. Journaling happens after. This happens between gates.

---

## The Ideal Reader's Default Response (What We're Interrupting)

The ideal reader (Green altitude, E2 femme) is a practiced processor. Her default:
- Feels the gate land
- Identifies it ("oh, that's my Controller")
- Immediately contextualizes it ("this is the same pattern I noticed in therapy last month")
- Files it under a known framework
- Moves on

The BAR prompt does not ask for contextualizing. It asks: *what happened in your body right now, in this chapter, at this gate.* Not the pattern. The instance. The specific heat of this moment, before it becomes an entry in the taxonomy she already has.

The E2 specificity: she will also want to immediately understand what the gate means for *other people* — her clients, her community. The prompt should keep her in first person. This is about her, not her people.

---

## How Hexagram Character Shapes Prompt Voice

Each Gate × Chapter intersection activates a specific hexagram. The hexagram's character does not appear in the prompt explicitly — the reader does not need to know they're activating a hexagram. But the character shapes *what the prompt asks for* and *how*.

**The translation rule:** The hexagram tells you the *quality of movement* at this intersection. Use that quality to name what the reader just encountered.

Example: Gate 8 (Vulnerable Child ☷) × Ch2 (Shaman ☷) = Hexagram 2 (Receptive/Pure Earth). The prompt asks for what *received*, not what was done. "What's alive in you right now" not "what did you learn."

**Ch2 upper trigram = Kun ☷ (Earth/Receptive).** Every gate encounter in the Shaman chapter is colored by Earth. Earth receives, holds, allows things to take root. The Shaman's work is fundamentally receptive — before action, before strategy, before any allyship move, there is the capacity to be affected. Each BAR prompt in this chapter should carry an Earth quality: *what entered, what was received, what took root.*

## Gate-by-Gate Map — Ch2 (Shaman)

For each gate: what the gate does in this chapter, what the ideal reader's default response is, what we want her to capture, the hexagram character, and the draft BAR prompt.

**Placement:** Each BAR prompt goes after the gate's closing line ("When you're ready, keep walking.") and before the `---` section divider. It replaces the divider. The divider appears after the prompt.

---

### Gate 1: Protector ☰ × Shaman ☷ = Hexagram 11 (Tai — Peace)

**What the gate does:** Asks the reader to thank the Protector and request permission to enter. The move is acknowledging the guard without fighting it.

**What the chapter prose asks her to do:** Find the part resisting the exercise. Say thank you. See what happens.

**Ideal reader's default:** Thinks "yes, I know my Protector — it's the perfectionist part that..." and writes a paragraph of self-analysis.

**What we want her to capture:** Did the Protector actually relax? What entered the body when it did? Not the name of her pattern — the physical event of the guard standing down, even briefly.

**Hexagram 11 character:** Heaven and Earth moving toward each other. Maximum exchange. Peace comes from the two moving together, not from one dominating. The thing that enters when the guard drops is as real as the guard. Capture both.

**Draft prompt:**
> *What happened when you said thank you? Not what you thought — what shifted. Something let go, or tightened, or surprised you. That's the one. Open the app. Name it before you walk past it.*

---

### Gate 2: Controller ☱ × Shaman ☷ = Hexagram 19 (Lin — Approach)

**What the gate does:** Asks the reader to notice what belief the Controller has been holding in place (I'm not enough, I'm not ready, I don't belong) and to relieve the Controller — take the lid from them rather than fighting the grip.

**What the chapter prose asks her to do:** Ask the Controller "how's the pay?" — surface its exhaustion. Take the lid.

**Ideal reader's default:** Identifies which of the 6 beliefs is "hers" and explains the childhood origin of it.

**What we want her to capture:** Which belief was the Controller's specific record — and what was trying to come through before the Controller managed it away. The belief is data about history, not capacity. What capacity was about to show up?

**Hexagram 19 character:** Lake rising toward Earth — something powerful approaching the boundary, about to overflow. The approaching thing is real and has force. The prompt asks for what was coming.

**Draft prompt:**
> *Which belief stopped you here? Not the story of where it came from — the belief itself, in its exact words, the way the Controller says it. And then: what was trying to come through before the Controller managed it away? Two captures. One minute each. → app*

---

### Gate 3: Skeptic ☲ × Shaman ☷ = Hexagram 36 (Ming Yi — Darkening of Light)

**What the gate does:** Names the Skeptic's move — turns a real embodied response into "you're probably overreacting." The Skeptic preempts humiliation by doubting first. The move: ask the Skeptic what would happen if you believed yourself.

**Ideal reader's default:** Recognizes the Skeptic as a pattern, notes it intellectually, moves on. Does not actually ask the question.

**What we want her to capture:** What came up when she asked the Skeptic "what would happen if you let me believe myself?" The Skeptic has no good answer. What was in the silence before doubt covered it over? That thing — the knowing that was almost there — is what to capture.

**Hexagram 36 character:** Fire buried below Earth — light obscured. Brilliance suppressed. The intelligence is still there, just underground. The prompt is asking for what was buried, not what replaced it.

**Draft prompt:**
> *What did you almost know before the Skeptic spoke? Not what the Skeptic said — what was underneath it, the thing the Skeptic was preempting. That's the real knowing. It's dim right now. Capture it before it goes darker. → app*

---

### Gate 4: Fixer ☳ × Shaman ☷ = Hexagram 24 (Fu — Return)

**What the gate does:** The Fixer arrives with a clipboard and wants to convert the feeling into a task. The move: tell the Fixer there will be action, but after Gate 8. The Fixer can wait if they have a deadline.

**Ideal reader's default:** Identifies her fixing pattern (probably in relational contexts — fixing other people's feelings). Makes a mental note.

**What we want her to capture:** The feeling that was present before the Fixer arrived. The thing the Fixer was about to convert into a task. The Fixer showed up because something was stirring — something that wanted to move. What was that?

**Hexagram 24 character:** Thunder beneath Earth — the first stirring of new energy underground. The turning point. Spring returning. The capture is about what is returning, not what was being managed.

**Draft prompt:**
> *What were you about to feel when the Fixer showed up? The Fixer doesn't arrive for nothing — there was something real moving. Name what was stirring before the plan arrived. That's the thing worth keeping. → app*

### Gate 5: Victim ☴ × Shaman ☷ = Hexagram 46 (Sheng — Pushing Upward)

**Placement note (Issue 8 CLOSED):** Issue 8 resolved 2026-05-20 — no manuscript swap needed. The two numbering systems are independent: "Gate 5" here means EH trigram position 5 (Victim/Xun ☴), not prose section 5. In Ch2, the Victim content sits at prose section **6** ("Gate 6: The Victim"). This prompt belongs after that section — after the prose labeled "Gate 6: The Victim," before the `---` divider.

**What the gate does (Victim section):** Lets the Victim speak. The move is unusual — not talking TO the part but letting the part talk. Listen without fixing, reframing, or rushing to "survivor." The Victim is right. Something did happen.

**Ideal reader's default:** Will want to quickly move to "but I'm not a victim, I've done the work" — will reframe toward agency before the Victim finishes speaking.

**What we want her to capture:** What the Victim said when she listened. Not her reframe of it — what the Victim actually said. The story the Victim has been holding. This is the circulating story that keeps her above the actual feeling — the BAR is the story itself, captured so it can stop circulating.

**Hexagram 46 character:** Wind beneath Earth — wood growing upward through soil. Gradual, persistent ascent. The story that has been circulating finds its ground when it's named. Capture the story so it can stop moving.

**Draft prompt:**
> *What did the Victim say when you actually listened? Not your reframe of it — what was the story, in their words. Capture it exactly. The story stops running you the moment it's named and set down. → app*

### Gate 6: Emotional Body ☵ × Shaman ☷ = Hexagram 7 (Shi — The Army)

**Placement note (Issue 8 CLOSED):** Issue 8 resolved 2026-05-20 — no manuscript swap needed. "Gate 6" here means EH trigram position 6 (Emotional Body/Kan ☵), not prose section 6. In Ch2, the Emotional Body content sits at prose section **5** ("Gate 5: Fear" / Petty Pendergrass section). This prompt belongs after that section — after the prose labeled "Gate 5: Fear," before the `---` divider.

**What the gate does (Emotional Body section / current "Fear" section):** The gate where the practice deepens. Names Fear as a character — Petty Pendergrass — so it becomes *a part of you* rather than *you.* The move: give the fear a name, a face, a costume. The sillier the better. A named part can be addressed rather than obeyed.

**Ideal reader's default:** Finds the exercise intellectually clever. Names a part. Describes its characteristics in detail. Does not actually feel it.

**What we want her to capture:** The felt-sense of the moment when the fear-part became separate from her — not named, but *distinct.* When did it shift from "this is just me" to "this is Petty"? What happened in her body at that moment?

**Hexagram 7 character:** Water beneath Earth — underground reservoir. Organized, disciplined, held below the surface and ready to be mobilized. The capture is the signal that arrived before she had a name for it.

**Draft prompt:**
> *What did the fear feel like in your body before you named it? Not after — before. The moment you noticed the production value on that movie. And then: what's the name you gave it? Both captures matter. The feeling is the signal. The name is the move. → app*

### Gate 7: Damaged Self ☶ × Shaman ☷ = Hexagram 15 (Qian — Modesty/Humility)

**What the gate does:** The Damaged Self says "I am broken." Has evidence. Cannot be argued with. The move: witness without fixing. Sit down. Stay. Don't say "that's not true" and don't say "that's true." Just keep company in the place where the Damaged Self has been alone.

**Ideal reader's default:** Will feel this one. Her E2 wiring means she has been attending to others' wounds for a long time and not sitting with her own. She will recognize this gate. She may cry. She will likely rush to forgive herself.

**What we want her to capture:** What the Damaged Self believes. Exactly. Not her reframe of it, not her compassion for herself, not the resolution — the belief, as the Damaged Self says it. And: the specific experience of staying. Not what she felt when she forgave herself, but what it was like to just sit there.

**Hexagram 15 character:** Mountain beneath Earth — the mountain leveled, made humble. Not destroyed but integrated. What has absorbed everything and does not announce it. The capture is what has been absorbed in silence.

**Draft prompt:**
> *What does the Damaged Self believe about you? In their exact words. Write it down before you reframe it. And: what was it like to just sit there with them instead of fixing it? Both. → app*

---

### Gate 8: Vulnerable Child ☷ × Shaman ☷ = Hexagram 2 (Kun — The Receptive)

**What the gate does:** The destination. The Vulnerable Child has been waiting behind all seven gates, holding the actual gift. The move is not elaborate — pick them up. Let them climb into your lap. Listen in whatever language they have.

**Ideal reader's default:** Will feel the relief of arrival. Will immediately want to describe what she found — the insight, the gift, the meaning of the whole walk.

**What we want her to capture:** Not the meaning — the aliveness. What's present in her right now that wasn't before she walked in. Pure Earth is about what receives and holds, not what accomplishes. The Receptive doesn't produce — it holds what arrives. What arrived?

**Hexagram 2 character:** Earth doubled — pure yin, pure receptivity. No force, no direction. Complete openness. The most fertile possible state. The capture is what took root.

**Draft prompt:**
> *What's alive in you right now that wasn't before you walked in? Not what you learned — what's different. What took root. Open the app. This one matters most. → app*

## The Pattern — Template for Other Chapters

Each chapter's 8 Gate prompts follow this logic:

1. **Gate character:** What does this gate's voice do in general (Protector guards survival, Controller manages what's acceptable, etc.)
2. **Chapter modifier:** How does this chapter's Face color the gate encounter? (Ch3 Challenger = the gate shows up as a version of avoidance/confrontation avoidance; Ch4 Regent = the gate shows up as a version of structure/authority)
3. **Hexagram character:** Gate lower trigram × Chapter upper trigram = hexagram. What quality of movement does this hexagram describe? Use that quality to name what was encountered.
4. **Ideal reader's default response:** What will she do with the gate encounter before the prompt can interrupt her?
5. **What to capture:** The specific felt-sense instance — not the pattern, not the insight, not the reframe. The body-level event.
6. **Draft prompt:** 2–4 sentences. Direct address. No explanation of what a BAR is. Points to app. No teaching mode.

---

## What the Prompt Is NOT

- Not a journaling prompt — no open-ended reflective questions
- Not a comprehension check — not "what did the Skeptic move teach you?"
- Not a practice instruction — the gate prose already gave the instruction
- Not therapeutic — not "how does this connect to earlier experiences?"
- Not an achievement — not "you've now completed Gate 3"

The prompt is a capture window. It opens briefly. It names what's in the room. It points to the app. It closes.
