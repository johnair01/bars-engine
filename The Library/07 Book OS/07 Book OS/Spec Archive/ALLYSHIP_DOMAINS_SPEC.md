# Allyship Domains — The Four Show Up Dimensions

**Source:** Wendell 2026-05-21 — identified while reviewing Ch1 hexagram cards
**Status:** Not yet in the manuscript. Not yet in the card format. Structural finding — needs integration decisions.

---

## The Four Domains

These are the four ways allyship shows up in the world as concrete action. They are the **Show Up dimensions** — the external expressions of any gate's gift operating at full capacity.

1. **Gather Resources** — money, time, access, connections, capacity. Allyship that creates the material conditions for others to act.
2. **Skillful Organizing** — building structures, coalitions, movements, containers. Allyship that makes coordination possible.
3. **Direct Action** — showing up, putting your body/voice/presence in the line. Allyship that enacts.
4. **Raise Awareness** — story, narrative, making visible what is invisible, naming what is avoided. Allyship that illuminates.

---

## Relationship to WAVE (clarified 2026-05-21)

**Critical correction:** The domains are NOT equivalent to the Show Up stage. The relationship is orthogonal.

- **Show Up** = the application of the domain — when you enact change, that's Show Up
- **But all 4 WAVE moves operate INSIDE each domain**

A player working in Gather Resources might need to Wake Up (realize they've been hoarding access), Clean Up (stop performing generosity without giving real capacity), Grow Up (scope what they can actually give), or Show Up (give it). The WAVE describes the quality of the work; the domain describes the type of allyship action.

**Why this matters:** This is what creates throughput through the domains. Players who can only Wake Up in their domain never resource the cause. Players who skip to Show Up without the prior stages perform the domain rather than enact it. The WAVE stages inside a domain move players from stagnation to action.

**Implication for cards:** A card drawn in the context of a chosen domain needs to run the WAVE stage appropriate to where the player is stuck IN THAT DOMAIN — not just at the general gate WAVE level.

## Relationship to Gates (non-exclusive — any gate can serve any domain)

The domains are landing zones, not gate assignments. Any gate can serve any domain. But there are natural affinities:

| Domain | Natural Gate Affinities | Why |
|--------|------------------------|-----|
| Gather Resources | Protector, Regent, Fixer | Protector names what's depleted; Regent holds what's worth preserving; Fixer scopes what's needed |
| Skillful Organizing | Controller, Architect, Regent | Controller routes the system; Architect designs structures; Regent creates lasting containers |
| Direct Action | Challenger, Damaged Self, Emotional Body | Challenger draws the clean line; Damaged Self chooses its cost; Emotional Body deploys the right channel |
| Raise Awareness | Victim/Storyteller, Skeptic, Shaman | Victim tells the right story; Skeptic asks the question nobody wants asked; Shaman reads and names the field |

**Note:** The Victim gate is the most direct map to Raise Awareness — the gate is literally about story, narrative, and making what happened visible. This is not accidental.

## Current Gap in the Book

**Not introduced anywhere in the manuscript.** The 4 domains are implicit in the chapter structure but never named as a framework. This is a problem for the card system specifically — the cards route readers toward a "move in the world" (Task 2) but without the domains, the reader doesn't know which KIND of show-up they're being called toward.

**Possible introduction points (to decide):**
- Ch0 (Infinite Arcade) — as part of the BAR system introduction; the domains are what BARs are tracking
- Ch1 (The Forest) — as the four ways the inner walk becomes outer action; what the gates are in service of
- Ch8 (Player) — as the synthesis; the Player learns to route their gifts deliberately toward the right domain
- The card system itself — as a Task 2 modifier: "Which domain does this move live in? Gather Resources / Skillful Organizing / Direct Action / Raise Awareness"

## Impact on Hexagram Cards

**Current Task 2 format:** "The move in the world" — specific, directive, often un-anchored to a domain type.

**Possible upgrade:** Task 2 routes to a domain. Two options:

**Option A — Domain as a question in Task 3 (BAR capture):**
Task 3 stays as capture, but adds: "Which domain did this move land in? Gather Resources / Skillful Organizing / Direct Action / Raise Awareness."
- Minimal card redesign
- Domains appear only at the capture point
- Risk: reader doesn't know which domain they're working in until after

**Option B — Domain as a context line above Task 2:**
Before Task 2: *Domain: [Raise Awareness / Direct Action / Skillful Organizing / Gather Resources]*
Each card has a suggested domain (based on gate × chapter intersection), but reader can redirect.
- More visible structural integration
- Requires a domain assignment for all 64 cards
- Benefit: Task 2 becomes more specific because the domain frames it

**Option C — Domain as oracle text component (not a label but woven in):**
Component 2 (Gift × Chapter intersection) names the domain implicitly — "The Skeptic's gift here is the question that makes Direct Action navigable" — without labeling it.
- Most elegant
- Requires oracle text rewrite for all 64 cards
- Least mechanical-feeling

**Confirmed decision (2026-05-21):** A player needs to DECLARE their domain before using the card — the domains are a DIAGNOSTIC for what allyship problem they're working on. The first read-through (the book) is introduction to the system; the cards are where the diagnostic fires. Card flow:
1. Player chooses domain: "What allyship problem am I working on?"
2. Card tasks run in that domain context
3. BAR capture includes domain tag

This means the domain selector is likely a pre-card interaction in the app, or a printed question above the tasks on the physical card.
