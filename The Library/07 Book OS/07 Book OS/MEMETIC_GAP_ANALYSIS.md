## The Six Memetic Gaps

### GAP 1 — Voice: Teaching Mode vs. Wendell Voice (CRITICAL)

**What it is:** The manuscript is ~70,000 words of "teaching mode" — careful, explanatory, protective. Tweet Wendell is none of these. The voice profile confirmed this as the primary editorial problem.

**The specific failure modes (from WENDELL_VOICE_PROFILE.md):**
- States insight → explains it → gives example → restates it
- Escalates register to signal importance ("Here is the truth:")
- Uses "you" where "I" would do more work
- Softens direct challenges with qualifiers
- Real-time processing entirely absent

**What 5/5 looks like:** Every section introduction has one compressed definition. Every challenging statement is direct, no apology. 5-6 personal "van tweet" moments — unpolished, in-process, honest.

**The concrete edit:** Replace every "Here is the truth:" with the truth at the same register as the sentence before it. Replace every explanatory "you" paragraph with one specific "I" observation from Wendell.

**Risk if fixed:** Some readers will find it too direct. That's the point — the readers who need this book will catch it; the ones who don't weren't the reader anyway.

### GAP 2 — Somatic: Emotional Abstraction Without Body (CRITICAL)

**What it is:** The EA pass on the Ch1 urgency section confirmed: the text describes exhaustion as noun ("exhaustion," "burnout") without any nervous-system signature. "More than we can carry" is metaphorical somatic — references the body but doesn't trace an actual sensation.

**The EA findings (confirmed):**
- Somatic Grounding: 2/5 (only metaphorical somatic)
- Shadow Articulation: 5/5 (strongest passage — concrete, honest)
- Alchemical Movement: 1/5 (depletion named but not transformed)
- Shadow without somatic anchor: reader lands on "made it worse" while ungrounded

**What 5/5 looks like:** Before every shadow confession, one felt-body beat. "There's a moment — jaw tight, phone still warm in your hand — when you know you've already lost the thread." The confession lands on a body, not an abstraction.

**The concrete edit:** Add one somatic anchor beat before each of the 3 major shadow moments in Ch1 Urgency (couldn't hold it / froze / made it worse). The beat names a specific body location + quality, not a metaphor.

**Risk if fixed:** Prose becomes more specific, which means more demands on the reader's attention. Worth it — specificity is what makes prose feel alive.

---

### GAP 3 — Personal Presence: Zero First-Person "Van Tweet" Moments (CRITICAL)

**What it is:** The voice profile identified this as the most important gap. The book needs 5-6 moments where Wendell is present as a person to whom things have happened — not as a narrator explaining a framework. The van tweet is the model: real moment, in-process, before resolution.

**The model (from WENDELL_VOICE_PROFILE.md):**
> "My Van, Carmen VanDiego, got stolen in Portland OR, last night. Filed police report but generally just feeling a little numb and sad. I can't shake the feeling the universe is trying to tell me something but I also don't feel like engaging in sense-making too quickly."

**Why this gap matters:** The reader who watches Wendell stay with a painful real moment learns how to stay with theirs. The reader who only gets the framework learns what staying is. There's a difference.

**What 5/5 looks like:** 5-6 passages across ch1-ch8 that look like the van tweet — a thing happened, the feeling arrived before the understanding, he stayed with the not-knowing. Each chapter needs at least one.

**The concrete edit:** This is not an edit task. This is a design session: Wendell maps his own body states (5 EA channels as somatic prose) and then writes 2 specific moments from his own history where he was in-process. The writing is his, not delegated. The agent can only structure and calibrate.

**Risk if fixed:** Personal exposure. This is the cost of the book being real. The risk of not fixing it is the book being a framework that teaches about something instead of an enactment of it.

### GAP 4 — EA Alchemical Movement: Depletion Named But Not Transformed (HIGH)

**What it is:** The EA pass identified "Unalchemized Depletion" as a failure mode. The text names exhaustion, burnout, shadow moments — but offers no Transcend move. The EA analyst flagged: "That's a lot to ask of an exhausted reader."

**The current arc (Ch1 Urgency):** Fire → Water → Metal → Fire (anger doing → sadness depletion → fear freeze → anger self-blame). No release valve.

**What 5/5 looks like:** At the depletion point ("more than we can carry"), one micro-Transcend: "We mistook the weight of the world for the work of showing up." Depletion becomes data, not just suffering. The exhausted reader gets a place to land before the shadow confession lands.

**The concrete edit:** Add one alchemical move at each major depletion point in ch1-ch3. Format: [DISSATISFACTION → SATISFACTION] Transcend [X] — Emotion Name → Alchemical Outcome. Energy economy stays in writer context, not book content.

**Risk if fixed:** If the book offers Transcend moves too early, it risks the "false inspiration arc" the Ch1 spec explicitly forbids. The timing of the release valve matters — it can't come before the grief is named.

### GAP 5 — I Ching Translation: 64 Hexagrams Untranslated (MEDIUM)

**What it is:** The I Ching ↔ Allyship framework translation hasn't been built. 64 hexagrams need translation into allyship mechanics before the BAR prompts can be piloted. This blocks the integration of one of the book's most distinctive tools.

**What 5/5 looks like:** A canonical translation table: hexagram → allyship principle → game move → EA channel → bar prompt format. 64 entries, each with the structural translation specified.

**The concrete edit:** This is a structured writing task — can be parallelized with the somatic poetics session. Each hexagram gets a one-paragraph translation and a one-sentence bar prompt example. The work is definitional, not creative.

**Risk if fixed:** The BAR gate in Ch1 (and the I Ching architecture throughout) remains unimplemented. The book's most distinctive measurement tool can't be used in practice.

1. Gates 5/6 swap — identified but not fixed across ch1-ch7

**The concrete edit:** These are surgical structural edits — no voice work, no personal content, no somatic anchoring. These can be delegated to an agent once the decision is made. The blocker is the decision, not the execution.

**Risk if fixed:** Inconsistency across chapters. Readers who compare chapters will notice the Gates ordering flip-flopping. EA table decision: if they're removed, the content needs to be replaced with prose EA analysis.
