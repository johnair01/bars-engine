## Who She Is

Integral Green altitude. Enneagram 2. Femme. White or POC from white-dominated culture. West coast. 

Works for a non-profit, is thinking about starting one, or is a therapist committed to "doing the work." Has achieved neoliberal success metrics (credentialed, employed, stable) but hasn't materially benefited beyond food security. Has "won" at society and the winning feels hollow.

She has been doing allyship — the evolved, therapy-fluent, land-acknowledgment-giving version — for years. She is still burning out.

---

## The Specific Trap She Is In

### 1. The Unwinnable Game (Advanced Level)

She has already graduated from the "courtroom" version of allyship. She knows that version failed. She is playing the more sophisticated version: credential-collecting, ongoing-work, check-your-privilege-daily allyship that is also an unwinnable game.

The rules keep moving. Enough is never enough. The language shifts before she can master it. She can be called out despite genuine effort. Her identity as an ally requires perpetual inadequacy — the game needs her to never arrive.

She suspects the architecture is wrong. She hasn't said it out loud.

### 2. Flatland (Green's Specific Cage)

Green altitude believes it has escaped hierarchy. It has achieved the view from nowhere — radically inclusive, all perspectives equally valid. This feels like liberation.

It is a cage.

Green cannot see altitude because acknowledging altitude means acknowledging hierarchy, which violates Green's core value of egalitarianism. So Green sees all "lower" altitudes only in their shadow/pathological forms:
- Red = aggression, selfishness, domination (not: vitality, courage, self-authorship)
- Amber = fundamentalism, rigidity, authoritarianism (not: loyalty, stewardship, earned trust)
- Orange = capitalism, exploitation, achievement-obsession (not: systems thinking, autonomy, effectiveness)

She cannot meet people at those altitudes with their gifts. She can only see their pathology. Her "allyship" with anyone not at Green is therefore actually an attempt to **convert them to Green values** — while calling it inclusion.

### 3. The Projection Mechanism

She has split her psyche along ideological lines:

**All her good qualities → projected onto marginalized people**
They are authentic, wise, spiritually alive, emotionally real, close to the truth the system stole from them. She approaches them with idealization, not full humanity.

**All her bad qualities → projected onto oppressors**
They are cold, ambitious, boundary-drawing, hierarchical, systemically-minded. She cannot access these qualities in herself because they are "oppressor energy."

**The result:** She doesn't date complex human beings. She dates her projection of what a marginalized person should be. When the actual person arrives — with their own Amber loyalties, their own Orange ambitions, their own Red anger — it ruptures her. The oppressed are supposed to be the good ones.

She also cannot develop the capacities she needs most (the Challenger's clean "no," the Architect's systems thinking, the Regent's stewardship) because those gifts live in the people she has made into villains.

### 4. Intra-Green Coalition Masquerading as Cross-Difference Solidarity

She is not doing cross-altitude allyship. She is building Green coalitions and calling it diversity. The marginalized people she allies with are Green-coded. When she encounters Red or Amber, she can only respond with conversion attempts or rejection.

This is why the work never feels like enough: she is trying to solve a cross-altitude problem with intra-altitude tools.

## What the Book Is Doing for Her

The six Game Master Faces are a Green-to-Teal journey. Each Face is a gift she has been either projecting outward or demonizing in others:

| Face | Altitude Gift | Her Current Relationship to It |
|------|--------------|-------------------------------|
| Shaman | Magenta/Red felt-sense wisdom | Projects onto marginalized people as if they're born with it |
| Challenger | Red's clean "no" | Demonized as oppressor energy; her most avoided face |
| Regent | Amber stewardship and loyalty | Reads as conservatism/fundamentalism; can't build lasting things without it |
| Architect | Orange systems thinking | Reads as capitalism; why her non-profit work doesn't scale |
| Diplomat | Green field-holding | Her home altitude; but only holds fields of Green-coded people |
| Sage | Teal panoramic vision | Where she needs to go; cannot access without acknowledging altitude exists |

The book is a reclamation project. It gives her back the capacities she split off and projected outward. By the time she reaches the Sage, she has practiced each gift — and is ready to see the altitude map that organized the whole journey.

### Why "Faces" and Not "Levels"

The Faces framing is load-bearing architecture, not just a pedagogical choice.

If the book called these "levels" or "stages" or "a developmental sequence," she would bounce off it immediately. That's hierarchy. That implies some people are more evolved than others. Her hierarchy allergy is principled and deep.

"Faces" — facets of a game, different ways of playing — lets her try on each altitude's gifts without triggering the allergy. She can embody Amber's Regent as a game face without having to believe Amber is "higher" than Red. She can practice Orange's Architect without feeling like she's selling out.

The faces framing also protects against the call-out reflex: "levels" would immediately become a new credential to collect and a new way to rank others ("she's only at Regent, I'm at Diplomat"). "Faces" stays in the register of capacity and practice.

## What Ch0 Currently Misses

The current Ch0 correctly critiques the courtroom version of allyship — which she graduated from years ago. She reads that section and thinks: *I already know this. What are you going to show me that I haven't heard?*

The book needs one surgical paragraph that names her specific trap — the advanced, evolved, therapy-fluent version — with enough precision that she feels caught (not shamed, but recognized):

> You are not here because you haven't tried. You are here because trying harder has stopped working, and some part of you has started to wonder whether the game itself is the problem.

The flatland trap and the projection mechanism should be named obliquely — through her experience, not through Integral vocabulary she will either dismiss or weaponize.

## Editorial Cautions

- **Do not name "Green altitude," "flatland," or "developmental hierarchy" explicitly.** She'll either feel judged or she'll co-opt the language as the next credential.
- **Do not shame the projection.** It's a coping mechanism from a system that required it. Name it as information, not failure.
- **Do not let her off the hook too easily in Ch0.** The permission-granting tone is right, but she needs to feel the specific recognition before she gets the relief.
- **The Challenger chapter is her most uncomfortable.** She needs the most permission there, and the most challenge. Don't soften it.
- **The Sage chapter is not confirmation — it's disruption.** She will expect the Sage to validate her existing meta-awareness. It needs to show her the altitude map she's been refusing to see.
