## THE SHAMAN
*Emotional alchemy — reaching feeling — staying with what's difficult*

| Influence | The Move | The Technique |
|-----------|----------|---------------|
| **Adams** | Feeling arrives disguised as a detail | Put the emotional moment inside a logistical problem. Let the reader feel it before they know they're feeling it. The tea being wrong while Earth is destroyed. |
| **Pratchett** | The emotional ambush | Build the comic frame solid, introduce what the frame can't hold, hold the dissonance. Use the footnote for the feeling the main text can't carry. |
| **Neely** | The incantation that becomes real | State the emotional truth directly, as though it's obvious. Repeat with full commitment. The mock-grandiosity that becomes genuine through conviction. |
| **Jung** | Descend before you describe | Report from inside the experience, not from above it. Use the register that fits the depth. Let the encounter take as long as it takes. Don't resolve it early. |
| **Whitman** | The inventory of sensation | When you want feeling, list everything it touches. Every surface, every context, every bodily location. Go long. Then cut to the one image that holds it all. |
| **Wilber** | The meditative pause | Turn the abstract claim into an invitation to verify it right now in experience. "Notice that..." "Try this..." The reader who checks it owns it. |

**For Wendell:** The book currently uses Adams (feeling as detail) and some Whitman (direct address). What's missing is Jung (the descent, the personal vulnerability reported from inside) and Pratchett (the thing the frame can't hold, allowed to be felt).

## THE CHALLENGER
*The clean no — moral clarity — naming the hard thing*

| Influence | The Move | The Technique |
|-----------|----------|---------------|
| **Adams** | Challenge through scale | Zoom out until the assumption looks small. Don't argue — demonstrate scale. Show what the thing looks like from sufficient distance. Let the absurdity speak. |
| **Pratchett** | The distinction that redraws the map | "I'm not a nice person. I'm a good person." State the distinction flatly. Don't defend it. Let the character's certainty do the work. The challenge lands before the argument can form. |
| **Neely** | Refuse the protective irony | Find what you're treating lightly because it's too big to treat seriously. Treat it seriously. Commit fully. The reader's discomfort with your sincerity is the challenge working. |
| **Jung** | Give the challenge a face | Don't argue. Create the encounter. The challenge arrives as a figure — something with a voice, a presence, a specific thing it says. Show the resistance and what happens when resistance breaks. |
| **Whitman** | The declaration as identity | "I am the hounded slave." State the challenge as identity, not proposition. You can't debate an identity statement in the normal way. Make it large enough that the reader's defenses have nowhere to stand. |
| **Wilber** | Name the error more precisely than the person making it | Give the confusion a name they didn't have. "The pre/trans fallacy." The precise name is the challenge and the gift simultaneously. |

**For Wendell:** The book currently challenges at the level of "old allyship is performative." The sharper Challenger move (Pratchett, Wilber) would name the *specific* error this reader is making — with enough precision that she can't dismiss it.

## THE REGENT
*Stewardship — what's worth keeping — honoring before reforming*

| Influence | The Move | The Technique |
|-----------|----------|---------------|
| **Adams** | Autopsy with affection | Honor the impulse the tradition was protecting. Expose how the form has betrayed it. Not with contempt — with genuine sadness for the gap. |
| **Pratchett** | Find what fills the vacuum | Steward institutions because you've seen what happens when they fall. The conservative argument made by a humanist. Reform, but know what you're reforming into. |
| **Neely** | Inhabit the form completely | Apply the inherited form without hedging. The tradition is honored by being practiced, not discussed. Use it for real and the reader feels the gravity it's accumulated. |
| **Jung** | Reverent excavation | Enter the old language before you translate it into contemporary terms. The alchemists' vocabulary before the psychological vocabulary. Honor the tradition by speaking it. |
| **Whitman** | The catalogue as inclusion | Name every person. Give every person their own line. The list is the stewardship. Inclusion is care practiced at the level of prose. |
| **Wilber** | Charitable reading before integration | Find the strongest version of what the tradition preserved. Honor it specifically. Then show the larger frame that extends rather than refutes it. |

**For Wendell:** The Regent chapters are mostly Pratchett (testing institutions) and Wilber (charitable reading). What's missing is Neely (fully inhabiting the form of inheritance) and Jung (the old language honored before it's translated).

## THE ARCHITECT
*Systems design — the design that reveals other designs — making structure visible*

| Influence | The Move | The Technique |
|-----------|----------|---------------|
| **Adams** | The system that does what it was designed to do | Build the system completely. Follow the internal logic to its conclusion. Show what the system produces at scale. Let the output reveal the specification's flaw. |
| **Pratchett** | The world that reveals this world | Design the fictional system to exaggerate the design logic of the real one until it's visible. Show what the system costs the people it doesn't serve. |
| **Neely** | One rule applied everywhere | Choose one design rule. Apply it to everything without exception. Trust the consistency. Let the form make the argument so the prose doesn't have to. |
| **Jung** | Let the system emerge from the journey | Build the architecture by walking through it. Don't announce the design before experiencing it. The map that emerges from the journey is more trustworthy than the map drawn before it. |
| **Whitman** | The long line earns the short line | Expand until the accumulation has done the argumentative work. Then deliver the short line that was always the destination. The architecture is the preparation. |
| **Wilber** | Make the implicit logic explicit | Find the design logic already operating. Name the dimensions every framework is already using without knowing it. The metatheory shows where every answer lives. |

**For Wendell:** The Architect chapter currently uses Wilber (naming the design logic) and some Adams (systems that produce their outputs). What's missing is Neely (one rule applied everywhere — what is the Architect's single rule?) and Whitman (the long accumulation that earns the short insight).

## THE DIPLOMAT
*Field-holding — presence across difference — the walk-away price*

| Influence | The Move | The Technique |
|-----------|----------|---------------|
| **Adams** | The field held by shared bewilderment | Don't need common values — need common acknowledgment of being lost. "Don't Panic." The field held by honest admission of not-knowing. |
| **Pratchett** | Interrogation in good faith | Find the question both parties are avoiding. Ask it. Don't mediate — witness honestly. The field held by the quality of the question, not the quality of the answer. |
| **Neely** | The dual field held simultaneously | Both worlds real at once. Never abandon either register. Let the reader live in the translation between frames. The productive tension between frames is the meaning. |
| **Jung** | The dialogue that holds the tension | When two forces are in genuine conflict, write the dialogue. Give both full expression. Don't resolve toward either. Wait for the third thing that emerges from the collision. |
| **Whitman** | The permeable self | Let the self become permeable to the other. Don't hold I and you tightly apart. Let the reader find themselves in your first person. The most intimate field possible — and the most fragile. |
| **Wilber** | The quadrant move | When two things seem to conflict, locate them. Which quadrant is each addressing? Often the conflict dissolves — they were orthogonal, not competing. |

**For Wendell:** The Diplomat chapter uses Wilber (quadrant location) and Adams (shared incomprehension). What's missing is Jung (the dialogue that holds the tension, the third thing) and Pratchett (the question both parties are avoiding).

## THE SAGE
*Altitude awareness — seeing which game you're playing — the view from above*

| Influence | The Move | The Technique |
|-----------|----------|---------------|
| **Adams** | The cosmic zoom-out then zoom-in | Zoom out until the thing looks absurd. Zoom back in until it looks precious. Hold both scales simultaneously. The long view doesn't make the short view small — it makes it precious. |
| **Pratchett** | Name the game and then the choice | Name the genre convention. Show the character who sees the design and consciously chooses. The choice doesn't need to be refusal — it needs to be conscious. |
| **Neely** | Narrate the subtext | What is the story actually about under what it says it's about? Narrate the truth the material is avoiding. Not as commentary — as narration. State it as the thing that is actually happening. |
| **Jung** | The individual experience as collective myth | Use first person as mythic register. Your specific experience, told precisely, becomes universal through the precision. The myth lives inside the particular. |
| **Whitman** | The view from sufficient future | See from after. Write as though time has passed over what you're describing. What will still be true when you are gone is the thing most worth saying. |
| **Wilber** | Altitude diagnosis — floor and ceiling | Every position has a floor (what it sees that the level below couldn't) and a ceiling (what it can't see from there). Honor both before showing the view from above. |

**For Wendell:** The Sage chapter currently uses Wilber (altitude diagnosis) and Adams (zoom out/zoom in). What's missing is Jung (your specific story as the collective myth — the personal Sage story), Neely (narrating the subtext of what allyship culture is actually about), and Whitman (the view from after — what will still be true).

## Cross-Influence Synthesis: What the MTGOA Voice Needs

The five influences pull in directions that create productive tension:

**Adams + Pratchett:** The joke that is also the truth. The premise that can't be delivered earnestly because the earnestness would kill it — it has to arrive sideways. MTGOA currently has some of this in Ch0 ("allyship as courtroom") but loses it in Ch5-7.

**Jung + Whitman:** The descent into the personal that becomes universal. The first-person narrator who risks their own story and finds that the specific details are the universal content. MTGOA currently avoids this almost entirely. This is the biggest gap.

**Neely:** The full commitment to the register. When MTGOA is mythic (the Shaman's exile, the Regent's stewardship), it pulls back just before going fully epic. Neely says: commit. Don't hedge the grandiosity. Earn it through conviction.

**Wilber:** The design logic made visible. MTGOA uses this well in the framework sections. The risk is staying at Wilber-altitude and never descending into the other influences. Wilber without Jung is theory without body.

**The voice that synthesizes all five:** Precise about the system. Funny about its absurdity. Fully committed to the mythic register when the material requires it. Willing to descend into the personal story and report from there. Able to see from altitude without using altitude to avoid arrival.

That's the voice. MTGOA has pieces of it. The editorial work is finding where it goes missing and restoring it.
