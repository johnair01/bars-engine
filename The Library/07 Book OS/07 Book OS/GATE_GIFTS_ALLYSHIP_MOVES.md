# Gate Gifts as Allyship Moves
**Purpose:** Canonical behavioral expression of each gate's gift — what the person DOES when the gift is operating, not just what the gift is conceptually. These become the 2-3 concrete tasks on every hexagram oracle card.

**Source:** Interview with Wendell 2026-05-20 + GATE_VOICES_CANONICAL.md (conceptual foundation)

**Design rule:** Tasks deploy the GIFT, not manage the shadow. The 3-2-1 app practice is for burning through the shadow to access the gift — not the primary path on the card.

**Card task format:**
- Task 1: Body/sensing move (Shaman principle — before anything else)
- Task 2: The move in the world (Diplomat/Challenger — specific, relational, directive)
- Task 3: Capture (Architect — this becomes the BAR)

**The 3-2-1 note** appears only when the shadow might be blocking the gift. It names which WAVE stage to use.

---

## Gate 1: Protector ☰
**WAVE slot:** Show Up (External) | **Trigram:** Qian / Heaven

**Gift summary (from canonical):**
The person who knows when a room is unsafe before anyone has named it. Can sense threat, name a boundary, and protect the conditions for genuine contact.

**Key insight (2026-05-20):** The body is the vehicle for the gift — not just "staying alive" but "staying functional." Each Face protects something specific: Shaman → signal clarity; Challenger → staying-power in the fire; Regent → the arc not just the moment; Architect → generative capacity; Diplomat → the container itself; Sage → altitude access. The Protector's gift is naming WHICH capacity this situation needs from you, and setting the one limit that keeps it intact.

**Gift as allyship move — behavioral tasks:**

1. Body check — what is this situation costing you right now? Name it specifically: signal clarity, staying-power, generative capacity, container stability, or altitude.
2. Set one concrete limit — not "take care of yourself" but the specific boundary that keeps THAT capacity functional.
3. Capture that limit as a BAR: what did you protect, and what did that make possible?

**3-2-1 note:** *If the Protector is in self-policing mode (doubt you'll say the wrong thing) or trying to protect others instead of yourself, take it into the 3-2-1 (Show Up) first.*

## Gate 2: Controller ☱
**WAVE slot:** Clean Up (External) | **Trigram:** Dui / Lake

**Gift summary (from canonical):**
The capacity to name what's working and what isn't — real discernment about quality, not just rule enforcement. The Controller's "good/bad" function, aimed at outcomes rather than compliance, is genuine strategic intelligence.

**Key insight (2026-05-20):** The Controller is the meta-voice — it decides which gate is present and at what altitude. Each Face defines "good" differently: Shaman → felt-sense was consulted; Challenger → the line held; Regent → it lasts; Architect → it scales; Diplomat → real contact happened; Sage → altitude match was right. The gift: consciously choosing the standard and the voice, rather than running the default. Drawing the Controller card = you've been handed the meta-move.

**Gift as allyship move — behavioral tasks:**

1. Name which voice is running right now, automatically. Is that the right one for what this situation actually needs?
2. Choose deliberately: which gate does this moment need — and at what WAVE stage (Wake Up / Clean Up / Grow Up / Show Up)? Say it out loud.
3. Act from the chosen voice, not the default. Capture: what did you consciously bring, and what did that make possible?

**3-2-1 note:** *If the Controller is enforcing discourse compliance (who's allowed to speak, what language is acceptable) rather than calibrating the system, take it into the 3-2-1 (Clean Up) first.*

## Gate 3: Skeptic ☲
**WAVE slot:** Wake Up (Internal) | **Trigram:** Li / Fire

**Gift summary (from canonical):**
The person who asks the question nobody wants to ask. Pattern recognition from experience — compressed history of what has failed before. Noticing precedes doubting.

**Key insight (2026-05-20):** The Skeptic slows things down — you cannot move fast while being skeptical. This is why it shows up as self-sabotage (shadow: paralysis, abjuration spell at the self). But the gift is discernment: the deliberate deceleration that enables a second look. Getting curious about the ESSENCE of something, not the surface. The pause IS the move. Pattern-recognition and "asking the hard question" are downstream of this deeper gift.

**Gift as allyship move — behavioral tasks:**

1. Stop. Before any response — don't post, don't reach out, don't act. The Skeptic's gift begins with deliberate deceleration.
2. Take another look: what is this situation actually about, underneath your first reaction? Name the essence, not the surface. One sentence.
3. Move from the second look, not the first. Capture: what did you see on the second look that you missed the first time? That's the BAR.

**3-2-1 note:** *If the Skeptic is casting the spell at you ("I'll get this wrong") rather than at the situation, take it into the 3-2-1 (Wake Up) first.*

## Gate 4: Fixer/Healer ☳
**WAVE slot:** Grow Up (External) | **Trigram:** Zhen / Thunder

**Gift summary (from canonical):**
Aimed at circumstances — systems, structures, conditions — not persons. Fix the conditions that lead to emergent allyship outcomes. The grown-up Fixer becomes a steward of conditions: clears obstacles, builds conditions, steps back.

**Key insight (2026-05-20):** The most overloaded gate — everyone thinks allyship means fixing. The constraint: you can only fix what you are responsible for. Each Face has a different scope: Shaman → the field I create; Challenger → the cleanness of my line; Regent → what's in my stewardship; Architect → the designs I author; Diplomat → the container I hold; Sage → the accuracy of my map. The permission question: what specifically is MINE to fix? Without naming the specific object within scope, the Fixer is just imposing. The Fixer's success = obsolescence: if you fixed it well, you're no longer needed there.

**Gift as allyship move — behavioral tasks:**

1. Map your scope — what in this situation is actually yours to fix? Name the specific object, structure, or condition (not the person, not the feeling, not the world). If you can't name it, you don't have permission.
2. Fix that one thing. One surgical intervention on the thing within your scope.
3. Step back deliberately. Capture: what did you fix, and does it still need you — or does it run without you now?

**3-2-1 note:** *If the Fixer is trying to fix a person (yourself included) or something outside your scope, take it into the 3-2-1 (Grow Up) first.*

## Gate 5: Victim ☴
**WAVE slot:** Wake Up (External) | **Trigram:** Xun / Wind

**Gift summary (from canonical):**
The Victim becomes the Hero — protagonist of their own story, not waiting to be rescued. Tells stories to invite others into effective allyship, not for clout or sympathy. The Storyteller: gives AND receives sympathy, knowing that without rapport, advocacy is less effective.

**Key insight (2026-05-20):** The Victim's gift is the ability to tell the story — raise awareness. Each Face needs a different KIND of story: Shaman → felt (lands in the body); Challenger → naming (says what's being avoided); Regent → contextual (historical arc); Architect → structural (makes the system visible); Diplomat → relational (holds all parties human); Sage → meta (names the game). The gift: knowing which story this moment needs, not just the one you're comfortable telling. The Victim becomes the Hero when the story is told for awareness, not for sympathy.

**Gift as allyship move — behavioral tasks:**

1. Name the kind of story this situation needs: felt / naming / contextual / structural / relational / meta. That choice is the move — most people default to the kind they're already comfortable with.
2. Tell that story in the right form for this moment — a post, a conversation, a document, a message to one person.
3. Watch what shifts in your audience. Capture: what awareness moved because of the story you told?

**3-2-1 note:** *If the story you're telling is generating sympathy for you rather than awareness for the situation, take the Victim into the 3-2-1 (Wake Up) first.*

## Gate 6: Emotional Body ☵
**WAVE slot:** Clean Up (Internal) | **Trigram:** Kan / Water

**Gift summary (from canonical):**
Locate the signal before acting. Name which channel (Fear/Grief/Anger/Shame/Joy). The location changes what happens next. Bring the emotion the situation calls for rather than defaulting to the available one.

**Key insight (2026-05-20):** The Emotional Body carries ALL 5 channels at ALL 5 levels — do NOT assign home channels to specific Faces (that is a hallucination). The 5 channels and their functional intelligence (neutral state): Anger = creates and destroys; Fear = detects threat; Sadness/Grief = shows what you care about and how far away; Joy = lightens; Neutrality = detaches to see the whole. The card's job is a pointer, not a lesson — the book already taught this. The move: name which channel is running, then deploy its function. Don't suppress it or perform it — work WITH it.

**Gift as allyship move — behavioral tasks:**

1. Name the channel that's running right now — one of five: Anger (creates/destroys), Fear (detects threat), Sadness (shows what you care about and how far away), Joy (lightens), Neutrality (detaches to see the whole). Say it out loud.
2. Use THAT channel's function as your allyship move. Anger clears space. Fear names the real threat. Sadness shows what matters. Joy shifts the field. Neutrality reveals the pattern. Deploy it.
3. Capture: what did the channel do when you worked WITH it instead of around it?

*All 5 channels are always available. This card asks you to work with whichever one is actually running.*

**3-2-1 note:** *If emotions are running without identification (acting from charge without knowing which channel), take it into the 3-2-1 (Clean Up) first.*

## Gate 7: Damaged Self ☶
**WAVE slot:** Show Up (Internal) | **Trigram:** Gen / Mountain

**Gift summary (from canonical):**
Resilience, endurance, calibration of necessary risk. Recognize the resilience in the person you're allying with before recognizing their damage. Offer support only where genuinely needed, not where the ally needs to feel useful.

**Key insight (2026-05-20):** The Damaged Self CHOOSES what damage to take — this is the gift. Most allyship burnout comes from unchosen damage (taking whatever lands with no decision). Each Face takes a different type of damage on purpose: Shaman → being genuinely moved/changed; Challenger → being disliked for naming what's avoided; Regent → invisibility, load-bearing work without credit; Architect → obsolescence, the design outliving your control; Diplomat → held tension, staying in difficulty without resolving it; Sage → being wrong, letting better information revise the map. The gift question: is this the damage I'm built for?

**Gift as allyship move — behavioral tasks:**

1. Name the damage this situation is asking you to take — specifically, what will it cost you? (Being moved? Disliked? Uncredited? Made obsolete? Holding tension? Being wrong?)
2. Choose deliberately: is this the damage worth taking — the kind that grows your capacity — or damage that depletes without return?
3. If yes, take it on purpose, not by default. Capture: what did you absorb, and what did it build?

**3-2-1 note:** *If you're taking all damage indiscriminately — no decision, just accumulation — take the Damaged Self into the 3-2-1 (Show Up) first.*

## Gate 8: Vulnerable Child ☷
**WAVE slot:** Grow Up (Internal) | **Trigram:** Kun / Earth

**Gift summary (from canonical):**
Curiosity-driven, genuinely exploratory, willing to be surprised and changed. The Vulnerable Child ally doesn't need to be right. It needs to learn. "What would I do here if I wasn't afraid of getting it wrong?"

**Key insight (2026-05-20):** The Vulnerable Child's job: grow, learn, play, explore. Each Face expresses these in its own domain — Shaman → felt experiment ("what happens if I let this land?"); Challenger → confrontation as learning instrument; Regent → discover what's worth stewarding by trying; Architect → prototype before committing, design through curiosity; Diplomat → discover the field's capacity by not protecting it from itself; Sage → hold the map lightly, let being wrong update it. The gift question: is there genuine uncertainty? If you already know what you're going to get, the child isn't leading — it's been replaced by the credential-collector. The test: did anything surprise you?

**Gift as allyship move — behavioral tasks:**

1. Identify which mode is live right now: grow (let it change you), learn (find out something), play (experiment without commitment), explore (go somewhere you haven't been). One is more available than the others — pick it.
2. Do the thing with that orientation. Not to get it right. To find out what happens.
3. Capture the surprise — what happened that you didn't expect? If nothing surprised you, the child wasn't leading. That's the BAR.

**3-2-1 note:** *If allyship feels like obligation, performance, or credential-collecting — the child is in exile. Take the Vulnerable Child into the 3-2-1 (Grow Up) first.*
