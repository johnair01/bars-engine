## How to use this doc

For each section, run the 6 questions. The answers live here. Update after every editing session.

**Reservations (the 6 self-sabotaging beliefs — comfortable, not dissatisfied):**
1. Not Good Enough — *"I'm not doing it right"*
2. Not Ready — *"I need to learn more before I start"*
3. Don't Know How — *"I don't have enough information"*
4. Not Enough Time/Energy — *"I can't add anything else"*
5. Already Know This — *"I've heard this before"*
6. Not My Place — *"This isn't for me"*

## SECTION: ENTERING THE INFINITE ARCADE

**Q1 — Experience:** Discovery. The reader encounters the idea that allyship has been a game all along.

**Q2 — EA outcome:** Excitement (Wood) — the charge of a door opening. *"Oh, I didn't know this was available."*

**Q3 — Reservation:** Already Know This (Belief 5). Dismissal of gamification language before the architecture is seen.

**Q4 — Felt in the reservation:** Defensive skepticism. Bracing against being sold something.

**Q5 — Prerequisite:** This version of "game" must be genuinely different from gamification they've encountered — not points, not badges. The game IS the practice.

**Q6 — Transformation:** Recognition — from observing a game to recognizing you've been a player all along.

---

## SECTION: TOKEN SYSTEM

**Q1 — Experience:** Being given something. The reader receives a real, renewable, personal resource.

**Q2 — EA outcome:** Joy (Wood) — expansion of *"I have something to spend."* Capacity that was compressed becomes available.

**Q3 — Reservation:** Not Good Enough (Belief 1) + Not Enough Time/Energy (Belief 4). Token treated as finite, something to hoard instead of deploy.

**Q4 — Felt in the reservation:** Compressed. Guarded. Holding something you're afraid to use.

**Q5 — Prerequisite:** The token must be renewable — spending doesn't permanently reduce capacity.

**Q6 — Transformation:** Agency — not just "I have tokens" but "I am someone who uses tokens."

---

## SECTION: TICKET SYSTEM

**Q1 — Experience:** Entry. The moment of being granted access.

**Q2 — EA outcome:** Relief (Water/Sadness) — quiet dignity of someone who was allowed in and knows it. Not celebration. Settled knowledge.

**Q3 — Reservation:** Not My Place (Belief 6) + Not Ready (Belief 2). Belonging held as provisional. Waiting for permission that never fully arrives.

**Q4 — Felt in the reservation:** Floating. Present but not anchored. Checking your position, monitoring whether you'll be found out.

**Q5 — Prerequisite:** The ticket must be a grant, not an invitation-to-earn. Not revocable, not requiring ongoing performance.

**Q6 — Transformation:** Belonging — not "I was let in" but "I am of this place."

---

## SECTION: THREE GAME TYPES

**Q1 — Experience:** Self-sorting. The reader discovers which game type matches their current reality.

**Q2 — EA outcome:** Clarity (Earth/Peace) — a map becoming legible. *"Oh, that's which one I'm in."*

**Q3 — Reservation:** Don't Know How (Belief 3). Sorting feels exposing — admitting which game you're in means admitting you're not in the one you wanted.

**Q4 — Felt in the reservation:** Diffusion. In all three games without being fully in any. Spreading across games because choosing means admitting.

**Q5 — Prerequisite:** All three game types must be legitimate entry points, not ranked stages. No hierarchy of games.

**Q6 — Transformation:** Discernment — knowing which game you're in without judgment. Not "I figured out the best one."

---

## SECTION: WHY GAMIFY ALLYSHIP

**Q1 — Experience:** Permission. Explicit license to use the game frame.

**Q2 — EA outcome:** Excitement (Wood) — the forward pull of *"I can do this."*

**Q3 — Reservation:** Already Know This (Belief 5). Previous gamification was thin. Bracing for collapse.

**Q4 — Felt in the reservation:** Defensive patience. Waiting to be disappointed. Listening but braced.

**Q5 — Prerequisite:** Gamification reveals the existing structure — it doesn't add a layer. The game is the allyship.

**Q6 — Transformation:** Permission granted — from "this is interesting" to "I am allowed to do this."

---

## SECTION: THE SIX FACES LADDER

**Q1 — Experience:** Recognition. Each face triggers *"that's me"* — in a specific moment, not abstractly.

**Q2 — EA outcome:** Peace (Earth) — rightness. Finding where you fit. *"That's my face."*

**Q3 — Reservation:** Don't Know How (Belief 3). Using faces without clarity about which is yours. Not ready to claim it.

**Q4 — Felt in the reservation:** Overwhelmed. Six faces, don't know which is yours. Floating between them, trying to find the one you're allowed to be.

**Q5 — Prerequisite:** Claiming a face is not the same as completing its work. You can claim before mastering.

**Q6 — Transformation:** Groundedness — from floating between faces to anchored in one. "I know my face."

---

## SECTION: ENTERING THE ARCADE (closing)

**Q1 — Experience:** Consignment. Acceptance of player responsibility as natural consequence of what was received.

**Q2 — EA outcome:** Triumph (Fire) — earned authority. Not given permission, but earned your position.

**Q3 — Reservation:** Not Good Enough (Belief 1) + Not Ready (Belief 2). Not prepared to be a player. Waiting for certainty before committing.

**Q4 — Felt in the reservation:** Deferred. Knowing you should be doing this but not feeling like you're the one who gets to.

**Q5 — Prerequisite:** Entering doesn't require being good at it first. The arcade is where you learn.

**Q6 — Transformation:** Commitment — from "I understand this" to "I am doing this." Quiet internal conscription.
