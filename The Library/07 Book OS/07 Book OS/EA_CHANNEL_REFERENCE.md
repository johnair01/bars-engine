## Purpose

So long as our EA definitions are fuzzy, our editorial moves using EA will be fuzzy. This document gives agents the precise vocabulary to recognize what channel is active in a passage and whether it is in dissatisfaction or satisfaction state. Without this, the EA pass cannot do its job.

## The Five Channels

### Water — Sadness/Grief

**Dissatisfaction anchors:** despair, depression, hopelessness
*(The channel blocked, collapsed, or stuck — grief with no movement)*

**Satisfaction state:** **Care, Connection, Home** — grief that has moved into action. The sadness is still present but it's generating something: care for what was lost, renewed relational opening, restored belonging. Water flowing, not frozen. The emotion has been processed enough to become fuel rather than weight.

**How dissatisfaction appears in prose:**

- Flatness — statements made without energy, as if the outcome doesn't matter
- Hopelessness as conclusion — "this is just how it is," "nothing will change"
- Past-tense heaviness — the present pulled under by what was lost
- The reader can feel the author has given up

**How satisfaction appears in prose:**

- Grief that accumulates toward depth rather than collapse
- Loss named without being resolved prematurely
- The present moment held alongside what's gone
- Water moving — not frozen

**EA move types for this channel:**

- **Integrate:** Name the despair/depression/hopelessness before the transformation. "Cynicism is grief with a better vocabulary" — integrates the grief hidden under the armor.
- **Transcend:** Move through the hopelessness by finding what's underneath it — often fear, often the original love that the grief is protecting.
- **Radiate:** Let the grief have its full weight before it moves. Three-part escalation that builds to "And yet I persist" — the grief is radiated fully before persistence arrives.

---

### Fire — Anger

**Dissatisfaction anchors:** rage, hatred, frustration
*(The channel hot, reactive, and unprocessed — anger that hasn't found its target)*

**Satisfaction state:** **Authority, Assertion, Protection** — anger that has found its boundary and held it. The charge is still present but it has a target and a verdict. Fire that has become precision rather than heat. The reader feels the clarity of a line drawn, not just the heat of a reaction.

**How dissatisfaction appears in prose:**

- Undirected heat — rage present but aimed everywhere or nowhere
- Hatred that collapses into the person rather than naming the behavior/system
- Frustration that loops — same complaint restated
- The reader feels the charge but can't locate what it's for

**How satisfaction appears in prose:**

- Fire that has a clear target — specific, named, not generalized
- Indignation that leads somewhere (a boundary, a demand, a verdict)
- Short declarative sentences — "And the rule of law in America is: if you're black, be who we want you to be." FULL STOP.
- The reader feels the clarity, not just the heat

**EA move types for this channel:**

- **Transcend:** "Wannabe allies will burn themselves out with anxiety over 'doing it right' as a way to not have to do anything." The frustration is transcended by naming its actual function.
- **Integrate:** Name the rage/hatred/frustration before moving it — especially when frustration is covering grief or fear underneath.
- **Radiate:** Let the anger have its full expression — the numbered list as weapon, "1) You don't like Star Wars. 2) You don't like women." — Fire fully expressed before it resolves.

---

### Metal — Fear

**Dissatisfaction anchors:** anxiety, panic, worry
*(The channel contracted, scanning, unable to locate the actual threat)*

**Satisfaction state:** **Courage, Clarity, Openness** — fear that has located its object and stepped through. The risk-detection is still active but it's generating discernment rather than scanning everything. Fear that has become an asset: the author knows what they're protecting and moves toward it anyway.

**How dissatisfaction appears in prose:**

- Hedging language — "might," "could," "perhaps," "sometimes"
- Over-qualification — every claim softened before it lands
- Breathless structure — sentences that can't stop because stopping feels dangerous
- Catastrophizing — the worst-case stated as likely
- The reader feels the author protecting themselves from the claim

**How satisfaction appears in prose:**

- Named boundary — specific, clear, not defensive
- Appropriate discernment — "this, not that"
- Fear that has located its object and can speak directly about it
- The reader feels safe because the author knows what they're protecting

**EA move types for this channel:**

- **Contextualize:** "Just a friendly reminder. Feeling like a completely different person can also feel like dying because in a very real sense it is." Places the anxiety/fear in a frame that makes it accurate rather than pathological.
- **Integrate:** Name the anxiety/panic/worry before addressing it — especially the fear under the allyship performance.
- **Transcend:** Cut through the worry by naming what it's actually protecting — often something the author already knows.

### Earth — Neutral

**Dissatisfaction anchors:** apathy, boredom
*(The channel flat, disengaged, present but not grounded — equanimity without contact)*

**Satisfaction state:** **Wisdom, Transparency** — neutrality that has moved into clear seeing and integrated knowing. The ground is present AND holding something real — not empty, not flat. The author sees the whole system without collapsing into any part of it, and that seeing generates something the reader can use.

**How dissatisfaction appears in prose:**

- Generic statements — low specificity, reader can't feel why the author cares
- Even pacing with no variation — nothing is more important than anything else
- The author is technically present but making no contact
- Teaching mode — organized and clear but the ground is missing
- The reader can summarize the argument without having felt it

**How satisfaction appears in prose:**

- Stillness that has weight — not absence, but presence without urgency
- The ground from which other channels can move
- A bridge section that knows its job is holding, not moving
- The reader feels oriented

**EA move types for this channel:**

- **Contextualize:** Earth channel is often the delivery register for Contextualize moves — "The true self knows; the small self thinks." The flat delivery IS the Earth channel at satisfaction.
- **Integrate:** Apathy/boredom named — "this is why you're not moved by this" — before finding what's underneath.
- Note: Earth channel often serves as the container for other channels' moves rather than the primary mover.

---

### Wood — Joy

**Dissatisfaction anchors:** mania, curiosity, distraction
*(The channel overactive, scattered, or performing — energy without direction or ground)*

**Satisfaction state:** **Vitality, Leadership, Legacy, Gift, Commitment** — joy that has direction and sustains. Energy that is committing, giving, building rather than scattering. The delight is present and it's doing something with itself: choosing, sustaining, offering.

**How dissatisfaction appears in prose:**

- Scattered energy — multiple threads started, none resolved
- Forced enthusiasm — exclamation points that don't land
- Distraction disguised as breadth — covering many topics without landing on any
- Mania — the prose accelerates without arriving anywhere
- Curiosity that opens but never commits

**How satisfaction appears in prose:**

- The punchline — joy that transcends what would otherwise be anger, despair, or shutdown
- Parenthetical wit — "(laughing forever)" — the Joy channel metabolizing what would otherwise be grief or rage
- Humor as alchemy — not deflection, completion
- The reader laughs and then realizes the laughter was the teaching

**EA move types for this channel:**

- **Transcend:** The primary move. The punchline, the parenthetical, the compressed definition's flip — these are all Wood/Joy transcendence moves. Dissatisfaction (often frustration or despair in another channel) is transcended via the Joy channel's humor and surprise.
- **Radiate:** Real-time processing — "Oh shit I think I just gave my inner demon an existential crisis! This feels....itchy? But maybe good?" — Joy/curiosity fully expressed without premature resolution.

## Move Type Summary

| Move | What it does | When to use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Integrate** | Names the emotion before transforming it. The naming IS part of the transformation. | When the dissatisfaction is hidden under armor, performance, or abstraction. Name it first. |
| **Transcend** | Moves through the dissatisfaction by finding what's underneath or beyond it. | When the dissatisfaction has been sufficiently felt and named. The move cuts through to the other side. |
| **Radiate** | Lets the emotion have its full expression before moving. | When the emotion hasn't been allowed its full weight yet. Build the full charge before release. |
| **Contextualize** | Places the emotion in a larger frame that changes its meaning. | When the emotion is accurate but its interpretation is wrong. The feeling is right; the story about it needs expanding. |

**Critical warning:** Contextualize without Integrate produces teaching mode. The frame arrives before the feeling that earns it. The reader gets the map but not the territory.

## The Transformation Mechanism — Why Somatic Language Doesn't Hit

**The original error:** The EA pass was built on the assumption that somatic language signals EA resolution. That was wrong.

**What actually hits:**

- Epiphany bridges — the compressed poetic turn that lets the reader *arrive* at the feeling themselves. ("Cynicism is grief with a better vocabulary.")
- Transformation moves completing at the meaning level, not the sensory prescription level
- Voice trustworthiness — confession-credential, compressed definition, the three-part escalation, the punchline
- The alchemical flip — where the reader exhales because the logic did the work

**What reads as manipulation:**

- Author-voice somatic prescription ("You feel your jaw tighten") — the writer telling the reader's body what to do
- Somatic grounding as a *technique* — functional language that signals "you should be feeling X now"
- Any phrasing that feels like the author is directing the reader's nervous system rather than trusting the reader to generate the feeling

**The editorial question:**

- ~~"Is there enough somatic grounding?"~~
- "Does the text structure allow the reader to generate the feeling, or does it instruct it?"
- "Are the EA moves (Integrate, Transcend, Radiate) completing at the textual level?"
- "Is the voice earning the transformations it promises?"

**What this means for the pass:** The `transformationMechanism` score captures this directly. A 5/5 means the text is doing the alchemical work through meaning, structure, and voice — not through somatic instruction. The body responds to good story, not to prescription.

## Hollow Satisfaction — The Editorial Diagnostic

**Source:** EA-Satisfaction-Mechanics.md (maturity 5, fully validated)

In the EA loop, "hollow satisfaction" is when the signal of completion appears but the body hasn't confirmed it. In prose, this shows up as:

- The text CLAIMS satisfaction state (resolution language, forward movement) without having executed the dissatisfaction → satisfaction arc
- The author arrives at the teaching before earning it through the emotion
- The reader can detect the gap: "I was told this resolved but I don't feel the resolution"

**The three signal types (editorial version):**

| Signal | What it looks like in prose | Editorial action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Real satisfaction** | Dissatisfaction fully expressed → EA move executes → satisfaction state named | Arc is complete — protect it |
| **Charged dissatisfaction** | Dissatisfaction present, move not yet made — but the charge is the real content | Move hasn't fired yet — find the right move type |
| **Hollow satisfaction** | Resolution claimed before dissatisfaction earned — "and so I understood" without the through-line | False catharsis — rebuild the arc from the dissatisfaction forward |

**Dissatisfaction as gate:** Charged dissatisfaction can be the real signal OR it can be a gate hiding what's underneath. Rage blocking excitement. Grief blocking connection. When EA pass detects charged dissatisfaction, the diagnostic question is: is this dissatisfaction the real content (stay with it, let it complete), or is it protecting something else (look for what it's blocking)?

## Agent Recognition Protocol (DRAFT)

When the Emotional Alchemist pass reads a passage, it should:

1. **Identify the active channel** — which dissatisfaction anchor words or structural signatures are present?
2. **Is the channel in dissatisfaction or satisfaction state?** — is the emotion moving or stuck?
3. **Which move type is being attempted?** — is it landing?
4. **If not landing:** which move type would earn the satisfaction state for this specific channel?

**Failure mode check:**

- Dissatisfaction present but no move initiated → Emotional Flattening
- Move initiated but Contextualize before Integrate → Teaching Mode / Ontology Float
- Emotion named but not moved → Abstraction Without Body (note: now reframed — the body follows clean EA, so this means the EA move didn't complete)
- Satisfaction delivered before dissatisfaction is earned → False Catharsis
