# Hexagram Oracle Cards — Chapter 2: Shaman
**Upper trigram:** Kun ☷ Earth
**Chapter goal:** Develop the capacity to read emotional pattern — in yourself, in others, in the room — before any allyship move is made.
**Pilot status:** All 8 cards complete. Formula tested and confirmed. Container discipline applied (V3 lesson). 6 GM analysis complete.

**Card formula:** Shadow running → Gift × Chapter intersection → Invitation (optional) + Gate / WAVE / 3 tasks with containers / 3-2-1 handoff

**Draw mechanic:** Option C — starts as personal earned deck (BARs captured during reading), expands to full 64 as book progresses.

## Card 1: Protector in the Shaman
**Hex 11 · Peace**
*Gate: Protector · WAVE: Show Up · Trigram: Qian ☰ × Kun ☷*

You're trying to protect yourself from getting this wrong. The protection is the problem — you can't read the room from behind a wall.

The Protector's gift in this territory is organizing the one condition that makes the read possible — keeping yourself resourced enough to receive signal accurately. A depleted body reads nothing.

**1.** Check your resource level — is your body able to receive signal right now? Name what's depleted. *One word, out loud.*
**2.** Take the one action that restores enough capacity to feel accurately — not comfortable, clearly. *Five minutes or less. Before you engage.*
**3.** Capture: what you did to resource yourself, and what the field showed you once you could actually receive it. *Open the app. Two minutes.*

*Skilled players: If the Protector is in self-policing mode ("I might get this wrong"), take it into the 3-2-1 (Show Up) first.*

## Card 2: Controller in the Shaman
**Hex 19 · Approach**
*Gate: Controller · WAVE: Clean Up · Trigram: Dui ☱ × Kun ☷*

There's a standard running for what good allyship looks like right now. That standard is telling you to act. It hasn't told you to feel first.

The Controller's gift in this territory is organizing the sequence — "read first, then act" is not a delay, it's the architecture. What the Controller decides is good here determines the order everything else runs in.

**1.** Name the standard you're running — what does "good allyship" mean to you right now? *One sentence, out loud.*
**2.** Update it: "felt-sense read before any action" is now the standard. Route the other voices from there. *Before you act — not after.*
*Solo: write the new standard before your next engagement. With: say it to someone who will hold you to the sequence.*
**3.** Capture: what you held the sequence to, and what the felt read found that acting first would have skipped. *One sentence in the app.*

*Skilled players: If the Controller is enforcing discourse compliance rather than calibrating the system, take it into the 3-2-1 (Clean Up) first.*

→ See Appendix A: The Four Allyship Domains — Skillful Organizing

## Card 3: Skeptic in the Shaman
**Hex 36 · The Light Goes Underground**
*Gate: Skeptic · WAVE: Wake Up · Trigram: Li ☲ × Kun ☷*

Something in you already knows what this moment needs. The Skeptic got there first and is casting its doubt at your capacity to sense correctly — not at the situation, at you. The knowing went underground. It's still there.

The Skeptic's gift in the Shaman's territory: deliberate deceleration IS the read — and an accurate read is what makes naming the field possible. You can't raise awareness from a moving vehicle.

**1.** Stop. Before any response — don't post, don't reach out, don't act. *Right now. Full stop.*
**2.** What is this situation actually about, underneath your first read? Name the essence. *One sentence. Say it out loud.*
**3.** Move from the second look, not the first. Capture what you sensed that you missed before. *Open the app. One sentence.*

*Skilled players: If the Skeptic is casting the spell at you ("I'll get this wrong") rather than the situation, take it into the 3-2-1 (Wake Up) first.*

## Card 4: Fixer in the Shaman
**Hex 24 · Return**
*Gate: Fixer/Healer · WAVE: Grow Up · Trigram: Zhen ☳ × Kun ☷*

You've already identified what needs to be fixed. You haven't felt the room yet. The fix is running ahead of the read.

The Fixer's gift in this territory: the emotional field tells you what actually needs direct action. The scope is in the felt signal, not the analysis. Read first — the action clarifies from there.

**1.** Pause the fix. Which channel is alive in this situation right now? *One word. Out loud, in your body.*
**2.** Let the read name what's broken. Then name the one circumstance within your scope. *One sentence. Before you move.*
**3.** Fix that one thing, having felt it first. Capture how the read changed the fix. *In the app, after.*

*Skilled players: If the Fixer is trying to fix a person or something outside your scope, take it into the 3-2-1 (Grow Up) first.*

## Card 5: Victim in the Shaman
**Hex 46 · Pushing Upward**
*Gate: Victim · WAVE: Wake Up · Trigram: Xun ☴ × Kun ☷*

You have a story ready about what's happening here. It came before the feel. The Victim circulates narrative before it has time to land in the body.

The Victim's gift in this territory: the story that raises awareness is the one told from felt-sense, not from preparation. Read the emotional field first — the awareness it creates comes from that read, not from the narrative you had ready.

**1.** Pause the story. Which channel is running right now? *One word. In your body, before you narrate.*
**2.** Tell the story that came from the felt read — not the prepared one. *Right now, in whatever form fits — post, message, conversation. Before the feeling fades.*
*Solo: your own journal or notes, immediately after. With: one person you trust with the real version — not the prepared one.*
**3.** Capture how the felt read changed the story you told. *One sentence in the app.*

*Skilled players: If the story you're telling is generating sympathy for you rather than awareness for the situation, take the Victim into the 3-2-1 (Wake Up) first.*

## Card 6: Emotional Body in the Shaman
**Hex 7 · The Army**
*Gate: Emotional Body · WAVE: Clean Up · Trigram: Kan ☵ × Kun ☷*
*(Home territory — this is the core gate of the Shaman chapter)*

The charge is running and you're moving from it. You haven't named which channel it is. Unidentified emotion is a mob — it goes everywhere and builds nothing.

This is the Shaman's core work: naming the channel gathers the resource. Five channels, each with a specific job — Anger clears space, Fear detects threat, Sadness shows what matters, Joy shifts the field, Neutrality reveals the pattern. Named, the signal becomes available.

**1.** Name the channel running: Anger / Fear / Sadness / Joy / Neutrality. *One word. Out loud.*
**2.** Deploy that channel's specific function as your move. *In the next five minutes, in the situation that's live.*
**3.** Capture what the channel did when you worked with it deliberately. *Open the app before the charge fades.*

*All 5 channels are always available. This card asks you to work with whichever one is actually running.*

*Skilled players: If emotions are running without identification, take the Emotional Body into the 3-2-1 (Clean Up) first.*

## Card 7: Damaged Self in the Shaman
**Hex 15 · Modesty**
*Gate: Damaged Self · WAVE: Show Up · Trigram: Gen ☶ × Kun ☷*

You're feeling the weight of what this situation is carrying and not all of it is yours. Everything is landing because you haven't decided what to absorb.

The Damaged Self's gift in this territory: choosing what damage to absorb is itself the direct action — not acting on the field, but choosing to be genuinely moved by it. The mountain doesn't announce what shaped it. It holds — and from that holding, it can feel what's true.

**1.** Name the damage this situation is asking you to take — what will it cost you? *One sentence. Out loud.*
**2.** Choose: is this the damage worth taking — the kind that builds reading capacity? *Name the choice before you act, not after.*
**3.** Capture: what damage you chose, what it cost to absorb it, and what you could sense once you were stable enough to receive. *In the app, while it's still fresh.*

## Design Notes (from 6 GM analysis, 2026-05-20)

**What these cards have that V3 ally deck doesn't:**
- Oracle text names the shadow before the task — reader is recognized before directed
- 64 distinct combinations (gate × chapter) — personal resonance from earned deck
- Deep-link to 3-2-1 practice in the app
- BAR capture closes the loop — the card produces evidence

**What was borrowed from V3 ally deck:**
- Container discipline on every task (how long, where, with whom)
- Body-first enforcement (Task 1 is always somatic or resource-checking)

**Relationship to V3 deck:**
- V3 = on-ramp, standalone, pre-book, relational/group use
- Hexagram cards = advanced practice, post-book, earned deck, solo divination that routes to app
- These are sequential, not competing

**Design decisions locked (6 GM analysis, 2026-05-21):**

**Q1 — Difficulty levels: Don't add them.**
These are earned cards — players who reach them have done the reading. If a task is badly specified, fix the task. The Vulnerable Child card's "which mode is live right now" is the template for how a task meets the person where they are without difficulty labels.

**Q2 — Relational Task 2 variant: "Solo / With:" modifier, applied selectively.**
Not all 64 cards. Only gates that are intrinsically relational: Controller, Victim, and context-dependent on Challenger. Interior gates (Skeptic, Vulnerable Child, Damaged Self) stay solo. Format: one line beneath Task 2 — *Solo: [form]. With: [who and what].* Applies to Card 2 (Controller) and Card 5 (Victim) in this pilot.

**Q3 — Hexagram names: Two-tier rule.**
- *Actively misleads* (Marrying Maiden, etc.) → replace with MTGOA oracle name
- *Opaque but not misleading* (Biting Through) → keep traditional name + parenthetical: "(Forcing Through Resistance)"
- *Already evocative* (Peace, The Receptive, Return, Modesty, The Army, Darkening of Light) → keep as is
- Credit line for lineage: "(I Ching: [pinyin])" in small text, for readers who know the system
- Ch2 pilot names: all 8 are in the evocative/keep category — no replacements needed

**Q4 — Visual spec: Lock what's already working, design for physical first.**
- Current hierarchy is correct: Gate × Chapter → Hex ID + symbol → Oracle text → Tasks 1-2-3 → Skilled players
- WAVE chip near Skilled Players line (its function is routing to 3-2-1, not top-level navigation)
- Hexagram symbol (☰ ☷ etc.) in the card header — belongs
- Word count ceiling: oracle text 50 words max, tasks 30 words each max, total ~150 words physical / ~200 words digital
- Design for physical first; digital is promotable, reverse is painful
