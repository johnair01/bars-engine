# MTGOA Book Overarch — Six Game Master Analysis

**Cast statement:** What is the **one governing tension** the whole book teaches — the polarity readers are still holding wrongly when they finish Ch0 and still integrating when they enter Ch8?

**Not in scope:** Structural Gate×Chapter hexagram math. Not Scene Atlas pair labels. Not inferring chapter polarities from milestone copy.

**Context:** Phase A captured four craft-level pairs (GP-A1–A4). Hypothesis chapter pairs from chapter goals **did not land** (Wendell 2026-05-25). Ch1 polarity confirmed: **Forest ↔ Village**. Ch8: **stuck**.

**Hexagram 52 — Keeping Still:** The overarch isn't more motion. It's naming what must be held still long enough for the right move to become obvious — presence before performance, practice before verdict.

### Architect constraint on overarch

The overarch must answer: **what transformation is the same in every chapter, even when the Face changes?**

| Layer | Question |
|-------|----------|
| **Book overarch** | What dual capacity is the reader learning to **hold across the whole journey**? |
| **Chapter polarity** | What **one facet** of that holding does this Face teach? |
| **Gate / part** | Which interior voice **distorts** that chapter's facet? |

**Architect warning:** If book overarch = Forest↔Village, then Ch2–Ch7 polarities must be **Face-specific elaborations** of learn/apply — not six unrelated pairs. That may be elegant OR too constraining. **Wendell must choose.**

**Architect lean (book overarch):**

```
Performance (played allyship, game, optics, debt, courtroom)
        ↕ held across book ↕
Practice (presence, parts, Faces, BAR evidence, village moves)
```

## 🌊 Shaman — What does the body say?

### Felt truths from Phase A + manuscript

- Allies **rarely feel they're doing well** — somatic marker of performance victory (absence of stress mistaken for OK)
- **Forest** = descent; body knows before mind; gates are **felt** encounters
- **Village** = where the body must **return** with what it learned — apply without dissociating
- Ch8 rewrite gap: reader needs **somatic walk**, not another framework — polarity that **adds homework charge** fails Shaman test for Ch8

### Shaman verdict on overarch

**Primary felt candidate: Presence ↔ Performance**

Not "courage vs avoidance" (FSR violation). Behavior: **looking busy** vs **sitting until the move is obvious**.

Shaman dissents on **Care↔Impact** as overarch: readers feel care; they don't feel impact gap until late. The **earlier** felt wound is **never doing well enough** — performance anxiety, not care generosity.

**Shaman on Ch1 Forest↔Village:**

| Pole | Body signal |
|------|-------------|
| **Forest** | Threshold, not-ready, descent, parts activated |
| **Village** | Return, apply, others' faces, real stakes |

**This pair passes somatic check for Ch1.** Do not rename poles to abstraction — keep **Forest** and **Village** as pole labels if Wendell agrees.

## 📖 Sage — Principle underneath

**The book teaches: stop playing allyship as a game you can win, and start practicing allyship as a capacity you can evidence.**

- **Performance** = the unwinnable game (Ch0), the credential six-pack (Ch8 shadow), the busy avoidance (Phase A)
- **Practice** = BAR evidence, gate walk, Face toolkit, village moves, optional authored game (Ch8 gift)

**Forest ↔ Village** is the **spatial metaphor** for the same principle: you cannot apply in the village what you refused to learn in the forest.

**Sage warning on Ch8:** The Player is not a seventh Face. Ch8 may teach **transcendence of polarity-as-lesson** — the reader **composes** polarities into a game. Forcing Ch8 into one pair may confuse **integration** with **one more lesson**.

**Sage candidate for Ch8 (not validated):** Not a polarity — a **grammar shift** from *holding tensions* to *authoring a practice that holds them for you*.

If Ch8 must have a pair: **Credentials ↔ Authorship** (shadow Player vs Player) — but only if Wendell's body accepts it.

### Proposed book overarch (for Wendell somatic check)

**GP-BOOK: Practice ↔ Performance**

| Pole | Meaning in MTGOA |
|------|------------------|
| **Performance** | Played allyship — unwinnable game, optics, debt discharge, busy avoidance, credential Faces, choir preaching |
| **Practice** | Evidenced allyship — BAR capture, gate walk, somatic read, domain moves, aligned impact |

**One sentence:** The book moves you from **allyship as performance you can't win** to **allyship as practice you can evidence** — in the forest first, then the village, then (if you're called) a game of your own.

**Alternate label:** **Presence ↔ Performance** if Wendell wants GP-A2 vocabulary exact.

### Ch1 (validated by Wendell)

**GP-A-Ch1: Forest ↔ Village**

| Pole | Gift | Overreach |
|------|------|-----------|
| **Forest** | Inner learning, parts, gate walk, center | Never returning; infinite interior |
| **Village** | Application, others, real allyship moves | Performing in village without forest work |

**Relation to book overarch:** Geographic / narrative **first chapter** of Practice↔Performance — you can't practice in the village what you won't learn in the forest.
