# Calrunia Pantheon — Cosmological Reference

The layered cosmology of Calrunia's divine order. A single creator-emanation differentiates through force/light/dark polarity into the 5 EA channels, which the nations split into 15 Aspected Gods.

## The Six Layers

```
LAYER 1 — THE SOURCE
    │
    ├── Creator (the game master — Wendell)
    │   └── The unbroken beginning. Not a god. The author.
    │
LAYER 2 — THE THREE FOLDING
    │
    ├── Force (Yang / The Father / Initiation)
    ├── Light (Yin / The Mother / Receptivity)
    └── Dark (Neutrality emerges between Force and Light)
            └── Earth/Neutrality is not emptiness — it is the
                space in which Force and Light can meet without
                consuming each other.
    
LAYER 3 — THE 5 EA CHANNELS (from nation-practice)
    │
    ├── Metal/Fear — precision, crystallized will, sharpness
    ├── Water/Sadness — depth, going inward, the long memory
    ├── Wood/Joy — growth, reaching, expansion
    ├── Fire/Anger — passionate commitment, transformation
    └── Earth/Neutrality — structural patience, steady presence
    
LAYER 4 — THE 8 TRIGRAMS (latent cosmic grammar)
    │
    ├── 8 trigrams exist as pure potential in the cosmic grammar
    ├── They emerge from binary yang/yin differentiation
    ├── They pre-exist the nations — discovered, not invented
    └── They are the underlying structure the 5 nations ACTIVATED
        through their cultivation practice

LAYER 5 — THE 5 NATIONS (each activates 2 trigrams; **national orthodox pair** matches [CALRUNIA-Sect-Architecture.md](CALRUNIA-Sect-Architecture.md) / inner-garden [NATIONS_CANON.md](../04%20Quests/Campaigns/inner-garden/Calrunia%20Game%20World/NATIONS_CANON.md) — *2026-05-10: Meridia corrected to Earth+Mountain, not Lake+Mountain.*)
    │
    ├── Argyra → Metal/Fear → Lake trigram + Mountain trigram
    ├── Pyrakanth → Fire/Anger → Fire trigram + Thunder trigram
    ├── Virelune → Wood/Joy → Wind trigram + Thunder trigram
    ├── Meridia → Earth/Neutrality → Earth trigram + Mountain trigram
    └── Lamenth → Water/Sadness → Water trigram + Wind trigram

LAYER 6 — THE 15 ASPECTED GODS
    │
    ├── 5 nations × 3 aspects = 15 First Cultivators
    ├── Each nation has: Satisfaction god + Dissatisfaction god + Neutral god
    └── The gods are RECOGNIZED by nations, not created by nations
```

## Trigram Naming — Two Moments

**First naming (cosmic grammar, Layer 4):** The 8 trigrams exist as pure archetypal patterns — the universe's eight possible stance-responses to any situation. These are discovered through meditation and practice, not assigned by culture. They are: Heaven, Earth, Fire, Water, Mountain, Wind, Thunder, Lake.

**Second naming (Bars-Engine archetypes, Layer 5):** Each trigram acquires an archetype name that makes it playable in the bars-engine. These names are derived from I Ching tradition and are the names the sects use: *Danger Walker* (Water), *The Brave Heart* (Fire), etc. A practitioner of a trigram learns to inhabit that stance.

**The order of operations:** The trigrams exist first. The nations activate 2 each through their cultivation practice. The First Cultivators embody archetypes that pre-exist their nations. The archetype names are a later overlay — convenient language for the bars-engine context, not the original form.

## Which Came First — Cosmological vs Historical Resolution

**Cosmological order:** The trigrams pre-exist the nations. Layer 4 precedes Layer 5. The First Cultivators found what was latent and made it active.

**Historical order:** The five nations emerged through cultural divergence around the lake region (per CALRUNIA world spec, Age 1-2). The First Cultivators are Argyran by birth — they grew up in a specific cultural context. Their nation predates their individual practice chronologically.

**Both are true:** Cosmologically, the trigrams precede nations. Historically, the nations precede the First Cultivators' public recognition. The resolution: the First Cultivators were born into a nation that shaped their individual practice, but the trigram they embodied was always latent — available to anyone who found it, not dictated by bloodline.

**Practical implication:** Player cultivators born in Argyra can still pursue Mountain (stillness) or other trigram archetypes. National identity is cultural context, not cosmological constraint. A Pyrakanthi who pursues Mountain practice is doing something their nation doesn't officially recognize — but the trigram doesn't care about national borders.

## The Path to Godhood

Players can ascend through individual cultivation outside their national identity — the same path the First Cultivators walked. The 15 Aspected Gods serve as the template: what a cultivator becomes through sustained archetype-aligned practice.

**The two jobs simultaneously:**

Every practitioner — from their first day of practice — is doing two jobs simultaneously:
1. Returning the world to balance as they see it (Dissatisfaction gods pull practitioners toward this)
2. Attempting to transcend the world itself (Satisfaction gods pull practitioners toward this)

**What transcendence costs:** Those who transcend lose their direct ability to affect the world — they can no longer act directly on physical reality. But they gain influence over practitioners within their domain. They become the gods who are venerated.

**What remaining in the world costs:** Those who never fully transcend remain in the world — they hold power but cannot ascend to heaven. They are the sect leaders, the powerful practitioners, the ones who remember what the gods have forgotten about life in form.

**The dissatisfaction balance:** The world's Dissatisfaction gods are *structurally necessary*. The world must contain genuine dissatisfaction to have genuine satisfaction. These gods are worshiped precisely because their work is hard — practitioners who devote themselves to the Dissatisfaction path are committing to the world's harder work. Their practice is legitimate even though it leads away from transcendence.
