## Core Collision Thesis

The book, engine, and deck surfaces are converging around one loop:

> The book teaches the transformation, the deck gives it a handhold, BARs preserve the evidence, and bars-engine turns that evidence into an ongoing practice loop.

This means MTGOA is no longer only a manuscript project. It is becoming a multi-surface practice system where:

- **Book OS** holds meaning, canon, editorial state, and the deep teaching frame.
- **The manuscript** teaches the reader why the practice matters.
- **BARs** capture lived evidence of transformation.
- **Decks** give the reader a playable interface.
- **bars-engine** stores, metabolizes, routes, and extends the practice.
- **zo.computer** may be functioning as a design/execution surface whose outputs need to be synced back into the Library.

## Collision Surface 1: Reader Practice vs Engine Practice

### Local evidence

`SPEC_READER_FACING_TOOLS.md` says every reader-facing tool needs three phases:

- Phase 1: encounter
- Phase 2: first practice
- Phase 3: reference

The tool table marks key engine-adjacent tools as incomplete:

- Token System / bars-engine: Phase 2 missing, Phase 3 missing
- Ticket System: Phase 2 missing, Phase 3 missing
- BARs: only brief mention, no first practice format, no later references

### Collision

The engine and deck prototypes are already behaving as if BARs, cards, tokens, and quests are core practice surfaces. The book still has to decide how explicitly it teaches those surfaces without becoming a software manual.

### Naming rule

The book should introduce the practice without over-explaining the infrastructure.

## Collision Surface 2: Chapter 1 as Quest Contract

## Collision Surface 3: BAR Capture to Deck Pipeline

## Collision Surface 5: Deck Product Grammar

### Local evidence

| Application 52 | Moves in the field |

It also says:

### Collision

The Casey/Valkyrie party deck is not a side quest. It is a living example of named-person allyship as a deck product. It also proves that the 52-card application layer can carry public/private play, keepsakes, discovery, and social exchange.

### Naming rule

Do not treat gift decks as off-canon. They are prototypes of the product grammar.

## Collision Surface 6: Public/Private Social Play

### Local evidence

The Valkyrie party specs add:

- Card Mail: private card-to-person exchange
- Shared Altar: public posts, replies, reactions, offerings
- Personal Saves / Keepsake Deck: saved artifacts
- Discovery Deck: only seen cards become fully knowable

### Collision

The book says allyship is relational, consent-based, and trust-earned. The party deck implements public/private boundaries, consent norms, social witnessing, and keepsake artifacts.

### Naming rule

The social product layer is where allyship claims get tested. Privacy and consent are not edge cases; they are core mechanics.

## Collision Surface 7: Book as Mythic Gravity

### Local evidence

`MTGOA Resonance Pilot.md` says the book functions best as mythic gravity and symbolic coherence rather than as the primary product itself.

The Geoff and Todd story bible frames the book, deck, workshops, podcast appearances, and landing page as one ecosystem rather than separate projects.

### Collision

The manuscript cannot carry every transformation by itself. It may need to become the trust-building, meaning-making artifact that points into smaller practice containers.

### Naming rule

Do not force the book to be the whole product. Let the book orient the reader; let the deck/engine/workshop carry practice.
