# Unstuck Deck

## Current Decisions

| Topic | Decision |
|---|---|
| Product category | A guided practice deck, not a prompt deck or oracle-only draw. |
| Flagship structure | 52 cards: 48 Door/Gate journey cards plus 4 meta cards. |
| Public promise | For people who can name the pattern but still cannot move. |
| Entry flow | Start with a plain-language self-sort quiz that infers EA channel and Door together, then routes to the first Gate. |
| Surface language | Use "stuckness," "signal," "Door," "Gate," "practice," and "next honest move" publicly. Keep MTGOA, BAR, 321, Big Mind, and integral developmental language mostly in the companion/practitioner layer. |
| First offer | Quiz + digital deck + short practice guide + 321/BAR worksheets. The deck is the interface; the sellable offer is the guided practice journey. |

## Core Thesis

The Unstuck Deck is not "another digital deck."

It is a card-guided practice system for people who can recognize the pattern but still cannot move.

The deck uses:

- **5 Emotional Alchemy channels** to locate the flavor of stuckness.
- **6 Doors** to locate the developmental stance or stuck style.
- **8 Gates / Big Mind voices** to move through the practice journey.
- **321** to deepen the encounter.
- **BAR capture** to preserve the insight as reusable pattern language.

The product should feel simple at the surface and deep underneath.

## Core Promise

> You are not stuck because you are broken. You are stuck because a real emotional signal got trapped inside a protective pattern.

The deck helps the player:

1. Name the emotional channel.
2. Find the Door organizing the stuckness.
3. Move through the 8 Gates.
4. Use 321 to face, speak to, and become the pattern.
5. Capture the insight as a BAR or practice seed.
6. Leave with one honest next move.

## Public Positioning

Working public line:

> A guided deck for people who can name the pattern but still cannot move.

Alternate lines:

- If you can feel the pattern but cannot find the door, start here.
- Stop treating stuckness as proof against yourself. Learn to read it as signal.
- The card-guided practice path for turning stuck patterns into honest next moves.
- Draw the card. Find the door. Meet the voice. Capture the shift.

## Ideal Client Avatar

### Primary Avatar

The primary buyer is the emotionally intelligent, pattern-aware person who is tired of turning every stuck place into proof that something is wrong with them.

They have probably tried some combination of:

- therapy,
- coaching,
- journaling,
- personality systems,
- spiritual practice,
- productivity systems,
- somatic work,
- shadow work,
- self-help books,
- meditation,
- online courses.

They are not lacking language. They are often drowning in language.

Their pain is:

> I can explain my problem beautifully and still not move.

### Trust Fit

This client trusts Wendell because he does not flatten the complexity.

They trust someone who can hold:

- emotional honesty,
- pattern recognition,
- spiritual/mythic framing,
- practical next moves,
- integral developmental nuance,
- game/deck/interface design,
- humor,
- shadow without humiliation,
- rigor without sterility.

The client is not looking for a minimalist productivity coach or a glossy self-help influencer.

They are looking for someone who can say:

> Yes, this is a pattern. Yes, it has a mythic shape. Yes, your body is involved. Yes, there is a practical next move. No, we are not going to flatten it.

## Anti-Avatar

This product is not primarily for:

- people who only want cute affirmation cards,
- people looking for instant certainty,
- people who want a purely clinical therapy replacement,
- people who want productivity hacks without emotional work,
- people who want mystical interpretation without practice,
- people who are not willing to reflect or act.

## Product Shape

### Working Name

**The Unstuck Deck**

Secondary / subtitle options:

- Six Doors, Eight Gates
- A Card-Guided MTGOA Practice Journey
- A Guided Deck for Pattern-Aware People Who Still Cannot Move
- A Practice Deck for Turning Stuckness into Signal

### Core Architecture

The deck has three nested layers:

1. **Emotional Channel** - What flavor of stuckness is active?
2. **Door** - What developmental stance or protective pattern is organizing it?
3. **Gate** - Which Big Mind voice needs to be met next?

This gives the product replay value:

> The same 8 Gates become different journeys depending on the Door and emotional channel.

## Canonical Emotional Alchemy Channels

| Channel | Element | Dissatisfied | Neutral | Satisfied |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Fear | Metal | anxiety, panic, guarding, constriction | orientation | excitement, clarity, openness |
| Sadness | Water | grief, heaviness, distance, longing | acceptance | poignance, allowing, surrender |
| Joy | Wood | blocked joy, restless desire, manic reaching | appreciation | bliss, vitality, aliveness |
| Anger | Fire | frustration, rage, impatience | clarity | triumph, warmth, connection |
| Neutrality | Earth | apathy, numbness, flatness, stuck stability | balance | peace, grounded presence |

### Shame Clarification

Shame is not an EA channel.

Shame is what happens when the player identifies with the dissatisfaction state instead of reading it as signal.

The deck's job is not to "fix shame" directly. The deck helps the player translate stuckness back into signal so shame loosens its grip.

## The 6 Doors

The 6 Doors are the developmental stances. They are also market-facing stuck styles.

| Door | Stuck Style | Core Pain |
|---|---|---|
| Shaman | Over-attuned signal confusion | I feel everything, but I do not trust what I am sensing. |
| Challenger | Edited truth | I know the truth, but I keep softening it. |
| Regent | Heroic over-responsibility | If I stop holding everything, I am afraid it falls apart. |
| Architect | Protective system-building | I keep designing systems that protect me from the risk of moving. |
| Diplomat | Self-erasing bridge-building | I can see everyone's side except my own. |
| Sage | Disembodied understanding | I understand the pattern, but I have not embodied the next level. |

### Door Sales Language

Each Door should be sold as the best of the person trapped in a protective exaggeration, not as a personality diagnosis.

| Door | Best Self | Stuck Distortion | Public Invitation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Shaman | Sensitive, perceptive, attuned to what others miss. | Signal overload becomes self-doubt. | Trust the signal without becoming consumed by it. |
| Challenger | Honest, courageous, allergic to false peace. | Truth gets sharpened or softened until it cannot liberate. | Let the clean truth become a clean move. |
| Regent | Devoted, responsible, able to hold the center. | Stewardship becomes bracing and resentment. | Lead without carrying what no longer belongs to you. |
| Architect | Strategic, discerning, able to build real structure. | Design becomes a shelter from exposure. | Build enough structure to meet reality, then let reality teach you. |
| Diplomat | Relationally intelligent, able to bridge worlds. | Harmony becomes self-erasure. | Include yourself in the peace you are trying to create. |
| Sage | Wise, spacious, pattern-literate. | Witnessing becomes disembodied delay. | Let understanding descend into action, body, and choice. |

## The 8 Gates / Big Mind Voices

The 8 Gates are the journey sequence and Big Mind voice sequence.

Working Gate set from vault references:

1. Protector
2. Controller
3. Skeptic
4. Fixer
5. Emotional Body
6. Victim
7. Damaged Self
8. Vulnerable Child

These are not merely "card topics." They are the voices/thresholds the player meets as they move through stuckness.

### Gate Names

Use the Big Mind voice names as the working product language for now. If these names become too sharp for public sales copy, create a backlog item to test softer labels later.

| Gate | Public Frame |
|---|---|
| Protector | The part that armors against harm and protects what matters. |
| Controller | The part setting the standard for what is acceptable, safe, clean, or correct. |
| Skeptic | The part that sees, doubts, and tests before it lets you commit. |
| Fixer | The part trying to repair what hurts, or hold broken things in place so they do not change too fast. |
| Emotional Body | The body-level emotional signal before story, shame, or strategy takes over. |
| Victim | The voice seeking sympathy, protection, or rescue. |
| Damaged Self | The part that actually took the damage and knows what it survived. |
| Vulnerable Child | The unarmored source of feeling, learning, growth, play, exploration, and creativity. |

Decision: keep "Victim," "Damaged Self," and "Vulnerable Child" in the working Gate language for now.

Decision: use **Emotional Body** rather than **Fear** for Gate 5. Original Big Mind used the voice of Fear, but Emotional Alchemy expanded the work to all five emotional channels. To keep the process usable, those channels collapse into the Emotional Body gate.

Decision: keep **Emotional Body** before **Victim** in the Unstuck Deck product sequence.

Decision: public Victim language should explicitly name sympathy.

## Core Practice Loop

### Player Flow

1. **Arrive:** Player names where they feel stuck.
2. **Channel:** Player identifies the emotional channel or lets the product help classify it.
3. **Door:** Player chooses or draws one of the 6 Doors.
4. **Gate:** Player draws or proceeds through one of the 8 Gates.
5. **Card Practice:** Card gives a recognition prompt, pattern mirror, and practice move.
6. **321 Deepening:** Player faces the voice in 3rd, 2nd, and 1st person.
7. **BAR Capture:** Player captures the clearest pattern phrase or insight.
8. **Integration:** Player chooses one honest next move.

### UX Summary

At the surface:

> What kind of stuck are you?

Under the hood:

> EA channel + Door + Gate -> practice card -> 321 -> BAR seed -> next move.

## Recommended Entry Flow

The first product should not ask the player to understand the architecture before receiving help. The entry should feel like recognition, not taxonomy.

### Primary Flow: Quiz Infers Channel and Door

1. **Name the stuck place:** one sentence free-write.
2. **Choose the pain language:** player selects the line from the 30-state matrix that feels most uncomfortably accurate.
3. **Confirm body/emotional signal:** short EA channel confirmation using sensations and emotional tone.
4. **Confirm stuck style:** short Door confirmation using behavioral pattern.
5. **Route to first card:** recommended Door + first Gate card.
6. **Draw or proceed:** player can accept the route or shuffle within that Door.
7. **Practice and capture:** card practice -> 321 -> BAR phrase -> next honest move.

### Why Not Channel-First

Channel-first is elegant internally, but public users may not know whether they are in fear, sadness, joy, anger, or neutrality. They usually know the sentence that hurts. The matrix should be the entry surface because it lets the user say, "That is me."

### Why Not Door-First

Door-first is useful for returning players and fans of the system, but it risks feeling like a type quiz too early. The product should avoid making the buyer ask, "Which archetype am I?" before they feel helped.

### Secondary Flows

| Flow | Use Case |
|---|---|
| Quick Draw | Returning player already knows the stuck place and wants a card now. |
| Door Path | Player identifies strongly with one Door and wants a coherent 8-Gate journey. |
| Channel Path | Player is in a clear emotional state and wants practices for that channel. |
| Full Journey | Player moves through all 8 Gates for one Door/stuck place over several days. |

## Card System

### Core Card Count

Recommended flagship structure:

- 48 core journey cards: 6 Doors x 8 Gates.
- 4 meta cards:
  - Orientation
  - 321 Capture
  - BAR Capture
  - Integration

Total: **52 cards**.

This 52-card structure is earned by the architecture. It is not decorative.

### Card Anatomy

Each journey card should include:

1. **Door + Gate**
   - Example: Architect / Skeptic Gate

2. **Recognition Prompt**
   - A short question that helps the player recognize whether this card is active.

3. **Pattern Mirror**
   - A precise sentence naming the stuck structure.

4. **Practice Move**
   - One concrete action or inquiry.

5. **321 Prompt**
   - Face it, speak to it, become it.

6. **BAR Capture**
   - A prompt to extract one reusable phrase.

7. **Integration Line**
   - A sentence that carries the learning forward.

### Example Card

#### Architect / Skeptic Gate

**Recognition:**  
Where are you asking for more certainty before you allow yourself to move?

**Pattern Mirror:**  
You may be using design as a way to postpone exposure to reality.

**Practice Move:**  
Name the smallest test that would give you real information in less than 24 hours.

**321 Prompt:**  
- 3rd person: What is "the system" trying to prevent?
- 2nd person: Ask the Skeptic what it needs before it will allow movement.
- 1st person: Become the Skeptic and complete: "I keep you safe by..."

**BAR Capture:**  
What phrase names the pattern clearly enough that you could recognize it next time?

**Integration:**  
A system that never meets reality is not structure. It is shelter.

## Unstuck Avatar Matrix

The Unstuck Avatar Matrix defines 30 clickthrough pain states:

> 5 Emotional Alchemy channels x 6 Doors.

This matrix should drive:

- landing page copy,
- quiz outcomes,
- ad angles,
- email subject lines,
- deck entry routing,
- first-card recommendations,
- offer segmentation.

### Matrix Refinement Rules

Each cell should do three jobs:

1. **Recognition:** the player feels accurately seen before being advised.
2. **Dignity:** the stuckness is framed as pattern intelligence, not a defect.
3. **Motion:** the hook implies a Door, a signal, and a next honest move.

Avoid hooks that sound like generic affirmation, diagnosis, or moral correction. The best hooks should feel slightly uncomfortable and relieving at the same time.

### Fear / Metal

Fear stuckness says:

> I can see the risk everywhere, so I keep waiting for certainty.

| Door | Pain State | Clickthrough Hook |
|---|---|---|
| Shaman | I feel danger before I can explain it, then doubt myself for feeling it. | You are not too sensitive. You may be untrained in your own signal. |
| Challenger | I know something must be confronted, but I keep calculating every possible cost. | The risk you keep calculating may be the truth asking for a cleaner blade. |
| Regent | I cannot stop holding the system because I am afraid it will collapse without me. | If it only works when you are bracing, it does not work yet. |
| Architect | I keep designing more safeguards before I let the thing touch reality. | Your structure may be protecting you from the risk of movement. |
| Diplomat | I am afraid that telling the truth will cost me the relationship. | Peace built on self-erasure is not peace. It is deferred rupture. |
| Sage | I keep needing one more map before I trust the next step. | The map may be true and still not be the gate. |

### Sadness / Water

Sadness stuckness says:

> I know something is over, but I cannot cross into what comes next.

| Door | Pain State | Clickthrough Hook |
|---|---|---|
| Shaman | I can feel the loss underneath everything, but I keep treating it like noise. | The heaviness may be trying to tell you what mattered. |
| Challenger | I am tired of pretending I am fine, but I do not know how to tell the truth without burning everything down. | The grief under the fire deserves a voice before it becomes a weapon. |
| Regent | I keep carrying what is already dead because people still depend on it. | Stewardship includes knowing what can no longer be carried. |
| Architect | I keep redesigning the plan instead of admitting the old version is gone. | Sometimes the system is not failing. Sometimes the season ended. |
| Diplomat | I keep preserving relationships with versions of myself I have outgrown. | You cannot bridge your way back into a self you have already left. |
| Sage | I understand the lesson but have not let myself mourn the cost. | Wisdom that skips sadness becomes abstraction. |

### Joy / Wood

Joy stuckness says:

> I can feel what wants to grow, but I keep choking it with pressure.

| Door | Pain State | Clickthrough Hook |
|---|---|---|
| Shaman | I feel aliveness trying to move through me, then immediately mistrust it. | The spark is not irresponsible just because it arrives before the plan. |
| Challenger | I want more, but I keep turning desire into a fight. | Not every surge has to become a battle. Some are invitations. |
| Regent | I do not trust joy unless it has earned its place on the schedule. | A kingdom without vitality becomes a machine with nicer furniture. |
| Architect | I keep optimizing the living thing until it stops breathing. | If the system kills the spark, the system is not serving the work. |
| Diplomat | I keep muting what I want so no one feels disrupted by my aliveness. | Your joy is not an inconvenience to manage. |
| Sage | I can explain what wants to grow, but I do not let myself be delighted by it. | Understanding aliveness is not the same as being moved by it. |

### Anger / Fire

Anger stuckness says:

> I know what is not okay, but I keep swallowing the heat.

| Door | Pain State | Clickthrough Hook |
|---|---|---|
| Shaman | I feel the heat in the room but cannot tell whether it is mine, theirs, or old. | The heat may be a boundary signal, not a character flaw. |
| Challenger | I know the truth, but I keep editing it until it cannot change anything. | The truth you keep softening is the door. |
| Regent | I resent being responsible for everything, but I keep calling it duty. | Duty without boundary turns into quiet rage. |
| Architect | I keep converting anger into plans so I do not have to feel the force of it. | The plan may be avoiding the boundary. |
| Diplomat | I keep making conflict workable by making myself smaller. | If everyone is comfortable because you disappeared, the conflict is not resolved. |
| Sage | I understand why everyone did what they did, and now I cannot access my own no. | Compassion without fire becomes self-abandonment. |

### Neutrality / Earth

Neutrality stuckness says:

> Nothing is exactly wrong, but I feel flat, stalled, and weirdly unreachable.

| Door | Pain State | Clickthrough Hook |
|---|---|---|
| Shaman | I am so regulated that I cannot tell what I actually feel. | Calm can become camouflage when signal feels risky. |
| Challenger | I have no clean fight left in me, just dull resignation. | Numbness may be anger that has stopped expecting impact. |
| Regent | Everything is stable, but nothing feels alive. | Stability is not the same as sovereignty. |
| Architect | The system works, but I do not feel present inside it. | A well-run life can still be a hiding place. |
| Diplomat | There is no visible conflict, but I feel absent from the agreement. | Harmony that costs your presence is not harmony. |
| Sage | I can witness everything, but I am not participating. | The witness has become a waiting room. |

## Landing Page Spine

### Hero

**Headline:**  
For people who can name the pattern but still cannot move.

**Subhead:**  
The Unstuck Deck helps you translate stuckness into signal, enter through one of six Doors, move through the eight Gates, and capture the insight with 321 and BAR practice.

**CTA:**  
Find Your Door

### Pain Section

You are not short on insight.

You can explain the pattern. You can name the old wound. You can feel when something is off. You may even know what you "should" do next.

But some part of you still will not move.

That stuckness is not proof that you are broken. It is signal trapped inside a protective pattern.

### Mechanism Section

The deck works by helping you locate:

1. **The channel** - fear, sadness, joy, anger, or neutrality.
2. **The Door** - the developmental stance organizing the pattern.
3. **The Gate** - the Big Mind voice asking to be met.
4. **The practice** - the next honest move.

### Differentiation Section

This is not a prompt deck.

Prompt decks ask, "What do you think?"

The Unstuck Deck asks:

> What pattern is organizing your response, and what practice would let it move?

## Prototype Plan

- Define all 48 Door/Gate pairings.
- Draft 4 meta cards.
- Write one completed sample card per Door.

- Give 5-10 trusted people the self-sort and 3-5 cards.
- Ask:
  - Did the pain language find you?
  - Did the card feel like a prompt or a practice?
  - Did the 321 deepen the experience?
  - Did you leave with a BAR-like phrase or next move?

- Build a simple landing page.
- Test three hooks:
  - "For people who can name the pattern but still cannot move."
  - "Find the Door under your stuckness."

## Resolved Decisions

- **Gate names:** keep the Big Mind voice names, including Victim, Damaged Self, and Vulnerable Child, as working product language for now.
- **Entry flow:** quiz-first, using pain-language recognition to infer both EA channel and Door.
- **Quiz routing:** primary route by pain language, confirmed by body/emotional signal and stuck style.
- **Public architecture:** sell the practice journey. Keep MTGOA/BAR/321/integral vocabulary mostly in companion/practitioner layers.
- **First offer:** quiz + digital deck + short practice guide + 321/BAR worksheets.

- How should the Emotional Body gate explain its relationship to the original Big Mind voice of Fear in public-facing guide copy?

- Should the first paid version include guided audio, or reserve audio for the $47+ bundle?

## Working Conclusion

The Unstuck Deck sells the thing Wendell already does:

> helping people stop treating stuckness as evidence against themselves and start reading it as a signal with a Door, a voice, and a practice.

The product should bring out the best of each Door while staying legible to the person who just knows:

> I have done enough work to recognize the pattern. Why am I still stuck?
