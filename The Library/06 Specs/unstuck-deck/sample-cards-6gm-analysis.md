# Sample Cards 6GM Analysis

## Purpose

Review the six full sample cards through the 6 Game Master faces before expanding the rest of the 48-card matrix.

Reviewed sample cards:

- Shaman / Emotional Body
- Challenger / Vulnerable Child
- Regent / Fixer
- Architect / Skeptic
- Diplomat / Victim
- Sage / Protector

## Short Verdict

The sample cards prove the format can work.

The strongest cards are:

- **Shaman / Emotional Body** - best embodiment of signal before story.
- **Regent / Fixer** - clearest practical transformation.
- **Diplomat / Victim** - strongest dignity around sharp language.
- **Sage / Protector** - cleanest map-to-practice move.

The card most improved by recent correction is:

- **Challenger / Vulnerable Child** - now correctly about power as growth/play/learning, not protection.

The main global risk:

> The cards are strong, but some are long enough that the user may experience them as worksheets rather than cards.

The MVP card format may need a visible short path and optional deepening path.

## Shaman - Does The Card Contact Signal Before Explanation?

### Verdict

Strong.

The Shaman / Emotional Body card is the best proof of concept for the deck's promise. It begins in field/noise/body before analysis, and the EA channel table gives the player a usable way to locate signal.

### What Works

- "Where does everything feel alive at once?" is a strong Shaman recognition prompt.
- "The body is giving you a signal, not a sentence" is memorable and BAR-like.
- The EA channel table gives enough structure without over-explaining.

### Risk

This card may be doing a lot at once: field perception, EA sorting, 321, and BAR capture.

For a flooded Shaman user, too many choices can become more noise.

### Revision Note

Consider making the EA channel table collapsible or optional in the digital version.

Short-card path:

1. Locate the strongest body sensation.
2. Pick the least-wrong channel.
3. Complete one sentence.

Deep path:

- open the channel table,
- then do 321.

### Shaman Requirement

Every card should include one signal-locating move before interpretation.

## Challenger - Does The Card Actually Move?

### Verdict

Improved and directionally right.

The revised Challenger / Vulnerable Child card now correctly treats challenge as a developmental medium: power grows through contact, resistance, choice, refusal, competition, and being met.

### What Works

- "Where does your power want contact?" is a strong Challenger opening.
- "Challenge can be a playground, a classroom, and a proving ground" is the right conceptual move.
- The practice asks what the player wants to learn through the challenge, which keeps power developmental.

### Risk

The card may still be a little too clean and coach-like for Challenger altitude.

Challenger wants force. It should not become sanitized into "growth mindset." There needs to be a little more bite: power, winning, losing, refusal, risk, contact, testing strength.

### Revision Note

Add one sharper cost line:

> If you only dominate, you do not find out what your power can become.

Add one sharper practice option:

> choose one worthy resistance to meet directly.

### Challenger Requirement

Every Challenger card must include a real edge: what is being challenged, what power is being practiced, and what cost appears if the force becomes domination or self-attack.

## Regent - What Must Be Preserved?

### Verdict

Strong.

The Regent / Fixer card has the clearest Regent transformation: personal rescue becomes structure. It preserves stewardship and dignity while naming the cost of over-functioning.

### What Works

- "Where do you keep stepping in before the structure has to learn?" is excellent.
- "Your body becomes the missing structure" is one of the strongest lines in the sample set.
- The practice move gives concrete Regent choices: role, boundary, cadence, owner, promise.

### Risk

The card is useful but may feel operational compared to the emotional depth of other cards.

That may be appropriate for Regent, but the card could use one more line that names the grief/resentment/duty underneath repeated rescue.

### Revision Note

Add a small emotional check before the practice move:

> Before you fix it again, notice what emotion arrives when you imagine letting the break be visible.

This keeps the card connected to Emotional Alchemy without overloading it.

### Regent Requirement

Every Regent card should preserve what is genuinely worth stewarding while moving the burden from the user's body into a role, boundary, structure, or vow that can actually hold.

## Architect - Does The Structure Hold?

### Verdict

Strong but slightly familiar.

The Architect / Skeptic card works. It is clear, practical, and aligned with the matrix. The risk is that it may sound closest to familiar product/design coaching unless the card keeps the Gate voice alive.

### What Works

- The model/test contrast is clean.
- "The system becomes more coherent and less tested" is strong.
- The test table makes the practice immediately actionable.

### Risk

The Skeptic voice could be more present.

Right now the card explains the pattern well, but the Skeptic itself has less personality than the Victim, Fixer, or Protector cards. Since Skeptic is a living Gate, the card should let the user meet it more directly.

### Revision Note

Add a Skeptic encounter line:

> The Skeptic is not asking you to stop. It is asking you to stop pretending uncertainty can be solved by abstraction alone.

Or:

> Give the Skeptic evidence, not another model.

### Architect Requirement

Every Architect card should end in a reality test, feedback loop, decision rule, prototype, or visible sequence.

## Diplomat - Is This Safe Enough To Enter?

### Verdict

Very strong.

The Diplomat / Victim card handles sharp language with dignity. It explicitly names sympathy without shaming the player for wanting it, then moves toward authorship.

### What Works

- "Sympathy is real nourishment" is essential and good.
- "You may finally be understood as the one who disappeared, while still disappearing" is sharp and non-shaming.
- The practice move separates sympathy from authorship cleanly.

### Risk

This card may be emotionally intense for first-contact users.

That is not necessarily a problem, but the digital UX may need an entry affordance: "Go gently" / "Deepen" / "Just name the sympathy first."

### Revision Note

Consider adding permission language:

> You do not have to renegotiate the relationship today. Start by telling the truth about the term you did not choose.

This makes the move safer without softening the edge.

### Diplomat Requirement

Every Diplomat card must keep self-inclusion and consent visible. It should never ask the user to preserve contact by disappearing.

## Sage - What Principle Integrates?

### Verdict

Strong.

The Sage / Protector card does what Sage cards must do: it turns perspective into embodiment. The Protector-as-armor correction is well integrated here.

### What Works

- "Where has perspective become the safest place to stand?" is excellent.
- "You can explain the threshold without crossing it" is a strong Sage cost line.
- The 24-hour embodied step gives the card enough gravity.

### Risk

The card is intellectually beautiful, which is both its strength and its trap.

For Sage, elegant language can accidentally become the same armor the card is critiquing. The practice move helps, but the card should stay ruthless about embodiment.

### Revision Note

Add one grounding constraint:

> The embodied step must be visible to another person, measurable in the world, or felt in the body.

This blocks the Sage from turning "embodiment" into another private insight.

### Sage Requirement

Every Sage card should include a descent test: what principle becomes behavior, body, relationship, schedule, speech, or visible action?

## Cross-Face Findings

| Face | Sample Set Strength | Sample Set Risk | Global Adjustment |
|---|---|---|---|
| Shaman | Strong contact with body/signal. | Too many choices may become noise. | Offer short path + deep path. |
| Challenger | Corrected toward power-as-development. | Could become too polished. | Keep more bite, force, and worthy resistance. |
| Regent | Excellent structure-building move. | Could under-name the emotional burden. | Add a one-line emotional check. |
| Architect | Clear testable structure. | Skeptic voice could feel generic. | Make the Gate voice more encounterable. |
| Diplomat | Dignified handling of sympathy and self-erasure. | Emotionally intense. | Add permission without removing edge. |
| Sage | Strong embodiment move. | Beautiful language can become bypass. | Require visible/body/world action. |

## Card Anatomy Findings

The current anatomy works:

1. Recognition
2. Pattern Mirror
3. Pattern Intelligence
4. Cost
5. Practice Move
6. 321 Prompt
7. BAR Capture
8. Integration Line

But for product usability, the card may need two levels:

| Level | User Experience |
|---|---|
| Card Face | Recognition, Pattern Intelligence, Cost, One Move, BAR Phrase. |
| Deepening | 321, extra table, expanded practice options. |

This would preserve depth without making every card feel like a full worksheet.

## Overall Recommendation

Proceed, but do one tightening pass before writing more full cards.

The six samples are strong enough to validate the system. They also revealed the next level of craft:

> Each card needs to sound like its Door, not just describe its Gate.

That means future drafts should be reviewed by two questions:

1. Does the Gate's intelligence stay accurate?
2. Does the Door's altitude shape the card's voice, not merely its topic?
