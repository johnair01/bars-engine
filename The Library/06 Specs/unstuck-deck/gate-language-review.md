# Gate Language Review

## Purpose

Review whether the current 8 Gate language is strong enough for the Unstuck Deck before writing full cards.

This file reviews:

- public-facing Gate frames in [[SPEC]],
- working voice definitions in [[big-mind-voice-definitions]],
- stable functions in [[48-card-matrix]],
- translation rules in [[door-gate-writing-rules]],
- and source canon in `07 Book OS/07 Book OS/GATE_VOICES_CANONICAL.md`.

## Short Verdict

The Gate system is strong, but the current product-facing language needs a tightening pass.

The biggest issue is that some Gate frames describe the emotional vibe of the Gate instead of the actual mechanism of the voice.

The strongest Gate language should answer:

> What job is this voice trying to do, how does it overwork, and what smaller cleaner job restores movement?

## Findings

| Priority | Finding | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| P0 | Emotional Body source language still contains older emotion taxonomy in source canon. | Current Unstuck Deck EA taxonomy uses Fear, Sadness, Joy, Anger, Neutrality; source canon mentions Fear, Grief, Anger, Shame, Joy. |
| P0 | Public frames for Damaged Self and Vulnerable Child are too wound-centered. | Damaged Self is the part that survived damage, not merely wound identity. Vulnerable Child is the source of growth, play, learning, exploration, and creativity, not merely exposed tenderness. |
| P1 | Controller is too often framed as management rather than standard-setting. | The Controller's actual mechanism is deciding what counts as good/bad, clean/unclean, acceptable/unacceptable. |
| P1 | Skeptic needs to stay perceptual, not merely cautious. | Skeptic sees, doubts, classifies, wards, and eventually wonders. "Refuses false certainty" is good but incomplete. |
| P1 | Victim needs sympathy mechanics, not only agency language. | Victim organizes around sympathy as its own goal. It may use a harm story, but the story is not required for the Gate to be active. |
| P2 | Fixer needs the two meanings of fix. | Fixer repairs and holds in place. Without that distinction, the Gate becomes generic helpfulness. |
| P2 | Protector language incorrectly implies scanning. | Protector does not scan for threat. Fear, Skeptic, and the Emotional Body do the scanning; Protector armors against. |

## Canonical Gate Language Principles

Each Gate should be described with four layers:

| Layer | Question |
|---|---|
| Core Job | What is this voice for? |
| Shadow | How does it overwork or misassign itself? |
| Gift | What does it become when repurposed? |
| Card Aim | What should a card help the player do with this voice? |

## Proposed Product Frames

These were proposed replacements for the short public frames in [[SPEC]] and have been incorporated into the current spec.

| Gate | Former Public Frame | Updated Product Frame |
|---|---|---|
| Protector | The part trying to keep you safe. | The part that armors against harm and protects what matters. |
| Controller | The part trying to manage risk and outcome. | The part setting the standard for what is acceptable, safe, clean, or correct. |
| Skeptic | The part that refuses false certainty. | The part that sees, doubts, and tests before it lets you commit. |
| Fixer | The part trying to solve pain before it is heard. | The part trying to repair what hurts, or hold broken things in place so they do not change too fast. |
| Emotional Body | The raw emotional signal before story. | The body-level emotional signal before story, shame, or strategy takes over. |
| Victim | The place where agency went offline. | The voice seeking sympathy, protection, or rescue. |
| Damaged Self | The identity built around what happened. | The part that actually took the damage and knows what it survived. |
| Vulnerable Child | The young, exposed, undefended truth. | The unarmored source of feeling, learning, growth, play, exploration, and creativity. |

## Gate-by-Gate Notes

### Protector

Current language is usable, but "keeps you safe" is too broad for card drafting.

Important correction: Protector does not scan for threat. Its lieutenants do. Fear and the Emotional Body detect charge and danger; Skeptic doubts, classifies, and tests. Protector is the part that armors against.

Best phrase:

> Calibrated armor.

Writing guardrail:

> Protector cards should not ask the Protector to scan. They should ask what is being armored against and whether that armor is proportionate.

### Controller

This Gate needs a strong standard-setting frame.

The Controller is not just a manager. It decides what counts as right/wrong, clean/unclean, good/bad, acceptable/unacceptable, satisfied/dissatisfied.

Best phrase:

> The Controller sets the standard.

Writing guardrail:

> Whenever Controller appears, ask what standard is being enforced and whose life it serves.

### Skeptic

The Skeptic should not be flattened into caution or distrust.

It has a perceptual sequence:

1. See.
2. Doubt.
3. Classify.
4. Test.
5. Wonder.

Best phrase:

> Doubt becomes useful when it can be tested.

Writing guardrail:

> Skeptic cards should convert doubt into a question, test, or wonder move.

### Fixer

Fixer needs the double meaning of "fix":

- repair,
- and hold in place.

That double meaning is what makes the Gate sing.

Best phrase:

> You can fix conditions. You cannot fix a person.

Writing guardrail:

> Fixer cards should identify whether the player is trying to repair, hold in place, or steward conditions.

### Emotional Body

The product definition is good, but the source canon needs a taxonomy note.

Use current Unstuck EA channels:

- Fear
- Sadness
- Joy
- Anger
- Neutrality

Do not use the older source-canon set as product language:

- Fear
- Grief
- Anger
- Shame
- Joy

Best phrase:

> The body is giving a signal before the mind has a story.

Writing guardrail:

> Emotional Body cards should name the channel and what the channel protects or reveals before asking for action.

### Victim

"Agency went offline" is true but not enough.

The mechanism is sympathy. The Victim may tell a harm story, but it does not need a story for the Gate to be active. Sympathy is the goal.

Best phrase:

> Sympathy is real nourishment, but it cannot be the whole organizing principle.

Writing guardrail:

> Victim cards must explicitly name sympathy while preserving dignity. The player is meeting a Victim voice, not being accused of being a victim.

### Damaged Self

Current public frame is too identity-heavy.

The Damaged Self is not "the identity built around what happened." That is one possible shadow. The core is the part that took the damage and survived.

Best phrase:

> The part that took the damage and knows what it survived.

Writing guardrail:

> Damaged Self cards should honor resilience before challenging wound identity.

### Vulnerable Child

Current public frame is too fragile.

The Vulnerable Child is not only young, exposed, and undefended. It is the source of growth, play, learning, exploration, creativity, and genuine contact.

Best phrase:

> The unarmored source of growth.

Writing guardrail:

> Vulnerable Child cards should not over-focus on wounded tenderness. They should restore curiosity, play, learning, and exploration.

## Completed Spec Updates

1. Updated the public Gate frames in [[SPEC]] using the revised product frames.
2. Updated the Voice Summary table in [[big-mind-voice-definitions]] using the tighter summary.
3. Preserved the note in [[big-mind-voice-definitions]] that source canon contains older Emotional Body channel language, but Unstuck Deck uses the current 5-channel EA taxonomy.
4. Updated [[48-card-matrix]] Gate Jobs to preserve the mechanism of each Gate:
   - Protector: calibrated armor; protect without asking Protector to scan.
   - Controller: standard-setting.
   - Skeptic: seeing, doubting, testing.
   - Fixer: repair versus holding in place.
   - Emotional Body: EA channel signal.
   - Victim: sympathy-seeking to authorship.
   - Damaged Self: survived damage to calibrated risk.
   - Vulnerable Child: growth, play, learning, exploration.

## Resolved Review Questions

Resolved 2026-05-28:

1. Public Victim language should explicitly say "sympathy."
2. Damaged Self remains the public Gate name.
3. Vulnerable Child remains the public Gate name.
4. Product sequence remains Emotional Body before Victim.
