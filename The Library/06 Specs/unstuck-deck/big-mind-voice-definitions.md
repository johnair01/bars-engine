# Big Mind Voice Definitions

## Purpose

Give the Unstuck Deck a local reference for the 8 Gates / Big Mind voices so the cards can be written as encounters with living inner voices, not abstract topics.

Use [[door-gate-writing-rules]] when translating these voices through the 6 Doors' developmental altitudes.

## Important Canon Note

Original Big Mind used the voice of **Fear**. As Emotional Alchemy developed, Fear alone became too narrow: the practice needed to account for all five emotional channels. Working every emotion separately made the process too long, so the evolved MTGOA/book canon collapses the five channels into **Emotional Body**.

Use this working set for the Unstuck Deck:

1. Protector
2. Controller
3. Skeptic
4. Fixer
5. Emotional Body
6. Victim
7. Damaged Self
8. Vulnerable Child

Fear remains the historical doorway into this gate, but the product should use Emotional Body so the deck can work with Fear, Sadness, Joy, Anger, and Neutrality without turning the 8-Gate journey into a much longer process.

## Voice Working Definitions

### Protector

The Protector is the first survival voice. Its original question is: "Will I live?" It protects the self from others, the self from itself, and others from the self. In stuckness, it armors against too much and spends its life building walls, shields, and defensive protocols.

Important distinction: the Protector does not scan for threat. Fear, Skeptic, and the Emotional Body do the scanning. The Protector is kind of dumb in the precise way armor is dumb: it protects the body and armors against whatever it has been told is dangerous.

Its shadow is total armor. It can defend against harm with force but end with no creative energy left. In social or allyship contexts, this becomes self-policing, savior energy, or cancellation: the person knows how to prevent harm but not how to build contact.

Its gift is calibrated armor. The Protector becomes useful when it is relieved of the scanning job and given one specific thing to protect.

**Card questions:**

- What are you protecting that genuinely needs protection?
- What are you protecting that may actually need contact?
- What are you armoring against, and is that armor proportionate?

**Card move:** Move from total armor to one specific protection, boundary, or defended value.

### Controller

The Controller sets the standard. It creates categories of good/bad, right/wrong, acceptable/unacceptable, worthy/unworthy. It works for the Protector: once life is protected, the Controller decides how life should be lived.

Its shadow is control addiction. It can script the living thing until there is no life left in it. It often frames control as safety and can merge with the Protector so completely that enforcing standards feels like survival.

Its gift is strategic discernment. When repurposed, it can name what is working, what is not, what standard matters, and what can be released.

**Card questions:**

- What standard is being enforced here?
- Who set this standard?
- Does this standard protect growth, or does it protect control?

**Card move:** Give the Controller one specific standard to evaluate, revise, or retire.

### Skeptic

The Skeptic is the lieutenant of the Protector. It refines perception through doubt. Its root question is not "How do I stay cynical?" but "Is this really what it appears to be?"

Its shadow is the abjuration spell: doubt becomes a ward that banishes possibility before anything can enter the field. In stuckness, the Skeptic often aims at the self: "I know what I need to do, but I do not trust myself to do it."

Its gift is discernment. Mature Skeptic energy asks the question nobody wants to ask, spots the likely failure point, and turns doubt into wonder.

**Card questions:**

- What are you doubting specifically?
- What is the single most likely failure point?
- What would wonder ask that doubt cannot?

**Card move:** Convert blanket doubt into one precise question or test.

### Fixer

The Fixer tries to repair, restore, and make things better. The trap is that "fix" means both repair and hold in place. The Fixer can either over-repair what does not need repair or stabilize dysfunction because change feels too risky.

Its shadow is trying to fix persons, including the self. That is the category error. People can heal, learn, grow, and develop skills, but they cannot be fixed like objects.

Its gift is stewardship of conditions. The mature Fixer clears obstacles, repairs structures, builds containers, and then trusts the living thing to grow.

**Card questions:**

- Are you trying to fix a person, an identity, a skill, a structure, or a condition?
- What specific circumstance can actually be changed?
- What would support growth without controlling it?

**Card move:** Redirect from person-fixing to condition-stewarding.

### Emotional Body

In original Big Mind, Fear says, "This is not safe." In MTGOA book canon, this gate expands into the Emotional Body: the place where raw emotional signal arrives before story. For Unstuck, Emotional Body is the compression layer for all five EA channels: Fear, Sadness, Joy, Anger, and Neutrality.

Its shadow is unmanaged charge. The emotion acts before the player knows which signal is active. Fear forecasts, anger erupts or disappears, sadness collapses, joy pressures, neutrality numbs.

Its gift is signal intelligence. The player learns to locate the active channel, name what the emotion protects, and choose a response from the signal rather than from shame or reflex.

**Card questions:**

- Where is the signal in the body?
- Which emotional channel is active?
- What is this emotion trying to protect or reveal?

**Card move:** Name the channel and the protection before making the next move.

### Victim

The Victim seeks sympathy. It may tell a story of harm to get that sympathy and enroll others, but the story is not required. Sympathy itself is the goal.

Its shadow is the Drama Triangle. The Victim needs a Rescuer and a Villain. In allyship, this can mean seeking victims to rescue or becoming the victim of one's own allyship failure. Both keep agency offline.

Its gift is protagonist energy. Repurposed, the Victim becomes Hero: the one authoring their story, receiving sympathy without surrendering agency.

**Card questions:**

- Where are you seeking sympathy as the main goal?
- Who becomes the Villain if this story stays intact?
- What would change if you became the protagonist again?

**Card move:** Step off the Drama Triangle and name one authorship move.

### Damaged Self

The Damaged Self is not the story about the wound. It is the part that actually took the damage and survived. Its essence is "did you die though": not denial of harm, but proof of endurance.

Its shadow is indiscriminate survival. It takes all damage, necessary or not, and can let the Victim turn that accumulated damage into identity or drama.

Its gift is calibration. The Damaged Self knows the cost of contact. When paired with the Protector, it helps distinguish necessary difficulty from unnecessary harm.

**Card questions:**

- What have you already survived?
- What damage was necessary for growth, and what was merely depletion?
- What capacity did this damage actually build?

**Card move:** Honor the survival without making the wound the whole identity.

### Vulnerable Child

The Vulnerable Child is the source of creativity, learning, growth, exploration, feeling, and play. It is not merely the fragile part at the end of the process; it is the rightful leader of the repurposed gate system.

Its shadow is exile. When the Vulnerable Child is exiled, the person performs, complies, credentials, and manages instead of learning through contact. There is obligation without curiosity.

Its gift is natural growth. When the Vulnerable Child leads, every other gate gets a better job: Protector protects learning, Controller sets standards for growth, Skeptic doubts what blocks play, Fixer maintains the playground, Victim becomes storyteller, Damaged Self calibrates risk.

**Card questions:**

- What would you do if this were allowed to be learning?
- What are you genuinely curious about underneath the performance?
- What tender truth wants to participate?

**Card move:** Let one vulnerable, curious, playful move lead.

## Cross-Gate Mechanics

| Relationship | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Controller works for Protector | Standards are often attempts to secure survival. |
| Skeptic is Protector's lieutenant | Doubt is usually trying to keep something safe. |
| Fixer works for Controller | Fixing executes toward the current standard. Change the standard or fixing loops forever. |
| Victim works for Controller | Sympathy-seeking enforces standards and can recruit stories to preserve them. |
| Protector + Damaged Self calibrate Show Up | One armors against harm; the other knows what has already been survived. |
| All gates protect Vulnerable Child | The whole system becomes healthier when vulnerability, learning, play, and growth are allowed to lead. |

## Writing Rule For Cards

Every Gate card should do three things:

1. Let the voice make sense.
2. Name how the voice is overworking or misassigned.
3. Give the voice a smaller, cleaner job.
