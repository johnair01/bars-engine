# Buyer Needs to Card Requirements

## Purpose

Reverse engineer what each Unstuck Deck card must do from the needs that make someone want to buy the deck.

See also: [[buyer-needs-6gm-analysis]] for the Six Game Master alignment check on these requirements.

## Confidence

Strong enough for a first card-design pass.

Not yet validated enough for final sales claims. The next proof should come from testing the quiz, 3-5 sample cards, and landing page hooks with real pattern-aware buyers.

## Core Buyer Need

The buyer is not asking for a cute prompt, a new framework, or another explanation.

They are asking:

> I can recognize the pattern. Why can I still not move?

They buy because they need a guided practice system that:

- starts with felt recognition,
- names the stuck pattern without shaming them,
- shows the protective intelligence underneath the pattern,
- gives a small move that creates real contact with reality,
- helps them preserve the insight as reusable language,
- and gives them enough structure to return when the pattern repeats.

## Buyer State Before Purchase

| Buyer State | What They Need From The Deck |
|---|---|
| Emotionally literate but over-languaged | Less explanation, more exact recognition. |
| Pattern-aware but still looping | A route out of the loop, not more naming of the loop. |
| Tired of self-blame | Dignity: the pattern is protective intelligence, not defect. |
| Suspicious of shallow prompts | A card that behaves like a practice, not a question list. |
| Done with one-size-fits-all advice | Routing by Door, Gate, and emotional channel. |
| Afraid the stuckness means something is wrong with them | A reframe: stuckness is signal trapped in protection. |
| Wants action but not motivational pressure | One honest next move small enough to do. |
| Wants depth but not endless processing | 321 and BAR capture as contained deepening. |

## Purchase Triggers

The deck becomes attractive when the buyer has one of these moments:

| Trigger | Underlying Need |
|---|---|
| "I explained this beautifully and still did nothing." | Move from insight to action. |
| "I have done therapy/journaling/coaching and still circle the same thing." | A different mechanism, not more reflection. |
| "I know something is off but cannot tell what." | Diagnosis that feels exact. |
| "I keep treating stuckness as proof against myself." | Shame relief and signal translation. |
| "I do not need motivation. I need the right door." | Routing and specificity. |
| "I keep almost becoming the person I am trying to become." | Integration and next move. |

## What Every Card Must Deliver

Every journey card should satisfy six needs in sequence:

| Card Moment | Buyer Need | Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| Recognition | "That is me." | A prompt or mirror specific enough to create clickthrough-level recognition. |
| Dignity | "This makes sense." | Name the pattern intelligence before naming the cost. For most Gates this is protective intelligence; for Vulnerable Child it is developmental intelligence. |
| Diagnosis | "Now I see the shape." | Combine Door distortion + Gate voice mechanics into one pattern. |
| Contact | "I can meet this part." | Invite the user to encounter the voice, not override it. |
| Movement | "I can do one thing." | Give one small practice move with a real-world or body-level constraint. |
| Capture | "I can recognize this next time." | Extract a BAR-like phrase or next honest move. |

## Card Rejection Tests

A card is not strong enough if:

- it could appear in any generic shadow-work deck,
- it only asks a question and does not create a practice,
- it names a wound without naming the protective intelligence,
- it gives advice before the player feels recognized,
- it motivates instead of routes,
- it uses mythic language without a concrete next move,
- it makes the player feel broken,
- it treats the Gate voice as a problem to eliminate,
- or it cannot produce a useful BAR phrase.

## Reverse Engineering Formula

Each card job should be built from this formula:

```text
Buyer pain
  -> Door distortion
  -> Gate voice overworking or misassigned
  -> pattern intelligence
  -> smaller cleaner job
  -> one honest next move
  -> reusable pattern phrase
```

Short version:

```text
What hurts?
What protective or developmental pattern is organizing it?
Which voice is overworking or being exiled?
What job should that voice have instead?
What can the player do now?
What phrase helps them catch it next time?
```

## Door-Level Buyer Needs

| Door | Buyer Pain | What The Cards Must Help Them Buy |
|---|---|---|
| Shaman | "I feel everything, but I do not trust what I am sensing." | Trustworthy signal, less noise, one move before certainty. |
| Challenger | "I know the truth, but I keep softening it." | Clean truth, precise courage, less edited self-expression. |
| Regent | "If I stop holding everything, I am afraid it falls apart." | Stewardship without bracing, boundaries, structure that holds. |
| Architect | "I keep designing systems that protect me from the risk of moving." | Reality-tested structure, feedback, enough design to act. |
| Diplomat | "I can see everyone's side except my own." | Honest accord, self-inclusion, contact without self-erasure. |
| Sage | "I understand the pattern, but I have not embodied the next level." | Integration, embodied practice, one path through complexity. |

## Gate-Level Buyer Needs

| Gate | Buyer Need | What The Card Must Reassign |
|---|---|---|
| Protector | Safety without total vigilance. | From defending everything to naming the real threat. |
| Controller | Standards without life-killing control. | From managing the whole field to evaluating one useful standard. |
| Skeptic | Discernment without paralysis. | From blanket doubt to one precise question or test. |
| Fixer | Helpfulness without fixing persons. | From fixing identity to stewarding conditions. |
| Emotional Body | Feeling without being run by charge. | From unmanaged emotion to readable channel signal. |
| Victim | Sympathy without losing agency. | From drama-triangle story to authorship. |
| Damaged Self | Survival without wound-identity. | From indiscriminate endurance to calibrated risk. |
| Vulnerable Child | Tenderness without exile. | From protected absence to curious participation. |

## Matrix Implication

The current 48-card matrix is structurally sound, but the next refinement pass should rewrite each card job through this buyer-need lens.

The 6GM analysis adds one important guardrail: the matrix should expose the buyer-need logic before full card drafting, so each card can preserve dignity, name cost, and create one practical move.

Each cell should become:

> For a buyer in this Door, what specific stuck pain makes this Gate the voice they need to meet, and what smaller cleaner job would make movement possible?

## Product-Level Minimum Bar

For the deck to feel worth buying, the first 3-5 cards a user encounters must make them feel:

1. "This sees the thing I usually have to over-explain."
2. "It does not shame me for it."
3. "It knows why my usual tools have not worked."
4. "It gives me one move I can actually do."
5. "It leaves me with language I will remember."
