# Door/Gate Writing Rules

## Purpose

Prevent the 48-card matrix from treating the 6 Doors as personality flavors.

The Doors are developmental operating systems. The Gates are voices. A card is created by asking:

> What does this Gate voice become at this Door's altitude?

Not:

> What would this kind of person do with this topic?

Use this file as the bridge between [[door-definitions]] and [[big-mind-voice-definitions]] when writing or revising [[48-card-matrix]].

## Core Rule

Each card must preserve three layers:

| Layer | Question |
|---|---|
| Door | What developmental concern organizes reality here? |
| Gate | What voice is active, and what job is it trying to do? |
| Card | How is that voice overworking at this altitude, and what smaller cleaner job would restore movement? |

If a row feels generic, ask:

> Could this exact row be moved to another Door without much changing?

If yes, the Door/Gate translation is not specific enough.

## Pattern Intelligence Rule

The buyer-needs model asks every card to preserve dignity by naming why the pattern makes sense before naming the cost.

Do not force every Gate into "protection" language.

Use the more precise intelligence for the Gate:

| Gate | Intelligence To Name |
|---|---|
| Protector | Armor / protection intelligence. |
| Controller | Standard-setting intelligence. |
| Skeptic | Discernment / testing intelligence. |
| Fixer | Repair / condition-stewarding intelligence. |
| Emotional Body | Signal / channel intelligence. |
| Victim | Sympathy-seeking intelligence. |
| Damaged Self | Survival / calibration intelligence. |
| Vulnerable Child | Developmental intelligence: growth, play, learning, exploration, creativity, and contact. |

The prior Challenger / Vulnerable Child error came from using the universal "Protective Intelligence" slot too literally. That made the card describe the system protecting the Vulnerable Child instead of describing how the Vulnerable Child itself uses Challenger power to grow, play, learn, explore, choose, refuse, and be met.

## Door Translation Summary

| Door | Developmental Concern | Gate Translation Principle |
|---|---|---|
| Shaman | Survival, instinct, emotional signal, raw felt reality. | Gates operate through signal, charge, intuition, emotional flow, and the pre-verbal field. |
| Challenger | Power, force, confrontation, rank, agency. | Gates operate through challenge, aggression, dominance, self-assertion, and who gets to hold power. |
| Regent | Order, duty, continuity, stewardship, role. | Gates operate through responsibility, loyalty, burden, hierarchy, boundary, and what must be preserved. |
| Architect | Strategy, mechanism, systems, feedback, execution. | Gates operate through models, tests, designs, standards, constraints, and contact with reality. |
| Diplomat | Belonging, relationship, consent, mutuality, inclusion. | Gates operate through contact, agreement, harmony, conflict, self-inclusion, and relational field dynamics. |
| Sage | Integration, principle, synthesis, embodied wisdom. | Gates operate through perspective, coherence, paradox, practice, and the descent from map to embodiment. |

## Shaman Gate Translations

Shaman cards should read through emotional alchemy, signal intelligence, intuition, shadow, and the field before language.

| Gate | At Shaman Altitude |
|---|---|
| Protector | Armors against threat in the field after the Emotional Body, Fear, or Skeptic has marked something as dangerous. |
| Controller | Sets standards for emotional flow: how emotions should move in the self and the room, and how to stay aligned with EA without controlling other people's controllers. |
| Skeptic | Doubts emotions, signals, and intuition. The healthy move is doubting the interpretation without gaslighting the experience. |
| Fixer | Converts attunement into rescue or emotional labor. |
| Emotional Body | Receives raw charge and must sort it into channel signal rather than field noise. |
| Victim | Turns overwhelm into helplessness when the field feels too loud to act inside. |
| Damaged Self | Treats present signals as old sensitivity wounds returning. |
| Vulnerable Child | The unhurt one: the part that grows, learns, plays, explores, and meets the world before injury organizes the field. |

## Challenger Gate Translations

Challenger cards should read through power, force, clean aggression, confrontation, rank, and the right to exist with impact.

| Gate | At Challenger Altitude |
|---|---|
| Protector | Protects by challenging people, claims, limits, and threats. When blocked outwardly, it turns aggression against the self. |
| Controller | Sets standards for who may hold power, who must be challenged, what challenge counts, and what authority is legitimate. |
| Skeptic | Doubts aggressively and ego-centrically. It challenges claims, motives, and authority rather than hesitating passively. |
| Fixer | Tries to repair through confrontation, correction, pressure, or forcing the issue. |
| Emotional Body | Converts charge into force, attack, heat, triumph, or collapse after blocked aggression. |
| Victim | Feels dominated, silenced, or defeated, and may use injury as justification for counterattack. |
| Damaged Self | Carries the memory of punishment, humiliation, domination, or failed assertion. |
| Vulnerable Child | The young force of growth: the part that uses challenge, power, refusal, competition, and direct contact to grow, play, learn, explore, choose, and be met. |

## Regent Gate Translations

Regent cards should read through duty, role, loyalty, order, boundary, governance, and continuity.

| Gate | At Regent Altitude |
|---|---|
| Protector | Protects roles, duties, people, promises, and the continuity of what matters. |
| Controller | Sets standards for responsibility, propriety, hierarchy, timing, and who is allowed to hold what. |
| Skeptic | Distrusts delegation, change, or loosened authority when continuity feels at risk. |
| Fixer | Rescues the system, patches recurring breaks, and carries what structure should hold. |
| Emotional Body | Converts burden, grief, anger, fear, or resentment into duty before the feeling can be read. |
| Victim | Experiences obligation as captivity and loyalty as a trap. |
| Damaged Self | Organizes identity around endurance, being needed, or having survived the burden. |
| Vulnerable Child | The part that needs care, rest, support, permission, and relief inside the role. |

## Architect Gate Translations

Architect cards should read through structure, design, models, mechanisms, execution, tests, and feedback.

| Gate | At Architect Altitude |
|---|---|
| Protector | Protects through structure, planning, safeguards, and buffers from exposure. |
| Controller | Sets standards for design quality, variables, process, efficiency, and acceptable risk. |
| Skeptic | Converts uncertainty into tests, or into endless model-improvement before action. |
| Fixer | Improves the mechanism before meeting the living problem. |
| Emotional Body | Translates feeling into requirements, safeguards, contingencies, or system complexity. |
| Victim | Uses complexity, constraints, or system conditions to prove movement is impossible. |
| Damaged Self | Protects against repeating visible failure, humiliation, wasted effort, or broken trust in the system. |
| Vulnerable Child | The unfinished seed: the prototype, ask, sketch, experiment, or desire not yet ready but ready enough to learn. |

## Diplomat Gate Translations

Diplomat cards should read through relationship, mutuality, harmony, consent, bridge-building, conflict, and self-inclusion.

| Gate | At Diplomat Altitude |
|---|---|
| Protector | Protects belonging, relationship, safety, and peace, often by disappearing the self. |
| Controller | Sets standards for acceptable feeling, speech, tone, conflict, inclusion, and relational process. |
| Skeptic | Doubts the player's own read of imbalance to avoid unfairness, projection, or conflict. |
| Fixer | Repairs tension before the player's own need has entered the room. |
| Emotional Body | Swallows or translates charge to preserve agreement and belonging. |
| Victim | Experiences agreement as self-erasure and loses authorship inside the relational field. |
| Damaged Self | Anticipates rejection, exclusion, abandonment, or misattunement before present evidence arrives. |
| Vulnerable Child | The tender need that wants to be included in the terms of peace. |

## Sage Gate Translations

Sage cards should read through synthesis, perspective, developmental pattern, principle, paradox, and embodiment.

| Gate | At Sage Altitude |
|---|---|
| Protector | Protects coherence and perspective by staying in the witness seat. |
| Controller | Sets standards for completeness, integration, elegance, and whether the map is adequate. |
| Skeptic | Uses nuance, paradox, and elegant doubt to avoid false closure or commitment. |
| Fixer | Solves the pattern conceptually before inhabiting the lesson. |
| Emotional Body | Carries the charge the map has not metabolized. |
| Victim | Hides agency inside cosmic, systemic, developmental, or archetypal explanation. |
| Damaged Self | Builds identity around being the one who understands, integrates, or sees the pattern. |
| Vulnerable Child | The simple young truth beneath the synthesis, before it is explained into coherence. |

## Matrix Writing Checklist

For each Door/Gate row, check:

1. Does the row clearly belong to this Door's developmental concern?
2. Does the Gate still behave like the canonical voice?
3. Does the shadow pattern arise from the Door/Gate combination, not from a generic stuck person?
4. Does the pattern intelligence make sense at that altitude?
5. Does the smaller cleaner job repurpose the voice instead of eliminating it?
6. Does the one move create dignified movement in the buyer's actual stuckness?

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Correction |
|---|---|
| Treating Shaman as generic sensitivity. | Write through emotional signal, intuition, field perception, EA channel flow, and pre-verbal knowing. |
| Treating Challenger as polite truth-telling. | Write through power, aggression, challenge, dominance, self-assertion, and clean force. |
| Treating Regent as generic responsibility. | Write through role, duty, stewardship, continuity, hierarchy, and inherited burden. |
| Treating Architect as generic planning. | Write through system design, tests, constraints, feedback, and reality contact. |
| Treating Diplomat as generic kindness. | Write through mutuality, consent, self-inclusion, agreement, belonging, and relational conflict. |
| Treating Sage as generic insight. | Write through integration, principle, perspective, embodiment, and the risk of staying above the practice. |
| Treating every Gate as protective. | Name the Gate's actual intelligence; Vulnerable Child is growth/play/learning intelligence, not protection intelligence. |

## Current Known Corrections

These corrections are now canonical for the matrix:

- Shaman / Controller is about controlling emotions and emotional flow, including the felt interaction between one's own Controller and other people's Controllers.
- Shaman / Skeptic is about distrusting emotions, signals, and intuition without gaslighting the actual experience.
- Shaman / Vulnerable Child is the unhurt one who grows, learns, plays, and explores at this altitude.
- Challenger / Protector protects by challenging. Its aggression goes outward when allowed and inward when blocked.
- Challenger / Controller sets standards for power, challenge, legitimacy, and who is allowed to hold force.
- Challenger / Skeptic is aggressive and ego-centric doubt, not passive or neurotic hesitation.
