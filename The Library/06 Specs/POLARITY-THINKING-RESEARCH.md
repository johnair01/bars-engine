# Polarity Thinking — External Research & Application

**Purpose:** Document **external** frameworks for polarity / paradox / complementary opposites, and map them to BARs **without conflating structural mapping with governing teaching tensions**.

**Research date:** 2026-05-25. If a section says **INTERVIEW NEEDED**, external sources were insufficient to capture Wendell's formal rules — run `GOVERNING-POLARITIES-INTERVIEW-PROTOCOL.md` or a dedicated **formal mapping interview** (see §6).

## 1. Executive summary

| Finding | Implication for BARs |
|---------|---------------------|
| **Polarity ≠ problem** (Johnson, CCL) | Governing tensions (care↔impact, safety↔growth) are **managed**, not solved — matches Wendell's teaching frame |
| **Both poles are legitimate goods** | Aligns with FSR Polarity Rules + `VALUE_PAIRS_AUDIT` lint |
| **Problem-solving vs polarity-holding is itself a polarity** | Scene Atlas teaches structural literacy; books teach governing tensions — different jobs |
| **Developmental "polarities" (Blatt, Jung)** | Related but distinct: personality axes, not deck grid axes — do not dump into Scene Atlas pair tables |
| **I Ching = combinatorial topology of change** | Validates 8×8 exploration structure as **exhaustive state space**, not fortune-telling |
| **Wendell's FSR rules are stricter than Johnson** | No moral language, behavior not identity, ~10 min actionability — **governing audit uses FSR**; structural axes use Johnson lint |

## 2. External frameworks (with sources)

### 2.1 Barry Johnson — Polarity Management / Polarity Map®

**Core claim:** Many persistent challenges are **not problems with a single correct answer** but **interdependent pairs** that must be leveraged over time.

**Key distinctions:**

- Use **"and"** not **"versus"** between poles ([Johnson via Wendy Smith](https://wendyksmith.substack.com/p/the-tenacity-of-paradox-what-brene))
- Each pole has **upsides** when honored and **downsides** when over-emphasized
- **Polarity Map** quadrants: upsides of A, upsides of B, downsides of over-A, downsides of over-B ([Polarity Management](https://books.google.com/books/about/Polarity_Management.html?id=z9NMoCO9PncC), [CCL](https://www.ccl.org/articles/leading-effectively-articles/are-you-facing-a-problem-or-a-polarity/))
- **Early warning signs** when drifting into downside of one pole ([Robert Winter — How to Build a Polarity Map](https://robert.winter.ink/how-to-build-a-polarity-map/))

**BARs application:**

| Polarity type | Johnson fit |
|---------------|-------------|
| **Governing** (care↔impact, safety↔growth) | **Primary fit** — teach balance/rhythm, card rejection tests |
| **Structural axis labels** (Rising·Rooting, Spark·Restraint) | **Secondary fit** — lint only; labels need not carry full Polarity Map copy |
| **Prisma move tags** | **No fit** — different taxonomy |

### 2.2 Problem vs polarity (organizational literature)

**CCL / Johnson:** A **problem** can be solved and closed. A **polarity** is ongoing — you navigate ebbs and flows ([CCL](https://www.ccl.org/articles/leading-effectively-articles/are-you-facing-a-problem-or-a-polarity/)).

**BARs application:**

- **Graduation mechanic** (Friendcraft): expression of polarity management — friendship practice **ends** a chapter, doesn't "solve" safety
- **Show Up domains** (Allyship): four domains are **ongoing capacities**, not a checklist to complete once

### 2.3 Carl Jung — opposites, enantiodromia, transcendent function

**Core claim:** Psyche develops through **tension of opposites**; ego tends to **lean** one way, creating one-sidedness; integration requires holding tension until a third perspective emerges ([Managing Polarities PDF — Augsburg](https://web.augsburg.edu/~erickson/edc490/downloads/Managing_Polarities.pdf)).

**Enantiodromia:** extremes transform into their opposite ([Digital Dharma on I Ching](https://digitaldharma.io/up/i-ching-binary-code/)).

**BARs application:**

- **321 / somatic charge translation** — closer to Jungian holding than Johnson org maps
- **Not** a license to label structural grid corners as "shadow selves"
- **Governing cards** may reference swing/overreach without Jung jargon in player copy

### 2.4 Sidney Blatt — relatedness ↔ self-definition

**Core claim:** Personality development is a lifelong negotiation between **relatedness (anaclitic)** and **self-definition (introjective)** poles; pathology = exaggerated emphasis on one ([Polarities of Experience](https://books.google.com/books/about/Polarities_of_Experience.html?id=iwQRAQAAIAAJ)).

**BARs application (speculative — INTERVIEW NEEDED):**

- May inform **Relationships** line (parts-of-partner) more than Friendcraft or Allyship
- **Do not** map Blatt poles directly to Gate×Chapter without Wendell confirmation

### 2.5 Integral Life — polarities + development

**Core claim:** Integrating either/or with both/and is an **engine of adult development**; polarities shift expression by stage ([Integral Life — Growing Beyond Conflict](https://integrallife.com/growing-beyond-conflict-the-power-of-polarity-thinking/)).

**BARs application:**

- **WAVE / vMeme language** = developmental containers (`VALUES_AND_POLARITIES.md`) — **not** the same as grid axis pairs
- Six-face Sage lens: levels vs types vs lines — keep schemas separate

### 2.6 I Ching — yin/yang combinatorics

**Core claim:** 8 trigrams × 8 trigrams = 64 exhaustive states of change; yin/yang are **complementary modes**, not moral opposites; derived hexagrams (inverse, reverse, nuclear) add perspective ([Hexagram Guide](https://www.hexagramguide.com/en/articles/yin-and-yang-in-i-ching/), [I Ching Quest](https://ichingquest.com/en/blog/i-ching-hexagrams/en), [Master Sean Chan reference](https://www.masterseanchan.com/iching-64-hexagrams/)).

**BARs application:**

- **Exploration 64** uses I Ching as **exhaustive exploratory structure** — confirmed for MTGOA and Friendcraft (`I-CHING-EXPLORATION-STRUCTURE.md`)
- **Structural** layer only; hexagram names/images are locale, not governing polarity substitutes

## 3. Application matrix — two polarity types × two projects

### 3.1 Governing polarities (teaching tensions)

| | **Mastering Allyship** | **Mastering Friendcraft** |
|---|------------------------|---------------------------|
| **Primary pair (captured)** | Care ↔ Impact (GP-A1) | Safety ↔ Growth (GP-F1) |
| **Johnson frame** | Over-care removes impact — classic over-pole downside | Safety-seeking undermines mutual becoming |
| **FSR rules apply?** | **Yes** — card copy, rejection tests | **Yes** |
| **Research gap** | Debt vs gift, performance care, courtroom allyship | Async safety, play vs safety, provenance vs stats |
| **Next step** | Interview Phase A — allyship supplement | Interview Phase A — friendcraft supplement |

### 3.2 Structural polarity mapping (engine / book topology)

| | **Mastering Allyship** | **Mastering Friendcraft** |
|---|------------------------|---------------------------|
| **Application 52** | Practitioner/workshop deck (WCGS suits) | Quest deck (FRIENDCRAFT-10 grammar) |
| **Exploration 64** | Gate (8) × Chapter (8) — **mapped** | 8 × 8 I Ching — **structure confirmed, axes TBD** |
| **Scene Atlas role** | Structural literacy (onboarding) | Same — not Friendcraft-specific |
| **Axis source** | Book-fixed (Earlier + Later Heaven) | Interview + locale session — **not MTGOA copy** |
| **Johnson lint on axes?** | Trigram/chapter names audited in book OS | Required before trigram table locked |

### 3.3 What NOT to conflate

| Wrong move | Why it fails |
|------------|--------------|
| Map care↔impact to pair1, impact↔??? to pair2 | Governing ≠ Cartesian axes |
| Copy MTGOA gates into Friendcraft lower trigram | Same structure, different locale and semantics |
| Use Scene Atlas pair2 as Friendcraft book axis | Split provenance (nation/playbook) ≠ book architecture |
| Treat I Ching hexagram as "the polarity" | Hexagram is **cell address**; governing tension lives in card fields |
