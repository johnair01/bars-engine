# Daemon Development: Altitude × Depth

Daemons develop on **two axes**:

- **Altitude** (horizontal) — which GM face the daemon currently operates from: Shaman → Challenger → Regent → Architect → Diplomat → Sage
- **Depth** (vertical) — which layer of the daemon's inner architecture the player has accessed

Most players encounter the **Protector** layer of their daemon first. The deeper layers only reveal themselves as the player builds trust and does sustained shadow work.

## The Depth Stack (BigMind Architecture)

Based on BigMind facilitation framework. Each layer is a distinct voice within the daemon's psyche.

| Depth Layer | Core Job | Player Encounter |
|---|---|---|
| **Protector** | Keep the self alive. "I keep us safe." | First contact. Reactive. Often nonverbal or blunt. Humorous in its simplicity. |
| **Controller** | Set the standard. "I decide what life should look like." | Stage 1: behavioral rules. Stage 2: identity ideals. Stage 3: philosophical worldviews. |
| **Skeptic** | Check what's actually here. "Is this really what it appears?" | Discernment, not despair. Doubt as eyesight sharpening. Becomes cynicism when detached from present perception. |
| **Fixer** | Enact the Controller's standards. "I'll handle it." | The system's hands: mechanic, builder, problem-solver. Works in verbs. Trapped in endless improvement loop when Controller's ideals are impossible. |
| **Fear** (+ all emotional channels as voices) | Signal threat / opportunity. | Fear, Anger, Sadness, Joy, Neutrality — each as a distinct inner voice with its own job. Fear = "danger detected." Anger = "boundary violated." etc. |
| **Victim** | Hold the narrative of suffering. | Story-holder. Maintains the wound's meaning. Not pathological — it is the Damaged Self taking damage so the system can function. |
| **Vulnerable Child** | Be the original wound. | The core. The one the Protector is ultimately protecting. |

## Key Principles

**The Protector serves the Vulnerable Child, not the Controller.**
When a daemon's Controller becomes rigid, it's because the Protector still feels the Vulnerable Child is at risk.

**The Skeptic's horizon is only the present.**
Doubt without a present object = rumination, not discernment. When doubt applies to memory, hypotheticals, or future projections, another layer is speaking (usually Protector or Damaged Self).

**The Fixer doesn't know about the Damaged Self.**
The Fixer believes it can eventually fix everything. It doesn't know the Damaged Self's job is to *take* damage — so something will always need repair. This misunderstanding is the source of the Fixer's exhaustion.

**Controllers relax when respected, not confronted.**
You do not confront the Controller. You recognize it. Your tone: "You make sense."

**Cynicism is Skepticism without a present object.**
Skepticism anchored in perception = discernment.
Skepticism fueled by memory, fear, or expectation = cynicism.

## In-Game Implementation (Planned)

### Level 1 Daemon (current)
- Player encounters daemon in Protector mode
- 3-2-1 shadow work addresses the Protector → reveals the daemon

### Level 2 Daemon (planned — jouissance fork)
- After 3-2-1 completion, optional fork: player feels into what satisfaction the daemon is currently experiencing in its stuck pattern
- The daemon is already experiencing satisfaction (that's why the pattern persists)
- This is the Protector revealing its Controller (the daemon's standards for why this pattern is "right")
- Player's task: help the daemon share its satisfaction generatively rather than hoard it

### Level 3+ Daemon (future)
- Player encounters the Skeptic layer: learning to check reality vs. assumptions
- Player encounters the Fixer: the daemon learning to choose rather than compulse
- Emotional channel voices emerge as distinct sub-voices within the daemon
- Eventually: Victim and Vulnerable Child — the original wound becomes visible

## Facilitation Notes (for Shaman agent)

The BigMind facilitation sequence for daemons:
1. Speak to the Protector first. Establish safety. Be curious, warm, lightly humorous.
2. Ask: "What do you protect?" / "How do you protect?" / "How do you feel when you fail to protect the self?"
3. When Protector softens, ask it to step aside for deeper work.
4. Follow the depth stack — do not rush toward the Vulnerable Child.
5. Each layer reveals the next; do not force the sequence.

## Connection to Emotional Alchemy

The emotional channel voices at the Fixer/Fear depth layer map directly to the 5 Wuxing elements:
- **Fear** → Metal channel (risk/opportunity)
- **Sadness** → Water channel (something I care about is distant)
- **Joy/Delight** → Wood channel (vitality, aliveness)
- **Anger** → Fire channel (boundary violated or obstacle present)
- **Neutrality** → Earth channel (whole-system perspective)

When a daemon's emotional channel voice is activated, it can be routed through the emotional alchemy system for metabolization.
