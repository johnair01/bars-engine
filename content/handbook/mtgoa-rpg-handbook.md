# Mastering the Game of Allyship

## The RPG Handbook

Allyship is a practice, not a position. This handbook is the rules reference for
the tabletop and digital game — the four moves, the five channels of emotional
alchemy, the six superpowers, the four domains, and the encounter loop that turns
a stuck relationship into an alliance.

This handbook compiles the canonical Core Architecture. Numbers marked
*provisional* are balancing defaults; everything else is canon.

# The Core Loop

You play an ally meeting someone who is stuck. Every encounter runs the same arc:

- Read the live need. The other person has a hidden need in the moment; attune to it before acting.
- Bank trust. Moves that meet the real need bank trust instead of spending it.
- Dissolve shadows. Their stuck patterns surface as shadow cards; metabolize them.
- Convert to ally. Cross the threshold and they join you.
- Engage the four domains. Together, do the actual work.
- Capstone. Resolve the milestone that brought you together.

You do not win by being right. You win by helping someone become someone who can.

# The Four Moves

Every action is one of four moves — the BARs. They are the spine of personal
throughput: Clean Up your own state, Grow Up your capacity, Wake Up to what's
real, and Show Up where it counts.

- Wake Up — Expand the map. See what you couldn't see before; widen what's possible.
- Clean Up — Spendable energy. Metabolize your own charge into fuel you can spend.
- Grow Up — Gate permissions. Develop the capacity that unlocks harder moves.
- Show Up — Victory points. The visible act in the world. Show Up is how you score.

# Emotional Alchemy — The Five Channels

Emotion is fuel, not judgment. Each of the five Wuxing elements is a channel of
emotional energy with a quality you can spend.

- Wood 木 — Joy. Growth, expansion, vision. Generates Fire.
- Fire 火 — Anger. Action, confrontation, boundary. Generates Earth.
- Earth 土 — Neutrality. Center, mediation, holding. Generates Metal.
- Metal 金 — Fear. Precision, perception, clarity. Generates Water.
- Water 水 — Sadness. Flow, repair, depth. Generates Wood.

## The Generative Cycle

Wood feeds Fire feeds Earth feeds Metal feeds Water feeds Wood. When a move
generates a channel, it also generates one of the next element in the cycle —
energy compounds when you move with the grain.

## Compound Emotions

Some feelings are blends of two channels:

- Betrayal = Water + Fire (sadness + anger).
- Shame = Water + Metal (sadness + fear).

Reading the compound correctly tells you which channels a person is stuck in.

# The Six Superpowers

Your superpower sets your channel affinity, what you perceive, your
characteristic shadows, and a synergy bonus — triggered when you play three
matching-channel cards in one turn.

## Strategist — Metal + Earth
Shadows: Overcautious Analysis, Detached Competence, Analysis Paralysis.
Synergy: reveal one face-down NPC shadow card.

## Connector — Water + Wood
Shadows: Conflict Avoidance, Performed Warmth, Loyalty Bind.
Synergy: +2 to all relational moves this round.

## Storyteller — Wood + Fire
Shadows: Narrative Takeover, Performed Emotion, Story Collapse.
Synergy: next scenario card costs 1 less power to resolve.

## Alchemist — Water + Earth
Shadows: Spiritual Bypass, Premature Resolution, Holding Too Long.
Synergy: metabolize one NPC shadow card for free.

## Disruptor — Fire + Metal
Shadows: Righteous Detachment, Bulldozing, Confrontation for Its Own Sake.
Synergy: counter any NPC shadow card regardless of type.

## Escape Artist — Wood + Metal
Shadows: Premature Pivot, Avoidant Reframe, Clever Deflection.
Synergy: treat one exhausted domain as having one card remaining.

# The Four Domains

Once trust is banked and an ally has joined you, the work happens across four
domains. Each does a different job.

- Gather Resources — increase available capacity.
- Raise Awareness — increase accuracy.
- Direct Action — create change.
- Skillful Organizing — create stability.

Balanced campaigns move through all four. Over-indexing on Direct Action without
Gather Resources burns people out; Raise Awareness without Skillful Organizing
makes noise without durability.

# Stress, Contagion, and Shadows

Stress is the pressure in the room, tracked from 0 to 7.

- 3+ — Contagion threshold: your stress begins to dysregulate the other person.
- 5+ — Sympathetic: you can still spend, but you cannot receive satisfaction.
- 7 — Dysregulation: skip your action phase until the source is resolved.

## Shadow Activation

The other person's shadows activate as their stress rises:

- Stress 0–2: no shadows active.
- Stress 3–4: one shadow active.
- Stress 5–6: two shadows active.
- Stress 7: three shadows active.

## Stress Contagion

- You play a shadow as-is: +1 to your own stress (and it ripples).
- Your shadow lands on them: their stress +1.
- Their shadow lands on you: your stress +1.
- You metabolize their shadow: their stress -1.
- They make a light move toward you: your stress -1.

## Metabolize or Exile

A shadow is not a loss — it is raw material.

- Metabolize: pay the matching channel to flip a shadow into its light form (provisional cost: 1).
- Exile: pay more of the matching channel to remove a shadow permanently (provisional cost: 2).
- Playing a shadow unmetabolized works, but adds 1 stress.

# Converting an NPC to an Ally

Each stuck person carries six shadows. Metabolize three of the six and they cross
the conversion threshold — they stop being a scenario and become an ally who acts
with you across the four domains.

This is the heart of the game: you do not defeat people, you free them.

# Setup and Victory

- Starting deck: 12 cards — 5 light, 4 shadow, 3 free.
- Choose your superpower (channel affinity + shadows + synergy).
- Read the encounter's milestone and the live need beneath it.
- Victory: reach the Show Up target — 10 by default.

# Core Moves Reference

A shared vocabulary of moves you will reach for in nearly every encounter:

- Bear Witness — be with what is true without fixing it. Often counters performed compliance.
- Check In — ask into the live state instead of assuming it.
- Name the Feeling — make the emotional charge explicit and namable.
- Sit With It — hold discomfort without collapsing or bypassing.
- Name the Pattern — surface the recurring shape underneath the moment.
- Speak Up — the visible boundary or truth; a Show Up move.

# The Cast

The encounter ladder of people you can ally with. Each has a real need beneath
a surface ask:

- Marcus — Go back to what you didn't address.
- Yuki — Stay true without disappearing.
- Bev — Bring the organization back to itself.
- Theo — Make the invisible harm visible.

- Jerome — Make the ask before the 72 hours run out.
- Priya — Find what's still possible inside the constraint. The hardest test: compound stuck emotions (betrayal = sadness + anger), high starting stress, Diplomat shadow patterns. If the mechanics work for her, they work for everyone.

# Closing

The game creates the game. Every move you make here is a rehearsal for a move you
will make in the world — noticing a charge, metabolizing a shadow, banking trust,
showing up. Play it messy. Miss the mark. Level up. Try again.

That is how every real ally trains.
