# BARs: Private vs Public

BARs come in two forms: **private** (notebook) and **public** (spellbook). Understanding the difference helps you know when your notes stay yours and when they enter play.

## Private BARs (Notebook)

- **Unlimited** — Create as many as you want
- **Freeform** — No structure requirements, no scarcity
- **No compost, no destruction** — Private BARs never enter the compost heap or get destroyed
- **Your space** — Raw signal, drafts, personal notes

Private BARs are your notebook. They stay in your pocket until you choose to refine and publish.

## Public BARs (Spellbook)

- **Cards in play** — Public BARs enter the collective spellbook
- **Require refinement** — Before publishing, you shorten, structure, and categorize
- **Subject to scarcity** — Equipped slots are limited; compost and destruction apply
- **Shared signal** — Others can steward, play onto quests, and build on your BARs

## The Membrane Rule

Moving from **private → public** requires **refinement**. You can't dump a raw note into the spellbook. Refinement means: shortened, structured, quadrant-tagged. The membrane protects the collective from unfiltered noise while letting refined signal through.
