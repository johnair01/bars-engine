# BARS Engine Design Principles

Core doctrine for feature decisions. When in doubt, return here.

---

## 1. Shadow work should lead to play
The 321 process, character creator, and EFA are not administrative tasks.
They are the on-ramp to the game. Design them to feel like the game has already begun.

## 2. Preserve provenance and lineage
Every artifact — BAR, daemon, vibeulon, quest — must be traceable.
What created it? What charge did it come from? Who authored it?
Lineage is not auditing. It is the game's memory of how things became.

## 3. Extend ontology before inventing new objects
Check whether the concept already exists under another name.
Prefer extending existing models over creating parallel ones.
A second kingdom is always a mistake.

## 4. Route charge into action
Emotional charge without dispatch is incomplete.
Every session should have a path to: BAR, quest, daemon, vibeulon, or witness note.
The artifact IS the satisfied state.

## 5. Avoid parallel systems
One 321 flow. One archetype model. One nation discovery flow.
Merging is always better than multiplying.

## 6. Maintain honorable Game Master principles
- Only plan 2–3 beats ahead. Stay responsive to authentic motivation.
- Give the gift of context at the exact moment it becomes useful — not earlier, not later.
- Don't frontload structure. Let it reveal as the player descends.
- Honor the descent. Anabasis follows naturally.

## 7. Deftness matters — quality of transformation is part of gameplay
How a player moves through a 321 matters as much as whether they complete it.
Deep answers, genuine naming, specific fear-recognition — these produce better artifacts.
The system should eventually reward depth, not just completion.

## 8. The concierge principle
Expensive human coaching gives individualized pacing and perfectly timed context.
The agent system approximates this through:
- GM voice lines timed to scene transitions
- Progressive reveal (not all scenes shown at once)
- Session depth signals (longer answers → slower pace)
- Adaptive artifact suggestions based on what was actually said

## 9. Teal holds the privacy container
Privacy questions are not legal questions first — they are trust questions.
The system captures structure, not content. Tension vectors, not journal text.
This is a design choice with ethical weight. Explain it from a teal developmental frame.
The Sage authors the privacy page. The Steward reviews it.

## 10. The descent is not a detour
"Learning to descend into hell is often the fastest way to reach anabasis."
Shadow work, fear-naming, and mask dialogue are the fastest paths to growth artifacts.
The UI should honor descent through spaciousness, not accelerate past it.
