# Developmental Logistics

## Core BAR

> Growth increases complexity. Wisdom creates containers for complexity.

## Problem

The Integral response to increasing complexity often emphasizes growing up:

- Increase perspective-taking.
- Increase cognitive complexity.
- Increase integration.
- Hold more viewpoints.
- Expand the circle.

This works until it does not.

At a certain threshold, the issue is no longer whether a person can see more perspectives.

The issue becomes:

> Where does all that perspective live?

## Blue Sky Case Study

The Blue Sky experience can be framed as a psychospiritual kernel panic.

The failure was not ignorance.

The failure was not a lack of empathy.

The failure was not a lack of perspective-taking.

The failure was an overload of perspective-management.

Too many people, needs, concerns, futures, responsibilities, and possible worlds were being held inside one nervous system.

## Existing Integral Response

Grow capacity.

Increase complexity.

Become more integrated.

## Missing Response

Externalize complexity.

Create containers.

Let the deck remember.
Let the quest board remember.
Let the campaign remember.
Let the village remember.

## BARs Response

BARs provide persistent containers for developmental complexity.

They help turn overwhelming perspective into housed, sorted, actionable, relational artifacts.

BARs do not ask the nervous system to become the sole database.

BARs allow insight, charge, relationship, story, and obligation to be given a place to live.

## Distinction

Integral has spent decades solving for developmental complexity.

BARs may be solving for developmental logistics.

Developmental complexity asks:

> How do I see more?

Developmental logistics asks:

> Where do I put what I see?

## Implication for the Book

The BARs book should not merely explain a note-taking system.

It should show how to remain coherent while increasing complexity.
