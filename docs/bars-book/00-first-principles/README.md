# 00 — First Principles

This section houses the deepest assumptions of the BARs system.

Not frameworks.
Not practices.
Not chapters.

Laws.
Axioms.
Invariants.
Ontological commitments.

These are the statements that appear to describe how the system behaves regardless of implementation.

---

## Distinction

BARs are compressed artifacts.

First Principles are generative laws.

A BAR may be memorable.

A First Principle should generate many BARs.

---

## Candidate Laws

### Dreams Illuminate Shadows

Dreams do not create shadows.

Dreams illuminate shadows that were already present.

Meaningful dreams naturally generate charge.

---

### The Shadow Dividend

Every metabolized charge generates a BAR.

Identified charge may itself be a BAR Seed.

The shadow is where the BARs are.

---

### Charge Precedes Meaning

The nervous system often detects significance before the mind understands significance.

Capture first.
Understand later.

---

### Growth Generates Complexity

Growth increases complexity.

Wisdom creates containers for complexity.

---

### Domains and Moves

Domains tell us where transformation happens.

Moves tell us how transformation happens.

Every meaningful domain requires all five moves.

The weighting changes.

The capacities do not.

---

### Recursive Transformation

Dreams become quests.

Quests generate charge.

Charge becomes BARs.

BARs create wiser action.

Action transforms reality.

Reality generates new dreams.

---

### Recursive Development Principle

Development does not occur by completing the Five Moves once.

Development occurs by repeatedly applying the Five Moves at increasing levels of complexity.

The Five Moves are how we spiral.

The Game Masters are where we spiral.

---

### The Five Moves are Developmental Metabolism

Open Up.
Wake Up.
Clean Up.
Grow Up.
Show Up.

These are not categories.

They are capacities.

Together they metabolize charge into wisdom, action, relationship, and development.

---

### Orientation Over Information

Humans do not primarily suffer from a lack of information.

They suffer from a loss of orientation.

BARs preserve orientation across time, complexity, transformation, and uncertainty.

---

### The Soul is a World

The inner life behaves more like a world than a machine.

It contains:

- allies
- daemons
- quests
- campaigns
- landmarks
- villages
- talismans
- maps

BARs are artifacts of cartography.

---

## Rule for Inclusion

A statement belongs here when it behaves more like gravity than advice.

If removing the statement causes the rest of the ontology to collapse, it probably belongs in First Principles.
