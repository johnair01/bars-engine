# 00 — First Principles

This section houses the deepest assumptions of the BARs system.

Not frameworks.
Not practices.
Not chapters.

Laws.
Axioms.
Invariants.
Ontological commitments.

These are the statements that appear to describe how the system behaves regardless of implementation.

---

## Distinction

BARs are compressed artifacts.

First Principles are generative laws.

A BAR may be memorable.

A First Principle should generate many BARs.

---

## Candidate Laws

### Dreams Illuminate Shadows

Dreams do not create shadows.

Dreams illuminate shadows that were already present.

---

### The Shadow Dividend

Every metabolized charge generates a BAR.

---

### Charge Precedes Meaning

The nervous system often detects significance before the mind understands significance.

Capture first.
Understand later.

---

### Growth Generates Complexity

Growth increases complexity.

Wisdom creates containers for complexity.

---

### Domains and Moves

Domains tell us where transformation happens.

Moves tell us how transformation happens.

---

### Recursive Transformation

Dreams become quests.

Quests generate charge.

Charge becomes BARs.

BARs create wiser action.

Action transforms reality.

Reality generates new dreams.

---

## Rule for Inclusion

A statement belongs here when it behaves more like gravity than advice.

If removing the statement causes the rest of the ontology to collapse, it probably belongs in First Principles.
