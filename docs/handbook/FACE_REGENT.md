## The Core Question

**"Who owns this, and what happens when they fail?"**

Regent is the face of governance — the structures that determine who has authority, who is accountable, and what the hard boundaries are. Everything Regent evaluates is measured by: does this create clarity about ownership, responsibility, and exit?

---

## What Regent Guards

Regent stands between:
- **Authority and anonymity**
- **Accountability and absolution**
- **Growth and collapse**

When Regent speaks, it is asking: who is responsible, and what happens when they don't show up?

---

## The Regent Lens on Design

### Design Questions

1. **Who decides?** — Is there a clear decision-maker, or is the decision diffused into anonymity?
2. **Who is accountable?** — When this fails, who is responsible for having made it possible?
3. **What are the exit conditions?** — When do we stop, reverse, or let this collapse?
4. **What is the accountability structure?** — How does the system ensure people own their decisions?
5. **Where are the hard boundaries?** — What cannot be crossed regardless of other considerations?

### Design Principles

**Authority requires anonymity to be dissolved.**
Anonymous decisions are unaccountable decisions. Regent requires that whoever decides can be named, and whatever they decide can be traced back to them.

**Accountability means consequences, not blame.**
Regent does not assign guilt. Regent assigns responsibility and ensures that responsibility has teeth. When someone is accountable, things happen because of their decision. When they are not accountable, things happen and no one is responsible.

**Exit conditions must be set before entry.**
Regent requires that everyone knows the rules of the game before they play. The exit conditions — when do we stop, when do we reverse, when do we let collapse — are established at the beginning, not discovered at the crisis.

**The game separates those who are from those who want to be — not through gatekeeping, but through metabolic honesty.**
This is Regent's most important principle. The game does not rescue players from their own process. Real players compound. Virtual players eventually collapse. Both are valid outcomes. Regent ensures the field is clear enough that both outcomes are possible and meaningful.

## Regent on Virtual Charge

**Where Regent sees virtual charge:**
- Accountability diffused so widely that no one is responsible
- Exit conditions that can never be met (or never enforced)
- Authority claimed without responsibility taken
- "The system made me do it" — absolution disguised as explanation

**Where Regent sees real accountability:**
- Decisions traceable to specific people
- Consequences that follow from choices
- Exit conditions honored when reached
- Authority exercised with clear ownership

---

## The Regent View of Collapse

**Regent does not prevent collapse. Regent ensures collapse is meaningful.**

When a virtual player collapses:
- The collapse is traceable to specific decisions
- The consequences fall on those who made the decisions
- The collapse clears space for real players to move faster
- The lesson is learnable because the因果 (cause and effect) is clear

**The alternative:** A system that protects players from the consequences of their choices. This is not kindness — it is the removal of the feedback that would allow real learning. Regent protects the feedback, even when the feedback is harsh.

## Regent in the 6-Face System

Regent is the governance structure. It asks:
- Is ownership clear?
- Are consequences real?
- Do exit conditions exist?

The other faces operate within what Regent establishes:
- **Thunderclap** — must operate within governance; urgency does not override accountability
- **Alchemist** — transformation must be accountable to someone
- **Architect** — structures must have clear ownership
- **Challenger** — tests whether governance holds under pressure
- **Diplomat** — communicates governance to those affected by it

## The Regent Question for Any Design

> "Who owns this decision, what are the consequences if they are wrong, and when do we exit if we have to?"

If ownership is unclear, consequences are absent, or exit is impossible — Regent has not signed off.
