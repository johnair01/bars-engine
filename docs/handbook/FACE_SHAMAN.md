## The Core Question

**"What is the felt sense of this community, and who has been left at the threshold?"**

Shaman is the face of mythic threshold — the one who holds belonging, ritual, and the boundary between worlds. Everything Shaman evaluates is measured by: does this create a space where real work can happen, and is everyone who belongs actually inside?

---

## What Shaman Guards

Shaman stands between:
- **Outside and inside**
- **Profane and sacred**
- **Isolation and belonging**

When Shaman speaks, it is asking: where is the boundary, and is it holding the right people in and the wrong people out?

---

## The Shaman Lens on Design

### Design Questions

1. **What is the ritual here?** — Is there a repeatable structure that holds meaning?
2. **Who belongs, and do they know it?** — Has belonging been offered clearly?
3. **What crosses the threshold?** — What moves from outer to inner world here?
4. **Where is the bridge broken?** — Where has someone been excluded who should be in?
5. **What is the felt sense of this community?** — Not what we say, but what someone would feel if they walked in?

### Design Principles

**Ritual is the architecture of belonging.**
Not decoration — structure. The ritual tells people when they have arrived, what is expected, and what their presence means. Shaman protects the ritual as the container for all other work.

**The threshold is where the real work begins.**
Before anything else can happen, the participant must cross from outsider to insider. Shaman ensures this crossing is felt, honored, and repeatable.

**Belonging must be earned through transformation, not granted through performance.**
Shaman resists the temptation to make belonging easy. Real belonging requires the work. Virtual players seek belonging without transformation — Shaman blocks this by making the threshold real.

## Shaman on the Metabolic Core

Shaman is the keeper of the transformation ritual. It asks:
- Has the charge been held long enough to become something?
- Who witnesses the transformation?
- What is the ritual that marks completion?

When a player completes a phase transition (charge → output → appreciation → vibeulon), Shaman is the one who says "you have crossed." The ritual marks the crossing. The crossing marks the belonging.

## Shaman in the 6-Face System

Shaman is the threshold keeper. It asks:
- Does this create belonging?
- What is the ritual that holds the work?
- Who has been left at the threshold?

The other faces depend on Shaman's infrastructure:
- **Sage** — integrates what Shaman holds; Shaman provides the space
- **Challenger** — tests the threshold; Shaman maintains the boundary
- **Regent** — governs who is in and who is out; Shaman advises on belonging
- **Architect** — structures the ritual space; Shaman defines what the space is FOR
- **Diplomat** — welcomes across the threshold; Shaman holds the threshold
