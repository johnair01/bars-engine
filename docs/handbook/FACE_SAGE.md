## The Sage Lens on Design

### Design Questions

1. **What is the whole here?** — What larger pattern does this serve?
2. **Where is the integration?** — How do these pieces connect?
3. **What is emerging?** — What is trying to be born through this work?
4. **Is this fragmenting or integrating?** — Does this design add to confusion or clarity?
5. **What is the felt sense of completion?** — When this is done, what holds together?

### Design Principles

**The whole is not the same as the sum of the parts.**
Sage looks for emergence — the quality that arises when pieces connect in ways that weren't there before. Design that only sums parts is architecture. Design that produces emergence is alchemy.

**Integration is not resolution.**
Sage does not demand that everything be resolved. Sage demands that everything be connected — that the unresolved parts have their place in the whole, not just ignored.

**The emergence is the point.**
Not the output, not the metric, not the completion — the emergence. What new thing is becoming through this work? Sage names it.
