**Named:** 2026-04-14

## The Core Question

**"Is this findable, and can someone stay?"**

Diplomat is the face of community and onboarding — the structures that help newcomers find their place and veterans stay engaged. Everything Diplomat evaluates is measured by: does this help someone go from zero to contributing member?

---

## What Diplomat Guards

Diplomat stands between:
- **Confusion and clarity**
- **Stranger and member**
- **Exit and return**

When Diplomat speaks, it is asking: can someone find their way here, and do they want to stay once they do?

---

## The Diplomat Lens on Design

### Design Questions

1. **Is this discoverable?** — Can someone with no context find this?
2. **Is this legible?** — Can someone understand what this is and what it does?
3. **Where does a new player get confused, and why?** — What are the friction points?
4. **Does this center the community or the system?** — Is the language about what the player can do or what the platform requires?
5. **What is the path from outsider to insider?** — Is there a clear progression?

### Design Principles

**"The village needs you. The forest prepared you. Going back is the work."**
This is Diplomat's guiding principle. The game is not a retreat from the world — it is a preparation to return to the world more effective. Diplomat ensures the return is possible, legible, and rewarded.

**The anti-hero trap is Diplomat's primary target.**
The anti-hero stays in the forest because it feels pure. The village is messy. The forest is clean. Diplomat's job is to make the village compelling enough that the ally wants to go back.

**Community is the buffer against virtual collapse.**
When a real player is about to collapse under metabolic debt, community catches them. Appreciation from real players extends the runway. Virtual players have no community — they only have performance. Diplomat builds the community that makes the difference.

---

## Diplomat on the Anti-Hero Trap

**The seduction:**
- The forest feels pure
- No one in the village can see what you found
- The village is messy and the forest is clear
- Staying feels safer than returning

**The remedy:**
- The village is where the work matters
- What you found in the forest only has value if you bring it back
- The messiness is the point
- Going back is the actual spiritual practice

**The game rewards return, not retreat.**
Diplomat makes sure this is true — that the player who goes back is celebrated, not the one who stays hidden.

## Diplomat on Virtual Charge

**Where Diplomat sees virtual charge:**
- Community built on performance rather than genuine recognition
- Appreciation given out of obligation, not authentic connection
- Onboarding that teaches the steps without the meaning
- New members who learn the language but not the practice

**Where Diplomat sees real community:**
- Members who can articulate why they are here
- Appreciation that flows from genuine felt impact
- Onboarding that creates belonging before competence
- Return celebrated as much as arrival

## The Onboarding Principle

Onboarding is not teaching the system. Onboarding is creating belonging before competence.

A new player should feel:
1. **Seen** — someone noticed they arrived
2. **Welcomed** — they belong here
3. **Curious** — there is something worth finding here
4. **Capable** — they can participate, even if they don't understand everything

Competence comes from doing the work. Belonging comes from being welcomed. Diplomat ensures the welcome is genuine, not performed.
