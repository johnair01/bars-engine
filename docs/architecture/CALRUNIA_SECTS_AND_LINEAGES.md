# Calrunia Sects and Lineages

## Purpose

Calrunia needs separate names for sects, practitioners, founders, and practices. The trigram archetype names describe the people shaped by a sect; they are not the sect names themselves.

This document defines the first canonical naming layer for the eight trigram sects and shows how those sects intersect with local nation-born lineages.

## Naming Stack

| Layer | Meaning | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| Sect name | The institution, school, or broad spiritual tradition | The Open Mirror Sect |
| Practitioner archetype | What a trained person of that sect is called archetypally | Joyful Connector |
| Founder / saint | A historical figure who revealed a path through the sect | Verathane |
| Practice lineage | A local technique tradition, often nation-specific | velath-sequ |

The same sect can have many local lineages. A lineage can be colored by nation, emotional alchemy aspect, founder trauma, geography, or historical crisis.

## Canonical Rule

The eight trigram sects are universal schools of practice. Nations are origin cultures and emotional channels. Lineages are where a sect takes root in a particular nation or historical moment.

A character can therefore be:

- Lamenth-born by nation
- trained by the First Motion Sect
- archetypically a Bold Heart
- initiated through a local grief-to-momentum lineage

Those layers are complementary, not competing labels.

## Sect Notes

### The First Motion Sect

The Heaven school teaches initiation, creative force, and earned conviction. Its practitioners are called Bold Hearts when the training has shaped them into people who can move first without confusing speed for wisdom.

### The Holding Field Sect

The Earth school teaches reception, stewardship, and sustainable support. Its practitioners are called Devoted Guardians when their care can hold others without erasing themselves.

### The Clear Flame Sect

The Fire school teaches illumination, discernment, and the ethics of revelation. Its practitioners are called Truth Seers when they can show what is real without turning clarity into exposure.

### The Deep Current Sect

The Water school teaches depth, uncertainty, danger, and adaptive passage. Its practitioners are called Danger Walkers when they can enter what cannot yet be understood and return with what was found.

### The Breakpoint Sect

The Thunder school teaches charge, timing, decisive disruption, and system reorganization. Its practitioners are called Decisive Storms when they know the difference between urgency and the moment that has actually arrived.

### The Quiet Pressure Sect

The Wind school teaches gradual influence, patient direction, and invisible transformation. Its practitioners are called Subtle Influences when their pressure accumulates into real change without needing spectacle.

### The Still Gate Sect

The Mountain school teaches boundary, stillness, refusal, and disciplined omission. Its practitioners are called Still Points when their stopping comes from discernment rather than fear.

### The Open Mirror Sect

The Lake school teaches openness, shared experience, delight, and reflective joy. Its practitioners are called Joyful Connectors when their openness deepens connection rather than dispersing it.

## Local Lineage Notes

### Argyra Velath Lineages

Argyra has at least three known velath practices. They are not separate universal sects; they are Argyra-born lineages inside broader trigram sects.

| Practice | Founder | Sect Alignment | Description |
|----------|---------|----------------|-------------|
| velath-sequ | Verathane | Open Mirror / Lake | Complete acknowledgment that opens into clarity and shared experience. |
| velath-sil | Caeleth | Still Gate / Mountain | The still-cut: fear followed to clarity without panic or transmutation. |
| velath-torr | Kerath | Breakpoint / Thunder | Dread-cutting: seeing a bad future fully enough to close it before it roots. |

The future Sage-level synthesis of the three velath practices should be treated as a later Argyran development, not as the origin of the sect system itself.

### Pyrakanth Border Lineages

Cindrel's Complete Kindling is a Pyrakanth-born Water lineage. It is a border tradition because the practitioner begins from Fire/anger but is drawn into Water/depth as the only way to let the burn complete.

This makes Cindrel a strong example of nation x sect texture:

- Nation: Pyrakanth, anger becoming power
- Sect: Deep Current, danger and depth
- Practice: Complete Kindling, anger held until only the fire-tested remains

## Relationship to Archetype Data

The new trigram archetype data gives each sect four behavioral dimensions:

- Inner expression
- Outer expression
- Developmental spectrum
- Hexagram position

Sect names should be used when naming institutions, schools, lineages, temples, halls, vows, and training paths. Practitioner archetype names should be used when describing people, roles, NPC types, and player-facing identity.

Example:

> The Open Mirror Sect trains Joyful Connectors. Verathane founded an Argyra-born Open Mirror lineage called velath-sequ.

## Resolved Canon Notes

### Kerath Alignment

Kerath is canonically Breakpoint / Thunder / Decisive Storm. Her dread-cutting practice uses danger language because the Long Undoing behaved like water under pressure: accumulated dissatisfaction, distorted reflection, and catastrophic futures gathering weight. That danger is the crisis texture, not her sect identity. Her actual response is Thunder: a decisive interruption that closes a future before it can root.
