# What Player Success Looks Like in BARS Engine

> This document defines success in game-native terms — not feature usage metrics. It is the source of truth for player-facing copy, the handbook, and orientation messaging.

## One sentence

A successful BARS player **metabolizes emotional charge into real-world action** through a practice of noticing, processing, growing, and showing up — session by session, quest by quest.

---

## Success at Session 1 (arrival)

You have arrived successfully if you:

- Captured **one charge** — something that felt alive, stuck, or charged in you today
- Named an **emotion** and an **intensity** for it (even roughly)
- Left with a sense of "I know what this place is for"

**What success is not:** completing all quests, understanding every system, having a perfect character. Session 1 is a Wake Up — you are practicing noticing.

---

## Success at Session 10 (developing)

You are developing successfully if you:

- Have used **two or more moves** (Wake Up, Clean Up, Grow Up, Show Up) — not all perfectly, but at least started
- Have at least one **active quest** you are working on (not just queued)
- Have run the **321 process** at least once and felt something shift — even if slight
- Know what your **Nation and Archetype** are and can say one true thing about each

**What the felt shift feels like:** not necessarily breakthrough — sometimes just a loosening, a breath, a "huh." That counts.

---

## Success at 1 campaign (established)

You are established when you:

- Have **completed** at least one quest end-to-end (captured charge → metabolized → quest → done)
- Have contributed to **another player's work** in some form (responded to a BAR, joined a thread, held a quest for someone)
- Can describe your **current developmental edge** — what you are working on growing
- Have felt the game **reflect you back to yourself** at least once in a way that surprised you

**The game's job** is to make you more capable in the world. If you leave a campaign feeling more able to act on what matters, the game worked.

---

## The four moves as success markers

| Move | You are succeeding here when |
|------|------------------------------|
| **Wake Up** | You regularly notice charge before it dissipates; you can name what's alive with some accuracy |
| **Clean Up** | Emotional energy moves through you — it does not accumulate indefinitely or drive you blindly |
| **Grow Up** | You encounter edges of your own worldview and step through them (with effort, not comfort) |
| **Show Up** | You take action in the world that aligns with what you care about; quests become commitments |

---

## What success is never

- Doing the game perfectly or never being stuck
- Having the highest number of completed quests
- Outperforming other players (the game is not competitive)
- Having instant, dramatic transformations (steady accumulation is the form)

---

## Antifragile note

**Stuckness is data, not failure.** A player who hits a Roadblock Quest and metabolizes it has succeeded at something harder than smooth progress. The 321 process exists precisely for the moments when charge is too thick to move through alone.

A player who has learned to use their stuckness is more capable than one who has not encountered it yet.

---

## Commons note

Your BARs, threads, and campaign contributions are not private journal entries — they are **nodes in a network of creative and developmental work**. When you place a quest into a campaign or respond to someone else's BAR, you are contributing to commons. Success includes the quality of that contribution, not just your personal throughput.
