# WAKE-RA (see the truth)

## `WAKE-RA-SHAMAN` — What's Going Unseen
- **Question:** What's true in this room that nobody's naming?
- **Your move:** Name one thing present in the room that no one is naming.
- **The practice:** Before you amplify anything, find what's already here and unspoken. Point at it first.
- **Flavor:** *The unseen runs the room until someone points at it.* *(kept — it's great)*
- **Avoid:** Assuming everyone already sees it · Amplifying noise · Performing awareness
- **How it slips:** Blindness to the obvious · Seeing only what confirms you
- **Applications:**
  - *A meeting* — "The tension's obvious and no one's saying it. Be the one who names it: 'Something shifted when that landed — can we look at it?' Naming the unspoken is the whole move."
  - *A group you're in* — "Everyone's talking around the thing. Point at it plainly — no case, no villain, just 'here's what I'm noticing.' You don't have to solve it. Seeing it out loud is the work."

## `WAKE-RA-CHALLENGER` — What Doesn't Want Light
- **Question:** What's the room working hardest not to see — you included?
- **Your move:** Say the one thing everyone's agreed not to mention. Out loud.
- **The practice:** Catch the flinch — yours first — then name what the flinch is protecting.
- **Flavor:** *The flinch is the map. Follow it.*
- **Avoid:** Polite avoidance · "Not my place" as cover · Flinching from the hard thing
- **How it slips:** Collusion with the cover-up · Comfort chosen over truth
- **Applications:**
  - *At work* — "There's a pattern nobody names because naming it costs something. Be specific and calm: 'We keep routing the hard accounts to the same person — can we say that?' The polite silence was the problem."
  - *In your own head* — "Notice the truth you skip about your own role — the one that makes you wince. The wince is the tell. Go toward it, not around it."

## `WAKE-RA-REGENT` — What Deserves the Spotlight
- **Question:** Of everything you could point at, what most deserves the light right now?
- **Your move:** Pick the one truth worth the room's full attention. Give the rest a rain check.
- **The practice:** Spend attention like a budget — on the one that matters, not the loudest.
- **Flavor:** *Attention is the scarcest currency. Stop spending it on everything.*
- **Avoid:** Chasing every injustice at once · Attention for attention's sake · Centering yourself
- **How it slips:** Scattered outrage · Spotlight on the trivial
- **Applications:**
  - *The cause you brought with you* — "You care about ten things and move none. Pick the one where your attention changes an outcome this month; let the other nine wait. Scattered light warms nothing."
  - *Online* — "Your feed wants you outraged at everything. Choose the one truth you'll actually stay with and act on — and let the rest scroll by without your energy."

## `WAKE-RA-DIPLOMAT` — Whose Voice Is Missing
- **Question:** Who isn't in this conversation who should be?
- **Your move:** Name who's not in the room. Then make a seat, or carry their question in.
- **The practice:** Before you speak for a group, check who could speak for themselves — and clear the way.
- **Flavor:** *Read the room by who isn't in it.*
- **Avoid:** Speaking for others · Tokenizing · Ignoring who is absent
- **How it slips:** Amplifying yourself over those affected · Missing the silenced
- **Applications:**
  - *A meeting* — "A decision's being made about people who aren't here. Say it: 'Who does this affect that we haven't heard from?' Then don't answer for them — go get them."
  - *A group you're in* — "The same voices fill the space every time. Notice who's gone quiet and make a real opening — 'I'd want to hear where you land' — instead of speaking on their behalf."

## `WAKE-RA-SAGE` — The Pattern Beneath the Incident
- **Question:** This isn't a one-off — what pattern is it part of?
- **Your move:** Connect this one incident to the pattern it belongs to. Name the pattern, not the moment.
- **The practice:** Ask if this has happened before. When it has, you've found the system, not the incident.
- **Flavor:** *The incident is the symptom. Find the machine.*
- **Avoid:** Treating the symptom as the whole · Isolated outrage · Bypassing the pattern
- **How it slips:** Missing the system · Framing a pattern as a one-off
- **Applications:**
  - *At work* — "One person got talked over. Instead of a one-off correction, ask whether it keeps happening to the same people — and name that, because the pattern is the thing that changes."
  - *The cause you brought with you* — "You keep fighting the same fire in different rooms. Step back, name the structure that keeps lighting it, and aim there. Whack-a-mole is not a strategy."

# SHOW-RA (say / broadcast it)

## `SHOW-RA-SHAMAN` — Aim the Awareness
- **Question:** Where exactly is this going — which audience, which channel?
- **Your move:** Name the one audience and the one channel this truth goes to first. Then send it there.
- **The practice:** Pick a target before you broadcast. A message to everyone reaches no one.
- **Flavor:** *A message to everyone lands on no one.*
- **Avoid:** Broadcasting everywhere · Vague "spreading the word" · Aiming at "the public"
- **How it slips:** Noise with no target · A message to no one
- **Applications:**
  - *Online* — "You want to 'spread the word.' Stop. Name the one group who needs this and the one place they actually are, and put it there — sharp, specific, aimed."
  - *A campaign* — "The message is going 'out there.' Pick the single audience whose action matters most this week and write only to them. Widen later."

## `SHOW-RA-REGENT` — Keep the Truth Alive
- **Question:** What keeps this truth alive after the news cycle moves on?
- **Your move:** Set one recurring act that keeps this truth in view. Put it on the calendar.
- **The practice:** Build a drumbeat, not a fireworks show. One repeated act beats ten one-offs.
- **Flavor:** *Awareness that spikes and dies was never awareness. It was a mood.*
- **Avoid:** Post-and-forget · Performative one-offs · Abandoning the issue
- **How it slips:** Awareness that spikes and dies · A hashtag you forget by Friday
- **Applications:**
  - *A campaign* — "The launch spikes and fades. Set a weekly rhythm — one update, one story, one ask, every Thursday — so the truth stays in view when the excitement doesn't."
  - *Online* — "You posted once and moved on. Pick a recurring format you can sustain (a monthly thread, a standing series) and let repetition do what a viral moment can't."

## `SHOW-RA-ARCHITECT` — Build the Megaphone
- **Question:** What structure would make this travel without you pushing it?
- **Your move:** Add one structural lever: a series, a shareable artifact, or a list people can join.
- **The practice:** Build the thing that repeats without you. Heroics don't scale; structure does.
- **Flavor:** *Don't shout louder. Build the thing that repeats.*
- **Avoid:** Chasing one-off virality · Structure for its own sake · Confusing a loud day with a system
- **How it slips:** A message that can't scale or repeat · A viral day with nothing behind it
- **Applications:**
  - *A campaign* — "You keep re-explaining from scratch. Build one artifact — a one-pager, a toolkit, a template — that says it for you, so the next hundred people don't need you in the room."
  - *Online* — "A post did numbers, then nothing. Turn it into a repeatable structure — a named series people expect, a resource they forward — so reach compounds instead of resetting."

## `SHOW-RA-DIPLOMAT` — Amplify, Don't Speak Over
- **Question:** Whose voice should be carrying this — and are you amplifying or speaking over?
- **Your move:** Amplify one affected voice — with their consent — and name your own role plainly.
- **The practice:** Hand the mic, don't borrow it. Center the affected; say honestly where you stand.
- **Flavor:** *Amplify means hand them the mic, not borrow their story.*
- **Avoid:** Speaking over · Savior framing · Using others' stories without consent
- **How it slips:** Centering yourself · Extraction of testimony
- **Applications:**
  - *Online* — "You want to share someone's story. Ask first, credit clearly, link to them — so it amplifies their voice instead of routing their pain through your feed."
  - *A meeting* — "You could make the point for them, or make room: 'I think [name] has the real answer here — can we hear it?' The second one is the move."

## `SHOW-RA-SAGE` — What the Telling Leaves
- **Question:** When the attention's gone, what's left — a record, a shift, or nothing?
- **Your move:** Name the artifact that outlives the post — the documented account, the changed mind — and make it.
- **The practice:** Build for the archive, not the algorithm. Leave something that outlives the spike.
- **Flavor:** *The feed forgets. The record remembers.*
- **Avoid:** Ephemeral outrage · Leaving no record · The story untold
- **How it slips:** Attention spent, nothing retained · A big moment nobody can find next year
- **Applications:**
  - *A campaign* — "The moment will pass. Capture it in something durable — a written account, a documented win, a resource — so a year from now there's proof and a place to point new people."
  - *Online* — "Your best thread vanishes into the scroll. Move it somewhere permanent (a page, a doc, a pinned resource) so the truth has an address after the attention moves on."
