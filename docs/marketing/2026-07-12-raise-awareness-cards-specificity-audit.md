# Raise Awareness Cards — Specificity Audit & Improvement Worklist

> **Purpose.** Score all 30 Raise Awareness (`*-RA-*`) cards on specificity so we know *which
> cards need what* before authoring improvements — then quality-gate. Pure analysis; no card
> copy is rewritten here. Source: `src/lib/allyship-deck/move-library.ts` (`AUTHORED`).

## Rubric (max 7 — lower = more work)

| Axis | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Applications** — worked real-life examples (`applications[]`) | none | 1 | 2+ |
| **Remediation** — is "your move" one unambiguous, doable step? | restates the question / not actionable | doable but abstract ("how?") | concrete & doable |
| **Forbidden/Failure** — do the anti-patterns name specific, recognizable edges? | generic abstractions | mixed / thin (1 item) | vivid & specific |
| **Flavor** — a memorable line (0–1) | none | present | — |

## The headline finding

**29 of 30 RA cards have zero applications.** Only `SHOW-RA-CHALLENGER` ("Say the Thing")
has them — and it's the only 7/7 card. **Adding 1–2 worked applications to each card is the
single biggest specificity lever, and it's ~the whole job for 17 of the 30.** The other two
gaps are narrower: abstract/jargon remediations (~6 cards) and thin single-item failure lists
(~6 cards) — now more visible since the card renders "How it slips."

## The template to write toward — `SHOW-RA-CHALLENGER` (7/7)

Concrete remediation ("Say the thing today — specifically, to the people who need to hear it"),
a flavor line ("The naming is the first move."), and **two contextual applications** (A meeting;
Online). Every other RA card should reach this shape. Use it as the pattern.

## Cross-cutting findings

1. **Applications are the universal gap (29/30 = 0).** This is the improvement pass's core.
   For 17 "Light" cards, adding 2 applications *is* the whole improvement.
2. **The `GROW-UP × RA` sub-domain is systematically weakest.** Identity/growth cards trend
   abstract in both remediation and failure modes (GROW-RA-SHAMAN is the only Heavy; 4 of 6
   GROW cards are Medium). Treat GROW-RA as a cluster and calibrate its concreteness together.
3. **Six cards carry a single-item failure list** (OPEN-RA-ARCHITECT, GROW-RA-CHALLENGER,
   GROW-RA-ARCHITECT, GROW-RA-SAGE, SHOW-RA-ARCHITECT, and GROW-RA-SHAMAN) — now visible under
   "How it slips," so pad each to two.
4. **One jargon leak:** `CLEAN-RA-ARCHITECT`'s remediation ("Pick transcend / translate /
   neutralize") assumes vocabulary a first-timer doesn't have — reword or gloss inline.
5. **Only 2/30 have flavor** (WAKE-RA-SHAMAN, SHOW-RA-CHALLENGER). Optional, but cheap
   memorability; add where a line earns its place, don't force it.
6. **Remediations are otherwise strong** — 24/30 already score 2 on concreteness. The deck's
   "your move" discipline is real; the gap is worked *examples*, not the move itself.

## Recommended improvement sequence

1. **Pass A — applications for all 30** (the universal lever). Two contextual applications per
   card in the `SHOW-RA-CHALLENGER` shape (`{ context, example }`). Draft → **you re-voice** →
   run through the QA overlay (`buildCardPracticeOverlay`) → clear `no_quick_example`.
2. **Pass B — the 11 Medium/Heavy cards** (#1–#11): sharpen abstract/jargon remediations, pad
   single-item failure lists, calibrate the GROW-RA cluster.
3. **Pass C — quality-gate** all 30 through the overlay harness; clear `same_tool_collapse` and
   remaining flags; sign-off = gated.

**AI-allergy guardrail:** every applications draft and reword is a *candidate* for your voice —
you ratify before it lands in the deck.
