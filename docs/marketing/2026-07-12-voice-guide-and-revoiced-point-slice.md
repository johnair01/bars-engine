## What Chapter 0 confirms (beyond voice)

1. **The book is a Witness move.** It opens with confession (three years late; "it was making me a
   worse ally"), metabolizes the charge (the anger at the delay), and only *then* earns the Point
   (the definition, "you are not here to be good; you are here to keep playing"). That is the
   metabolize→express arc. **Lead with Witness, earn the Point** — the deck's content should mirror
   the book's own structure.
2. **Anti-performance is the spine.** The applause counter vs. the real prize; "the hollow ring of a
   man collecting tickets"; myth #7 "saying the right words." Honesty ↔ Craft is the book's core,
   not an import.
3. **The Six Faces = the deck's operations.** Same object. The deck is the book's engine.

## The Voice Guide (11 moves)

1. **Second person, present, direct.** "You are a Game Master." "You have done this before."
2. **Confession that implicates the author.** "I ran the machine from the inside; I ran it for three
   years while writing a book that told other people not to." Never lecture from above.
3. **Short sentences as landings.** "That was the beginner's game. You graduated from it years ago."
   "The only way to lose is to stop playing."
4. **The reframe move: name the trap, then flip it.** "Allyship looks like the honorable place to
   bring those questions, which is exactly the trap."
5. **Concrete over abstract, always.** Arcades, tokens, tickets, poker cards, the reorg three floors
   up. When an image is available, use it.
6. **Name the reader's hidden experience precisely.** "the low and steady kind that never switches
   all the way off."
7. **Anti-jargon, anti-fluency.** The book distrusts the right words; so should the copy. No "spread
   the word," no "center the marginalized" as a slogan, no therapy-speak.
8. **Parenthetical asides that break the fourth wall.** "(Yes, already. You just walked in and I'm
   handing you homework. Stay with me.)"
9. **Em-dash for the turn.** Long build — then the pivot.
10. **Warm command to close.** "So pick one." "Make the move." "Say it."
11. **No slogan stands unearned.** Every claim is paid for by a concrete or a confession — the
    opposite of performance.

**Diction bank:** metabolize · charge · fuel · the tank · the move · the game · the field ·
walk-away price · clean tiredness · player / play · home face · shadow.
**Avoid:** virtue language ("be a better ally"), activist boilerplate, marketing gloss, anything
that reads as a receipt for goodness.

## Re-voiced Point cards (candidates — your ratification)

### `WAKE-RA-ARCHITECT` "The Story That Could Land" · Point
*Card move: name the one fact that, if everyone saw it, would change things.*

- **MTGOA:** "Here's the one fact that changes the whole game: allyship was never a verdict on
  whether you're good. You've been grading yourself, and the emptier you got, the more you were sure
  the care must have counted. It didn't. You're not the defendant waiting on a verdict — you're a
  player who can stand up and build a different game. Say that to the people still grading
  themselves, and watch the shame drop."
- **Cascade Camp:** "The fact that changes who signs up isn't 'it's a great community.' It's [the
  one move a camper walks out able to make that they couldn't walk in with]. Name the thing they
  leave holding, not the vibe they arrive to."

### `SHOW-RA-CHALLENGER` "Say the Thing" · Point
*Card move: say the thing today — specifically, to the people who need to hear it.*

- **MTGOA:** "You keep softening it. Say it the way the book does — plainly, at your own expense:
  '[the sentence you've been hedging — e.g. "My allyship was about me for years. Here's what I did
  about it."]' — to the people who follow you because they want the real thing, not the
  performance."
- **Cascade Camp:** "Stop hinting people should come. Say the specific ask, name who it's for, name
  by when — the way you'd want it said to you, not the way a flyer says it."

> Note the voice shift from the earlier draft: shorter landings, the confession baked in, the trap→flip
> structure, no marketing gloss. That's the Chapter-0 register.

## Witness cards — the model is already in Chapter 0

`OPEN-RA-SHAMAN` and `GROW-RA-SHAMAN` stay **scaffolds** — but you don't need a new example of how to
do Witness: **paragraphs 1–13 of Chapter 0 are a worked Witness move.** The three-year confession,
the anger you wouldn't let yourself feel, the gap between who you said you were and what you did —
that *is* the register. When you fill the brackets, write them the way you wrote the opening: the
real moment, the real feeling, at your own expense.

- `OPEN-RA-SHAMAN` "What Seeing Costs" → what did seeing your own gap actually cost you? (Ch0 model:
  "it was making me a worse ally.")
- `GROW-RA-SHAMAN` "The Witness Growing in You" → the capacity you're mid-rep on, named at the edge,
  not from mastery. (Ch0 model: "I wrote a whole book about not doing this move, and I did it the
  entire time.")
