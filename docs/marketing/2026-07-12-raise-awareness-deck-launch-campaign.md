## The core mechanic: "Draw a card in public"

Every card in the Raise Awareness domain already has the three things a post needs:

- a **title** (the hook),
- a **`campaignQuestion`** (the public-facing prompt — already written for a campaign, not a journal),
- a **`remediation`** — literally captioned as **"your move"** (the concrete next step / the CTA).

So the reusable post is just:

```
🃏 Today's card: "{title}"

The question it asks a campaign:
"{campaignQuestion}"

Where I'm using it right now: {one real project — the launch, Cascade Camp, a client}

The move it names: {remediation}

Your move: {a low-friction quest — see below}
👉 {the ONE link: Zoom / Chapter 0 / register}
```

That's the "taste of the game." People experience the actual mechanic — a card meets a real
charge and names a move — without downloading anything. The card does the demo.

## Easy shareable quests (the low-friction "your move")

Quests must be completable in <30 seconds from a phone. Each one is also a piece of content
*you* get to reshare (the reply becomes tomorrow's raw material — this is the podcast-mining
trick, generalized).

1. **One-word charge.** "Reply with one word for what this brings up." (Lowest friction; high reply rate.)
2. **The unseen thing.** "What about [issue] do you wish everyone could see?" (Wake Up quest.)
3. **Tag the ally.** "Tag someone who's been a real ally to you — no explanation needed." (Reach quest; pulls in new eyes.)
4. **Draw-for-me.** "Drop your stuck project; I'll draw the card that names the next move." (You publicly play the deck ON them — the strongest "taste.")
5. **Bring-it-Friday.** "Screenshot this card and bring the charge it surfaces to the launch." (Converts a viewer into a registrant with homework.)

Rotate one quest per post. #4 is the flagship — it's a live, public demo of the whole product.
