## One engine, two channels

The deck drives both plays. Same act — *draw a card, apply it to a real thing* — in public
and in private.

- **Primary play: the direct personal invite** (low touch for you, high engagement for them).
- **Support play: the daily public post** (builds the recognizable "today's card" habit and
  countdown urgency).

The primary play is primary because for a Friday event with a warm audience, a personal
invite converts far harder than a cold feed post. Public posting alone was v1's mistake.

## PRIMARY: the personal-invite engine (low-touch / high-engagement)

**The problem with normal outreach:** 40 custom messages is high touch; one blast is low
engagement. The deck breaks the tradeoff.

**The move:** for each warm person, you write *one* personal line. The **Chapter 0 gift** and a
**hand-drawn card** carry the rest. It reads as hand-picked (high engagement) because it *is*
— but you didn't compose it from scratch (low touch).

**The 3-line template (re-voice before sending):**
> Hey [Name] — [ONE specific line: "I keep thinking about your work on ___"].
> I just finished the first chapter of my book and thought of you — here it is: [CHAPTER 0].
> There's a card in my deck that fits what you're navigating, "[Card Title]": [one-line move].
> I'm running a live launch Friday where we play the deck on real stuff — I'd love you there:
> [REGISTER]. No pressure either way.

**Why it's low touch:** the only bespoke part is the `[one specific line]` + which card fits.
You're *drawing from a deck you know*, not writing.
**Why it's high engagement:** it leads with a gift (the chapter), it's specifically for them
(the card), and the ask is soft.

**Batching so it stays low touch:**
1. List 20–40 warm people (people who already know you — the highest-conversion pool).
2. Group them by the *charge* they're carrying (3–4 buckets). One card per bucket → you pick
   from 4 cards, not 40.
3. Personalize only the opening line. Send 5–10/day, not all at once.

**Revenue tie:** the invite's ask is "come Friday" (warm, soft). Pre-order/deck/coaching get
shown *in the room*. Don't sell in the invite — invite to the room where you sell.

## SUPPORT: the daily public post ("draw a card in public")

Every RAISE_AWARENESS card already carries a public prompt (`campaignQuestion`) and a concrete
"your move" (`remediation`). **But the campaignQuestion is a strategy prompt, not a hook** —
so it goes in the *body*, and you write a real scroll-stopping first line yourself.

**Post shape (re-voice; vary it so it's not formulaic):**
```
[YOUR HOOK — a real first line, not the card's question]

Today's card: "{title}"
The move it names: {remediation}

Where I'm using it right now: {one real project — the launch, a client, Cascade Camp}
Your move: {a <30s quest}
👉 {ONE rotating CTA — see below}
```

**CTA rotation (this is the revenue engine — one per post):**
- **Buy the deck** — "this card is from my deck; get your own: [DECK LINK]" (tightest loop).
- **Pre-order the book** — the launch centerpiece: [PRE-ORDER LINK].
- **Book coaching** — for the "go deeper" crowd: [COACHING LINK].
- **Register for Friday** — the event: [REGISTER LINK].

Rotate so you're not asking the same thing daily. The deck CTA is strongest because the post
*is* a deck demo.

## Easy shareable quests (the <30s "your move")

Completable from a phone in seconds; each reply becomes your next post's raw material.
1. **One-word charge** — "reply with one word for what this brings up."
2. **The unseen thing** — "what about [issue] do you wish everyone could see?"
3. **Tag the ally** — "tag someone who's been a real ally — no explanation needed." (reach)
4. **Draw-for-me → coaching upsell** — "DM me your stuck project; I'll draw the card that names
   your next move." **Run this in DMs, not public replies** (publicly diagnosing someone on
   DEI terrain can feel exposing) — and it's your warmest coaching lead-in.
5. **Bring-it-Friday** — "screenshot this card, bring the charge it surfaces to the launch."
