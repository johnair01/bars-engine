# OPEN-RA (feel / receive the truth) — Witness

## `OPEN-RA-CHALLENGER` — The Truth You're Numb To
- **Q:** What truth have you gone numb to because feeling it would cost you?
- **Your move:** Pick the thing you've gone numb to. Let yourself feel it for real, then decide from there.
- **Practice:** Numbness is a choice you stopped noticing. Feel the thing before you act on it.
- **Flavor:** *Numbness is not neutral. It's a position.*
- **Avoid:** "Compassion fatigue" as excuse · "I can't with this" · Selective numbness
- **How it slips:** Chosen ignorance · Outrage with no real contact
- **Apps:** *A cause you scroll past* — "You've stopped feeling the thing you used to care about; it's easier that way. Let one story land for real before you decide it's too much. The numbness was the problem, not the caring." · *A hard truth about yourself* — "There's a fact about your own impact you keep at arm's length. Feel it for one minute without managing it. What you do next will be honest instead of defended."

## `OPEN-RA-REGENT` — Stay Awake to It
- **Q:** Can you keep this in view without burning out or looking away?
- **Your move:** Choose one truth you'll keep in view past the news cycle. Just one.
- **Practice:** Awareness is a muscle for staying, not a spike of feeling. Keep one thing in view.
- **Flavor:** *The hard part of seeing is not looking away.*
- **Avoid:** Doom-scrolling · One-day outrage · Abandoning the issue
- **How it slips:** Awareness that flares and fades · Attention with no staying power
- **Apps:** *A crisis in the feed* — "The story that gripped everyone last week is gone. If it still matters, be the one who keeps it in view — a note, a reminder, a check-in — past the moment everyone moved on." · *A long fight* — "You can't hold all of it. Pick the single thread you'll stay with when the outrage fades, and let the rest go so you don't burn out holding everything."

## `OPEN-RA-ARCHITECT` — The Energy in the Story
- **Q:** What will-to-act is buried inside this hard truth?
- **Your move:** Name the energy this truth gives you — anger, grief, resolve — and point it at one action.
- **Practice:** A hard truth is fuel, not just weight. Find the charge in it and aim it.
- **Flavor:** *Despair is awareness with nowhere to go.*
- **Avoid:** Hoarding outrage · Despair as the endpoint · Information with no mobilizing energy
- **How it slips:** Awareness that deadens instead of activates · Outrage that never becomes a move
- **Apps:** *After reading the news* — "The story left you heavy and stuck. Instead of carrying it as dread, name what it makes you want to do — then do the smallest version today. Weight becomes fuel when it moves." · *A campaign that's flagging* — "Everyone knows the problem and nobody's moving. Find the live wire in the awareness — the thing people actually feel — and build the next action off that, not off more facts."

## `OPEN-RA-DIPLOMAT` — Hold the Pain With Care
- **Q:** How do you hold someone's pain without turning it into content?
- **Your move:** Relate to one person's reality as a person, not a case. Ask, don't broadcast.
- **Practice:** Care is specific. Meet one real person where they are before you speak on their behalf.
- **Flavor:** *Pity looks down. Care looks across.*
- **Avoid:** Trauma as content · Pity · Centering your feelings over theirs
- **How it slips:** Extractive empathy · Performing care
- **Apps:** *Someone shares something hard* — "Your instinct is to relate it back to yourself or post about it. Instead, stay with them — 'that sounds heavy, what do you need?' Care is a question, not a repost." · *A community you're supporting* — "You could turn their struggle into your advocacy content. Don't. Ask how they want to be represented, and let that shape what you say — or whether you say anything."

## `OPEN-RA-SAGE` — When You Let It Be True
- **Q:** What gets clear when you stop arguing with the truth and just watch it?
- **Your move:** Watch the hard truth for three breaths without rebutting it. Then note what you see.
- **Practice:** Stop debating reality. Witness it first — clarity comes after you stop fighting.
- **Flavor:** *You can't see clearly what you're still arguing with.*
- **Avoid:** Forcing acceptance · Debate as avoidance · Spiritual bypass
- **How it slips:** Analysis instead of witness · Arguing reality away
- **Apps:** *A truth you keep debating* — "You've got ten counterarguments ready. Set them down for three breaths and let it be true. What you notice in that gap is the thing the arguing was hiding." · *A conflict you're in* — "You're marshaling your case. Before you fire it off, witness what's actually happening — including your own part — without spinning it. The clarity changes what you send."

# CLEAN-RA (metabolize / aim the charge) — hinge

## `CLEAN-RA-SHAMAN` — Name the Awareness Feeling
- **Q:** What's actually driving this — anger, grief, fear, numbness, or reach?
- **Your move:** Name the channel out loud before you publish a single word.
- **Practice:** Find the feeling under the message. You can't aim a charge you won't name.
- **Flavor:** *Every hot take has a feeling driving it. Name yours.*
- **Avoid:** Messaging from raw charge · Assuming it's "just the facts" · Calling your anger "objectivity"
- **How it slips:** Rage-posting · Grief dumped as advocacy
- **Apps:** *Before you post* — "You're about to fire off a take. Name what's actually driving it — anger? fear? — because the feeling leaks into the words whether you name it or not. Named, you can aim it." · *A heated thread* — "You're typing fast and hot. Label the channel: 'this is grief,' 'this is fear.' The label doesn't kill the energy — it lets you use it instead of spray it."

## `CLEAN-RA-CHALLENGER` — The Story About the Truth
- **Q:** What's the story you're adding to the facts — and might it be wrong?
- **Your move:** Write the version of events that complicates your story. Sit with it.
- **Practice:** Your read isn't the truth — it's a story about it. Draft the version that troubles yours.
- **Flavor:** *Certainty is the first thing to get suspicious of.*
- **Avoid:** Certainty · The single story · Defending the narrative over accuracy
- **How it slips:** Righteous distortion · Truth bent to fit the story
- **Apps:** *An outrage you're sure about* — "You've got the villain and the arc worked out. Before you broadcast, write the version where it's more complicated — not to excuse it, to be accurate. Righteous and wrong is still wrong." · *A conflict at work* — "You know exactly who's to blame. Draft the account a fair observer would write. If yours can't survive that, it wasn't the truth — it was your case."

## `CLEAN-RA-REGENT` — What Awareness Lacks
- **Q:** Awareness isn't moving anything — what capability is actually missing?
- **Your move:** Name the capability awareness needs next — to act, to connect, to organize — and build that.
- **Practice:** When knowing isn't changing anything, stop adding knowing. Find the missing move.
- **Flavor:** *Awareness is the map, not the mileage.*
- **Avoid:** More awareness as the answer to everything · Willpower over capacity · Confusing knowing with doing
- **How it slips:** Awareness theater · The knowing-doing gap left unaddressed
- **Apps:** *A cause you're educated on* — "You've read everything and nothing's changed in how you act. More reading won't fix it. Name the capability you're missing — the ask, the boundary, the plan — and grow that." · *A team that 'gets it'* — "Everyone's aware and nothing moves. Awareness isn't the gap. Ask what capability the group lacks — decision, coordination, courage — and build that, not another training."

## `CLEAN-RA-ARCHITECT` — From Outrage to Aim  *(de-jargoned)*
- **Q:** Before you speak, do you let the charge cool, aim it somewhere useful, or set it down?
- **Your move:** Pick one — let it cool, aim it, or set it down — then write the message from there.
- **Practice:** Don't publish a raw charge. Move it first: let it ripen, redirect it, or release it.
- **Flavor:** *Outrage is ore, not a message. Refine it first.*
- **Avoid:** Venting as advocacy · Suppressing the charge · Performative calm
- **How it slips:** Dumping charge on an audience · Sanitized into meaninglessness
- **Apps:** *A post written in anger* — "The draft is hot and feels righteous. Before it goes out, decide: let it cool overnight, aim the anger at a clear ask, or release it in private first. Raw charge repels the people you need." · *A meeting that lit you up* — "You want to fire back. Refine the charge into one clean sentence — a boundary, a question, a name for what happened — instead of spraying the heat across the room."
- *Note: replaces the transcend/translate/neutralize wording (audit-flagged jargon) with plain language; the glossary still teaches the formal terms.*

## `CLEAN-RA-DIPLOMAT` — The Tone That Reaches
- **Q:** What tone would let this truth actually reach the people you want to change?
- **Your move:** Re-aim the message from a tone that opens people instead of bracing them.
- **Practice:** Pick the tone for who you want to reach, not for how you feel. Invite, don't indict.
- **Flavor:** *You can be right in a tone no one can hear.*
- **Avoid:** Shaming the audience · Purity tone · Despair-casting
- **How it slips:** Messages that repel the persuadable · Preaching only to the choir
- **Apps:** *A post aimed at 'them'* — "It's technically true and lands like an accusation. Rewrite it for the person you actually want to move — warmer, curious, an open door — not the version that vents at them." · *A hard conversation* — "You could come in hot and correct. Instead, aim for the tone that keeps them in the room: 'here's what I'm seeing, help me understand yours.'"

## `CLEAN-RA-SAGE` — What the Truth Taught
- **Q:** What's the one lesson from this you'll actually keep?
- **Your move:** Write the single insight you'll carry past this moment. One sentence.
- **Practice:** Before you post the hot take, mine the lasting lesson. Insight outlives outrage.
- **Flavor:** *A hot take expires. An insight compounds.*
- **Avoid:** Rushing to post · The hot take over the insight · Closing too soon
- **How it slips:** Lessons unlearned and repeated · Shallow takes
- **Apps:** *After a hard week* — "The instinct is to post the reaction. Instead, write the one thing this taught you that you'll still believe next month. That's the BAR worth keeping; the take was just weather." · *A conflict that's resolving* — "Before you move on, name the durable lesson — about the pattern, about yourself — in a sentence you can reuse. Otherwise you'll learn it again the hard way."

# GROW-RA (develop the capacity to see / speak) — Witness

## `GROW-RA-CHALLENGER` — The Edge of Speaking
- **Q:** What's the true sentence you're still too scared to say?
- **Your move:** Name the sentence you're scared to say. That's your edge — say it once, to one person.
- **Practice:** Growth lives at the sentence that scares you. Find it, then close the gap to saying it.
- **Flavor:** *The sentence you're avoiding is the assignment.*
- **Avoid:** Safe statements that aren't the edge · Hiding behind nuance · Practicing the brave thing you already do
- **How it slips:** Training comfortable speech, avoiding the edge · Calling your comfort zone "strategy"
- **Apps:** *In a group you're part of* — "There's a thing you believe and haven't said because it might cost you. That's the rep. Say it once, plainly, to one person who needs to hear it — and notice you survived." · *With someone close* — "You keep almost telling them the real thing. The edge isn't more nuance — it's the plain sentence you keep swallowing. Say that one."

## `GROW-RA-REGENT` — Practice the Hard Conversation
- **Q:** Which communication skill — listening, naming, calling in — deserves real practice?
- **Your move:** Pick one rep — a weekly hard conversation — and put it on the calendar.
- **Practice:** Skill comes from reps, not resolve. Schedule the hard conversation you keep hoping to be ready for.
- **Flavor:** *Bravery you schedule beats bravery you wait for.*
- **Avoid:** Hoping skill appears · One brave post, then silence · Waiting to feel ready
- **How it slips:** Inconsistency · Practicing the wrong thing
- **Apps:** *At work* — "You keep meaning to raise the thing. Meaning to isn't practice. Put one hard conversation on the calendar this week — a real time, a real person — and treat it as a rep, not a verdict." · *In your circle* — "You want to be someone who speaks up. Pick one recurring rep — a weekly 'here's what I actually think' — and do it on schedule until it stops feeling like a cliff."

## `GROW-RA-ARCHITECT` — Sharpen the Signal
- **Q:** Which one communication skill, sharpened, would make your truth land better?
- **Your move:** Pick the highest-leverage skill — clarity, story, or listening — and drill it this week.
- **Practice:** Don't spread practice thin. Sharpen the one skill that makes everything else land.
- **Flavor:** *A sharper signal beats a louder one.*
- **Avoid:** Spreading practice thin · Vanity skills · Polishing the irrelevant
- **How it slips:** Growth without leverage · Getting better at the thing that wasn't the problem
- **Apps:** *Your posts don't land* — "You keep adding more — more facts, more length. Instead pick one lever — telling it as one concrete story — and drill only that for a month. Sharper beats more." · *In meetings* — "You have good points that don't move people. Choose the single skill that would change that — brevity, naming the stakes, or listening first — and practice that one deliberately."

## `GROW-RA-DIPLOMAT` — Speaking Without Burning
- **Q:** As you get bolder, how does your louder voice land on the people around you?
- **Your move:** Name one relationship your bolder voice affects. Tend it on purpose.
- **Practice:** Grow your voice without torching your people. Bring them with you as you get louder.
- **Flavor:** *A bridge burned is a voice with nowhere to go.*
- **Avoid:** Bravery that burns bridges needlessly · Leaving people behind
- **How it slips:** Truth-telling that isolates · Collateral harm
- **Apps:** *As you speak up more* — "Your new boldness is landing hard on someone who's actually on your side. Notice who, and tend that relationship on purpose — bravery that isolates you isn't strength, it's a slow exit." · *In your family* — "You've found your voice, and it's straining the people closest. You don't have to soften the truth — but bring them along: 'this matters to me, and so do you.'"

## `GROW-RA-SAGE` — Who Truth-Telling Makes You
- **Q:** Who are you becoming as someone who sees and names what's real?
- **Your move:** Finish it: "I am becoming someone who can see and say ______." Then live one line of it today.
- **Practice:** Let truth-telling reshape you, not just your posts. Notice who you're becoming and integrate it.
- **Flavor:** *You become the truths you're willing to say.*
- **Avoid:** Skipping reflection · A righteous new mask · A new identity worn like a costume
- **How it slips:** Capacity gained, self un-integrated · A bolder voice, the same blind spots
- **Apps:** *After a season of speaking up* — "You've gotten braver — but is it integrated, or a role you play online? Name who you're actually becoming, and check it against how you are with the people who see you offstage." · *A value you champion* — "You post about it constantly. The rep isn't the post — it's becoming the person who lives it when no one's watching. Name the gap, honestly, and close a little of it."
